## What Is Mnemosyne?

Mnemosyne gives any AI assistant **26,000× more context** — locally, privately, and for free. No account required. No ads. No strings attached.

Your AI today forgets everything the moment the chat window closes. Mnemosyne writes a persistent substrate alongside every session, then feeds that substrate back as a structured prefix on your next one. The result: your AI remembers your projects, your preferences, your ongoing work — across apps, across sessions, across reboots.

**One-click install. Yours to keep.**

## Installation

### Windows
Download the NSIS installer from the [Releases page](https://github.com/liana-banyan/mnemosyne/releases):

```
Mnemosyne-Setup-0.1.11.exe
```

Run the installer and follow the prompts. Mnemosyne starts automatically on login (opt-out available in Settings).

### macOS
Download the DMG from the [Releases page](https://github.com/liana-banyan/mnemosyne/releases):

```
Mnemosyne-0.1.11.dmg
```

Open the DMG, drag Mnemosyne to your Applications folder, and launch.

## How It Works

Mnemosyne maintains a local **Cathedral substrate** — a structured, compressed index of everything you've worked on — stored in a folder on your machine. When you open a new AI session, Mnemosyne injects a distilled summary of relevant past context as a prefix, expanding your effective working memory from ~4 MB (a typical Claude session) to ~103 GB of indexed substrate (the 26,000× Banyan Metric™ ratio).

The substrate never leaves your device. Mnemosyne uses no cloud sync, no telemetry, and no third-party AI services. It's local-first by design.
