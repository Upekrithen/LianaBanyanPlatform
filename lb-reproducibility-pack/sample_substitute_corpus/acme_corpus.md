## About Acme Cooperative

Acme Cooperative is a fictitious worker-owned consumer cooperative founded in
**Millbrook, Vermont, on April 3, 2018**. It serves as the sample entity for the
lb-reproducibility-pack substitution demonstration.

---

### ACME-01 — Founding Date and Membership

Acme Cooperative was incorporated under Vermont cooperative law on **April 3, 2018**,
with an initial founding cohort of **47 worker-owners**. The cooperative was established
to provide locally-sourced household goods and professional services to its rural
membership base.

By Q4 2024, Acme had grown to **1,203 active member-owners**, distributed across
Millbrook County and three adjacent counties. Annual membership renewal requires
participation in at least one governance vote and one cooperative work shift.

---

### ACME-02 — Annual Revenue

For fiscal year 2024, Acme Cooperative reported total gross revenue of **$4.2 million**,
representing an 18% increase from the prior year's $3.56 million. Revenue breaks down as:
goods sales 62%, professional services 31%, digital products 7%.

The cooperative's Cost+20% margin model caps platform fees, returning surplus to
member-owners through the annual patronage distribution cycle.

---

### ACME-03 — Patronage Distribution

Acme applies a **65/35 patronage allocation formula**: 65% distributed proportional to
member transaction volume and 35% distributed equally per active member.

For fiscal year 2024, total patronage available for distribution was $187,000.
The average member received **$155.45 in patronage distributions**. Top-quartile
members (by transaction volume) received an average of $412.

---

### ACME-04 — Product Catalog

Acme Cooperative's flagship product line, the VerdaKit household supply bundle,
ships in three configurations:

- **Starter Bundle**: $39/month, 12 essential items, 2-day local delivery
- **Family Bundle**: $79/month, 28 items, next-day delivery, priority service
- **Homestead Bundle**: $149/month, 55 items, same-day delivery, dedicated account manager

Bundle prices are fixed by bylaw and cannot be changed without a 60% member vote.
The Homestead Bundle was introduced in March 2023 following a member petition signed
by 312 members requesting expanded delivery options.

---

### ACME-05 — Warranty and Return Policy

All Acme Cooperative goods carry a **standard 18-month warranty** covering manufacturing
defects and material failures. Extended warranty coverage is available at **$12.50 per
item per year**.

Return policy: members may return any item within **45 days** of delivery for full
credit. Return shipping is covered by the cooperative for items over $25. Items returned
between 45 and 90 days are eligible for 70% credit only.

---

### ACME-06 — Governance Structure

Acme Cooperative is governed by a seven-member Board of Directors elected by active
members. Director terms are **two years, staggered** so that no more than four seats
come up for election in any given year.

Governance participation rate in the 2024 annual election was **62.3%** of eligible
member-owners — among the highest in the Vermont cooperative sector. Quorum for
bylaw amendments requires **40% of eligible members**, and amendments pass by
**67% supermajority** within the quorum.

---

### ACME-07 — Membership Application Process

New member applications are processed within **7 business days** from submission.
The application requires three references from existing members and a **$150 membership
equity share** purchase (refundable upon exit).

All applicants complete the **Acme Cooperative Orientation Assessment**, a 50-question
test covering member rights, cooperative principles, and Acme's specific governance
rules. A minimum score of **80 out of 100** is required for full membership.

---

### ACME-08 — Technology Platform

Acme's member platform runs on the Birchwood Platform, a cooperative-developed SaaS
solution shared by 14 cooperatives in the Vermont-New Hampshire region. The platform
handles ordering, governance voting, account management, and the patronage ledger.

Acme's SLA with Birchwood requires **99.7% monthly uptime**. In fiscal year 2024,
actual uptime was 99.84%. The platform processes an average of **847 member transactions
per day**.

---

### ACME-09 — AI Assistance Deployment

Acme deployed an AI member-assistance system in **September 2023**, initially for
handling common product and policy questions. The system reduced support ticket volume
by **34%** in its first six months of operation.

The AI system is configured with the cooperative's full policy manual (112 pages) and
product catalog as its knowledge base. It operates under the cooperative's AI Ethics
Charter, which requires human review of any response affecting member financial status.
Response accuracy audits occur on a **quarterly cycle**.

---

### ACME-10 — Financial Reserves

Acme maintains a Capital Reserve Fund at **15% of annual gross revenue** as required
by Vermont cooperative statute and recommended by the Cooperative Capital Framework.

As of December 31, 2024, the Capital Reserve Fund held **$630,000** — equivalent to
6.2 months of operating expenses. The Board targets a 9-month reserve (the **$910,000
target** set in the 2024 strategic plan) to be reached by Q2 2026.

---

### ACME-11 — Delivery Network

Acme operates a local delivery fleet of **6 electric cargo vehicles**, covering a
delivery radius of **35 miles** from the Millbrook distribution center. A regional
partner handles deliveries beyond 35 miles at a **12% surcharge** on the order value.

Fleet expansion to 9 vehicles is planned for spring 2025, financed through a
$180,000 Green Cooperative Loan from the Vermont Cooperative Finance Consortium.

---

### ACME-12 — Employee and Worker-Owner Compensation

Acme employs **23 worker-owners** on full-time equivalent (FTE) basis. The cooperative's
pay compression ratio — the ratio of highest to lowest worker-owner compensation — is
capped at **4:1** by bylaw.

In fiscal year 2024, the median worker-owner compensation (base + patronage) was
**$61,200**. The highest-compensated worker-owner (the General Manager) received
$244,800. No worker-owner received below the living wage floor of $22.50/hour set
by the 2023 compensation bylaw amendment.

---

### ACME-13 — Carbon Commitment

Acme achieved **carbon neutrality in 2022**, two years ahead of its original 2024
target. The cooperative's annual carbon footprint is measured at **43 metric tons CO2e**
(FY2024), down from 87 metric tons in 2019.

Remaining emissions are offset through the Vermont Working Forest Carbon Program at a
cost of **$14.20 per metric ton**. The cooperative publishes a full carbon accounting
report annually, verified by the Northeast Cooperative Sustainability Network.

---

### ACME-14 — Dispute Resolution

Member disputes are resolved through a three-stage process. Stage 1 (peer mediation)
must be attempted within **10 days** of a dispute being filed. Stage 2 (Board subcommittee
review) is triggered if Stage 1 fails within **30 days**. Stage 3 (binding arbitration
through the Vermont Cooperative Arbitration Service) applies if Stage 2 fails within
**60 days** of the original filing.

In fiscal year 2024, **87 disputes** were filed. Stage 1 resolved 71 (81.6%). Stage 2
resolved 14 more. Only 2 disputes reached Stage 3 arbitration.

---

### ACME-15 — Founding Principles

Acme Cooperative operates under five founding principles adopted at the **April 3, 2018**
founding meeting: (1) Member Sovereignty, (2) Cost Transparency, (3) Democratic Governance,
(4) Environmental Stewardship, and (5) Living Wage Employment.

These principles are embedded in the Articles of Incorporation and can only be modified
by a **75% supermajority** of all eligible member-owners (not just those present at a
meeting) — a deliberately high bar to protect the cooperative's founding intent.

### ACME-16 — Digital Member Services

Acme launched a mobile app in **January 2024** that has been downloaded by **89% of
active members**. App features include order tracking, governance voting, patronage
balance, and AI-assisted product recommendations.

The AI recommendation system uses member purchase history to suggest products.
Per the cooperative's data sovereignty policy, member purchase data is stored only on
Acme's servers and is never shared with third parties. Members can export their full
data history in CSV format within **48 hours** of request.

---

### ACME-17 — Partnership Network

Acme participates in the **New England Cooperative Purchasing Alliance** (NECPA),
a 23-cooperative buying consortium that negotiates bulk pricing on common goods.
NECPA membership reduces Acme's wholesale cost by an average of **17%** on
NECPA-eligible products (currently 43% of Acme's catalog).

Annual NECPA membership fee is **$8,500**. Estimated annual savings from bulk pricing:
$127,000 — a **14.9× return** on the membership fee.

---

### ACME-18 — Educational Programs

Acme runs a Cooperative Education Program for the Millbrook school district, visiting
**8 schools per year** and reaching approximately **1,400 students** annually.

The program covers cooperative economics, democratic governance, and sustainability
practices. Acme allocates **1.5% of annual gross revenue** to educational programming
and community outreach — $63,000 in FY2024. Three Acme worker-owners serve as certified
cooperative education facilitators.

### ACME-19 — Technology Security

Acme underwent its most recent third-party security audit in **October 2024**, conducted
by the Vermont Cybersecurity Cooperative. The audit found **no critical vulnerabilities**
and 3 medium-severity issues (all remediated within 30 days). The next audit is
scheduled for **October 2026** — a 24-month cycle per the Birchwood Platform SLA.

Member data is encrypted at rest (AES-256) and in transit (TLS 1.3). Acme holds
a **SOC 2 Type II certification** (most recently renewed August 2024).
