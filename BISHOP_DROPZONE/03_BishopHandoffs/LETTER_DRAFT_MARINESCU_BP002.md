## LETTER BODY (Knight scaffold — Founder revoices at prose-pass; 350-700 word target)

Dear Prof. Marinescu,

Your work on labor market monopsony — particularly the mechanisms by which online labor markets concentrate employer power even when they appear competitive — identified the structural problem before the AI shock arrived. I have been building toward the structural solution.

The problem you documented: market concentration in online labor platforms erodes worker bargaining power in ways invisible to standard wage statistics. The AI version of this problem is not just concentration — it is **exit elimination.** When AI substitutes for an entire skill category, there is no adjacent employer to walk to. The labor market friction that protected workers has evaporated, and no amount of retraining restores the wage floor.

The architecture I have built does not assume the wage floor returns. It builds a new one.

Liana Banyan is a Wyoming cooperative corporation — a member-owned economic operating system with three currencies:

- **Credits** — transaction medium ($1 = 1 Credit equivalent)
- **Marks** — effort-differential currency; earned through participation; governance weight scales with them; they do not expire
- **Joules** — surplus; a "forever stamp" — once contributed, the record stands

An AI worker who participates in cooperative platforms — food, retail, health, professional services — builds a participation record that compounds into infrastructure ownership. The floor is something the worker built, not something given to them. **The cooperative is not UBI. It is not wage insurance. It is a member-owned floor that rises with participation.**

The mechanism you might find most relevant: because Marks are earned through effort (not purchased), the cooperative structure resists the monopsony failure mode. There is no single employer of Marks. Governance weight — the lever that controls price-setting and term-setting — accumulates across all cooperative participation, not just one platform. A worker displaced from one initiative carries her record into the next. **The labor market power asymmetry does not transfer.**

I am writing to you as a member of Anthropic's Economic Advisory Council because the cooperative substrate I have built is relevant to the research agenda you are advising on. The AI systems your advisory work shapes will determine whether the permanent shock creates a monopsonistic floor or whether there is an architectural alternative. I believe there is.

*W-012: "All the ants can work alongside the Grasshoppers and still be profitable for all. Interdependency."* — The cooperative model is not zero-sum. Efficient AI adoption and worker floor are not competing goods when the architecture routes the surplus to both.
