## 2. The Bounty Sizing Scenario (from Founder turn B111)

**Scenario A — 10,000 testers, $100 avg Credit purchase, 1:1 matching Marks (loyalty-reward only, not patent-backed per B111 correction):**
- Credits purchased: $1,000,000 (operating revenue to platform via Cost+20% structure)
- Matching Marks issued: $1,000,000 face value (loyalty-reward instrument — NOT securities, NOT patent-backed per B111 Founder ratification)
- Dilution impact on Pedestal Stake backing: **Zero.** Marks are not a claim on patent cash flow. They're loyalty tokens redeemable only for cooperative services. This was Bishop's pre-B111-correction error; corrected now.

**Scenario B — 10,000 testers, $100 avg Credit purchase, + 1,000 of them also buy a Pedestal Stake at $500 avg:**
- Pedestal Stake issuance: $500,000 against $900M–$1.3B/yr patent-portfolio cash-flow backing
- Dilution: $500K / $900M to $500K / $1.3B = **0.04% – 0.06% of annual backing**
- Safe. Well within the "1–2% per year maximum issuance discipline" Bishop recommends for Pedestal dilution.

**Scenario C — scale up. 100,000 testers, 10,000 Pedestal purchasers at $500 avg:**
- Credits revenue: $10M/yr
- Matching Marks: $10M face value (still non-securities)
- Pedestal issuance: $5M against $900M–$1.3B backing = **0.4% – 0.6% of annual backing**
- Still safe.

## 6. What to watch for (red-flag tracking)

- **Mark issuance exceeding 2× Credit purchases** — if Marks are given out faster than Credits flow in, we're diluting loyalty value without backing. Trigger for review.
- **Pedestal Stake issuance exceeding 1% of annual patent-portfolio backing in any single quarter** — too-fast dilution of investor-adjacent exposure. Trigger for Upekrithen LLC review + counsel consult.
- **Witness Program participation dropping below 10 independent replicators in any month post-launch** — signals the external-replication flywheel is stalling; need to rework recruitment framing or add incentives.
- **R10 (and later R11) replication variance exceeding 10% between canonical Bishop-run and median community-run** — signals either grader bias in the canonical run or tooling gap in community runs. Either way it's a paper-level finding worth surfacing.
