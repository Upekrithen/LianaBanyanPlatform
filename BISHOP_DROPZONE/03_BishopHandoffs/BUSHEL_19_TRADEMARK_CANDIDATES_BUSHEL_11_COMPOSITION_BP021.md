## Tier-1 Trademark Candidates

### 1. THE SCALES ARE VISIBLE
**Class:** Slogan-class / Tier-1 mark
**Context:** Platform governance transparency architecture.
The canonical phrase expressing the member-visible rubric commitment. "The scales
are visible" is a structurally novel UX posture: the evaluation criteria applied to
every content decision are publicly displayed, not hidden in a black-box algorithm.

**Distinctiveness argument:**
No existing platform governance system publishes its content-evaluation rubric in
real-time to the users it governs. This is architecturally novel AND phrasally distinct.

**Use in commerce:** Helm portal route /helm/scales-rubric. Displayed as page title,
marketing copy ("You can see exactly what the scales weigh"). Characterizes the Liana
Banyan governance philosophy company-wide.

**ICC Classification:** Class 42 (technology services), Class 35 (cooperative business services).

---

### 2. MEMBER-VISIBLE VERDICT
**Class:** Tier-1 mark
**Context:** The verdict-history UX product surface.
Refers to the architectural commitment that every governance verdict issued about a
member's content is displayed back to that member, not siloed in admin dashboards.

**Distinctiveness argument:**
Standard content-moderation platforms hide verdicts or issue opaque "policy violation"
notices. "Member-Visible Verdict" is both a product name and a governance philosophy.

**Use in commerce:** Helm portal route /helm/verdicts. UI page header. Marketing copy.

**ICC Classification:** Class 42 (technology platform services), Class 38 (communications services).

---

### 3. APPEAL VIA DECREE-COMPOSITION
**Class:** Tier-2 mark
**Context:** The Mordecai-Esther appeal mechanism.
The appeal mechanism's canonical name. Members don't merely "appeal" — they author a
"decree-composition" with co-equal authority. The phrasing captures the legal-class
weight of the mechanism (named for the Book of Esther: permanent royal decrees cannot
be reversed, only added to).

**Distinctiveness argument:**
This is a specific coined phrase for a specific legal mechanism. No prior art found.
The "decree-composition" framing is unique to this platform's IP.

**Use in commerce:** Helm portal appeal UI, help documentation, canonical product name.

**ICC Classification:** Class 42, Class 45 (legal services / dispute resolution).
