## Governing Sequence Principle

**"Prove it first. Product it second."**

The first wave of Substack posts maps to the empirical claim ladder. A post about a number lands after the number is real. A post about an architectural capability lands after that capability is demonstrated. Content that doesn't have empirics yet is labeled "conceptual" and posts in the "framing / essay" register.

This sequence is not about restraint. It is about the editorial voice standard: we can only publish what we can defend.

## Cross-Recommendation Strategy (Post-Launch)

After reaching 100 subscribers, reach out to 3–5 AI newsletter peers for recommendation exchange. Target publications with:
- Empirical orientation (not AI hype)
- Independent/solo author (not VC-backed)
- Technical OR cooperative-economy focus

Do NOT pursue cross-recommendation with:
- Publications that frame AI as a product-tier race ("best AI tools this week")
- Publications that are marketing vehicles for a specific vendor
- Publications that have monetized their recommendation slot (pay-to-recommend)

The recommendation flywheel is organic or it doesn't serve the editorial voice.
