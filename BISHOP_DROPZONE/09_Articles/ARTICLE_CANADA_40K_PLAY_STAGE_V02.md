# Canada 40K: You Have a Play. I Have a Stage.

## 42,200 Dreams. One Platform. A Different Path Forward.

*Op-Ed — April 2026 (refreshed June 2026)*

————————————————————————————————————————

Canada's Bill C-12 (*Strengthening Canada's Immigration System and Borders Act*) received Royal Assent on March 26, 2026. It gave Canada's immigration ministry the statutory authority to cancel Start-Up Visa applications en masse — and the hard deadline is now in force: commitment certificate holders must apply by **June 30, 2026**.

42,200 entrepreneurs — people who uprooted their lives, invested their savings, bet on a dream — are now stranded.

Some came from India. Some from Pakistan. Some from Nigeria. They came because Canada promised a path: build a company, create jobs, become Canadian.

Then Canada changed its mind.

————————————————————————————————————————

## What Happens Now?

Most of these founders are stuck in limbo. Their commitment certificates are expiring. Their startups are in jeopardy. The investors they relied on are pulling back.

The conventional wisdom says: go home, or find another country willing to take a chance on you.

But what if there were a third option?

Not rescue in the sense of charity. Infrastructure. A stage.

————————————————————————————————————————

## You Have a Play. I Have a Stage.

You already know how to build. You've proven you can execute — you navigated the SUV application process, assembled a team, got commitment letters from recognized incubators. Canada didn't cancel your talent. They canceled the bureaucratic path that was supposed to recognize it.

**Liana Banyan is a cooperative platform that doesn't care where your visa is issued.**

Here's what we offer:

**A platform where you keep 83.3% of everything you earn.** On a $500 transaction, you receive $416.67. The platform margin is permanently locked at Cost + 20% — it's written into the operating agreement and cannot increase.

**Real ownership.** Blockchain-verified medallions that represent actual equity. Contribute to the platform, earn ownership stakes. This isn't a gig economy that uses you up. It's a cooperative that grows with you.

**A global network.** The platform doesn't care where you're located. A developer in Lagos has the same access as a developer in London. A designer in Karachi competes on quality, not geography.

**Fifteen initiatives that support entrepreneurs.** From Village Savings & Loans (VSL) for microfinancing to Let's Make Bread for distributed manufacturing to Didasko for cooperative education — infrastructure that helps you build real businesses.

————————————————————————————————————————

## Why This Matters

I know what it's like to be stranded. Christmas Eve 1992, I opened a letter that told me I'd been expelled from college. Lost my dorm room, lost my R.A. job, lost everything at once — while home in Montana for the holidays. I slept in my Jeep for three days, then rented a one-bedroom apartment with a missing wall for $175 a month — about half my income. The landlord covered the gap with taped tarp.

42,200 entrepreneurs just opened their version of that letter.

The Canada 40K crisis exposes something broken in how we think about entrepreneurship. The current model assumes you need permission: a visa that depends on investors who may or may not keep their commitments, government programs that can be canceled by statute, gatekeepers who decide whether your dream is worth a stage.

What if the stage came first?

What if entrepreneurs could build real businesses without being dependent on gatekeepers?

What if ownership was distributed from the start — so success didn't require concentrating power in a few hands?

What if the platform that supported your work was something you actually owned a piece of?

————————————————————————————————————————

## The Model

**Cost + 20%** — The platform margin is fixed. No bait-and-switch.

**83.3% to creators** — You keep the vast majority of what you earn.

**$5 entry** — The barrier is low enough that anyone can try.

**Medallion ownership** — Contribute, earn equity, have a real stake.

**Permanent charitable funding** — Fifteen initiatives funded automatically through commerce.

This isn't a promise. It's architecture.

————————————————————————————————————————

## The Three-Gear Currency

One of our most important innovations: a currency system that works for entrepreneurs from any economy.

**The Problem:** A $10 platform fee represents hours of labor in some economies, minutes in others. Traditional platforms either exclude people from weaker economies or create complex pricing tiers that don't really work.

**Our Solution:** Three interconnected currencies (Credits, Marks, Joules) that automatically adjust for economic differences. Everyone has equal purchasing power inside the platform, regardless of where they're located.

A Nigerian entrepreneur and a Swiss banker both receive the same internal value for their equivalent local payment. The system handles the adjustment automatically.
