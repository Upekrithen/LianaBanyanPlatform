## Section 1 — Field of Invention

This invention relates to cooperative-platform governance and quality-control architectures with member-visible verdict UX. More specifically, it relates to a trio of structurally-distinct governance primitives — Bouncer (gate-keeping at member-action boundary, fast-pass whitelist class), Scales (criteria-weighing across multiple weighted axes producing verdict-issuance), and Judge (appellate authority resolving Scales-escalations and setting precedent) — coupled with a member-visible Verdict UX that exposes governance reasoning to the gated party.

The invention addresses two structural tensions long present in governance architectures: (i) gating-class authority typically operates as a black box (member sees BLOCK without seeing why; appellate path opaque); (ii) automated gates produce false-positives that erode member trust without principled appeal. The invention resolves both tensions structurally.

---

## Section 2 — Background / Prior Art

Prior art in this field comprises:

- **Content moderation systems** (social-platform class) — gate decisions opaque to gated party; appeal mechanisms procedural and editorial-class authority concentrated; no separation of fast-pass / weighing / appellate functions.
- **CI/CD gate systems** (software engineering class) — automated gates with logged failures but no member-facing verdict UX; appeal is "fix the underlying issue" not "appeal the gate."
- **Judicial-system metaphors in computing** — bouncer, judge, jury patterns appear in folklore but are typically uni-directional (gate decides, gated party loses) without the criteria-weighing-with-rubric-display pattern.
- **The Augur Living Gate** (LB #2314 KN038 BP004) — base gating primitive that this invention augments; produces BLOCK decisions without internal appellate path; BP011 empirical receipt established false-positive blocks on quotation-context content (Pawn-research-quotation Augur-Securities-Language false-positive).
- **Existing LB Catechist Scribe** (#2313 KN036) — sister-class judging Scribe with self-grading function; the Bouncer-Scales-Judge trio extends the judging-Scribe pattern from self-grading to other-grading with appellate escalation.

The novel combination is a **member-visible governance trio** — Bouncer (whitelist fast-pass) → Scales (criteria-weighing verdict-issuer) → Judge (appellate precedent-setter) — with a member-facing Verdict UX exposing the criteria, weights, verdict, and precedent at routes `/helm/verdicts`, `/helm/miner-grading`, `/helm/scales-rubric` such that the gated party sees the reasoning in real time and can engage the appellate path with principled grounds.

## Section 3 — Summary of the Invention

A method and system for cooperative-platform governance with member-visible verdict UX where:

(i) a **Bouncer Scribe** functions as first-line low-cost fast-pass — whitelist-class gating that lets clearly-OK content pass even when an upstream automated gate has blocked it; cheaper than weighing; lookup-only authority;
(ii) a **Scales Scribe** functions as criteria-weighing verdict-issuer — applies a rubric of weighted criteria to produce one of three verdict classes: PASS / BLOCK / JUDGE-escalate; verdict + criteria + weights logged as Pheromone-Anchored Decision (KN050 schema);
(iii) a **Judge Scribe** functions as appellate authority — resolves Scales JUDGE-escalation cases; sets new precedent (criteria YAML or Bouncer registry update queued); cannot override Founder-direct rules without Founder ratification;
(iv) a **member-visible Verdict UX** exposes the trio's reasoning to the gated party at production routes `/helm/verdicts`, `/helm/miner-grading`, `/helm/scales-rubric`;
(v) the trio composes with #2314 Augur Living Gate as the appellate-class extension — Augur block triggers Bouncer fast-pass attempt, Scales weighing if Bouncer doesn't recognize, Judge escalation on weighing-conflict;
(vi) verdict + criteria + weights stamp to Year of Jubilee append-only ledger (#2308) under BC / AD calendar convention — verdicts are forever-stamped and auditable;
(vii) transparency-as-defense composes with Powered Work Loader Exoskeleton + X-Ray Mode — the gated party sees the reasoning, which makes the gate harder to circumvent (gating-by-clarity) and harder to weaponize (gating-by-public-criteria);
(viii) Reminder Scribe and Maintenance Scribe sister-class primitives compose alongside — Reminder enforces the gate's prerequisites; Maintenance reconciles synchronization debt between gate-state and runtime-state;
(ix) **BC / AD calendar convention** governs the time-axis canonical labeling for Verdict ledger entries.

## Section 4 — Detailed Description

### 4.1 The trio (canonical structural decomposition)

| Scribe | Function | Authority | Implementation class |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Bouncer** | First-line low-cost fast-pass; whitelist-class | Pass-through only; cannot block; routes unrecognized cases to Scales | YAML safe-pattern registry + quick regex/scribe-id check; <10ms decision |
| **Scales** | Criteria-weighing verdict-issuer; PASS / BLOCK / JUDGE-escalate | Pass / Block authority within Augur-class blocks; cannot override Founder-direct rules | Python module `librarian-mcp/src/scribes/scales.py`; criteria YAML at `scales_criteria_v1.yaml`; verdict scribe-log to `Scales` scribe-id |
| **Judge** | Appellate authority; resolves Scales-escalations; sets precedent | Can override Scales (creates new precedent → criteria YAML update queued); can override Bouncer (creates new safe-pattern → registry update queued); cannot override Founder-direct rules | Python module `librarian-mcp/src/scribes/judge.py`; precedent YAML at `judge_precedents_v1.yaml`; Founder appeal CLI |

### 4.3 The trio flow (canonical)

```
Member action attempt
   ↓
[Augur Living Gate (#2314 KN038)]
   ├── PASS → action proceeds
   └── BLOCK
        ↓
   [Bouncer Scribe] — known-safe pattern lookup
        ├── known-safe → action proceeds (override Augur with reason; logged)
        └── unknown
             ↓
        [Scales Scribe] — criteria weighing
             ├── PASS verdict → action proceeds (criteria + weights logged; member sees at /helm/verdicts)
             ├── BLOCK verdict → action rejected (member sees reasoning at /helm/verdicts; appeal path available)
             └── JUDGE verdict → escalate
                  ↓
             [Judge Scribe] — appellate decision
                  ├── PASS → action proceeds; new precedent recorded; Bouncer registry updated; Scales criteria updated; member sees decision + precedent at /helm/verdicts
                  └── BLOCK → action rejected; Founder ratifies or appeals; member sees decision + appellate-path-status at /helm/verdicts
```

### 4.4 The Scales criteria rubric (BP011 starter set; member-visible at `/helm/scales-rubric`)

| Criterion | Weight | Pro-pass | Pro-block |
|---|---|---|---|

| **Negation context** | High | "Zero X", "no Y", "anti-Z" structure | Affirmative-form usage |

| **Past-precedent same-context** | Medium | Similar prior content passed Founder ratification | No precedent or prior-rejected |

The member sees the rubric at `/helm/scales-rubric`. Transparency is the substrate — there is no hidden criterion. If a criterion is added or weight is adjusted, the member sees the change in real time.

### 4.5 Transparency-as-defense — composition with Powered Work Loader Exoskeleton + X-Ray Mode

The Verdict UX is not merely an information-disclosure feature; it composes structurally with two other LB primitives to produce a defense-stack:

- **Powered Work Loader Exoskeleton** (LB primitive — capacity-amplification framework) — exposes the underlying mechanics of member work amplification; the Verdict UX similarly exposes the underlying mechanics of governance gating
- **X-Ray Mode** (LB primitive — internal-state visualization) — surfaces internal state to the operator; the Verdict UX surfaces internal Scales reasoning to the gated party

Together: **transparency-as-defense**. Closed-platform gating relies on opacity for stability — if members saw the reasoning, they could circumvent. LB's structural inversion: open the reasoning, and gates become harder to weaponize (anyone weaponizing the gate is visible doing so), harder to false-positive (members see the false-positive reasoning and submit appeal grounds), harder to drift (gate-state and gated-party-expectation are co-evident).

### 4.6 Composition with Augur Living Gate (#2314)

The Bouncer-Scales-Judge trio is the appellate-class extension of #2314 Augur Living Gate:

- Augur is the base primitive — fires on forbidden-term detection / quotation-context exemption / Bishop-discipline-check staleness
- Augur false-positives (BP011 empirical receipt: Pawn-research-quotation Augur-Securities-Language false-positive on `BISHOP_DROPZONE\00_FOUNDER_REVIEW\PATENT_PORTFOLIO_VALUE_UPDATE_BP009_2026-05-01.md`) trigger the trio
- Bouncer fast-pass attempts known-safe-pattern recognition; Scales weighs criteria; Judge resolves on escalation
- The trio does NOT replace Augur; it augments Augur with principled appellate path

### 4.7 Composition with Catechist Scribe (#2313)

Catechist Scribe (KN036 BP005) is sister-class — it is a self-grading judging Scribe. The trio is other-grading (gating member action). The two compose:

- Catechist self-grades Bishop discipline (e.g., session-open `brief_me` consult check)
- Bouncer/Scales/Judge other-grade member action (e.g., Write attempt)
- Judge can take Catechist self-grading verdicts as criteria input (e.g., "Bishop's most recent self-grade was within the past 30s" as a Scales pro-pass weighting)

### 4.8 Composition with Reminder Scribe + Maintenance Scribe

The trio composes with two additional sister-class scribes:

- **Reminder Scribe** (BP021 R-CHECK-1 productization canon) — operationalizes prerequisite reminders; the trio's Bouncer can consult Reminder before fast-pass (e.g., "did the member just see a Reminder for this gate? if yes, weight pro-pass higher")

**Claim 1 — Three-Scribe Governance Trio Method**
A computer-implemented method for cooperative-platform governance comprising: (i) a first scribe-class module ("Bouncer") performing first-line low-cost fast-pass gate decisions via lookup against a whitelist-class safe-pattern registry, the Bouncer having pass-through-only authority and routing unrecognized cases to a second scribe-class module; (ii) a second scribe-class module ("Scales") performing criteria-weighing verdict-issuance applying a rubric of weighted criteria to produce one of PASS, BLOCK, or JUDGE-escalate verdict classes, the Scales logging verdict + criteria + weights as a Pheromone-Anchored Decision; and (iii) a third scribe-class module ("Judge") performing appellate authority resolving Scales JUDGE-escalation cases, setting precedent through registry updates, and stamping Judge decisions to an append-only ledger — wherein the Bouncer, Scales, and Judge are structurally distinct (lookup-class / weighing-class / appellate-class) and operate in sequence as a governance trio.

**Claim 2 — Member-Visible Verdict UX**
The method of Claim 1 further comprising: a member-visible verdict-UX surface presenting the trio's reasoning to the gated party at production routes `/helm/verdicts`, `/helm/miner-grading`, and `/helm/scales-rubric`; the verdict UX displaying for each Verdict event (a) the criteria the Scales weighed, (b) the weights applied, (c) the verdict outcome, and (d) the appellate path status if BLOCK or JUDGE-escalation occurred; such that the gated party sees the governance reasoning in real time and can engage the appellate path with principled grounds.

**Claim 3 — Augur Living Gate Augmentation**
The method of Claim 1 wherein the trio operates as appellate-class extension of a base gating primitive (Augur Living Gate, #2314): when the base gating primitive issues a BLOCK decision, the Bouncer fast-pass is invoked; if the Bouncer does not recognize a safe-pattern, the Scales weighing is invoked; on Scales JUDGE-escalation, the Judge resolves and may override prior decisions or set new precedent; the trio does NOT replace the base gating primitive but augments it with a principled appellate path.

**Claim 4 — Verdict Ledger Composition (Year of Jubilee #2308)**
The method of Claim 1 further comprising: stamping each Verdict event (Bouncer fast-pass, Scales verdict, Judge precedent) to a Year of Jubilee append-only ledger at the event's Chronos timestamp under BC / AD calendar convention; the ledger entries forever-stamped and auditable; running Year-of-Jubilee reconciliation cycles on the ledger without removing Verdict entries; reconciling claim-graph and provenance-graph at each Jubilee reset such that the trio's history is permanent and replayable.

**Claim 5 — Transparency-as-Defense Composition**
The method of Claim 1 wherein the member-visible verdict UX of Claim 2 composes with two further cooperative-platform primitives to constitute a defense-stack: a Powered Work Loader Exoskeleton primitive (capacity-amplification framework) and an X-Ray Mode primitive (internal-state visualization); the resulting transparency-as-defense composition makes governance gates structurally harder to weaponize (gating actors visible) and structurally harder to false-positive (gated parties see reasoning and submit principled appeal grounds) compared to opaque-gate architectures.

**Claim 6 — Sister-Scribe Composition (Catechist + Reminder + Maintenance)**
The method of Claim 1 further comprising composition with at least three sister-class scribe primitives: (a) a Catechist Scribe (#2313) self-grading Bishop discipline, the Catechist's self-grade verdicts available as input to Scales weighing; (b) a Reminder Scribe enforcing the gate's prerequisites and providing Reminder-state input to Bouncer; (c) a Maintenance Scribe reconciling synchronization debt between Scales criteria YAML and runtime state, between Bouncer registry and canonical safe-pattern list, and between Judge precedent and claim-graph.

**Claim 7 — Production Embodiment with Live Routes**
A system implementing the method of any preceding claim, embodied at production routes `/helm/verdicts`, `/helm/miner-grading`, and `/helm/scales-rubric` of a cooperative-platform Helm member-surface, the system comprising: a cooperative-platform compute substrate (LB Frame), a Year of Jubilee append-only ledger, a Pheromone-Anchored Decision substrate, a Bouncer YAML safe-pattern registry, a Scales criteria YAML, a Judge precedent YAML, and a Founder-appeal CLI mechanism; the system operating under cooperative-license + member-cooperative-ownership and BC / AD calendar convention; the system having LANDED in production at Bushel 19 commit `b5a4dee` per the bound Codex anchor.

**Claim 8 — Precedent-Setting Recursive Property**
The method of Claim 1 wherein Judge decisions create new precedent by registry updates: a Judge PASS that overrides a prior Bouncer non-recognition adds the now-recognized pattern to the Bouncer safe-pattern registry; a Judge decision that adjusts criteria-weights updates the Scales criteria YAML; the cumulative effect is that the trio's governance evolves through Judge-precedent-class composition rather than through editorial-class authority concentration — precedent-setting is structural rather than ad-hoc.

**Claim 9 — Vendor-Fungibility / AI-Agnostic Composition**
The method of any preceding claim, where the trio architecture (Bouncer YAML registry, Scales criteria YAML, Judge precedent YAML, Verdict ledger entries, Verdict UX surfaces) is AI-vendor-agnostic — the governance verdicts persist when the underlying AI compute changes; vendor substitution does not alter the canonical trio schema or the member-visible Verdict UX (per AI-Agnostic Platform Principle).
