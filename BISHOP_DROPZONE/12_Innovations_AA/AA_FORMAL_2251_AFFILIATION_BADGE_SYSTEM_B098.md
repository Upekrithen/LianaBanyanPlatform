## TL;DR

A unified group affiliation display system implementing category-based default visibility with two-sided member-controlled filters. Groups (professional, business, sports, gaming, casual, political, religious) receive default visibility settings calibrated to "splinter risk" — likelihood of provoking divisive reactions in a cooperative context. A two-sided filter enables both publishers (members choosing which badges appear on their own profile) and viewers (members choosing which badge categories they see on others' profiles) to control visibility independently. **Full cooperative participation benefits persist regardless of visibility settings**: hidden affiliations still earn Marks, grant governance voting rights, and provide access to group feeds and channels.

## Independent Claim

**Claim 1.** A computer-implemented method for displaying group affiliations of cooperative platform members, comprising:

(a) Maintaining, for each member, a set of group affiliation records, each record comprising a group identifier, a group category classification, and a visibility-preference state;

(b) Assigning default visibility-preference states per group category, wherein professional, business, sports, gaming, and casual categories default to visible and political and religious categories default to hidden;

(c) Maintaining, for each member, a set of viewer-side category-level visibility preferences controlling which category classes of other members' affiliations are displayed to the viewing member;

(d) Rendering a member profile to a requesting viewer by intersecting the profile member's publisher-side visibility preferences with the requesting viewer's viewer-side category preferences, displaying only affiliations that pass both filters;

(e) Preserving full cooperative participation rights (currency earning, governance voting, group feed access, group channel access) for all group memberships regardless of visibility preference state.

**Dependent Claim 1.1** — The method of Claim 1, wherein affiliation badges are rendered using the cooperative platform's Wardrobe Department component system, enabling equippable visual badges that integrate with avatar customization.

**Dependent Claim 1.2** — The method of Claim 1, wherein the default visibility assignment of (b) is calibrated to a discourse-risk assessment specific to cooperative contexts, distinguishing between categories likely to produce divisive reactions (political, religious) and categories unlikely to do so (professional, gaming).

**Dependent Claim 1.3** — The method of Claim 1, wherein the two-sided filter preserves publisher-side override authority such that a member may individually opt to display hidden-by-default categories or hide visible-by-default categories on their own profile regardless of the category's default state.

**Dependent Claim 1.4** — A system comprising a processor, a persistent affiliations database, a visibility-preference database, and instructions stored on a non-transitory computer-readable medium which, when executed, cause the processor to perform the method of Claim 1 through Claim 1.3.

## Prior Art Distinction

Existing social platforms (LinkedIn, Facebook, Discord) use binary or single-sided visibility controls for group memberships. No platform combines (a) two-sided filters in which both publisher and viewer control visibility, (b) category-level defaults calibrated to discourse-risk assessment, (c) preservation of full participation benefits regardless of visibility setting, and (d) integration with a cooperative reputation economics system where hidden affiliations still earn cooperative currency. The combination is novel in platform architecture.
