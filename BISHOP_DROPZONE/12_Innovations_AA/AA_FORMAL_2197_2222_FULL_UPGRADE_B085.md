### Innovation #2198: Progressive Pricing with Credit Voucher Refund

**Category**: Economics / Pricing
**Crown Jewel**: NO
**Patent Relevance**: Yes

**Description**: A pricing mechanism where early buyers of a product or service pay a higher initial price, but automatically receive Credit vouchers for the difference once the product reaches its stable Cost+20% price point. This rewards early adopters who took a chance on an unproven offering without penalizing later buyers — the early buyer ends up paying the same effective price as everyone else, but received the product sooner.

**Mechanics**: When a new product or service launches on the platform, its initial price may exceed the eventual Cost+20% floor because production costs are higher at low volume. Early purchasers pay the listed price at time of purchase. The platform records the delta between what was paid and the eventual Cost+20% stabilized price. Once the product reaches its Cost+20% threshold (verified by the platform's cost-tracking system), Credit vouchers equal to the price difference are automatically issued to all prior purchasers. These Credits are standard platform Credits — usable across the entire ecosystem but never redeemable for fiat currency. The system creates a natural incentive to buy early without the speculative dynamics of traditional early-bird pricing.

**Prior Art Distinction**: Traditional early-bird pricing rewards early buyers with lower prices, creating artificial scarcity pressure. Kickstarter-style pre-orders offer no price adjustment mechanism. No existing system automatically refunds the pricing difference as platform Credits once a product reaches its stable cost-plus-margin price, eliminating the early-adopter penalty while maintaining cooperative pricing integrity.

**Claims**:
1. A computer-implemented progressive pricing system comprising: a product cost tracking module that monitors per-unit production cost over time; an initial price recording module that stores the purchase price paid by each buyer; a price stabilization detector that identifies when a product reaches a defined cost-plus-margin threshold; and an automatic Credit voucher generation module that computes the difference between each buyer's recorded purchase price and the stabilized price, issuing closed-loop platform Credits equal to said difference to each qualifying purchaser.
2. A method for implementing progressive cooperative pricing, the method comprising: listing a product at an initial price above the eventual cost-plus-margin floor; recording each purchase transaction with buyer identity and price paid; continuously monitoring production cost per unit as volume increases; detecting when per-unit cost stabilizes at a cost-plus-twenty-percent threshold; computing a price differential for each prior purchaser; and automatically issuing non-redeemable platform Credit vouchers equal to said differential to each prior purchaser's account.
3. The system of claim 1, wherein the Credit voucher generation module further comprises a progressive issuance scheduler that distributes vouchers in tranches as the price differential narrows incrementally, rather than waiting for full stabilization, providing early buyers with partial Credit recovery as production costs decrease.

### Innovation #2207: Tab Trading Marketplace with Platform Market-Maker

**Category**: Economics / Cooperative Finance
**Crown Jewel**: NO
**Patent Relevance**: Yes

**Description**: A marketplace where cooperative tabs (inter-member service obligations) can be transferred between members with the platform acting as market-maker to ensure liquidity. The platform facilitates these transfers while maintaining economic integrity. Tabs are cooperative service obligations, not securities.

**Mechanics**: Members holding unsettled tabs can list them on the Tab Trading Marketplace. The platform's market-maker engine provides standing offers at a fair-value rate calculated from the tab's age, the debtor's settlement history (reputation), and current platform liquidity conditions. Upon transfer, the original creditor receives immediate partial settlement, the acquiring member becomes the new creditor, and the debtor is notified of the transfer. The platform's market-maker role ensures there is always a baseline offer for any listed tab, preventing illiquid lockups. All transfers are recorded on-platform with full audit trails. The market-maker does not profit from spreads — it operates at Cost+20% like all platform services, with the spread covering operational costs only.

**Prior Art Distinction**: Traditional factoring and debt trading markets operate as extractive financial instruments with significant spreads and no transparency into pricing mechanics. Peer-to-peer platforms do not allow obligation transfers. No existing system implements a cooperative tab trading marketplace with a non-extractive platform market-maker that operates at transparent cost-plus-margin, ensures baseline liquidity for all listed obligations, and maintains full audit trails within a closed-loop multi-currency economy.

**Claims**:
1.
