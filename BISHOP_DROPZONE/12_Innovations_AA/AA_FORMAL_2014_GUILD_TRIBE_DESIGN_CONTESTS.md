## Innovation #2014 — Guild and Tribe Funded Design Contests: Collective Treasury for Visual Identity Competitions

### Description

A system and method within a cooperative commerce platform wherein:

(a) Guilds (professional groups) and Tribes (geographic/interest groups) can pool Credits into a collective contest treasury, then run their OWN design competitions — for Guild Banners, group themes, mascots, icons, landing page layouts, or any visual identity element.

(b) The contest flow:
  - Guild/Tribe members contribute Credits to the contest pool (voluntary, any amount)
  - Contest is announced with a brief, deadline, and prize pool
  - Members (guild/tribe or platform-wide, depending on contest rules) submit designs
  - The guild/tribe votes using internal governance (Document Voting — reputation-only)
  - Winner receives the pooled Credits + Marks for the accepted design
  - Winning design becomes the guild/tribe's official visual identity element

(c) Contest types:
  - **Guild Banner**: The visual flag/header for the guild's pages and member profiles
  - **Guild Theme**: A complete CSS theme applied to guild members (Layer 2 in the governance hierarchy)
  - **Tribe Banner**: Geographic/interest group visual identity
  - **Tribe Theme**: Community-specific visual presentation
  - **Mascot/Icon**: Guild or tribe character or symbol
  - **Seasonal**: Holiday or event-specific visual identity refresh

(d) This creates **internal guild economies** around creative work. Guilds that pool more Credits attract more design submissions. The contest system reuses the existing Contest Pipeline (K120) infrastructure — same voting, same submission flow, same governance — applied to visual identity instead of products.

(e) Benefit flows:
  - **Contest creators (guild leadership)**: Strengthened group identity, member engagement
  - **Design submitters**: Credits from the prize pool + Marks for participation
  - **Guild/Tribe members**: Ownership of their group's visual identity through democratic choice
  - **Platform**: Design diversity, member engagement, Credits circulation

### Connection to Existing Systems
- **Contest Pipeline (K120)**: Same infrastructure, new application
- **Design Pipeline (Arena → Emporium → Crew Tables)**: Design contest winners progress in reputation
- **Tiered Theme Governance (#2013)**: Contest-winning themes slot into Layer 2 (Guild) or Layer 3 (Tribe)
- **CSS Zen Garden model (#2011)**: Guild/Tribe contests are a SCOPED version of the platform-wide design democracy

### Patent Relevance: HIGH

A cooperative commerce platform wherein professional guilds and community tribes pool cooperative currency to fund design competitions for their group's visual identity — with submissions voted on by group members, winners receiving the pooled treasury, and winning designs automatically applied as the group's theme in a layered governance hierarchy — represents a novel integration of collective funding, democratic design, and cooperative group identity.
