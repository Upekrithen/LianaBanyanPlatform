## Claim 1 -- Five Dollar Stack as economic primitive

A platform-cooperative economic structure denominated in discrete five-dollar increments, opt-in per layer, member-chosen, **non-subscription**. Member buys what they value, leaves what they don't. No tier-trap. No auto-renewal trap. Identical pricing for every member day-one through year-fifty (per Structural Bylaw, project_membership_pricing_identical_for_all.md).

## Claim 2 -- Stack composition

Each unit independently subscribable / cancellable, no bundle requirement:

| $5 unit | What it buys | Recurrence |
|---|---|---|
| LB membership | Cooperative AI infrastructure access, substrate sovereignty, voting, Pedestal-allocation eligibility | Per year |
| Vendor starter (Together / Fireworks / Anthropic / OpenAI / Groq paid -- or any future approved vendor) | Personal API key for cross-vendor cooperative AI; member runs Cathedral Effect on own data; portable across vendors via Conductor's Baton (#2277) | One-time deposit, refills as used |
| Pedestal Stake initial | 1 unit IP participation in 60/20/10/10 split | One-time allocation |
| Glass Door dispatch | Funds one Crown letter through LB outreach; member-voted target | Per dispatch |
| Cooperative Defensive Patent Pledge contribution | Co-funds Pledge legal infrastructure (#2260) | Annual |
| Patent Prosecution Defense Fund | Per-claim defensive co-fund | Per defense |
| Sphinx Federation Member certification | Trademark/attestation right to display Member badge | One-time |

Open list -- additional Stack units may be added by Bylaws process; existing units may be deprecated only with grandfathering.

## Claim 3 -- Auto-vendor enrollment for the Stack vendor-starter unit

Per Founder ratification B127: "if another place wants to be in the list, let them sign up automatically." The vendor-starter list is **self-service additive** -- any vendor offering an OpenAI-compatible API at competitive rates can register under standard cooperative terms (Cooperative Defensive Patent Pledge attestation + Member-Portability Covenant honoring) without LB vetting overhead. Vendor inclusion is by attestation, not invitation. Reduces LB operational burden; widens member choice.

## Claim 4 -- Why this powers the Token Pricing Gauge (#2309)

**Critical structural connection** (Founder ratified B127): the Five Dollar Stack vendor-starter creates the population that powers the public Token Pricing Gauge. Each member running Cathedral Effect on their own vendor key, with consent, contributes telemetry to the aggregate cross-vendor cost-per-correct-answer measurement. The Gauge becomes empirically dense in proportion to Stack adoption.

This is the **public-facing demonstration of the value of joining LB and the Federation**: not a marketing claim, but a price-discovery instrument that any third party can consult to verify cross-vendor cost-equalization claims. Members who fund their own keys *create* the Gauge by using LB. Members who consult the Gauge benefit from the data without contributing -- but the Gauge improves only when the Stack grows.

This is the **cheapest and easiest empirical demonstration of cooperative network value**: $5 in, real data out, public-good Gauge benefits everyone. It is also the strongest argument for joining the Federation -- the more vendors, the richer the Gauge, the better the price-discovery for the entire ecosystem.

## Claim 5 -- Anti-subscription as structural feature

Every existing AI-economic model defaults to either:
- Subscription tiers (ChatGPT Plus, Claude Pro, etc.) -- recurrence + tier-trap + lock-in
- Pay-per-token (API direct) -- variable cost, no community ownership

Five Dollar Stack is **neither**. It is discrete units of cooperative participation, priced symmetrically, all voluntary, all member-chosen. Pairs with Member-Portability Covenant (#2293) so members can leave any layer without leaving others.

## Claim 6 -- Identical-for-all guarantee extends to Stack units

Same Structural Bylaw that governs LB membership pricing applies to every Stack unit: identical price for the first member as the five-millionth, day-one through year-fifty, no tiers, no early-adopter pricing, no enterprise discount, no founder discount. Anchor phrase: *"Same deal for the first member as the five-millionth."*

## Claim 7 -- Composition with Keystone #46 ("Generosity Lowers the Cost of Doing Business")

The Stack is the operator-side mirror of #46. Generous defaults (low-friction $5 units, no auto-renewal trap, vendor self-enrollment, Member-Portability) reduce aggregate friction-cost across the cooperative. Cooperative-economic surplus emerges from generosity-as-default, not from optimization-as-default.
