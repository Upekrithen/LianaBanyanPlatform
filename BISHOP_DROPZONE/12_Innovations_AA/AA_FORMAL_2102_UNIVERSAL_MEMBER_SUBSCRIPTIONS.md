### Description

A system and method within a cooperative commerce platform wherein EVERY member can offer subscription channels and EVERY member can subscribe to other members' channels, with subscribers choosing to pay in either:

**(a) Marks** — cooperative effort-differential currency, transferred member-to-member, OR

**(b) Real money** — via Stripe recurring payments, processed through Commerce Engine at Cost+20%

Both payment methods coexist on the SAME subscription. The subscriber picks. The creator accepts both.

### The Core Architecture

```
ANY MEMBER (creator)
    │
    ├── Creates a subscription channel:
    │   "Diana's Deal Drops" / "Joe's Workshop Tips" / "Maria's Recipe Week"
    │   
    ├── Sets pricing (BOTH are always available):
    │   ├── Marks price: 10 Marks/month
    │   └── Dollar price: $5/month (Stripe recurring)
    │
    └── Subscribers choose their payment:
        │
        ├── OPTION A: Pay 10 Marks/month
        │   ├── Marks transfer: subscriber → creator
        │   ├── Platform facilitation: 16.7% of Marks (Cost+20% applies)
        │   ├── Creator receives: 8.33 Marks/month per subscriber
        │   └── No real money moves. Fully internal cooperative economy.
        │
        └── OPTION B: Pay $5/month (Stripe recurring)
            ├── Stripe processes payment
            ├── Commerce Engine applies Cost+20%
            ├── Creator receives: $4.17 in Credits per subscriber
            ├── Platform receives: $0.83 per subscriber
            └── Standard Stripe fees apply on top
```

### Why BOTH Currencies

**Marks subscriptions** serve members who are ACTIVE in the cooperative economy. They've earned Marks through transactions, photography, deal-finding, Guild participation, or any other contribution. They're spending cooperative currency they earned through contribution — keeping value circulating INSIDE the cooperative.

**Dollar subscriptions** serve members (or non-members via Ghost mode) who want to pay cash. Maybe they're new and haven't earned Marks yet. Maybe they prefer the simplicity of a credit card charge. Maybe they're testing the waters before going all-in on the cooperative economy.

**The creator doesn't care which.** They set both prices. They receive value either way. The dual-currency model means the subscription system works at Day 1 (when nobody has Marks yet) AND at Day 1,000 (when the internal Marks economy is thriving).

### The Marks Economy Flywheel

This is where it gets powerful:

```
Member earns Marks (through transactions, photography, Pearl Diving, etc.)
    │
    ├── Spends Marks on subscriptions (Diana's Deal Drops, Joe's Workshop Tips)
    │
    ├── Creators receive Marks from subscribers
    │   ├── Creators spend those Marks on OTHER subscriptions
    │   ├── Or convert to Joules (permanent store of value)
    │   └── Or use for governance (Backed Marks = voting power)
    │
    └── The internal Marks economy circulates WITHOUT real money moving.
        Every subscription paid in Marks is value created, exchanged,
        and retained INSIDE the cooperative.
```

This is the three-gear currency system (#1936) in action. Credits come in (one-way valve). Marks circulate internally. Joules store surplus. The subscription system gives Marks a powerful new REASON to circulate — you earn them through contribution, you spend them on content you value.

### Quality & Trust

No minimum follower count to START a subscription. But market discipline keeps quality high:

- **Subscriber ratings:** After each month, subscribers rate the channel (1-5 stars). Visible on channel page.
- **Churn signals:** If 20%+ subscribers cancel in a single month, the platform flags the channel and notifies the creator.
- **Refund policy:** Subscribers can cancel anytime. Current month prorated for dollar subs. Marks subs: Marks returned proportionally if cancelled before month-end.
- **No lock-in, ever.** The cooperative doesn't trap people. If the content isn't worth it, they leave. That's the market working correctly.

### SEC Implications

**Marks subscriptions:** Marks are transactional byproducts (#1936). A member spending Marks on a subscription is spending cooperative currency they earned through retail transactions. This is equivalent to spending loyalty points — NOT a securities transaction.

**Dollar subscriptions:** Standard commerce at Cost+20%. The creator provides a service. The subscriber pays for it. Stripe processes the payment. This is a straightforward service transaction, no different from paying for a meal on the platform.

**Neither payment type** creates a securities concern because:
1. No "investment of money" for the purpose of profit from others' efforts
2. The subscriber is purchasing a SERVICE (deal alerts), not an investment instrument
3. Marks are spent, not invested — they don't appreciate in value
4. The creator earns through their OWN effort (finding deals), not through a common enterprise

### Patent Relevance: HIGH — Crown Jewel Candidate

A cooperative commerce platform with a universal member subscription system wherein:

(a) ANY member can create subscription channels offering recurring content/service to other members,

(b) Subscribers choose to pay in EITHER cooperative effort-currency (Marks) OR traditional currency (dollars via Stripe recurring billing),

(c) Both payment methods coexist on the same subscription with the creator setting parallel pricing,

(d) Cost+20% margin applies uniformly to both payment types,

(e) The dual-currency subscription creates an internal cooperative economy flywheel where Marks circulate member-to-member without real money moving,

represents a novel dual-currency universal subscription marketplace within cooperative commerce.

**No existing platform** combines:
1. Universal member subscriptions (no follower minimum, no creator tier)
2. Dual-currency payment (cooperative currency OR fiat via Stripe)
3. Same subscription, subscriber's choice of payment method
4. Cost+20% fixed margin on both currency types
5. Integration with cooperative purchasing, Guild systems, and ADAPT scoring
6. Marks-economy flywheel where subscriptions drive internal currency circulation

Patreon, Substack, YouTube Memberships, and Twitch subscriptions all use ONLY fiat currency. No platform offers cooperative-currency subscriptions alongside fiat, with subscriber choice, within a cooperative commerce framework.
