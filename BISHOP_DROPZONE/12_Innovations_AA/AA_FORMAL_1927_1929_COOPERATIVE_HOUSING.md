# Innovation #1927: Cooperative Housing Acquisition
## Patent Relevance: CRITICAL (Crown Jewel)

### Classification
| Field | Value |
|-------|-------|
| Innovation # | 1927 |
| Name | Cooperative Housing Acquisition |
| Parent Initiative | Mission TWO (Shelter) |
| Session Originated | Bishop 022B |
| Status | Designed, DEPLOYED in basic form (K89). Legal review required before crowdfunding activation. |

### Description
Members crowdfund residential property purchase through the Commerce Engine. LB Housing LLC (subsidiary) holds title. Members contribute Credits, eligible Marks, or cash as housing access deposits — NOT investments. Contributors receive priority access to cooperative housing at Cost+20%. No individual member has ownership stake in specific properties. Dual-use model: AirBnB units generate revenue covering mortgage; cooperative units provide below-market housing.

### Patentable Elements
1. **Crowdfunded cooperative property acquisition through closed-loop commerce engine** — members contribute cooperative currency (Credits/Marks) through existing transaction infrastructure to fund real property acquisition held by cooperative subsidiary
2. **Dual-use cooperative property model** — single property contains both market-rate short-term rental units (revenue generation) and below-market cooperative housing units (member benefit), with STR revenue subsidizing cooperative rent
3. **Eligible Mark application to real property** — effort-denominated cooperative currency convertible to housing access deposits, creating a pathway from labor participation to shelter access
4. **Priority access system based on fund contribution** — contributors to specific property fund receive priority booking/housing rights without receiving equity ownership or financial returns
5. **Decreasing rent model** — as mortgage principal reduces over time, Cost+20% base cost decreases, so cooperative rent falls progressively toward maintenance-only levels (15-30 year trajectory)

### SEC/Legal Notes
- **CRITICAL**: Members receive HOUSING, not financial returns. AirBnB revenue stays in LLC, never distributed to members
- No expectation of profit — expectation of shelter
- Structurally distinct from REIT or real estate syndication
- REQUIRES securities attorney review before crowdfunding launch
- REQUIRES tax attorney review (cooperative housing under Subchapter T)
- LB Housing LLC holds title — liability isolation from parent corporation
- Licensed property manager required (state-dependent)
- Constructive receipt mitigation: members never touch AirBnB revenue
- Pawn Batch 11 confirmed: CLT structure valid under 501(c)(3), LIHTC stackable, dual-use legal with clean books

# Innovation #1928: AirBnB Revenue Subsidy Model
## Patent Relevance: CRITICAL (Crown Jewel)

### Classification
| Field | Value |
|-------|-------|
| Innovation # | 1928 |
| Name | AirBnB Revenue Subsidy Model |
| Parent Initiative | Mission TWO (Shelter) |
| Session Originated | Bishop 022B |
| Status | Designed. Legal gates pending. |

### Description
Dual-use property where short-term rental revenue from one portion subsidizes below-market cooperative housing in another portion. In a 4-unit building: 2 units on AirBnB at market rate generate $3,000/mo; 2 cooperative units at Cost+20% generate $1,000/mo. Total $4,000 covers $3,200 in costs (mortgage, insurance, taxes, maintenance, management). Surplus $800/mo flows to Housing Fund via WaterWheel.

AirBnB management creates cooperative jobs: cleaning (Household Concierge bounties), guest communication (Steward position), turnover (Crew Call), supply delivery (Local Wheels), listing optimization (Shepherding bounty). The housing project CREATES jobs within the cooperative.

### Patentable Elements
1. **STR-subsidized cooperative housing model** — short-term rental revenue from market-rate units structurally subsidizes below-market cooperative housing in the same property, with automated surplus routing to expansion fund
2. **Housing-as-job-creator architecture** — property management tasks decomposed into cooperative bounties (cleaning, communication, turnover, delivery, optimization), each flowing through the cooperative's existing labor marketplace
3. **Progressive rent reduction through cooperative ownership** — as mortgage principal decreases, Cost+20% base decreases, creating a long-term trajectory toward maintenance-only housing costs without requiring individual equity ownership

### SEC/Legal Notes
- AirBnB revenue stays in LLC — no distribution to members as financial returns
- Members receive housing access at below-market rates, not passive income
- STR permits required per city (San Antonio: Type 1/Type 2, $300-$450 + $200 app)
- HOT collection: 10.75% total in San Antonio (7% city + 2% convention + 1.75% county)
- Pawn Batch 11: Converse, TX has no standalone STR permit ordinance
- Pawn Batch 13-02: Steward trust + CLT + golden share = Tier 1 bankruptcy resistance

# Innovation #1929: Housing WaterWheel
## Patent Relevance: HIGH (Crown Jewel)

### Classification
| Field | Value |
|-------|-------|
| Innovation # | 1929 |
| Name | Housing WaterWheel |
| Parent Initiative | Mission TWO (Shelter) / Treasury |
| Session Originated | Bishop 022B |
| Status | Designed. Modeled in WaterWheels paper (Scenario 6). |

### Description
Surplus AirBnB revenue beyond property costs flows automatically into the Housing Fund for next property acquisition. Each property generates revenue that funds the next. The housing portfolio self-expands without additional member contributions. Growth model: Year 0 → 1 property ($800/mo surplus). Year 2 → 2 properties ($1,600/mo). Year 3.5 → 3 properties. Year 10 → 8-10 properties. Self-sustaining cooperative housing expansion.

### Patentable Elements
1. **Self-expanding cooperative real estate acquisition** — surplus revenue from dual-use properties automatically funds down payments on next properties without requiring additional member contributions, creating compound growth in cooperative housing stock
2. **Cross-mission reinforcing loop** — housing residents need transportation (Mission THREE/Local Wheels), delivery drivers need housing (Mission TWO), creating bidirectional demand that strengthens both systems
3. **Physical-asset WaterWheel** — extends the digital commerce WaterWheel model (#1918) to real property, demonstrating that cooperative value recirculation applies to both service and physical asset economies

### SEC/Legal Notes
- The WaterWheel is an operational reinvestment mechanism, not a financial return
- Members do not receive distributions from property surplus — surplus funds next acquisition
- No individual member has a financial claim on the Housing Fund balance
- SAA holders vote on fund allocation (which property to buy next) — governance authority, not ownership
- WaterWheels paper provides mathematical backing for all public claims
