## ATTORNEY READINESS — FORMAL DOCUMENT QUALITY

Each of the 27 A&A formal documents contains:

1. **DESCRIPTION** — Plain-English explanation of what the innovation does and why it matters, written so a patent attorney unfamiliar with the platform can understand the invention on first read.

2. **MECHANISM** — Step-by-step technical specification of how the system works, with component names, data flows, and architectural relationships. Numbered steps, specific enough to implement from.

3. **PRIOR ART ASSESSMENT** — Explicit statement of what prior art exists, why this innovation is distinct, and what specific combination of features has no known precedent. Names competitors and explains the gap.

4. **FORMAL CLAIMS** — 3 independent claims per innovation (~81 total across 27 innovations), written in patent-claim style with "comprising," "wherein," and structured dependency language.

**No guesswork required.** An attorney reading any formal document will understand: what the invention is, how it works, why it's novel, and what specifically to claim.
