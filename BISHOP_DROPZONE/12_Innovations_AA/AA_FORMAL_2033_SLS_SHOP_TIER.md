### Description

The addition of Selective Laser Sintering (SLS) as the third tier in the Manufacturing Escalation Ladder (#2032). SLS is the inflection point where a maker with a bench **becomes a shop**.

**The Four-Level Manufacturing Escalation:**

| Level | Equipment | What You Are | Marks Required | Investment (1/3 model) |
|-------|-----------|-------------|----------------|----------------------|
| 1 | Canister System (manual) | **Maker with a printer** | 0 (buy the kit) | $250-400 |
| 2 | Desktop Molder (Babyplast) | **Maker with a bench** | 2,000 (Partner) | ~$6,700 per party |
| 3 | **SLS Machine** | **SHOP** | 2,000+ (Partner) | ~$10,000-12,000 per party |
| 4 | Industrial Press | **FACTORY** | 5,000 (Senior Partner) | $16,000-66,000 per party |

### Why SLS Is the Shop Threshold

SLS changes the game in three ways:

1. **No molds required.** SLS prints directly from powder — nylon PA12, glass-filled nylon, TPU. No tooling cost at all. This means a shop can produce ANY part from ANY customer's STL file without investing in molds first.

2. **Engineering-grade materials.** SLS nylon parts are functional, durable, and production-ready. Not prototypes — real parts you can sell to real customers.

3. **Complex geometries.** Undercuts, internal channels, living hinges — things that require expensive multi-part molds in injection molding are trivial in SLS. This means the shop can serve customers the injection molders can't.

A maker with a Canister System makes THEIR parts. A maker with a Babyplast makes parts at VOLUME. A maker with an SLS machine makes parts for OTHER PEOPLE. That's a shop.

### The Shop Business Model

An SLS shop in the LB network:
- Takes orders through the LB marketplace (Commerce Engine)
- Produces parts for other members, businesses, and Kickstarter fulfillment
- Earns the 83.3% creator split on every order
- Can also produce for non-LB customers (open market pricing)
- Uses Canister System for small thermoplastic runs (Level 1 still active)
- Uses Babyplast for high-volume identical parts (Level 2 still active)
- Uses SLS for custom/complex/engineering parts (Level 3)

Three capabilities under one roof = a real manufacturing shop.

### What Makes It Novel

The threshold for becoming a shop is measured in Marks, not capital. Traditional path: secure $30K-50K in capital, lease commercial space, buy equipment, hope for customers. LB path: earn 2,000 Marks of proven contribution → cooperative co-funds your equipment → you're a shop on day one with an existing order pipeline through the marketplace.
