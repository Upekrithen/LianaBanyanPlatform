## Section 1 — Innovation Summary

### What this paper discloses

*"A Considered Approach to Universal Caregiving"* is the daily-life caregiving sovereignty paper — Paper 11 of the 12-paper Save-the-World Series.

**Thesis**: Caregiving labor (eldercare + childcare + family caregiving + community caregiving) is the largest economic activity not measured by GDP. It is vast in scale, non-extractive in motivation, and structurally unrecognized by capital-class economics. Making it visible + economically recognized requires only coordination + currency that doesn't extract — both already exist in LB Frame.

The paper discloses:
1. **Marks attention-currency for caregiving** — caregivers earn Marks for time + attention given; Marks back economic-recognition + share-back-pay; one-way ratchet ensures value accumulates
2. **Pedestal initiatives for caregiver recognition** — 10% IP allocation: caregivers get Pedestals; visibility + ownership + agency over their caregiving contributions within the cooperative pool
3. **Family Table composition (Paper 4)** — the family-tier coordination surface includes caregiving schedule + meal-prep + grocery; caregiving IS daily-life-coordination
4. **The Hive primitive for community caregiving** — neighborhood / Tribe-class / Guild-class caregiver coordination; eldercare cooperatives via Apiarist Librarian Hive
5. **Distinction from Health Care (Paper 7)** — clinical vs. daily-life sovereignty are distinct claims; both essential; neither is subordinate to the other

## Section 2 — Composing Claim (Scaffold)

*Constructed from `paper_a_considered_approach_to_universal_caregiving_canon_bp021.eblet.md`. Founder + counsel refine at fire-time.*

**Composing Claim — Universal Caregiving via Cooperative Labor-Visibility Architecture**

Method + system for cooperative-platform daily-life-caregiving sovereignty where:

(i) **Marks attention-currency for caregiving labor** provides economic recognition for daily-life caregiving work — eldercare, childcare, family caregiving, community caregiving — via the effort-differential Marks currency (non-purchasable, ratchet-backed, one-way ratchet only ever increasing in backing value): caregivers earn Marks for verified time + attention contributions to cooperative caregiving; Marks accumulate as cooperative-surplus-backed value and are convertible within the cooperative economy;

(ii) **Pedestal initiatives for caregiver recognition** apply the 10% IP allocation framework to caregiving contributions: each caregiver receives a Pedestal — a member-visible surface with visibility + ownership + agency — for their caregiving contributions to the cooperative, providing individual-contributor recognition within the cooperative pool alongside other creative and economic contributions;

(iii) the **Family Table composition (Paper 4)** integrates caregiving coordination with food coordination: the Family Table surface includes caregiving scheduling + meal-prep for care recipients + grocery coordination for caregiving households, recognizing that daily-life caregiving and food sovereignty are structurally composed activities;

(iv) the **Hive-as-caregiving-cooperative** applies the Apiarist Librarian Hive primitive to community caregiving coordination: neighborhood-scale eldercare cooperatives, childcare share networks, and family-caregiving Guilds form via the same Tribe / Family / Project / Guild cohort structures, enabling cooperative caregiving without institutional intermediary;

(v) the **daily-life / clinical distinction** is structural: Paper 11 (this paper) claims daily-life caregiving sovereignty (food prep, bathing, companionship, transport, child supervision, companionship); Paper 7 (Health Care) claims clinical healthcare sovereignty (diagnosis, medication, clinical treatment, emergency coordination); the cooperative-platform substrate makes both operational while preserving their distinct sovereignty claims;

(vi) the cooperative-platform layer (Liana Banyan) productizes caregiving coordination + Marks-recognition + Pedestal-allocation + community-caregiving-Hive formation at Cost+20% structural margin, making daily-life caregiving an economically recognized cooperative infrastructure function rather than invisible extractive labor;

(vii) the architecture makes the largest economic activity not measured by GDP **visible**: caregiving labor appears in the cooperative economy as Marks + Pedestal contributions; caregivers are economic participants, not invisible-labor-class subsidizers of market-class production;

(viii) the architecture composes with Papers 4 (food coordination), 7 (clinical care), and 10 (shelter) to form the full daily-life sovereignty stack: food + healthcare + shelter + caregiving coordination = household-scale life sovereignty;

(ix) the paper composes as the caregiving-sovereignty leg of the 12-paper Save-the-World Series, completing the Life vector alongside Papers 4 (nutrition), 7 (clinical health), and 9 (education).
