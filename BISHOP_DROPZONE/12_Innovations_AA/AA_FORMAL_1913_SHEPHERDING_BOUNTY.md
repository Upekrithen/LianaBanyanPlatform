## Description

The Shepherding Bounty is a **standing, on-call specialty service position** within the Liana Banyan cooperative. It is architecturally distinct from every other labor arrangement on the platform:

| Labor Type | Duration | Scope | Example |
|-----------|----------|-------|---------|
| **Bounty** | One-off | Single deliverable | "Design this menu" |
| **Crew Table Seat** | Project-length | One project role | "Photographer for this storefront launch" |
| **Stewardship** | Ongoing | One business/entity | "Manage this storefront's operations" |
| **Shepherding** | Standing, indefinite | On-call specialty across ALL businesses | "Available for design work for anyone who needs it" |

A Shepherd is a **specialist who remains available**. They are not assigned to a single business or project — they are listed in the cooperative's service directory, ranked by proximity and reputation, and auto-matched to incoming requests.

## Mechanics

### Activation
- Member declares a Shepherding specialty (e.g., "Graphic Design," "Photography," "Bookkeeping," "Delivery")
- Minimum commitment: 1 Mark/month to maintain active Shepherd status
- 1 Mark = declaration of availability. It is consumed monthly. If the Shepherd lets their Mark lapse, they become inactive (not removed — just unlisted until re-activated)

### Matching
- **Proximity-based listing advantage**: Shepherds closer to the requesting business appear higher in results
- **Reputation-weighted**: XP score and completion history affect ranking
- **100+ point reputation difference required** to override proximity advantage — a Shepherd 2 miles away with 500 XP outranks a Shepherd 20 miles away with 550 XP, but NOT one with 650 XP
- This prevents reputation gaming from centralizing work to a small elite while still rewarding quality

### Compensation
- Per-task at Cost+20% (standard platform split: 83.3% to Shepherd, 13.3% to LB, 3.3% to Gleaner's Corner)
- Optional subscription arrangement with specific businesses (recurring Shepherding relationship)
- Eligible Marks earned if the task is on a funded project (#1911 Two-Domain integration)

### The Gravity Well (Connection to #1914)

When a Shepherd designs a Cue Card for Business A, they create a **gravity well**:

1. Business A's customers who later start their OWN projects have that Shepherd **auto-slotted** as their designer (first-mover advantage)
2. The customer can override the auto-slot, but the default is: "Your cue card was designed by [Shepherd]. Would you like them to design yours too?"
3. Remaining service slots for the new project are filled by nearby Shepherds by proximity + reputation
4. One Cue Card creates a chain of future work opportunities

This is Innovation #1914 (Cue Card Slingshot → Shepherd Auto-Slot Pipeline) — a viral growth mechanic embedded in the labor system.

## Patentable Elements

1. **Standing on-call cooperative labor position** with monthly Mark activation fee
2. **Proximity + reputation hybrid ranking** with 100-point override threshold
3. **Auto-slot pipeline** from prior work to future default assignments (#1914 extension)
4. **Mark-funded availability declaration** — using effort-debt currency to maintain service listing

## SEC/Legal Notes

- Shepherding is a **labor arrangement**, not a securities relationship
- Compensation is for services rendered (1099-reportable), not for investment
- The 1 Mark/month activation fee is an internal cooperative participation requirement, not a financial obligation
- No expectation of passive income — Shepherds earn by doing work
