### Description

A system and method within a cooperative commerce platform wherein members operate home-based or community-kitchen-based meal preparation and frozen food storage hubs ("Freezer Nodes"), producing batch-cooked meals for freezer storage and scheduled distribution to subscribers and on-demand customers, with cooperative purchasing integration (Let's Get Groceries + Pearl Diver deal intelligence) reducing ingredient costs and Cost+20% pricing ensuring the cook keeps 83.3% of every meal sold.

```
FREEZER NODE OPERATOR (any member with a kitchen + freezer)
```

### Why Freezer, Not Fresh

Fresh meal delivery (DoorDash, Uber Eats) requires:
- Same-day prep for every order (no economies of scale)
- Hot delivery within 30 minutes (expensive logistics)
- Food waste on unsold meals (perishable inventory)

Frozen meal prep requires:
- Batch cooking once (economies of scale)
- Flexible delivery/pickup window (no 30-minute rush)
- Weeks of shelf life (minimal waste)
- Reheating is trivial (microwave, oven, stovetop)

**Frozen is the cooperative advantage.** A cook who makes 50 portions of beef stew on Sunday has inventory for the entire week. No rush. No waste. No delivery pressure.

### Food Safety & Legal

- **Cottage food laws** vary by state. Texas allows home-based food sales with limitations (no potentially hazardous foods unless operating under a permit). Freezer Node operators must comply with their state's cottage food regulations.
- **The platform provides:** food safety checklist, labeling templates (ingredients, date, allergens, reheating instructions), and links to state-specific cottage food registration.
- **ADAPT Score Alignment dimension** checks: proper labeling, allergen disclosure, temperature logging (if applicable), customer complaint resolution.
- **Pawn research needed:** State-by-state cottage food law matrix (add to B28 or B29).
