## INNOVATION #2139 — SKIPPING STONES

### Description
A four-layer content depth architecture enabling progressive disclosure from micro-teaser to full academic paper. Each piece of content exists at four layers: Skipping Stone (one-sentence hook), "Proof is in the Pudding" article (accessible version), "This is NOT Pudding" (full source paper), and Reading Beacon (position-saved for return).

### Mechanics
- A Skipping Stone is ~100-200 chars, links to a Pudding
- Each Pudding references its source paper in the "This is NOT Pudding" section
- Readers can dive deeper layer-by-layer or skim at the top layer
- ~300 stones at full scale across ~30 papers

### Prior Art Distinction
Unlike traditional content marketing funnels (awareness → consideration → decision), Skipping Stones is NOT a conversion funnel. It's a depth architecture where the reader chooses their own reading commitment. No algorithmic push. No funnel coercion.

### Claims
1. A four-layer content depth system where each piece exists at distinct reading commitments
2. A method for atomizing long-form content into nested micro-teasers that link to intermediate and source forms
3. A position-tracking mechanism ("Reading Beacon") that preserves the reader's depth layer across sessions

## INNOVATION #2140 — SPOONFULS

### Description
A content atomization pattern extracting 5-8 bite-sized (~280 char) standalone insights from longer Pudding articles. Each Spoonful is self-contained, spice-tagged, linked back to its source Pudding, and distributable via Battery Dispatch as a third content channel alongside BST Episodes and Skipping Stones.

### Mechanics
- Pudding article → 5-8 Spoonfuls extracted
- Each Spoonful has ID pattern `S-NNN-MMM` (pudding number + sequence)
- Each Spoonful carries ONE spice tag (may differ from pudding's primary)
- Each Spoonful is postable as standalone micro-content on Twitter/X/Threads/Bluesky
- Includes `#Spoonfuls #SpiceName` hashtag suffix
- ~280 chars max, self-contained, links back to parent Pudding

### Prior Art Distinction
Unlike content repurposing (blog → tweet thread), Spoonfuls are STRUCTURED atomization with explicit numbering, spice tagging, and traceable parent-reference architecture. Not improvised snippets — systematic extraction.

### Claims
1. A method for structured atomization of long-form content into standalone micro-posts with parent-child traceability
2. A numbering scheme (parent-ID + sequence) enabling content-unit tracking across distribution channels
3. A spice-tag system enabling per-Spoonful taxonomic classification distinct from parent article's tags

## INNOVATION #2141 — CONCURRENT DISTRIBUTION GRID

### Description
A multi-series, multi-channel content scheduling architecture that staggers content types across social media channels to maximize coverage and minimize repetition. ~24 posts/day across 6+ channels, with ~720 posts/month and ~50 days before any content repeats.

### Mechanics
- Three content series: BST Episodes, Spoonfuls, Skipping Stones (+ News Slot)
- Six+ channels: Twitter/X, LinkedIn, Threads, Bluesky, Facebook, Instagram
- Per-platform batch-size configuration (Twitter burst=3, LinkedIn burst=1, etc.)
- Hourly cron dispatch with platform-specific timing
- Viewing Schedule access control (private → semi-public → public)

### Prior Art Distinction
Unlike scheduled-posting tools (Hootsuite, Buffer), the Distribution Grid treats content as multi-series programming requiring cross-channel coordination. Unlike editorial calendars, it's automated with per-platform rules. Novel: series-as-channel mental model.

### Claims
1. A method for scheduling multiple content series concurrently across heterogeneous platforms with per-platform batch rules
2. A content grid with staggered repeat intervals preventing cross-channel audience fatigue
3. A Viewing Schedule access gate with private/semi-public/public state transitions

## INNOVATION #2142 — THE SPICE RACK

### Description
A ten-spice taxonomy system mapping culinary spices to business skill domains, used simultaneously for content tagging, audience filtering, and project skill-matching. Salt=Operations, Garlic=Finance, Sugar=Marketing, Cinnamon=Design, Pepper=Legal, Ginger=Innovation, Cumin=Engineering, Paprika=Leadership, Basil=Education, Oregano=Coordination.

### Mechanics
- 10 spices map 1:1 to 10 business skill domains
- Every content piece carries one primary spice + optional secondary spices
- Members self-declare their spices (skills they bring)
- Content filtering: "follow the Garlic" = see all finance content
- Project matching: projects declare needed spices, members bring spice

### Prior Art Distinction
Unlike skill tags (LinkedIn, Upwork), the Spice Rack uses culinary metaphor deliberately — memorable, non-corporate, anti-jargon. Unlike taxonomies (Dewey, ontologies), it's fixed at 10 (limiting cognitive load). Unlike folksonomy tags, it's closed (no tag sprawl).

### Claims
1. A closed ten-category taxonomy mapping culinary spices to business skill domains
2. A method for simultaneously using a single taxonomy for content classification, audience filtering, and project skill-matching
3. A fixed-cardinality tagging system preventing taxonomy sprawl while enabling multi-dimensional classification

## INNOVATION #2143 — THE RECIPE POT

### Description
A project-to-skill matching system where projects declare their "recipe" (needed spices) and members contribute their declared spices. Operationalizes the Stone Soup fable — every villager brings an ingredient, the pot feeds everyone.

### Mechanics
- Project creator declares recipe: "Need Garlic, Cumin, and a pinch of Pepper"
- Members with declared spices see matching projects
- "Bring your Garlic to our Pot" = four-function sentence (content filter + skill declaration + project match + cooperative invitation)
- Match scoring by spice overlap + proximity + reputation
- Auto-notification to matched members

### Prior Art Distinction
Unlike freelance marketplaces (Upwork, Fiverr), the Recipe Pot uses COOPERATIVE matching (members bring what they have, projects take what they need) rather than EXTRACTIVE bidding. Unlike LinkedIn job matching, it's project-scoped not role-scoped.

### Claims
1. A skill-matching system using closed-cardinality taxonomy (Spice Rack) as matching primitive
2. A method for project recipe declaration and member spice contribution with auto-matching
3. A cooperative matching mechanism distinguishing "bring what you have" from bidding/competitive markets

## INNOVATION #2145 — SCHEDULED VIEWING BEACON

### Description
A member-controlled content scheduling primitive enabling platform members to schedule future viewings of content (Puddings, BST Episodes, Spoonfuls, Skipping Stones, Papers) via tooltip-style entry (time, date, recurrence, reminder offset, label). Beacons auto-sync to member's Helm Calendar, transforming passive feed-scrolling into deliberate appointment-based learning.

### Mechanics
- Per-content-item "Schedule Viewing" button
- Tooltip-style entry form (matches phone alarm UX)
- Inputs: scheduled_at, reminder_offset (5min/15min/1hr/1day), recurrence_rule (RRULE), label
- Insert into `viewing_beacons` table with member_id
- Auto-sync to Helm Calendar (creates calendar event)
- Reminder dispatch via cron (email/push/in-app)
- Member can edit/cancel/snooze from Helm

### Prior Art Distinction
Unlike "read later" services (Pocket, Instapaper) that accumulate unread content with guilt, Viewing Beacons schedule ACTIVE future time blocks. Unlike calendar apps, they specifically integrate with platform content. Unlike newsletter subscriptions, they're per-content-item granular.

### Claims
1. A per-content-item scheduling primitive with tooltip-style entry and calendar sync
2. A method for transforming feed-scrolling consumption into appointment-based learning via member-scheduled viewings
3. A recurrence-capable content-reminder system using RFC 5545 RRULE format for repeating viewings

## INNOVATION #2146 — SHARED SCHEDULING PRIMITIVE (Three-Surface Architecture)

### Description
A single scheduling UI component pattern deployed across three distinct user roles: Staff (broadcast schedule), Creators (cue card dispatch), Members (viewing beacons). Same underlying code, same UI pattern, different target contexts. Architectural coherence without sacrificing role-specific needs.

### Mechanics
- Single `SchedulingEntryBox` component
- Target parameter switches context: `helm-calendar` | `distribution-grid` | `cue-card-dispatch`
- Shared data structure: scheduled_at, recurrence_rule, reminder_offset, label
- Shared calendar sync layer
- Each surface adds context-specific behavior (staff: burst rules; creator: team assignment; member: personal recurrence)

### Prior Art Distinction
Most platforms duplicate scheduling logic per user role — admin scheduler, creator scheduler, consumer calendar sync all have separate implementations. The shared primitive unifies these at the component level while preserving context-specific behavior. DRY architecture meeting cooperative role distribution.

### Claims
1. A method for deploying a single scheduling UI component across three distinct user roles (operator, creator, consumer)
2. A context-parameter pattern enabling role-specific behavior without code duplication
3. An architectural pattern distributing scheduling power across user roles in a cooperative platform

## INNOVATION #2147 — "ALL THE PUDDING" TV GUIDE

### Description
A temporal content discovery page displaying all platform content (Puddings, BST Episodes, Spoonfuls, Skipping Stones, Papers) as a programming schedule — old TV Guide metaphor applied to cooperative platform content. Horizontal time axis, vertical channel lanes, visual programming blocks. Subtitle: "There is No Spoon" (Matrix reference).

### Mechanics
- Route: `/cephas/all-the-pudding`
- Three view modes: Listings, Schedule (TV Guide), Calendar
- Filter bar: spice, series, depth, date range, reading time
- Channel lanes: BST, Spoonfuls, Skipping Stones, Puddings, News Slot
- Programming blocks sized by reading time
- "Schedule Viewing" button per block → opens SchedulingEntryBox → creates Viewing Beacon
- Aesthetic toggle: old-school TV Guide vs modern streaming schedule

### Prior Art Distinction
Unlike streaming service schedules (Netflix recommends, doesn't publish schedules to users), this is a transparent programming grid. Unlike broadcast TV guides (no interactivity), every block is actionable (Schedule Viewing). Novel: hybrid of TV Guide visual + calendar interactivity + cooperative content transparency.

### Claims
1. A temporal content discovery interface presenting platform content as programming schedule with visual blocks
2. A method for integrating scheduled-viewing actions into content discovery interface
3. A multi-series multi-channel content schedule visualization with dual aesthetic modes

## INNOVATION #2148 — TEMPORAL CONTENT ARCHITECTURE (Paper-Level Pattern)

### Description
A platform design pattern where scheduling is a shared primitive exposed to three distinct user roles — transforming content consumption from algorithmic feed colonization into member-controlled appointment-based learning. Breaks the operator monopoly on temporal decisions.

### Mechanics
- Three surfaces: Staff Broadcast (operator), Cue Card Dispatch (creator), Viewing Beacons (consumer)
- Each surface uses the same scheduling primitive (Innovation #2146)
- Each role controls scheduling at their own target context
- No role monopolizes time as a resource
- Pattern is extensible to other operator-monopolized primitives (notifications, transactions, governance timing, access control)

### Prior Art Distinction
Algorithmic feed platforms (TikTok, Meta, X) monopolize scheduling as operator function. Calendar systems give time control to individuals but don't integrate with platform content. Temporal Content Architecture distributes scheduling as cooperative infrastructure — paper-level innovation, not feature-level.

### Claims
1. A platform architecture pattern distributing scheduling primitives across three user roles with context-specific behavior
2. A method for breaking operator-monopolized temporal decisions via shared primitive deployment
3. A cooperative design pattern extensible to other operator-monopolized platform primitives
