## Innovation #2034 — Project-Entity Requirement (Every Project = Business Entity)
**Category:** Legal Architecture / Cooperative Structure / Governance
**Impact:** HIGH
**Crown Jewel Candidate:** YES

### Description

EVERY project launched on the Liana Banyan platform requires the project creator to operate through a business entity:
- **Minimum:** Sole Proprietorship (free to form, easiest path)
- **Recommended:** LLC (asset protection, tax flexibility, professional credibility)
- **The Founder's projects:** Each uses its own LLC (Canister Manufacturing LLC, HexIsle LLC, etc.) as wholly owned subsidiaries of Liana Banyan Corporation

**This means LB Corporation is NOT the manufacturer.** LB is the cooperative infrastructure:
- Marketplace (Commerce Engine)
- Payment rails (LB Card for cash, Credits/Marks for cooperative currency)
- Quality standards (tier classification, QA bounties)
- Bounty system (Crew Call, X-Ray Arena)
- Equipment co-funding (1/3 model)
- Patent portfolio (shared IP protection)
- Reputation system (Marks, tiers, Crew Call Primary)

The PROJECT-ENTITY does the manufacturing, hiring, selling, and fulfillment. LB provides the infrastructure they run on.

### Why This Matters

1. **SEC clean:** Each project-entity is a separate business. Contractor payments are normal 1099 compensation. No cooperative "revenue share" language needed — it's just a business paying its contractors.

2. **Liability isolation:** If a Canister System kit has a defect, Canister Manufacturing LLC is liable — not LB Corporation, not HexIsle LLC, not the member's personal assets (if they used an LLC).

3. **Tax clarity:** Each project-entity files its own taxes. Contractors receive 1099s from the project-entity, not from LB. Clean paper trail.

4. **Scalability:** 1,000 projects = 1,000 business entities, each responsible for their own operations. LB doesn't need to manage 1,000 product lines — it manages the PLATFORM.

5. **Cooperative purity:** LB Corporation remains a cooperative infrastructure provider, not a conglomerate. Each project-entity operates independently but benefits from cooperative membership.

### The Fork Clarified

The LB Card carries **CASH ONLY** — real USD via Stripe Issuing prepaid. This is the cash side of the fork.

When a project-entity pays a contractor:
- **Cash payment** → LB Card (Stripe Issuing, real USD, 1099-reportable)
- **Cooperative reward** → Marks (differential currency, earned through contribution, used within cooperative economy)

These are TWO SEPARATE payment streams. The contractor gets cash for their work AND Marks for their contribution. The cash comes from the project-entity. The Marks come from the cooperative.

### Implementation

Platform needs:
- Project creation flow requires: entity type (sole prop / LLC / corp), entity name, EIN or SSN
- Project page shows entity information (for trust/transparency)
- Contractor payment flow routes through the project-entity, not through LB
- LB Card funding for contractors: project-entity funds the card via Stripe, contractor spends it

## Innovation #2035 — Multi-Vendor Prototype Validation
**Category:** Market Validation / Manufacturing / Competition
**Impact:** HIGH
**Crown Jewel Candidate:** YES

### Description

When a manufacturing bounty is posted (like "Build the S Piston Prototype"), MULTIPLE people can complete it. Each completer:

1. **Earns the bounty** (500 Marks each)
2. **Starts their own Project** on the platform (with their own business entity)
3. **Sells their version** of the prototype through the LB marketplace
4. **Competes on quality, price, and delivery** with other builders

This creates MULTI-VENDOR market validation:
- If 5 people build working S piston prototypes and list them for sale, you have 5 data points on: build cost, retail price, quality, delivery time, customer satisfaction
- The market tells you which version is best — not a committee
- The best sellers earn more Marks, reach Partner faster, and get equipment co-funding first
- Competition improves the product for everyone

### Why This Is the BEST Way to Validate

Traditional product validation: build one prototype → survey customers → guess at demand → risk everything on one design.

Multi-vendor validation: 5-10 people build prototypes → each sells theirs → real customers buy with real money → the market reveals which design, price point, and builder wins.

This is a LIVE A/B test with REAL revenue. Zero guesswork.

### How It Works

1. Bounty posted: "Build S Piston Prototype — 500 Marks, open to multiple builders"
2. Builder A completes prototype, creates "A's Canister Shop" (sole prop), lists for sale at $279
3. Builder B completes prototype, creates "B's Maker Lab LLC", lists at $319 (higher quality finish)
4. Builder C completes prototype, creates "C's Workshop", lists at $249 (budget version)
5. Customers buy from whichever they prefer
6. After 3 months: Builder B has 40% of sales (best quality), Builder A has 35%, Builder C has 25%
7. Builder B reaches Partner first → gets Babyplast co-funding → scales to Level 2
8. All three continue selling — competition keeps quality high and prices fair

### What Makes It Novel

No cooperative platform lets bounty completers SIMULTANEOUSLY compete as vendors selling their own versions of the same product. Traditional cooperatives centralize production. This decentralizes it AND uses market competition to drive quality — while the cooperative provides the shared infrastructure (marketplace, payment, reputation, IP).

## Innovation #2036 — Founder Project-Entity Portfolio (LLC per Product Line)
**Category:** Corporate Structure / Governance
**Impact:** MEDIUM

### Description

The Founder operates each major product line through its own LLC, all wholly owned by Liana Banyan Corporation:

| Entity | Product Line | Purpose |
|--------|-------------|---------|
| Liana Banyan Corporation | Platform + cooperative | Parent entity, Wyoming C-Corp |
| Upekrithen LLC | Financial infrastructure | LB Card, Stripe Issuing, DSS |
| HexIsle LLC (proposed) | HexIsle terrain + Kickstarter | 13-campaign product line |
| Canister Manufacturing LLC (proposed) | Canister System kits | Manufacturing bounty project |
| [Future LLCs] | Future product lines | As needed |

Each LLC:
- Has its own bank account / Stripe account
- Hires its own contractors (1099, paid via LB Card)
- Takes its own Kickstarter revenue
- Files its own taxes
- Operates through the LB marketplace
- Benefits from cooperative IP portfolio protection

This is NOT required for regular members. Regular members can use sole proprietorship for their projects. The Founder uses LLCs because he's operating at scale and needs liability isolation between product lines.

### Recommendation for Members

- **Starting out:** Sole proprietorship (free, easy, just start selling)
- **Growing:** LLC recommended ($100 Wyoming filing, asset protection)
- **At scale:** LLC required for Factory Node equipment co-funding (1/3 model requires a real entity for the operator's 1/3)
