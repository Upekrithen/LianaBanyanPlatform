## Innovation #1989 — Onboarding Brand Bounty: Instant Bounty Poster for Logo, Domain, Email, and Designed Cue Card at Signup

### Description

A system and method within a cooperative commerce platform for offering new members immediate access to brand-building services during the onboarding flow, wherein:

(a) Immediately after a new member signs up (or during their Red Carpet onboarding), the system presents a "Build Your Brand" bounty panel with checkboxes:

```
┌──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│  🎨 Want to look professional from Day 1?                     │
│                                                               │
│  Our designers are standing by. Check what you need:          │
│                                                               │
│  □ Custom Logo                          ~50–200 Marks        │
│    (A real designer makes YOUR logo)    depending on tier     │
│                                                               │
│  □ Domain + Email Setup                 ~30–100 Marks        │
│    (yourbusiness.com + you@yourbiz.com) depending on tier     │
│                                                               │
│  □ Designed Cue Card                    ~20–80 Marks         │
│    (Your branded calling card, ready    depending on tier     │
│     to hand out or send digitally)                           │
│                                                               │
│  ┌────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐      │
│  │  💡 Pay in Credits for priority queue placement     │      │
│  └────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘      │
│                                                               │
│  [Post My Bounties →]     [Remind Me Later]                  │
│                                                               │
└──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘
```

(b) When the member checks boxes and confirms, the system automatically creates BOUNTY POSTERS on the platform's Crew Call board, visible to all registered designers. (c) Each bounty is posted at a price determined by the member's chosen URGENCY TIER (see Innovation #1990). (d) "Remind Me Later" dismisses the panel but saves it as a task in their dashboard — they can come back anytime. (e) The bounties are VOLUME-PRICED because the platform aggregates demand: if 200 new members per week need logos, that's a pool of 200 logo jobs that designers can bid on or claim in bulk.

### Why This Matters at Onboarding

A new member — especially a new Captain or business owner — needs to look legitimate FAST. The difference between "some guy with a card" and "a professional with a branded card, matching logo, and custom email" is enormous for credibility. This system lets them get professional branding in their FIRST HOUR on the platform, powered by the cooperative's own designer community.

### The Volume Pool

```
Week 1: 50 new members sign up
├── 20 check "Logo" → 20 logo bounties posted
├── 15 check "Domain + Email" → 15 setup bounties posted
├── 30 check "Designed Cue Card" → 30 card design bounties posted
│
Week 2: More signups → more bounties → designers see steady work
Week 12: Pool of 500+ logo jobs, 300+ domains, 800+ cards
│
DESIGNERS see: "There's always work here."
NEW MEMBERS see: "I can get a logo in 24 hours."
```

### Patent Relevance: HIGH

An onboarding-triggered brand service bounty system that automatically generates marketplace work orders (logo design, domain setup, card design) from new member checkboxes, aggregates them into a volume-priced designer pool, and offers urgency-based tiered pricing — represents a novel integration of onboarding and cooperative marketplace dynamics.

## Innovation #1990 — Six-Tier Rush Pricing: Good/Fast/Cheap Production Queue for Design Bounties

### Description

A system and method within a cooperative commerce platform for pricing design bounties (logo, domain, card, and other creative services) across six production tiers based on the Good/Fast/Cheap triangle, wherein:

(a) Every design bounty is posted with a TIER that determines price and turnaround time:

```
┌──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│  ⏱️ How fast do you need it?                                  │
│                                                               │
│  TIER    TURNAROUND    PRICE         GOOD/FAST/CHEAP         │
│  ─────   ──────────    ─────         ───────────────         │
│                                                               │
│  🔴 T1   TODAY         200 Marks     Fast + Good = Expensive  │
│          (4-8 hours)                                          │
│                                                               │
│  🟠 T2   Tomorrow      150 Marks     Fast-ish + Good         │
│          (24 hours)                                           │
│                                                               │
│  🟡 T3   3 Days        100 Marks     Balanced                │
│          (72 hours)                                           │
│                                                               │
│  🟢 T4   1 Week        70 Marks      Good + Cheap = Slower   │
│          (7 days)                                             │
│                                                               │
│  🔵 T5   2 Weeks       50 Marks      Cheapest standard       │
│          (14 days)                                            │
│                                                               │
│  ⚪ T6   Whenever       30 Marks      "I'm in no rush"        │
│          (designer picks up when ready)                       │
│                                                               │
│  💡 Pay in Credits: Get bumped up one tier in the queue       │
│                                                               │
└──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘
```

(b) Designers who claim bounties see the tier and deadline. A T1 bounty means "this person needs it in 4-8 hours" — only designers confident in fast turnaround should claim it. (c) The pricing follows the Good/Fast/Cheap rule: you can have any two. Fast + Good costs more. Good + Cheap takes longer. (d) The platform takes its constitutional 20% margin on each bounty. At T6 (30 Marks), the designer gets 24 Marks ($24 equivalent). At T1 (200 Marks), the designer gets 160 Marks. (e) Designers set their own tier availability: "I do T4-T6 only" (relaxed pace) or "I do T1-T3" (premium rush work).

### The Credits Incentive

The Founder asked: "Should there be an incentive to pay in Credits?"

**Yes. The incentive: paying in Credits bumps you up one tier in the queue.**

If you post a T4 bounty (1 week, 70 Marks) but pay in Credits instead of Marks, your job moves to T3 position in the designer's queue (3-day turnaround) while still paying the T4 price. Why? Because Credits represent real money entering the system ($1=1, one-way valve), which is more valuable to the cooperative than Marks being shuffled around. The platform rewards real-money inflows.

### Patent Relevance: HIGH

A six-tier urgency-based pricing system for cooperative marketplace design bounties, where: tier pricing follows the Good/Fast/Cheap production triangle, designers self-select their tier availability, and payment in platform currency (Credits) vs. effort currency (Marks) provides differential queue priority — represents a novel tiered pricing mechanism for cooperative creative services marketplaces.

## Innovation #1991 — Designer Production Pool: Reputation-Gated Creative Services Marketplace with XP Time Rating

### Description

A system and method within a cooperative commerce platform for operating a reputation-gated designer marketplace, wherein:

(a) Any platform member can register as a designer and sign up for a WEEKLY PRODUCTION COMMITMENT: "I will complete X logos per week at Tier Y." For example: "10 logos per week at T4-T6" or "3 rush logos per week at T1-T2."

(b) **Reputation gate:** A designer MUST complete 2 successful bounties (confirmed by the requester) before their XP time rating starts counting. This prevents untested designers from claiming bounties and failing. The first 2 are "tryout" jobs — they still get paid, but they don't yet appear in the ranked designer pool.

(c) After 2 successful completions, the designer's **XP Time Rating** activates — tracking:
- Completion rate (% of bounties finished on time)
- Quality rating (requester satisfaction 1-5)
- Average turnaround (actual vs. tier deadline)
- Volume (total bounties completed)

(d) Designers choose their pricing model:

| Model | Description | Benefit |
|-------|-------------|---------|
| **C+20** | Maximum discount for requesters | Maximum volume (most bounties routed to you), all platform benefits, BEST DEAL badge |
| **C+40** | Moderate discount | Good volume, advance payment after trust period |
| **C+60** | Small discount | Moderate volume |
| **C+90** | Near-retail pricing | Entry-level volume |
| **Retail** | Full price, no discount | Lowest platform volume but highest per-job payment |

"You only get C+20 when you give C+20." Designers who offer the deepest discount get the most work. The market prevails upon itself.

(e) The platform maintains **6 production-level pools** (one per rush tier). Designers are sorted into pools by their tier availability. When a new bounty is posted at T3, all designers who accept T3 work see it and can claim it. First-come or best-rated gets the job.

### The Self-Regulating Market

```
TOO MANY DESIGNERS AT T6 (cheap/slow):
→ Bounties get claimed instantly → no wait for requesters
→ Some designers move UP to T4-T5 to earn more per job
→ Market rebalances

TOO FEW DESIGNERS AT T1 (rush):
→ T1 bounties sit unclaimed → requesters see "no rush designers available"
→ Designers see opportunity → some register for T1 at premium rates
→ Market rebalances

TOO MANY DESIGNERS CHARGING RETAIL:
→ They get very few bounties (C+20 designers get priority)
→ They lower their price → "you only get C+20 when you give C+20"
→ Market rebalances
```

### Patent Relevance: HIGH

A reputation-gated creative services marketplace within a cooperative commerce platform, with: 2-completion tryout gate, XP time rating tracking, designer self-selected weekly production commitments, 6-tier urgency pools, and C+20-to-retail pricing that rewards deeper cooperative commitment with higher volume — represents a novel cooperative creative services matching and pricing system.

## Innovation #1992 — Volume-Priced Cooperative Services: Aggregated Demand Creates Wholesale Pricing for Logo, Domain, Email, and Design Services

### Description

A system and method within a cooperative commerce platform for achieving wholesale-level pricing on individual creative services by aggregating demand from all platform members, wherein:

(a) The platform doesn't charge individual retail prices for logos, domains, or design work. Instead, it aggregates ALL demand across ALL members and negotiates/structures volume pricing:

```
INDIVIDUAL RETAIL:
├── Logo design: $300-$1,000 (Fiverr, 99designs)
├── Domain + setup: $50-$200/year
├── Email hosting: $5-$15/month
├── Business card design: $50-$200
└── Total "look professional" cost: $500-$1,500

LIANA BANYAN VOLUME-PRICED:
├── Logo design: 50-200 Marks (cooperative designers, volume pool)
├── Domain + setup: 30-100 Marks (bulk domain purchasing)
├── Email hosting: Included with domain (platform-negotiated)
├── Designed Cue Card: 20-80 Marks (cooperative designers)
└── Total "look professional" cost: 100-380 Marks
    = $100-$380 equivalent (at $1=1 Marks)
    = 50-75% cheaper than retail
```

(b) The discount is possible because:
- Designers are platform members who earn Marks (not cash overhead)
- Domain registration is bulk-purchased (thousands at a time)
- Email hosting is negotiated at platform scale
- The 20% margin is constitutionally capped (not 50%+ like design platforms)
- Volume creates predictability for designers (steady work vs. feast/famine)

(c) Every service follows the same C+20 to retail pricing structure. Members who pay at C+20 rates get the cheapest possible services AND contribute the most to the cooperative.

### Patent Relevance: HIGH

A cooperative demand aggregation system that converts individual creative service needs (logo, domain, email, card design) into volume-priced wholesale offerings through: platform-scale bulk purchasing, cooperative designer pools, constitutionally capped margins, and tiered member pricing — represents a novel application of cooperative economics to creative services.
