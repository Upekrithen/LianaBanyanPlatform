## DESCRIPTION

A multi-agent arbitration visualization system where four distinct AI judges (Oracle, Morpheus, Red Queen, Dredd) evaluate dispute cases in a structured reasoning matrix. The matrix displays judge responses across four columns with shared question rows, making convergence and divergence visible at a glance. Founder override exists as an annotation layer — not as dominant control — with timestamps and reasoning visible in the audit trail. An escalation ladder (Areopagus) provides a visible pathway for cases that exceed the automated arbitration threshold.

## MECHANICS

1. **Four canonical judges** with distinct reasoning profiles:
   - Oracle (Claude backend) — principled, precedent-oriented
   - Morpheus (Claude backend) — contextual, empathy-weighted
   - Red Queen (Perplexity backend) — adversarial, stress-tests claims
   - Dredd (Perplexity backend) — rule-literal, enforcement-focused
2. **JudgeReasoningMatrix** component: 4 columns (one per judge), rows are structured questions about the case. Each cell contains the judge's response. Convergence (3-4 judges agree) highlighted in one color; divergence (split decisions) highlighted in another.
3. **Convergence/divergence indicators** at a glance — not buried in text. Visual pattern makes unanimous vs. split visible without reading every cell.
4. **Founder override** rendered as an **annotation layer**: timestamp, reasoning text, overlaid on the matrix without replacing judge responses. The override is CONTEXT, not COMMAND. Judges' reasoning persists — the override sits alongside, not above.
5. **EscalationLadder** component: visible path showing Standard Review → Star Chamber → Areopagus escalation. Not hidden. Not buried in settings. The member can see where their case sits in the process.
6. **AuditTrailTable**: Full chronological record of every judge response, every escalation, every annotation. Permanent, immutable, visible to case participants.
7. **Aesthetic constraint**: Arbitration panel, not courtroom fantasy. No judge avatars. No AI oracle theatrics. Solemn but not ominous.

## PRIOR ART DISTINCTION

- **Traditional arbitration platforms** (JAMS, AAA Online): Text-based rulings with rationale. No multi-agent parallel reasoning. No visual convergence/divergence matrix. Single arbitrator model.
- **AI moderation systems** (Meta Oversight Board, Trust & Safety tools): Binary decisions (approve/reject) with optional explanation. No multi-judge reasoning matrix. No transparent divergence visualization.
- **Multi-agent AI debate** (Anthropic constitutional AI, OpenAI debate): Research methods for AI alignment. Not member-facing arbitration tools. No visual matrix. No founder annotation layer.
- **No prior art found** for: (a) a four-agent parallel reasoning matrix with visual convergence/divergence indicators for member-facing dispute resolution, (b) founder override as an annotation layer that preserves rather than replaces AI judge reasoning, (c) a visible escalation ladder integrated into the arbitration interface.

## CLAIMS

1. A multi-agent arbitration visualization system comprising: four distinct AI judge agents with differentiated reasoning profiles; a structured matrix displaying each judge's response to shared questions in parallel columns; visual convergence and divergence indicators enabling at-a-glance assessment of judge agreement.

2. An authority annotation method for AI-assisted arbitration comprising: a founder override mechanism rendered as an annotation layer alongside AI judge responses; said override preserving the original judge reasoning rather than replacing it; timestamp and reasoning text displayed as contextual overlay.

3. A dispute escalation visualization comprising: a visible escalation ladder showing standard review, multi-agent chamber, and exceptional-case appeal pathways; integrated into the arbitration interface so that case participants can see current position and available escalation paths at all times.
