## Innovation #2015 — Guild Formation as Fifth Cold Start Pathway: "Start a Guild" Onboarding

### Description

A system and method within a cooperative commerce platform wherein starting a Guild is presented as a fifth Cold Start pathway alongside Food, Manufacturing, Service, and Local Business — extending the "All paths lead to Captain" principle to community organizers:

(a) The Cold Start Hub gains a fifth card: **Guild** (purple gradient). The wizard guides a member from zero to Guild Leader through:
  - Step 1: **Define Your Guild** — Name, purpose, professional domain (makers, designers, farmers, drivers, tutors, etc.)
  - Step 2: **Set Your Banner** — Upload a temporary banner or launch a design contest immediately
  - Step 3: **Recruit Your Crew** — Share an invite link, set minimum member count to activate (default: 10, matching the Captain threshold)
  - Step 4: **Fund Your Treasury** — Seed the guild's collective Credit pool (even $5 from the founder starts the treasury)
  - Step 5: **Launch Your First Contest** — The guild's first act is a Banner Contest, funded from the treasury, voted on by founding members

(b) Guild formation creates a Captain pathway: the Guild Leader IS a Captain of the guild's domain. "All paths lead to Captain" holds — even the community organizer path.

### Patent Relevance: HIGH

A cooperative commerce platform wherein guild formation is presented as a primary onboarding pathway with a guided wizard that culminates in the guild's first collective action (a funded design contest for visual identity) — creating immediate member engagement, treasury activation, and democratic governance from the moment of formation.

## Innovation #2016 — Guild Collective Treasury: Pooled Credits for Group Economic Activity

### Description

A system and method within a cooperative commerce platform wherein:

(a) Each Guild maintains a **Collective Treasury** — a pooled Credit account funded by voluntary member contributions. The treasury is:
  - Funded by: member donations (any amount), contest entry fees, guild-earned Credits from platform activity
  - Governed by: guild-internal voting (how to spend the treasury)
  - Transparent: all contributions and expenditures visible to guild members on a guild-specific ledger
  - NOT extractable: Credits in the treasury follow the same one-way valve rule — they can be SPENT within the platform, never withdrawn as fiat

(b) Treasury uses:
  - **Design Contests** — Fund banner, theme, mascot, and icon competitions
  - **Group Purchases** — Aggregate buying power for materials, tools, services
  - **Member Support** — Fund a member's LB Card for a specific purpose (connects to #2009 Community-Supported Card Funding)
  - **Initiative Voting** — Pool Credits to vote on platform-wide initiatives (amplified collective voice)
  - **Bounties** — Post guild-specific Larks with Credit rewards for improvements
  - **Events** — Fund guild gatherings, workshops, showcases

(c) Treasury governance:
  - Expenditures below a guild-set threshold (e.g., 50 Credits): Guild Leader can approve unilaterally
  - Expenditures above threshold: Requires guild member vote (simple majority)
  - Emergency fund: 10% of treasury is reserved and requires supermajority (75%) to access
  - Audit trail: Every transaction logged, timestamped, member-visible

### Patent Relevance: HIGH

A cooperative commerce platform wherein professional guilds maintain collective Credit treasuries with tiered spending authority (leader approval for small amounts, democratic vote for large amounts, supermajority for reserves) and transparent ledger — enabling group economic activity including funded design contests, aggregate purchasing, member support, and initiative voting — represents a novel sub-cooperative economic structure within a cooperative platform.

## Innovation #2017 — Guild Visual Identity Stack: Banner + Theme + Mascot + Icon as Democratic Visual Package

### Description

A system and method within a cooperative commerce platform wherein each Guild develops a complete visual identity stack through democratic contests:

(a) The Visual Identity Stack:
  - **Guild Banner**: Header image displayed on guild pages, member profiles (opt-in), and guild directory listings
  - **Guild Theme**: Complete CSS override applied to guild members' platform view (Layer 2 in theme governance)
  - **Guild Mascot**: Character or figure representing the guild (used in Beacon Runs, contests, events)
  - **Guild Icon**: Small symbol/logo used in badges, nav items, and member profiles
  - **Guild Color Palette**: Primary/secondary/accent colors used across all guild visual elements

(b) Each element is created through a funded contest:
  - Guild pools Credits into the contest treasury
  - Open call for submissions (guild members, or platform-wide depending on guild rules)
  - Guild members vote using Document Voting (reputation-only, one vote per submission)
  - Winner receives the prize pool Credits + Marks
  - Winning design becomes the guild's official visual identity

(c) The stack is **modular** — a guild can have a banner without a theme, or an icon without a mascot. Each element is independently contestable and replaceable. A guild can re-run a contest to refresh any element at any time.

(d) The stack is **progressive** — new guilds start with just a banner (the first contest in the formation wizard). As the guild grows and its treasury fills, it can contest more elements. A mature guild has the full stack.

### Patent Relevance: MEDIUM

A cooperative commerce platform wherein professional guilds develop a modular visual identity stack (banner, theme, mascot, icon, color palette) through a progressive series of treasury-funded design contests with democratic member voting — creating a living visual identity that evolves with the guild's maturity and collective investment.

## Innovation #2018 — Guild Benefit Cascade: Compounding Value of Guild Membership

### Description

A system and method within a cooperative commerce platform wherein guild membership unlocks a cascade of compounding benefits:

**Identity & Pride:**
- Guild Banner on your profile (visual affiliation signal)
- Guild Theme (your platform looks like YOUR guild)
- Guild Mascot appears in your Beacon Runs
- Guild Badge on your member card and Deck Cards

**Economic:**
- Access to Guild Treasury (collective purchasing power)
- Guild Volume Discounts (aggregate orders for better prices)
- Guild-Funded Design Contests (earn Credits by winning)
- Guild-Specific Larks (bounties for guild-relevant improvements)
- Shared Librarian Context (guild's institutional knowledge through Sipping Tea architecture — cheaper, smarter AI for guild-specific questions)

**Governance:**
- Vote on guild-internal decisions (themes, banners, treasury spending)
- Guild representation in portal governance
- Guild Design Democracy (your guild's look is YOUR vote)
- Guild can nominate themes for platform-wide consideration

**Professional:**
- Crew Tables (form work crews with guild members)
- Skill Recognition (guild membership = verified capability)
- Mentorship (Captain's Apprentice within the guild)
- Guild Side Quests (guild-specific job board)
- Guild Portfolio (showcase of guild members' work)

**Community:**
- Guild Beacon Runs (discovery routes featuring guild members' work)
- Guild Cue Cards (tailored onboarding for new guild members)
- Guild Events (funded from treasury, organized by leadership)
- Guild Contests (not just banners — contests for best product, service, innovation)
- Guild Chat/Forum (guild-specific communication channel)

**The Compound Effect:** Each benefit reinforces the others. The treasury funds contests, which create visual identity, which attracts new members, who contribute to the treasury, which funds more contests. The guild becomes a self-sustaining economic and creative unit within the cooperative.

### Patent Relevance: HIGH

A cooperative commerce platform wherein guild membership provides a cascade of compounding benefits across five domains (identity, economic, governance, professional, community) — with each domain reinforcing the others through treasury-funded contests, democratic governance, and shared institutional knowledge — creating self-sustaining sub-cooperative units within the larger cooperative structure.

## Innovation #2019 — Tribe Mirror: Geographic and Interest Groups with the Same Benefit Stack

### Description

A system and method within a cooperative commerce platform wherein Tribes (geographic and interest-based community groups) receive the SAME benefit stack as Guilds (#2018), adapted for community context:

(a) **Tribe = Guild, but for geography or interest instead of profession.** Same treasury, same contests, same visual identity stack, same governance. Different organizing principle.

(b) Tribe types:
  - **Geographic**: "San Antonio South Side Tribe", "Austin East Tribe" — neighbors supporting neighbors
  - **Interest**: "Gardeners Tribe", "Board Gamers Tribe", "Homeschool Tribe" — shared passion
  - **Cultural**: "Spanish-Speaking Tribe" (maps to hexislo.com), "Veterans Tribe" — shared identity
  - **Hybrid**: "San Antonio Food Trucks" — geographic + professional overlap (could be a Guild OR a Tribe)

(c) Tribes have their own:
  - Tribe Banner, Theme, Mascot, Icon (contested from treasury)
  - Tribe Treasury (pooled Credits with governance)
  - Tribe Beacon Runs (discover your neighborhood)
  - Tribe Theme (Layer 3 in governance hierarchy)
  - Tribe Captains (leaders who organize the geographic/interest community)

(d) A member can belong to ONE Guild and ONE Tribe simultaneously. Their theme governance resolves: Personal Override > Tribe > Guild > Community Default.

### Patent Relevance: MEDIUM

A cooperative commerce platform wherein geographic and interest-based community groups (Tribes) receive the same benefit stack as professional groups (Guilds) — including collective treasury, design contests, visual identity, and internal governance — with dual membership allowing simultaneous Guild and Tribe affiliation and a hierarchical theme resolution system.
