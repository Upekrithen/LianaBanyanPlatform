## Definition

**Skipping Stones Depth Navigation** is a four-layer reading depth system that makes every section of every academic paper independently discoverable and progressively accessible. Each first-level section becomes a "Skipping Stone" — a surface-touch entry point distributed via Cue Card (with skipping stone logo and QR code). The system uses the platform's existing Pudding verbiage as structural navigation labels for three content depth tiers.

---

## Architecture

### The Four Layers

| Layer | Name | Content | Surface |
|-------|------|---------|---------|
| 1 | **Skipping Stone** | Section title + 1-sentence hook from academic paper | Cue Card with skipping stone logo + QR |
| 2 | **"The Proof is in the Pudding"** | Transition CTA — "Want to sink a little deeper?" | Landing prompt when QR scanned or card clicked |
| 3 | **Pudding** | Accessible 500-1000 word version of the section | Cephas article (Pudding series format) |
| 4 | **"This is NOT Pudding"** | Full uncompromised academic paper, deep-linked to exact section | Source paper with section anchor |

### Relationship to BST Episodes

BST (Blood, Sweat, and Tears) episodes and Skipping Stones are **parallel distribution channels** serving different reading modes:

- **BST Episodes** = **temporal** — serial narrative, posted hourly, thread-based, tells the story in order
- **Skipping Stones** = **spatial** — section-level navigation, non-sequential, enter any paper at any section

Both feed into the same Reading Beacon (#2134) tracking system. A member can discover a topic through a Skipping Stone, then find the full chronological treatment in the BST episode timeline — or vice versa.

### Scale

| Paper Count | Avg Sections per Paper | Skipping Stones | Pudding Articles | Deck Cards |
|-------------|----------------------|-----------------|-----------------|------------|
| ~30 academic | ~8-12 sections | **240-360** | **240-360** | **240-360** |

At full scale: **~300 Skipping Stones**, each paired with a Pudding article and a Deck Card. Combined with BST's ~3,000-5,000 episodes at full scale, this creates **two complete paper discovery systems** — one for browsing (Skipping Stones) and one for bingeing (BST).

### Verbiage System

The three Pudding phrases are structural navigation labels, not marketing:

| Phrase | Structural Role | UX Position |
|--------|----------------|-------------|
| **"The Proof is in the Pudding"** | Engagement invitation — CTA on Skipping Stone landing | Between Layer 1 and Layer 3 |
| **"Pudding"** (or "This IS Pudding") | Accessible version label | Layer 3 header/badge |
| **"This is NOT Pudding"** | Dense academic version label | Layer 4 link from within Pudding article |

### Reading Beacon Integration

Reading Beacons (#2134) track progress through ALL four layers:
- Skipping Stone scan = beacon created at section entry point
- Pudding completion = beacon updated to Layer 3 depth
- Full paper completion = beacon updated to Layer 4 depth
- Layer 4 completion triggers "proof" — the literal "proof in the pudding" — which feeds vote-gating, Linchpin attribution, and tier progression

### Physical Distribution

Each Skipping Stone gets a Deck Card (#2135):
- **Front**: Skipping stone logo + paper title + section title
- **Back**: QR code linking to Layer 2 ("Proof is in the Pudding" landing)
- **Distribution**: Same channels as BST Deck Cards — local businesses, events, Cue Card sharing

## Differentiation

No platform offers section-level academic paper navigation with branded depth tiers, physical distribution cards, reading position tracking, and engagement-gated progression through content layers. The combination of physical Deck Cards, digital Cue Cards, Pudding-branded content depth labels, and Reading Beacons creates a complete alternative to traditional academic paper distribution — papers meet people where they are instead of demanding people come to where papers live.
