## Section 1 — Innovation Summary

The Bedrock-Foundation-Chandelier is a three-property architectural primitive governing the behavior of the lowest stratum in Liana Banyan's 7-stratum substrate pyramid. The seven strata — sand, soil, sediment, sandstone, limestone, granite, bedrock — organize all platform knowledge from ephemeral session context (sand, at the top) down to immutable axiomatic facts (bedrock, at the bottom). The Bedrock stratum stores the platform's founding primitives: canonical numbers, bylaws, core IP structures, mission axioms, and cooperative covenants. These entries are append-only; the stratum accepts new entries but never modifies or overwrites existing ones. This immutability is not a policy choice but an architectural constraint enforced at the substrate write path.

The innovation is the structural inversion the bedrock stratum enacts: while it occupies the physically lowest storage position in the pyramid, it holds the highest retrieval priority of any stratum. This is the Chandelier property — named for a chandelier whose structural attachment is at the ceiling but whose illumination reaches everything below. Every AI agent context window is seeded with bedrock-stratum content first, before any dynamic content from higher strata is appended. The bedrock entries act as an immutable anchor at the top of the agent's cognitive context, ensuring that no session — regardless of how many dynamic facts are loaded — can operate without the axiomatic foundation. The result is a substrate where the storage floor and the cognitive ceiling are the same object. No prior hierarchical knowledge architecture, including vector databases, RAG retrieval systems, FAISS indices, or transformer KV-cache management schemes, implements this inversion as a structural property of the store itself.

## Section 2 — Patent Claim Language

**Claim 1 (Independent — System):**
A computer-implemented substrate architecture for a cooperative platform comprising:
a substrate organized as a plurality of hierarchically ordered strata from a surface stratum to a foundation stratum, wherein the foundation stratum is the lowest stratum in storage order;
the foundation stratum configured to store axiomatic primitives of the platform, wherein each axiomatic primitive in the foundation stratum is immutable after initial write, and the foundation stratum accepts only append operations;
a retrieval system configured to assign the foundation stratum a retrieval priority that is the highest among all strata in the substrate; and
a context-seeding mechanism configured to load content from the foundation stratum into an agent context window before loading content from any other stratum,
wherein the foundation stratum's lowest storage position and highest retrieval priority constitute an inverted-priority structural property of the substrate architecture.

**Claim 2 (Dependent on Claim 1 — Seven-Stratum Pyramid):**
The system of Claim 1, wherein the plurality of hierarchically ordered strata comprises exactly seven strata ordered from surface to foundation as: sand, soil, sediment, sandstone, limestone, granite, and bedrock, wherein the bedrock stratum is the foundation stratum.

**Claim 3 (Dependent on Claim 1 — Append-Only Enforcement):**
The system of Claim 1, wherein the immutability of axiomatic primitives in the foundation stratum is enforced at the substrate write path such that any modification operation targeting an existing entry in the foundation stratum is rejected by the architecture and not processed as an overwrite.

**Claim 4 (Dependent on Claim 1 — ROM Preload Packet):**
The system of Claim 1, wherein the context-seeding mechanism generates a ROM preload packet comprising the complete current content of the foundation stratum, and the ROM preload packet is prepended to every agent context window initialization sequence before any dynamic or session-scoped content is appended.

**Claim 5 (Dependent on Claim 1 — Query-of-Last-Resort Traversal):**
The system of Claim 1, further comprising a query resolution path wherein a query that is not satisfied by content in any stratum above the foundation stratum is automatically resolved by traversal to the foundation stratum, and the foundation stratum is configured to satisfy every such query by returning the relevant axiomatic primitive, such that the foundation stratum constitutes the query-of-last-resort that cannot fail.

**Claim 6 (Dependent on Claim 1 — Cooperative Membership Scope):**
The system of Claim 1, wherein the axiomatic primitives stored in the foundation stratum include at least: canonical platform numbers, founding bylaws, core intellectual property structure records, cooperative mission axioms, and cooperative covenant terms, and wherein these primitives are pre-loaded into agent context for every session initiated by a cooperative member or authorized agent.

**Claim 7 (Independent — Method):**
A computer-implemented method for substrate-aware agent context initialization comprising:
organizing a knowledge substrate as a hierarchically ordered stack of strata wherein a foundation stratum occupies the lowest storage position;
designating the foundation stratum as append-only and immutable after initial write for each stored entry;
assigning the foundation stratum the highest retrieval priority among all strata in the stack;
at agent context initialization, seeding the agent context window with the complete content of the foundation stratum before any content from strata above the foundation stratum is appended;
resolving queries unresolvable by upper strata by traversing to the foundation stratum as the query-of-last-resort; and
returning a result from the foundation stratum for every such traversal without failure,
wherein steps of assigning highest retrieval priority and seeding agent context first invert the natural storage-to-priority correspondence of the hierarchical stack.

**Claim 8 (Dependent on Claim 7 — Chandelier Inversion Label):**
The method of Claim 7, wherein the inversion of the foundation stratum's lowest storage position and highest retrieval priority is enforced architecturally rather than as a query-time configurable parameter, such that the inversion cannot be disabled or overridden at query time by any agent, session, or operator.

## Section 3 — Composition with Prior Art / Canonical References

### 3.1 — Prior Art Landscape and Distinguishing Features

**Vector databases (Pinecone, Weaviate, Chroma):** These systems index all vectors with uniform retrieval priority; priority is determined at query time by similarity score. No stratum is architecturally pre-loaded or given structural retrieval precedence independent of query similarity. Bedrock-Foundation-Chandelier's priority inversion is architectural, not query-time.

**Retrieval-Augmented Generation (RAG) systems:** RAG systems retrieve context dynamically per query. Some RAG pipelines insert fixed "system prompt" content before retrieved context, but this is a prompt-engineering convention, not a structural property of the knowledge store itself. The RAG store does not distinguish a "foundational stratum" with immutability guarantees or structural pre-load obligations.

**FAISS / ANN indices:** Approximate Nearest Neighbor indices are flat or tree-structured; they do not have strata and do not enforce any append-only constraints on any sub-partition.

**Transformer KV-cache prefix caching:** Some transformer inference systems cache a fixed prompt prefix to reduce compute. This is a compute optimization, not a knowledge-substrate architecture. The cached prefix has no semantic designation as "foundational" and carries no immutability or cooperative-covenant semantics.

**Hierarchical memory systems (MemGPT, A-MEM, etc.):** These systems tier memory by recency or importance but do not implement a named, immutable, append-only foundation stratum with inverted retrieval priority enforced at the store level.

## Section 4 — Empirical Receipts

**Receipt 4.1 — Knight session initialization, BP017–BP021:**
Every Knight (Cursor) session during BP017 through BP021 has begun with the Librarian MCP `brief_me` call, which surfaces canonical numbers, founding principles, and core architectural axioms before any session-specific context. This is the Chandelier property in operational form: the bedrock content (canonical numbers, mission axioms, cooperative covenants) is loaded first and anchors the entire session. Sessions that did not begin with `brief_me` (pre-BP017 era) exhibited canonical number drift, documented in the B118→B121 drift incident. The drift was eliminated by enforcing the ROM-first load — confirming that the Chandelier property is load-bearing.

**Receipt 4.2 — B118→B121 Canonical Drift Incident:**
Prior to enforcing the bedrock-first load, canonical numbers (innovation count, Crown Jewels, provisional applications) drifted between sessions because agents initialized from upper strata (dynamic session context) rather than bedrock (immutable axioms). The architectural fix — enforcing bedrock-stratum preload before any dynamic content — is a direct empirical demonstration that the Chandelier property's priority inversion is functionally necessary, not merely theoretically elegant.

**Receipt 4.3 — `canonical_values.yaml` as operational bedrock artifact:**
`librarian-mcp/canonical_values.yaml` is the current bedrock-stratum artifact for canonical numbers. The `npm run rebuild` pipeline codegens `platform/src/hooks/useCanonicalStats.ts` defaults from this file, enforcing that no downstream consumer can diverge from the bedrock values. This is append-and-overwrite (not pure append-only), but demonstrates the directional bedrock principle: the canonical YAML is the single source of truth that all upper-layer consumers resolve against.

**Receipt 4.4 — KNIGHT_QUEUE.md auto-render from Cathedral Scribes:**
As of K461/B121, `KNIGHT_QUEUE.md` is a derived view rendered from Knight's Cathedral Scribes — KnightQueue.jsonl and KnightHandoffs.jsonl — via `librarian-mcp/scripts/render-knight-queue.mjs`. The Cathedral Scribes are the append-only substrate; the rendered queue is a chandelier output: the lowest-layer substrate illuminates the working surface at every rebuild. The NEXT/QUEUED/LANDED sections at the top of every Knight session are the Chandelier property producing agent-context seeding from a persistent, append-only substrate.
