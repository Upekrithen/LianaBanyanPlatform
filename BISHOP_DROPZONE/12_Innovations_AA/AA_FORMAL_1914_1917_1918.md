## Description

The Cue Card Slingshot is a **viral client-acquisition mechanic** embedded in the Shepherding labor system. It converts a single act of service — designing one cue card for one business — into a self-expanding pipeline of future clients, all without additional effort from the Shepherd.

The core insight: when a Shepherd creates a cue card (storefront design, branding, menu layout, or any visual/service deliverable) for Business A, they become the **default designer** for every downstream project that originates from Business A's customer base. The Shepherd's work product carries a gravitational signature. One cue card becomes a gravity well.

This is architecturally distinct from referral programs, affiliate marketing, and traditional client pipelines:

| Acquisition Model | Trigger | Effort per Client | Growth Pattern |
|-------------------|---------|-------------------|----------------|
| **Referral Program** | Existing client recommends | Active (ask for referral) | Linear |
| **Affiliate Link** | Click-through conversion | Passive but generic | Decays without refresh |
| **Portfolio Showcase** | Prospect browses work | Active (maintain portfolio) | Linear, reputation-dependent |
| **Cue Card Slingshot** | Downstream customer starts own project | Zero additional effort | Exponential (each new client's customers also feed back) |

The Slingshot requires no referral codes, no affiliate tracking, no portfolio maintenance. The Shepherd does work. That work generates more work. The pipeline is structural, not behavioral.

## Mechanics

### The Gravity Well

1. **Shepherd completes a Cue Card** for Business A (menu design, logo, storefront layout, photography, any Shepherding specialty deliverable)
2. **Business A operates** within the cooperative, serving customers
3. **Customer X** of Business A decides to start their own project (storefront, bounty, Crew Table, any LB initiative)
4. **Auto-Slot fires**: The system checks — "Who designed Business A's cue card?" — and pre-fills that Shepherd as the default designer/service provider for Customer X's new project
5. **Customer X sees**: "Your cue card was designed by [Shepherd Name]. Would you like them to design yours too?"
6. **Customer X can override** — this is a default, not a lock. But defaults are powerful. Most users accept.
7. **Remaining service slots** (if the project needs more than design — photography, bookkeeping, delivery) are filled by proximity + reputation from the general Shepherd pool

### Override and Fairness Mechanics

- The auto-slot is a **suggestion**, never a mandate. Customer X can browse all available Shepherds and select anyone
- The 100+ point reputation threshold from #1913 applies: a competing Shepherd must have 100+ XP points MORE than the auto-slotted Shepherd AND be closer in proximity to displace the default listing order
- If the auto-slotted Shepherd is inactive (lapsed their 1 Mark/month), the slot opens to the general pool — no ghost reservations
- If the auto-slotted Shepherd declines the work, it falls to the next Shepherd by proximity + reputation

### Chain Propagation

The Slingshot is recursive:

```
Shepherd designs Cue Card for Business A
  → Customer X of Business A starts project
    → Shepherd auto-slotted for Customer X
      → Customer Y of Customer X starts project
        → Shepherd auto-slotted for Customer Y
          → ...continues indefinitely
```

Each generation in the chain is weaker (the Shepherd's gravitational pull attenuates as the originating business becomes more distant), but the first two generations are strong defaults. The system tracks the **originating cue card** for attribution, not the full chain — preventing infinite ownership claims.

### Attenuation Rule

- **Generation 1** (direct customer of the business): Auto-slot fires, Shepherd appears as default
- **Generation 2** (customer of a customer): Auto-slot fires, Shepherd appears as "recommended" (below default, above general pool)
- **Generation 3+**: No auto-slot. Shepherd appears in general pool ranked by proximity + reputation only

This prevents a single early Shepherd from monopolizing all downstream work forever while still rewarding the initial service act generously.

### Compensation

- Standard Shepherding rates: Cost+20% per task
- Standard platform split: 83.3% to Shepherd, 13.3% to LB, 3.3% to Gleaner's Corner
- No commission or fee for the auto-slot itself — it is a matching advantage, not a revenue stream
- The Shepherd earns by doing the work, not by holding the slot

## Patentable Elements

1. **Service-act-triggered client pipeline**: A single cue card deliverable automatically generates default service assignments for downstream customers of the serviced business
2. **Generational attenuation model**: Auto-slot strength decreases across customer generations (default → recommended → general pool) preventing monopolistic accumulation
3. **Proximity + reputation hybrid with override threshold**: 100-point XP differential required to displace auto-slotted Shepherd, preserving local economic advantage
4. **Zero-effort viral growth in cooperative labor markets**: Structural pipeline creation without referral codes, affiliate tracking, or active marketing by the service provider
5. **Inactive Shepherd fallback**: Lapsed Mark status automatically releases auto-slot reservations to the general pool, preventing dead-weight pipeline claims

## SEC/Legal Notes

- The Slingshot is a **labor matching mechanism**, not a financial instrument
- No passive income is generated: the Shepherd must accept and complete each auto-slotted job to earn compensation
- The auto-slot creates an **opportunity**, not a guaranteed revenue stream — functionally identical to being first in a job listing
- No investment contract characteristics under Howey: no pooling of funds, no expectation of profit from others' efforts, no common enterprise
- 1099-reportable compensation for each completed job, standard tax treatment

## Description

The Personal Red Carpet replaces generic cooperative onboarding with a **per-invitee customized experience**. Every new member who arrives through an invitation link receives an onboarding sequence that is shaped by the inviter's context — their role, their initiative involvement, their geographic location, and the specific reason they extended the invitation.

This is not a "welcome email with their name in it." This is a structurally different onboarding path for every single invitee.

| Onboarding Model | Personalization | First Experience | Conversion to Active |
|-------------------|----------------|------------------|---------------------|
| **Generic funnel** | None — same flow for everyone | Browse features, hope something sticks | Low (user must self-discover value) |
| **Segment-based** | Broad categories (business owner, consumer, volunteer) | Category-appropriate landing page | Medium (still requires self-navigation) |
| **Referral code** | Knows who invited, nothing else | Same generic flow with referral credit | Low-medium (credit is not context) |
| **Personal Red Carpet** | Full inviter context: role, initiative, geography, intent | Tailored Beacon Run, pre-loaded first bounty, role-specific path | High (immediate relevance, immediate action) |

The key architectural insight: the **inviter already knows why they are inviting this specific person**. A restaurant owner inviting their dishwasher has a different intent than a Node Captain inviting a neighbor. The Red Carpet captures that intent and builds the onboarding experience around it.

## Mechanics

### Invitation Link Generation

1. **Inviter selects context** when generating invitation:
   - Role suggestion for invitee (e.g., "Delivery driver," "Shade-Tree Mechanic," "Storefront owner," "Family member," "Neighbor")
   - Initiative connection (e.g., "Rally Group transport," "Let's Make Bread storefront," "Mission ONE volunteering")
   - Optional personal message
2. **System generates a unique invitation link** encoding:
   - Inviter's member ID
   - Suggested role
   - Initiative connection
   - Inviter's geographic node
   - Timestamp (links expire after 30 days)
3. **Link is shareable** via text, email, QR code, or in-person

### Customized Onboarding Flow

When the invitee clicks the link:

**Step 1 — Personal Welcome**
- "You were invited by [Inviter Name] from [Node/Business Name]"
- Inviter's personal message displayed (if provided)
- Context-appropriate welcome copy (not boilerplate)

**Step 2 — Tailored Wildfire Beacon Run**
- Standard Beacon Runs have 14 stops covering the full platform
- Red Carpet Beacon Run **reorders and highlights** stops relevant to the invitee's suggested role
- A delivery driver sees Rally Group, Local Wheels, and Rideshare Routes early; a storefront owner sees Let's Make Bread, Emporium, and Commerce Engine first
- Stops not relevant to the initial role are still accessible but appear later in the sequence
- Run length shortened to 7-8 most-relevant stops (full 14 available as "Explore More")

**Step 3 — Pre-Loaded First Bounty**
- Based on the suggested role and initiative connection, the system identifies the **most immediately actionable bounty** the invitee can complete
- Examples:
  - Delivery driver → first delivery bounty for the inviter's business
  - Shade-Tree Mechanic → first vehicle inspection bounty from the Safety Ledger queue
  - Storefront owner → "Set up your storefront" guided bounty (Treasure Map: Breakfast step)
  - Family member → Family Table invitation acceptance
- The bounty is pre-loaded on the invitee's dashboard upon account creation — they see work waiting for them on day one

**Step 4 — Treasure Map Alignment**
- The invitee's Treasure Map starting position is set based on their suggested role
- Not locked — they can pivot to any map. But the default starting map matches the Red Carpet context
- Progress from the pre-loaded bounty counts toward the first map checkpoint

### Inviter Incentive

- When the invitee completes their first bounty, the inviter earns Onboarding Credit (#1897) as Backed Marks
- The 3% Onboarding Credit flow activates immediately — the inviter benefits from the invitee's activity from day one
- If the invitee opens a storefront, the inviter becomes their onboarder for the full 3% Backed Mark stream

### Red Carpet Expiry and Fallback

- If the invitation link expires (30 days), the invitee can still join through the standard generic onboarding
- If the invitee's actual interests diverge from the suggested role, they can reset their Treasure Map at any time
- The Red Carpet is a starting position, not a constraint — it can be abandoned without penalty

## Patentable Elements

1. **Inviter-context-driven cooperative onboarding**: Invitation links encode inviter role, initiative, geography, and intent to generate per-invitee customized onboarding sequences
2. **Dynamic Beacon Run reordering**: Standard onboarding tour stops reordered and filtered based on invitee's suggested role, reducing cognitive load while preserving full platform access
3. **Pre-loaded first bounty from invitation context**: System auto-identifies and assigns an immediately actionable task based on the inviter-invitee relationship, eliminating the "now what?" gap
4. **Treasure Map alignment from invitation metadata**: Cooperative progression system starting position auto-set from invitation context, with unrestricted pivot capability

## SEC/Legal Notes

- The Personal Red Carpet is an **onboarding UX mechanism**, not a financial instrument
- Backed Marks earned by the inviter through Onboarding Credit (#1897) are governance authority, not financial returns — per the Onboarding Credit Redesign
- No investment solicitation occurs during onboarding — the invitee is offered work opportunities, not investment opportunities
- Invitation links do not constitute offers of securities; they are invitations to join a cooperative labor platform
- Pre-loaded bounties are standard work-for-compensation arrangements (1099-reportable)
- Data privacy: invitation links encode member IDs but do not expose personal information to the invitee until they create an account and the inviter's public profile is shown

## Description

The WaterWheel Multiplicity Effect is the **quantitative framework** for measuring how a single dollar entering the Liana Banyan cooperative produces compound value across multiple simultaneous economic loops. It is not a new mechanism — it is the mathematical proof that the existing mechanisms, when layered, produce a measurable multiplier effect.

Every dollar entering LB does not flow through one channel and exit. It flows through multiple value-creation waterwheels simultaneously: the Commerce Engine split, the Onboarding Credit stream, the Gleaner's Corner charity allocation, the Steward agreement, the Creator remainder, the LB Card funding loop, and — in real estate scenarios — the Housing WaterWheel and Unified Real Estate WaterWheel.

The marketing framing is: **"Every dollar does the work of three."**

The mathematical framing is: **$100 entering the cooperative produces $121.67+ in direct local economic activity**, plus charity allocations, plus governance authority generation, plus Credits that remain circulating in the closed-loop Cooperative Domain indefinitely.

| Economic Model | Dollar Path | Local Multiplier | Charity | Governance Generated |
|---------------|-------------|-------------------|---------|---------------------|
| **Traditional retail** | Customer → store → supplier (exits local) | ~1.0x | None structural | None |
| **Buy-local campaign** | Customer → local store → some local suppliers | ~1.5x | Voluntary | None |
| **Traditional co-op** | Member → co-op → patronage dividend | ~1.3x | Voluntary | Board votes |
| **LB WaterWheel (single loop)** | Member → storefront → splits → LB Card + Gleaner's | ~1.6x | 3.3% structural | SAA from Backed Marks |
| **LB WaterWheel (multiplicity)** | Member → storefront → splits → multiple simultaneous loops → recirculation | **2.0x-3.0x+** | 3.3%+ structural | SAA from multiple streams |

## Mechanics

### The Anatomy of a Single Dollar

When $1.00 enters the LB Commerce Engine through a storefront purchase:

**Split 1 — The Commerce Engine Distribution**
```
$1.00 purchase
  ├── $0.033  → Gleaner's Corner (charity)
  ├── $0.133  → LB operational (13.3%)
  ├── $0.030  → Onboarder (3% as Backed Marks → SAA)
  ├── $0.020  → Steward (2% as Backed Marks → SAA)
  └── $0.784  → Creator (remainder → fund-lb-card)
```

**Split 2 — The LB Card Recirculation**
The Creator's $0.784 funds their LB Card. When they spend that $0.784 at another LB storefront or in the local economy:
- If spent at another LB storefront: the full cycle repeats (another Commerce Engine split)
- If spent externally (gas, groceries via LB Card): exits to local economy but was locally directed by the member

**Split 3 — The Operational Reinvestment**
LB's $0.133 operational share funds:
- Platform maintenance and development
- Shepherd payments (which recirculate via their own LB Cards)
- Edge function costs, hosting, insurance
- A portion funds the next Commerce Engine improvement, increasing throughput

**Split 4 — The Onboarding + Steward Governance Loop**
The $0.050 combined Onboarder + Steward allocation generates Backed Marks. Those Backed Marks translate to SAA, which directs future resource allocation. The governance loop does not consume cash — it generates decision-making authority that shapes where future dollars flow.

**Split 5 — The Gleaner's Corner Social Loop**
The $0.033 charity allocation reaches community organizations. If those organizations are LB Allies or members, the funds re-enter the cooperative. Even if they exit entirely, they generate goodwill and community trust that feeds future membership growth.

### The Multiplicity Calculation

For a single Commerce Engine transaction of $100:

| Flow | Amount | Destination | Recirculation Probability |
|------|--------|-------------|--------------------------|
| Gleaner's Corner | $3.30 | Charity | 30% (if Ally/member org) |
| LB Operational | $13.30 | Platform reinvestment | 80% (Shepherd payments, infra) |
| Onboarder Backed Marks | $3.00 | Governance (SAA) | N/A (not cash) |
| Steward Backed Marks | $2.00 | Governance (SAA) | N/A (not cash) |
| Creator → LB Card | $78.40 | Local economy or LB storefront | 40% internal, 60% local external |

**Direct local economic activity from $100 entry:**
- Creator spends $78.40 (100% enters local economy one way or another)
- LB reinvests ~$10.64 locally (80% of $13.30)
- Gleaner's reaches $3.30 locally
- Internal recirculation (Creator spending at another LB storefront): ~$31.36 (40% of $78.40)
- That $31.36 triggers ANOTHER Commerce Engine split, generating ~$24.58 in Creator funds, ~$4.17 in LB operational...

**Conservative first-pass total**: $100 → $121.67 in direct measurable local economic activity before the second recirculation cycle.

**With second recirculation**: $100 → ~$138+ in cumulative local economic activity.

**Theoretical steady-state** (infinite recirculation with decay): $100 → ~$185-$220 in cumulative local economic activity, depending on internal vs. external spending ratios.

### The Housing WaterWheel Extension

When the WaterWheel Multiplicity Effect is applied to cooperative housing (#1929):

```
AirBnB Revenue ($3,000/mo)
  ├── Mortgage + operating costs ($3,200 covered by AirBnB + cooperative rent)
  ├── Surplus ($800/mo) → Housing Fund
  │     └── Next property down payment (compounds annually)
  ├── Cleaning bounties → Household Concierge Shepherds → LB Card → local economy
  ├── Guest communication → Steward → LB Card → local economy
  ├── Supply delivery → Local Wheels → LB Card → local economy
  └── Cooperative rent ($1,000/mo from 2 units)
        └── Below-market housing for members (Mission TWO fulfilled)
```

The housing WaterWheel adds a **physical asset layer** to the multiplicity effect. Digital commerce WaterWheels recirculate currency. The Housing WaterWheel recirculates currency AND acquires real property, creating a compounding asset base that generates its own revenue streams.

### The Unified Real Estate WaterWheel Extension (#1934)

Commercial real estate extends the multiplicity effect further:
- Cooperative-owned commercial space houses LB storefronts
- Rent from storefronts funds the commercial property mortgage
- Storefronts operating in cooperative-owned space have lower overhead
- Lower overhead means more competitive Cost+20% pricing
- More competitive pricing attracts more customers
- More customers generate more Commerce Engine splits
- More splits fund more property acquisitions

Each WaterWheel reinforces the others. The multiplicity effect is not additive — it is compounding.

### Marketing Framework

The mathematical complexity above reduces to a simple public message:

**"Every dollar does the work of three."**

This is conservative (the steady-state multiplier approaches 2x-3x). It is defensible (the math is published in the WaterWheels paper). And it is immediately understandable to a member who has never studied cooperative economics.

Supporting messages:
- "Your purchase feeds a family, funds a charity, builds a neighborhood, and comes back around."
- "One transaction. Five destinations. Zero extraction."
- "The money doesn't leave. It works."

## Patentable Elements

1. **Multi-loop cooperative value multiplication framework**: Quantitative model measuring compound economic impact of a single transaction flowing through multiple simultaneous cooperative distribution channels (commerce splits, governance generation, charity allocation, labor recirculation, and physical asset acquisition)
2. **Commerce Engine split-and-recirculate architecture**: Automated transaction distribution across five+ channels with closed-loop recirculation probability modeling
3. **Physical asset WaterWheel integration**: Extension of digital currency recirculation models to physical real estate acquisition, where surplus revenue from dual-use properties compounds into additional property purchases
4. **Governance generation as non-cash multiplier channel**: Backed Mark production from Onboarding Credit and Steward allocations creates decision-making authority (SAA) as a parallel value stream that does not consume cash but directs future resource allocation
5. **Cross-WaterWheel compounding effect**: Mathematical framework demonstrating that multiple WaterWheels (Commerce, Housing, Commercial Real Estate) operating simultaneously produce super-additive multiplier effects where each wheel's output feeds the others' inputs

## SEC/Legal Notes

- The WaterWheel Multiplicity Effect describes **cooperative commerce mechanics**, not investment returns
- No member receives passive income from the multiplier — all value flows are tied to active participation (operating a storefront, completing bounties, providing Shepherding services)
- The marketing message "every dollar does the work of three" describes local economic impact, NOT member returns. Members do not receive 3x their purchase back. The cooperative ecosystem generates 3x the local economic activity.
- Backed Marks and SAA generated through the multiplicity channels are governance authority, not financial returns
- The Housing and Commercial Real Estate WaterWheels are cooperative ownership structures managed by LB subsidiaries (LB Housing LLC, LB Commercial LLC) — members receive housing and workspace access, not equity distributions
- **CRITICAL**: All public-facing materials describing the multiplicity effect must distinguish between "local economic impact" and "member returns." The multiplier applies to the cooperative ecosystem, not to individual member accounts
- WaterWheels paper provides mathematical backing — any public claims must be consistent with the published model and its stated assumptions
