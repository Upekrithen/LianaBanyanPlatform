## TL;DR

A computer-implemented method for accumulating, indexing, and distributing a corpus of operator-scale guides authored by cooperative platform members describing the specific operational steps by which they scaled a business, service, or cooperative activity from one vessel-tier rung to the next, such that each guide is a first-person case study indexed by operator rung, industry, vessel-tier progression, and cooperative pathway, and the corpus collectively forms a **distributed peer-to-peer training substrate** for operators at earlier rungs to learn from operators one or two steps ahead of them. The corpus is the output of #2244 Opt-In Member Documentation consent at scale, and its existence operationalizes the #2242 Peer-Proximity Expertise Matching thesis by providing durable, queryable, searchable guide material that members can consult between Patron engagements.

---

## Problem Solved

Conventional operator education has three failure modes: (1) case studies written by observers about operators (retrospective, filtered, interpretation-heavy); (2) generic frameworks written by consultants (abstract, non-operator-voice, missing the texture of lived decision-making); (3) one-off mentorship that doesn't scale beyond the mentor's bandwidth. **A corpus of first-person operator guides, authored voluntarily by members under #2244 opt-in consent, solves all three failure modes simultaneously** by producing (a) first-person rather than observer voice, (b) operator-scale specificity rather than generic framework, and (c) durable searchable content that scales independently of any individual operator's bandwidth.

## Independent Claim

**Claim 1.** A computer-implemented method for accumulating and distributing a member-generated guide corpus in a cooperative platform, comprising:

(a) Receiving, from members who have opted in under a member documentation consent framework, one or more first-person guides, each guide comprising: a narrative description of a specific operational transition from a first vessel-tier rung to a second vessel-tier rung, a list of specific steps taken during the transition, an enumeration of specific resources deployed, a list of specific obstacles encountered, and a set of specific lessons learned;

(b) Tagging each received guide with structured metadata comprising: originating member identifier (subject to per-contribution consent), starting vessel-tier rung, ending vessel-tier rung, industry classification, cooperative pathway classification, and a timestamp of the originating operational transition;

(c) Storing the tagged guides in a persistent corpus database accessible to cooperative members;

(d) Providing a query interface enabling members to retrieve guides matching one or more filter criteria comprising: target ending vessel-tier rung, originating industry, vessel-tier transition delta (e.g., "guides for transitioning from Dinghy to Rowboat"), cooperative pathway, and keyword search;

(e) Ranking query results by structural proximity to the querying member's current vessel-tier rung and industry, such that guides authored by members one vessel-tier step ahead of the querying member are ranked higher than guides authored by members multiple steps ahead;

(f) Maintaining the corpus such that guides cannot be removed except by member consent withdrawal under the underlying opt-in consent framework, preserving the corpus as a durable reference resource across time.

**Dependent Claim 1.1** — The method of Claim 1, wherein the structural proximity ranking of (e) implements the peer-proximity expertise matching principle by surfacing the most proximally-relevant guides first, consistent with empirical findings that one-step-ahead operators produce more actionable guidance than multiple-step-ahead operators for earlier-rung learners.

**Dependent Claim 1.2** — The method of Claim 1, wherein the corpus is exposed to the cooperative platform's growth-stage advisory engagement system (Open Water) such that members publishing growth briefs receive suggested guides from the corpus relevant to their stated transition goals before they engage a human Patron.

**Dependent Claim 1.3** — The method of Claim 1, wherein the corpus is additionally exposed to external academic researchers under a Living Laboratory research framework, subject to per-guide granular consent, such that the corpus serves as an empirical substrate for research on peer-proximity expertise transfer, cooperative economic outcomes, and operator skill acquisition.

**Dependent Claim 1.4** — The method of Claim 1, further comprising a credit-allocation mechanism whereby members whose guides are cited or referenced by other members' successful transitions receive Service Allocation Authority accrual proportional to the derivative transitions, creating a compounding recognition loop for operators who document effectively.

**Dependent Claim 1.5** — The method of Claim 1, wherein the corpus is rendered in a format that enables derivative platform content (puddings, papers, cue cards, mission briefings) to cite specific guides by stable identifier, preserving citation integrity across future platform iterations.

**Dependent Claim 1.6** — A system comprising a processor, a persistent corpus database, a query interface, and instructions stored on a non-transitory computer-readable medium which, when executed, cause the processor to perform the method of Claim 1 through Claim 1.5.

## Prior Art Distinction

Case study libraries (Harvard Business Review, MIT Sloan Management Review), mentorship networks (SCORE, SBDC), and online operator communities (IndieHackers, MicroConf forums) all provide operator education content but none combine (a) strict first-person operator voice (not observer voice), (b) vessel-tier-indexed structural metadata enabling proximity-ranked retrieval, (c) derivation from an opt-in cooperative consent framework rather than from external solicitation, (d) integration with a peer-proximity advisory engagement system, and (e) exposure to academic research under a living-laboratory consent architecture. **The combination is novel in cooperative platform architecture.**
