## Innovation #2013 — Tiered Theme Governance: Guild, Tribe, Personal Override, and Community Default

### Description

A system and method within a cooperative commerce platform wherein visual themes are applied through a layered governance hierarchy, with each level having different rules for adoption and cost:

(a) **Community Default (free):** The theme the portal's full membership voted into the default position. Every member sees this unless they or their group has chosen otherwise. Costs nothing — this is the democratic baseline.

(b) **Guild/Crew Theme:** A guild (professional group — designers, makers, captains) can vote among themselves for a guild-specific theme. All guild members see the guild theme instead of the community default. Guild voting is internal — only guild members vote. Free for guild members.

(c) **Tribe Theme:** A tribe (geographic or interest-based community group) can vote for their own theme. Tribe members see the tribe theme. Tribes can be neighborhoods, cities, language groups, or affinity groups. Free for tribe members.

(d) **Personal Override (at a cost):** Any individual member can lock their view to a specific theme regardless of what the community, guild, or tribe voted for. This costs Credits — a small ongoing fee to maintain the override. The cost serves three purposes:
  - Creates a signal: if many members are paying to override, the community default may need revisiting
  - Generates revenue for theme creators (the Credits flow to the creator of the chosen theme)
  - Prevents trivial fragmentation while preserving individual choice

(e) **"Just let it be" (free):** Accept whatever flows down from the governance hierarchy — community default, or guild/tribe theme if you belong to one. Zero friction, zero cost. This is the expected path for most members.

### The Hierarchy

```
LAYER 4: Personal Override ($) ← costs Credits to maintain
LAYER 3: Tribe Theme (free)    ← geographic/interest group vote
LAYER 2: Guild Theme (free)    ← professional group vote
LAYER 1: Community Default (free) ← portal-wide democratic vote

Resolution order: Layer 4 > Layer 3 > Layer 2 > Layer 1
(Higher layers override lower layers)
```

### The Economic Signal

```
If 5% of members pay for personal override → healthy diversity, no concern
If 25% of members pay for personal override → the default might be bad
If 50%+ override → governance should trigger a re-vote on the default

The override rate IS the feedback mechanism.
The cost IS the signal.
```

### Patent Relevance: HIGH

A cooperative commerce platform wherein visual theme application follows a hierarchical governance model — community default (democratic), guild/crew (professional group), tribe (geographic/interest), and personal override (individual at cost) — with override adoption rates serving as a built-in feedback signal for design governance quality — represents a novel multi-layer approach to cooperative visual identity that balances collective governance with individual expression.
