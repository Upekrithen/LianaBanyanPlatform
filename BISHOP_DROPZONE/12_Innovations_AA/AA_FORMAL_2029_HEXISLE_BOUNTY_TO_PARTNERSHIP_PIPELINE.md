## Innovation #2029 — HexIsle Prototype Bounty Escalation Pipeline
**Category:** Manufacturing / Recruitment / Economics / Partnership
**Impact:** HIGH
**Crown Jewel Candidate:** YES

### Context

The HexIsle Kickstarter strategy (B011) established a 13-campaign rolling cadence with a Chain Loyalty Engine, Open IP model, and Maker-to-Node pipeline. The Canister System (#2022) provides the manufacturing infrastructure. This innovation CONNECTS the two: makers who contribute to the modular HexIsle prototype earn Marks through the bounty system, and their aggregate Marks level determines their escalating relationship with the cooperative — from bounty hunter to contractor to partner.

The Kickstarter also established that individual Hexel components ship to backers who assemble locally — reducing shipping cost (flat-pack vs. assembled), reducing total cost (distributed assembly), and creating hands-on engagement with the product architecture.

### Description

**The Pipeline: Bounty → Contract → Partnership**

Makers sign up to help build the Modular HexIsle Prototype — the product that becomes the Kickstarter flagship. Their contributions flow through three escalating tiers, each gated by aggregate Marks earned:

**Tier 1 — BOUNTY HUNTER (0-500 Marks)**
- Pick up posted bounties: print a piece, test a tolerance, validate a fit
- Each completed bounty earns Marks (10-75 Marks per task, as defined in Crew Call)
- No commitment, no contract — just task-by-task contribution
- Marks accumulate in their profile
- This is the X-Ray Bounty Arena (#2023-#2027) applied to PHYSICAL manufacturing QA

**Tier 2 — CONTRACTOR (500-2,000 Marks)**
- At 500 aggregate Marks, the maker unlocks Contractor status
- Contractor gets: recurring task assignments, priority on new bounties, Marks multiplier (1.5x)
- A lightweight agreement — not employment, but a repeatable relationship
- Contractor can now claim Crew Call Primary roles (first right of refusal on tasks in their specialty)
- Access to advanced CAD files and design documentation
- Monthly check-in with project leads (async, via platform)

**Tier 3 — PARTNER (2,000+ Marks)**
- At 2,000 aggregate Marks, the maker unlocks Partnership track
- Partnership means: revenue share on products they helped develop
- The 83.3% creator split applies to their contributed components
- They become a Factory Node seed — first in line for SLS machine placement (the 1/3 model from B011)
- They can propose NEW products through the cooperative, funded by Guild Treasury
- Their name goes on the product permanently (IP Ledger entry)
- Steward eligibility — they can govern the HexIsle product line

**The key insight:** The aggregate Marks threshold is COMPLETELY dependent on how much they contribute. Not on who they know, not on credentials, not on applications. Pure meritocracy through measurable contribution. A teenager who prints 200 test pieces and submits 15 improvements can reach Partner faster than an engineer who does 3 bounties and stops.

### Why This Works for the Kickstarter

The Kickstarter campaign raises MONEY. The Bounty Pipeline raises PEOPLE.

1. **Campaign launches** → backers pledge → some backers download STLs
2. **STL downloaders** → some become bounty hunters → earn Marks printing/testing
3. **Bounty hunters** → aggregate Marks → some reach Contractor → recurring work
4. **Contractors** → aggregate more → some reach Partner → revenue share + Factory Node
5. **Partners** → manufacture the NEXT campaign's product → cooperative grows

Each Kickstarter campaign funds bounties for the NEXT campaign's product. The pipeline is self-funding.

### Shipping Cost Reduction (The Modular Advantage)

The HexIsle prototype ships as COMPONENTS, not assembled units:
- Flat-pack hex tiles = standard envelope shipping ($3-5 vs. $12-20 for assembled)
- Multiple components per envelope = lower per-piece shipping
- Backer assembles locally → engagement + reduced return rate
- Components made by distributed Factory Nodes → ship from NEAREST node
- At scale: a Factory Node in Portland ships to Oregon backers, a Node in Austin ships to Texas backers

This is the Decentralized Factory (#1939) in action: closer manufacturing = less shipping = lower total cost = faster delivery.

### Marks Threshold Details

| Threshold | Status | Unlocks | Marks Multiplier |
|-----------|--------|---------|-----------------|
| 0-499 | Bounty Hunter | Task-by-task bounties, basic Crew Call access | 1.0x |
| 500 | Contractor | Priority bounties, recurring tasks, advanced CAD access, Crew Call Primary | 1.5x |
| 1,000 | Senior Contractor | Bounty creation rights, design review participation, monthly project meetings | 1.75x |
| 2,000 | Partner | Revenue share (83.3% split), Factory Node seed, IP Ledger entry, Steward eligibility | 2.0x |
| 5,000 | Senior Partner | Product proposal rights, Guild Treasury access for new product funding, mentor new makers | 2.5x |

**Half-life applies to non-members** (#2026): If a bounty hunter doesn't convert to $5/year membership, their Marks decay. This naturally filters for committed contributors — Partners will ALWAYS be members.

### What Makes It Novel

No manufacturing platform offers a meritocratic escalation from "do one task" to "own a piece of the revenue" through pure Marks accumulation. Traditional manufacturing partnerships require: applications, interviews, NDAs, equity negotiations, minimum order quantities. This requires: DO THE WORK. The Marks ledger IS the resume. The threshold IS the interview. The revenue share IS the contract.

Combined with the Canister System (cheap molds, distributed production) and the Kickstarter campaign cadence (rolling product launches), this creates a self-assembling manufacturing network where the BEST contributors automatically rise to partnership.
