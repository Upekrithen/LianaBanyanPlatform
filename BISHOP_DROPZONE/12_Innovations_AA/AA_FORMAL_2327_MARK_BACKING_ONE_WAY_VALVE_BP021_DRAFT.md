## Section 1 — Innovation Summary

### What this is

The Liana Banyan Platform operates a three-currency cooperative economy. Two of those currencies — **Credits** and **Marks** — implement structural guarantees that, taken together, constitute a novel anti-extraction architecture no prior community-currency or cooperative-token system has achieved.

**Mark Backing** is the mechanism by which all Marks are backed by real cooperative surplus. Marks are earned through effort and contribution — they cannot be purchased. Critically, the backing rate operates on a one-way ratchet: once set, it can only go up, never down. This mirrors the gold-standard concept adapted for cooperative effort-value: the platform cannot debase the Mark. Every Mark a member holds is guaranteed to be worth at least as much tomorrow as it is today, and the backing grows as the cooperative accumulates surplus. The ratchet is structural — it is written into cooperative bylaws and cannot be overridden by management discretion.

**The One-Way Credit Valve** is the architectural mechanism preventing Credits from cashing out to fiat currency. A member can spend Credits within the cooperative marketplace; Credits can flow between members; but Credits cannot exit to external fiat currency. This is not a withdrawal policy that can be changed by a board resolution — it is a constitutional element of the cooperative's bylaws. The valve exists at the currency architecture level. Platform operators, future acquirers, and even the founding team cannot open the drain without a formal bylaw amendment subject to member vote.

### Why the combination is novel and important

Prior art in community currencies — the Swiss WIR franc, time banks, Local Exchange Trading Systems (LETS), neighborhood SCRIP programs — implements either partial backing or withdrawal restrictions, but not both simultaneously in a bylaw-codified two-currency architecture. The novel combination creates a **closed cooperative currency loop**:

- Members earn Marks through effort (backed, ratchet-only-up, non-purchasable)
- Members hold Credits through marketplace participation (stable, non-fiat-convertible, per-person cap $5,000)
- Value circulates within the cooperative but never drains to extractive external parties
- Neither currency degrades over time

This is an **anti-enshittification guarantee by construction**. The term "enshittification" (Doctorow, 2023) describes the documented lifecycle of platform businesses: lock in users → degrade service → extract value. The Mark Backing ratchet + One-Way Credit Valve make this lifecycle architecturally impossible within the cooperative's own bylaws. No future management team, no future acquirer, no future board can execute the enshittification playbook without first achieving a supermajority bylaw amendment under member governance.

The "Slow Blade" design discipline (patience and deliberate accumulation; per Dune: "the slow blade penetrates the shield") governs the ratchet: backing rates accumulate through cooperative surplus over time. The ratchet's deliberate slowness is a feature — it prevents gaming, prevents speculative manipulation, and creates a durable store of effort-value for members who contribute over long time horizons.

**Independent Claim 1 — The Mark Backing Ratchet Method**

1. A computer-implemented method for operating a cooperative effort-value currency comprising:
   a) maintaining a first digital currency unit ("Mark") that is issuable exclusively in exchange for recorded cooperative effort contributions and not purchasable for fiat currency or other consideration;
   b) associating each outstanding Mark unit with a backing reserve maintained from cooperative surplus, wherein the backing reserve per Mark unit is governed by a ratchet constraint such that the backing rate is only permitted to increase over time and is structurally prohibited from decreasing;
   c) encoding the ratchet constraint as a bylaw provision of the cooperative legal entity such that the constraint is enforceable against cooperative officers, management, and successors and cannot be modified by internal management resolution alone;
   d) recording effort contributions and corresponding Mark issuances in a persistent ledger accessible to cooperative members; and
   e) distributing surplus allocations from cooperative operations to the backing reserve in accordance with the ratchet constraint, such that aggregate backing value increases monotonically as a function of cumulative cooperative surplus.

---

**Independent Claim 2 — The One-Way Credit Valve Architecture**

2. A computer-implemented method for operating a cooperative marketplace currency comprising:
   a) maintaining a second digital currency unit ("Credit") with an exchange rate pegged to a fiat reference currency at a fixed ratio and subject to a per-member issuance cap;
   b) implementing a structural fiat-exit prohibition ("one-way valve") that prevents conversion or redemption of Credit units into fiat currency by any member, operator, or third-party service, wherein the fiat-exit prohibition is encoded at the currency architecture layer and not implemented as a revocable policy configuration;
   c) permitting Credit units to be transferred between members and exchanged for goods and services within the cooperative marketplace;
   d) codifying the one-way valve as a bylaw provision of the cooperative legal entity such that any modification to the valve constraint requires a formal member vote under cooperative governance procedures; and
   e) maintaining a per-member Credit balance ledger subject to the issuance cap such that no member may accumulate Credits in excess of the cap regardless of marketplace activity volume.

**Independent Claim 3 — The Two-Currency Anti-Extraction Architecture**

3. A system for implementing a cooperative economy with structural anti-extraction guarantees comprising:
   a) a first currency ("Mark") implemented according to claim 1, representing effort-based cooperative value with ratchet-backed appreciation;
   b) a second currency ("Credit") implemented according to claim 2, representing marketplace-participation value with a structural fiat-exit prohibition;

   e) a governance mechanism requiring member vote for any modification to the constitutional constraints of (c), such that no operator, management team, or acquiring entity may alter the anti-extraction architecture without member consent.

## Section 3 — Composition with Prior Art / Canonical References

### How this differs from prior community-currency art

**Swiss WIR franc (1934–present)**: WIR is a complementary currency used among Swiss SMEs. It maintains a 1:1 CHF peg for accounting, circulates within the WIR network, and is not convertible to CHF — a partial one-way valve. However: (a) WIR is purchased (not effort-earned), defeating the effort-differential property of Marks; (b) WIR has no backing ratchet — its value does not appreciate; (c) WIR's non-convertibility is a network policy, not a bylaw-codified constitutional constraint. WIR establishes the non-convertibility concept but implements none of the ratchet, effort-differential, or bylaw-constitutional elements.

**Time Banks / LETS (1980s–present)**: Time banks and Local Exchange Trading Systems issue units for time/service contributions, creating an effort-differential currency analog to Marks. However: (a) time bank units are not backed by cooperative surplus reserves — a member's hours are "backed" only by future reciprocal service availability; (b) no ratchet mechanism exists — time bank units do not appreciate in backing value; (c) time bank governance varies widely and is not bylaw-codified at the constitutional level. LETS establishes effort-differential issuance but implements none of the backing ratchet, fiat-exit valve, or anti-enshittification architecture.

**Mutual Credit Systems (e.g., C3, Sardex)**: Mutual credit creates credit balances through bilateral trade commitments. Some mutual credit systems restrict fiat exit, but: (a) mutual credit balances are not effort-earned — they arise from trade; (b) there is no backing ratchet; (c) restrictions are operational policies, not constitutional bylaws.

**Platform tokens (e.g., community tokens, loyalty points)**: Platform loyalty points and community tokens sometimes implement fiat-exit restrictions, but: (a) they are almost universally purchasable, eliminating the effort-differential property; (b) no backing ratchet; (c) restrictions are unilaterally revocable by the platform operator — this is the exact enshittification vulnerability the One-Way Valve constitutionally forecloses.

**The novel combination not present in prior art**: The simultaneous presence of (a) effort-differential issuance only (non-purchasable Marks); (b) cooperative-surplus backing with ratchet (value-up-only); (c) fiat-exit prohibition encoded as constitutional bylaw; (d) two-currency architecture where one currency appreciates (Marks) and one stays stable (Credits); and (e) governance requiring member vote to modify any of the above. No prior art combines all five.
