## Innovation #2031 — Desktop Injection Molder Factory Node Placement
**Category:** Manufacturing / Infrastructure / Cooperative Economics
**Impact:** HIGH
**Crown Jewel Candidate:** YES

### Description

After the initial 5,000 Canister Systems prove the model (hand-cranked, manual, $250-400), the NEXT production tier places **desktop electric injection molders** (Babyplast 6/12T, APSX-PIM V3, MicroMolder, or equivalent) at Factory Nodes across the cooperative network.

These are real machines — electric, semi-automated, capable of 1,000+ shots per day with consistent quality. They bridge the gap between hand-cranked Canister kits and full industrial injection presses.

**Target class: Micro/desktop electric injection molders**

| Machine | Clamp Force | Shot Size | Injection | Automation | Price Range |
|---------|-------------|-----------|-----------|------------|-------------|
| Babyplast 6/12T | 6.25 tons | 6.5 cm³ | Electric screw | Semi-auto | ~$15,000-$25,000 |
| APSX-PIM V3 | ~5 tons | 30 in³ | All-electric | Semi-auto | ~$13,500 |
| Morgan Press GT-100T | 20 tons | Variable | Semi-auto | Manual feed | ~$25,995 |
| MicroMolder | ~2 tons | Small | All-electric | Automated | ~$2,000-$3,000 |
| Desktop CNC + Mold | N/A | N/A | Canister System | Manual | ~$1,000-$2,000 |

**The 1/3 Funding Model applies:**
- 1/3 funded by customer pre-orders (the products the machine will make)
- 1/3 funded by LB cooperative treasury (collective investment)
- 1/3 funded by the Node operator (skin in the game)

A Babyplast at ~$20,000 = ~$6,700 per party. A Partner (2,000+ Marks) who's already proven their manufacturing ability through the Canister bounty pipeline is the ideal Node operator — they've earned the right to operate higher-value equipment.

**Production targets at desktop molder scale:**
- 5,000-15,000 units per machine per year (depending on cycle time and part complexity)
- SlottedTops on a Babyplast: ~30 second cycle → ~100/hour → ~800/day → 15,000 in ~19 working days
- Canister sleeves: similar volume, different tooling
- Aluminum molds ($500-2,000 each) become justified at this volume — Pawn B26 will confirm

### The Escalation Ladder

```
LEVEL 1: Canister System (Hand-Cranked)
├── Cost: $250-400 per kit
├── Pressure: 5,207 PSI (screw press)
├── Volume: 10-500 parts per mold
├── Molds: 3D printed ($5-25 each)
├── Operator: Any member with the kit
└── Gateway: proves the model, trains operators

LEVEL 2: Desktop Electric Molder (Babyplast Class)
├── Cost: $13,500-$25,000 per machine
├── Pressure: 5,000-20,000 PSI
├── Volume: 5,000-15,000 parts per year
├── Molds: Aluminum ($500-2,000 each)
├── Operator: Partner (2,000+ Marks) at Factory Node
├── Funding: 1/3 pre-orders, 1/3 LB treasury, 1/3 operator
└── Gateway: consistent production quality, real throughput

LEVEL 3: Industrial Press (Full Factory)
├── Cost: $50,000-$200,000+
├── Pressure: 10,000-30,000+ PSI
├── Volume: 50,000+ parts per year
├── Molds: Steel ($5,000-$80,000 each)
├── Operator: Senior Partner (5,000+ Marks) with Guild support
├── Funding: 1/3 pre-orders, 1/3 LB treasury, 1/3 operator
└── Gateway: full production scale
```

Each level feeds the next. Canister System operators who prove reliability graduate to desktop molder Nodes. Desktop molder operators who prove volume graduate to industrial press consideration. **The Marks ladder IS the manufacturing ladder.**

### What Makes It Novel

No manufacturing network uses a gamified contribution system to determine who gets placed with increasingly expensive equipment. Traditional equipment leasing requires credit checks, business plans, and bank guarantees. This requires: DO THE WORK. 2,000 Marks of proven manufacturing contribution = you're eligible for a $20,000 machine placement. The cooperative co-funds it. Your track record IS your credit score.

## Innovation #2032 — Manufacturing Escalation Ladder (Three-Level Production Tier)
**Category:** Architecture / Manufacturing Strategy
**Impact:** HIGH

### Description

The formalized three-level production architecture where each level uses different equipment, different mold types, and different operator qualifications — all connected through the same Marks economy:

- **Level 1 (Canister):** 3D-printed molds, manual operation, any member
- **Level 2 (Desktop):** Aluminum molds, semi-auto electric, Partner-level members
- **Level 3 (Industrial):** Steel molds, fully automated, Senior Partner with Guild backing

Products designed for the Canister System at Level 1 are FORWARD-COMPATIBLE with Level 2 and Level 3 tooling. The same hex terrain tile designed as an A/B canister pair can be re-tooled as an aluminum mold for Babyplast and eventually a steel mold for industrial press. Design once, scale through three tiers.
