## Definition

**The Recipe Pot** is a project skill-matching system where every cooperative project declares its "recipe" — the combination of spices (skills) it needs to succeed — and members contribute by bringing their spice to the pot. The system uses the Spice Rack taxonomy (#2142) to express both project needs and member capabilities in the same culinary language.

A project says: "I need Garlic (accounting), Cinnamon (design), and Sugar (marketing)."
A member says: "I bring Garlic. It's vital, a little unpleasant raw, but I make everything taste better."
The platform matches them. The pot fills. The soup gets made. Everyone who contributed eats.

## Architecture

### The Recipe

Every project on the platform gets a **Recipe** — a declaration of which spices it needs:

```
PROJECT: "Handmade Candle Storefront"
RECIPE:
  - Salt (operations): [FILLED by project owner]
  - Garlic (accounting): [NEEDED — pricing, inventory, tax]
  - Cinnamon (design): [NEEDED — storefront layout, product photos]
  - Sugar (marketing): [NEEDED — social media, local outreach]
  - Cumin (engineering): [FILLED — storefront uses platform templates]
  - Pepper (legal): [FILLED — standard cooperative terms apply]
```

The Recipe shows what's covered and what's missing. Members browse open Recipes and offer their spice.

### The Pot

The **Pot** is the project workspace where contributors gather. Each contributor is listed by their spice:

```
POT STATUS:
  🧂 Salt: Maria (owner, operations)
  🧄 Garlic: [OPEN — seeking accountant/bookkeeper]
  🍬 Sugar: James (marketing, joined Apr 3)
  ✨ Cinnamon: [OPEN — seeking designer]
```

When all required spices are filled, the Recipe is "complete" — the pot is full, the soup is ready to cook. The project transitions from "seeking contributors" to "active production."

### Soup and Bread

The Founder's directive names two outputs:
- **Soup** = the viable business / project output (the thing they make together)
- **Bread** = the income earned and kept (at 83.3%, the cooperative share)

"We can all make soup and bread" = "Together we build something that works AND generates income we keep."

This maps directly to cooperative economics:
- The soup is the collective product
- The bread is the individual share
- The recipe is the skill combination
- The spices are the contributions
- The pot is the project workspace
- The stone is the platform

### Fable Integration

| Stone Soup Element | Recipe Pot Feature |
|-------------------|-------------------|
| Stranger arrives with a stone and a pot | Founder creates project with platform infrastructure |
| "This stone makes wonderful soup, but it could use a little garlic..." | Project Recipe declares: "Needs Garlic (accounting)" |
| Villager brings garlic | Member joins as Garlic contributor |
| "A bit of salt would make it perfect..." | Recipe updates: "Needs Salt (operations)" |
| Everyone contributes, everyone eats | All contributors share the bread (83.3% to creators, split by contribution) |

The Little Red Hen variant:
- "Who will help me plant the wheat?" = "Who will bring Cumin (engineering) to build this?"
- "Who will help me grind the flour?" = "Who will bring Oregano (project management) to coordinate?"
- "Who will help me bake the bread?" = "Who will bring Sugar (marketing) to sell it?"
- "Then I will eat the bread myself" → In the cooperative: those who bring their spice earn their share. Those who don't contribute don't eat from this pot. But there are always more pots seeking their spice.

### Crew Call Integration

The existing **Crew Call** system (live production system) gains spice language:

**Before**: "Seeking: Graphic designer for storefront. Must have experience with product photography."
**After**: "Seeking: 🍬 Cinnamon (Design/UX) — storefront layout and product photography. Recipe also needs: 🧄 Garlic (Finance)."

The spice tag makes Crew Calls scannable. A member who brings Garlic can filter all open Recipes that need Garlic. Instant skill-to-project matching.

### Cold Start Pathway Blends

Each of the Six Cold Start Pathways has a default spice blend:

| Pathway | Default Recipe |
|---------|---------------|
| **Food** | Salt + Garlic + Sugar + Pepper |
| **Manufacturing** | Cumin + Salt + Pepper + Garlic |
| **Service** | Cinnamon + Sugar + Salt + Oregano |
| **Local Business** | Salt + Sugar + Cinnamon + Garlic |
| **Guild** | Varies by guild type (professional domain) |
| **Tribe** | Salt + Cinnamon + Oregano (community-focused) |

New projects in each pathway start with the default blend and customize as needed.

### Member Spice Profile

Each member's Cue Card gains a **Spice Profile** — the spices they bring:

```
DIANA JONES
🧂 Salt (operations) — ★★★★☆
📸 Cinnamon (design/photography) — ★★★★★
🧄 Garlic (accounting) — ★★☆☆☆
```

Proficiency is demonstrated through completed projects and ADAPT score subscores, not self-declared. You earn your spice stars by contributing that spice to successful pots.

## Differentiation

No platform uses a culinary skill taxonomy to match project needs to member capabilities through a fable-derived framework where:
- Projects declare recipes (skill combinations needed)
- Members contribute spices (skills they bring)
- The same taxonomy categorizes content, skills, and project needs
- Proficiency is demonstrated, not declared
- The entire framework traces back to a cooperative fable (Stone Soup) that the platform has told since day one
- The output metaphors (soup = product, bread = income) directly map to cooperative economics

The Recipe Pot turns "we need a designer" into "we need Cinnamon" — and everyone immediately understands what that means because they've been reading Cinnamon-tagged Spoonfuls for weeks. The content distribution system teaches the skill language that the project-matching system uses. The marketing IS the onboarding IS the matching.

## Claims

**Claim 1 (Independent, System).** A computer-implemented system for project-to-member skill matching in a cooperative platform, comprising: one or more processors and a memory storing instructions; a project store configured to maintain a plurality of project records, each project record including a recipe specifying required taxonomy tags and associated fulfillment states; a member store configured to maintain member profiles including declared taxonomy tags representing skill capabilities; a matching module configured to compare open recipe requirements against member taxonomy tags and identify candidate members; and a presentation module configured to render a pot interface displaying filled and unfilled recipe positions and candidate members grouped by taxonomy tag.

**Claim 2 (Independent, Method).** A computer-implemented method for cooperative project staffing via taxonomy-based matching, the method comprising: storing project records each including a recipe specifying required taxonomy tags; storing member profiles including declared taxonomy tags; identifying, for each open position in a recipe, candidate members whose declared taxonomy tags match the required tag; presenting candidate members to project owners within a pot interface; and updating the recipe fulfillment state when a candidate member is accepted into a position.

**Claim 3.** The system of claim 1, wherein each recipe position indicates a fulfillment status selected from at least: filled, open, and pending; and wherein the matching module excludes filled positions from candidate queries.

**Claim 4.** The system of claim 1, wherein the taxonomy tags used in recipes and member profiles comprise culinary spice identifiers shared with a content-classification taxonomy used elsewhere on the platform.

**Claim 5.** The method of claim 2, further comprising assigning, upon project completion, a contribution record to each member participant reflecting the taxonomy tags they fulfilled in the recipe.

**Claim 6.** The method of claim 2, further comprising surfacing open recipe positions to members via an automated distribution channel when the member's declared taxonomy tags match unfilled positions.
