## Section 1 — Innovation Summary

### What this paper discloses

*"A Considered Approach to Universal Cooperative Shelter"* is the housing sovereignty paper — Paper 10 of the 12-paper Save-the-World Series.

**Thesis**: Housing is broken because of rentier-capital extraction, geographic concentration of capital-class housing, and no cooperative-class household-buildable shelter alternative at scale. The substrate to fix it exists across Papers 3, 5, and the cooperative-platform architecture already built.

**Founder direct (canonical closing)**: *"The same hydraulics that run the gameboard run the household."* — HexIsle Water Table IS the engineering test-bed for this paper's infrastructure claims.

The paper discloses:
1. **Member capital pooling for shelter** — pre-order volume-discount class applied to housing: co-op members pre-buy units before manufacturing begins; volume discounts pass through; no speculative developer margin
2. **Decentralized Manufacturing of housing components** — modular construction units produced via SLS / CNC / fabric / electronics Nodes (Paper 5); ship + assemble locally; community-installable without contractor gatekeeping
3. **Tereno Hydraulic for water + plumbing infrastructure** — same hydraulic architecture proving on HexIsle Water Table extends to household-scale water systems
4. **Energy paper stack composing** — heating + cooling + electricity + hot water from Paper 3 energy architecture; shelter is energy-substrate-extended
5. **Homelessness sovereignty** — non-extractive cooperative housing breaks rentier-capital pattern; structural alternative, not charity overlay

**Composing Claim — Universal Cooperative Shelter via Cooperative Capital-Pooling + Manufacturing Architecture**

Method + system for cooperative-platform community-scale housing sovereignty where:

(i) **member capital pooling for shelter** applies the pre-order volume-discount aggregation architecture (established in Paper 5 manufacturing) to housing production: cooperative members pre-commit to housing units before production begins; the cooperative platform presents aggregated demand; construction cost volume discounts pass through at Cost+20% structural margin with no speculative developer intermediary;

(ii) **Decentralized Manufacturing of housing components** uses the Node architecture from Paper 5 (Decentralized Factory Manufacturing) to produce modular housing components at community scale — SLS, CNC, fabric, structural panels, and hardware — designed to ship flat and assemble locally by cooperative-member labor without construction-contractor gatekeeping;

(iii) **Tereno Hydraulic infrastructure** (Crown Jewel #3, proven on HexIsle Water Table — `platform/src/lib/hexisleProjectSpec.ts`, 33 patented mechanical innovations) provides household-scale water and plumbing systems using gravity-fed hydraulic architecture requiring no external utility infrastructure dependency;

(iv) the **Energy paper stack (Paper 3)** provides the heating + cooling + electricity + hot water substrate for cooperative shelter: Stirling-cycle energy + hydrogen-canister modular end-use cluster from Paper 3's architecture powers the cooperative housing units produced by this paper;

(v) the cooperative-platform layer (Liana Banyan) productizes design-spec distribution + member-capital coordination + Node production routing + assembly scheduling at Cost+20% structural margin, making housing a cooperative infrastructure service rather than a rentier-capital investment vehicle;

(vi) **homelessness sovereignty** emerges as a structural property: cooperative-class housing produced at Cost+20% with member capital pooling provides a non-extractive alternative to rentier housing markets; the architecture makes homelessness structurally optional rather than systemic;

(vii) the architecture composes recursively with Paper 3 (energy infrastructure) and Paper 5 (manufacturing Nodes): Paper 10 housing is built by Paper 5 Nodes, powered by Paper 3 energy stacks, watered by Paper 3 Tereno Hydraulic — the three material-sovereignty papers form a self-provisioning physical-infrastructure stack;

(viii) the cooperative-platform layer makes the housing architecture portable: the same Node + energy + hydraulic composition that builds a house in Wyoming builds one in rural Kenya or suburban Thailand — wherever sun + gravity + water + dirt exist (Paper 3 universal-inputs constraint applies);

(ix) the paper composes as the shelter-sovereignty leg of the 12-paper Save-the-World Series, completing the Material vector's physical-infrastructure cluster alongside Papers 1 (economics), 3 (energy), and 5 (manufacturing).
