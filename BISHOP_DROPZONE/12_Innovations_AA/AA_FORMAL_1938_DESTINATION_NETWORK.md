## 1. Description

A repeatable model for transforming underutilized rural and remote land into revenue-generating destinations by layering Liana Banyan cooperative infrastructure on top of traditional real estate development. The pipeline converts raw land into a self-sustaining economic node by combining lot sales, anchor businesses, vacation rentals, supply chain logistics, and cooperative labor — then packages the entire pattern as a replicable SOP for additional locations.

The first instance is **Destination: Montana** — a 132-lot property on Wheat Basin Road near the ghost town of Nora, Montana.

---

## 2. The Destination Pattern (Replicable to Any Location)

The pipeline follows a fixed ten-step sequence. Each step activates one or more existing LB systems. The entire pattern is designed to be executed by a Node Captain with Multipliers, measured by ADAPT Score, and adapted through Local SOP.

### Step 1: IDENTIFY
Locate underutilized land with divisible lots. Target categories:
- Rural parcels with road access but no commercial development
- Ghost towns or abandoned townsites with existing platted lots
- Agricultural transition land (farms going fallow, ranches downsizing)
- Timber or mining land where extraction has ended

Selection criteria: minimum 20 lots, road access, within 2 hours of a touring route or highway corridor, land cost below $5,000 per lot.

### Step 2: ANCHOR
Build essential infrastructure that converts raw land into a place people can use:
- **General Store** — sourced via Stocked Local Larder cooperative wholesale. This is the minimum viable anchor: it gives the location a reason to exist for residents and travelers.
- **Restaurant** — incubated via Let's Make Bread. The Abruzzo model: a single outstanding food experience in a remote location creates destination pull. The Wheat Basin instance is BBQ; other instances adapt to local food culture.

### Step 3: ATTRACT
Create destination appeal that draws visitors beyond the immediate area:
- Motorcycle and RV touring route placement (Rally Group integration)
- Vacation rental listings for lot owners who build cabins or structures
- Seasonal events (BBQ competitions, harvest festivals, stargazing weekends)
- Media narrative: "ghost town revival" for press, crowdfunding, and social content

### Step 4: CONNECT via Member Vacation Network (#1931)
Every lot owner can list their property for short-term rental through LB's cooperative booking platform. This is the AirBnB alternative applied to destination-created properties rather than existing housing stock.

Key distinction: LB does not compete with AirBnB for existing vacation rentals. LB CREATES new vacation destinations from underutilized land, then manages bookings through cooperative infrastructure. This is destination creation, not destination discovery.

### Step 5: SUPPLY via Stocked Local Larder
General store inventory sourced through cooperative wholesale purchasing. Benefits:
- Lower cost of goods than independent retail ordering
- Consistent supply chain even in remote locations
- Larder network connects the destination store to regional producers (Montana beef, grain, dairy)

### Step 6: BUILD via Crew Calls
Construction and maintenance teams assembled from LB member network:
- Phase 1: Roads, utilities, general store structure
- Phase 2: Restaurant build-out, campground improvements
- Phase 3: Cabin construction for lot owners who want turnkey builds
- Ongoing: Seasonal maintenance, winterization, repairs

Crew Calls use the standard LB dispatch model. Shade-Tree Mechanic calls handle vehicle maintenance for fleet and guest vehicles on-site.

### Step 7: TRANSPORT via Local Wheels + Rideshare Routes
- **Local Wheels** (#1915): Fleet vehicles for goods delivery to the destination and guest shuttle from nearby towns or airports
- **Rideshare Routes** (#1923): Commute matching for workers traveling to the destination site during build and operation phases
- **Lemon Lot** (#1922): Peer-to-peer vehicle sharing for guests who fly in and need wheels

### Step 8: MEASURE via ADAPT Score (#1937)
Each Destination node receives an ADAPT Score measuring:
- **A**daptability — how well the node adjusts to seasonal demand and local conditions
- **D**elivery — on-time performance of supply chain, bookings, and crew dispatch
- **A**ctivation — percentage of lots sold, occupied, or generating revenue
- **P**erformance — revenue per lot, guest satisfaction, repeat visit rate
- **T**hreshold — minimum viability metrics (break-even timeline, infrastructure completion)

ADAPT Score determines the node's priority for additional LB resource allocation and serves as the quality signal for crowdfunding investors.

### Step 9: FUND via Crowdfunding Integration
Lot contracts serve as the underlying asset for equity crowdfunding campaigns:
- **StartEngine** or **Wefunder** listing with lot-contract revenue projections
- Sports Afield brochure (already exists for Montana instance) as marketing collateral
- ADAPT Score as the investor-facing quality metric
- Hybrid model: crowdfund infrastructure first, then sell lots at appreciated value

### Step 10: REPLICATE
The SOP from Destination: Montana becomes the template for every subsequent instance:
- Destination: [Location] uses the same ten-step pipeline
- Local SOP framework adapts for regional conditions (climate, regulations, food culture, touring routes)
- Each new Destination node connects to the existing network, creating cross-destination booking and touring route integration

## 4. Connection to Existing Cooperative Infrastructure

The Destination Network does not require building new cooperative relationships from scratch. It plugs into existing cooperative structures in the target region:

- **Rural Electric Cooperatives** — Already serve remote Montana areas. Natural ADAPT integration partner for utilities at the destination site. Integration Bounty available for programmers who build the interface between LB systems and rural co-op billing.
- **Agricultural Cooperatives** — Nearby grain, beef, and dairy co-ops become supply chain partners for the general store and restaurant. Stocked Local Larder sourcing connects directly to existing ag co-op distribution.
- **Housing Cooperatives** — Can participate in lot ownership, development investment, or member placement at destination sites. Cooperative Housing Acquisition (#1927) applies directly.
- **Credit Unions** — Lot financing through cooperative lending rather than commercial bank loans. Lower rates, member alignment.

Each external co-op connection gets an Integration Bounty for the programmer or team that builds the technical interface between LB and the partner system.

## 5. Revenue Model

All revenue flows through standard LB commerce with Cost+20% floor pricing.

| Stream | Source System | Currency Generated |
|---|---|---|
| Lot sales (contract payments) | LB Commerce | Credits to landowner |
| Vacation bookings | Member Vacation Network (#1931) | Marks (Cost+20%) |
| General store transactions | Stocked Local Larder | Marks (Cost+20%) |
| Restaurant transactions | Let's Make Bread | Marks (Cost+20%) |
| RV and camping fees | LB Commerce | Marks (Cost+20%) |
| Vehicle rental (guest transport) | Lemon Lot (#1922) / Local Wheels (#1915) | Marks (Cost+20%) |
| Crew Call labor | Crew Call Dispatch | Marks to workers |

All Marks-generating streams feed the **Housing WaterWheel (#1929)** for the local Destination node. The WaterWheel recycles a percentage of every transaction back into cooperative housing fund, which in this context means infrastructure improvement and lot-value appreciation for the entire development.

The **Unified Real Estate WaterWheel (#1933)** connects this Destination node's revenue streams to the broader LB housing network, so a percentage of Montana destination commerce contributes to Mission TWO housing goals network-wide.

## 6. Patentable Elements

### 6a. Cooperative Land Development Pipeline
A method for transforming underutilized rural properties into revenue-generating destinations through a fixed-sequence pipeline that layers cooperative booking, supply chain, transport, construction, and measurement systems onto traditional lot-sale real estate development. The pipeline is encoded as a replicable SOP with regional adaptation through a Local SOP framework.

### 6b. Destination Creation Model
A vacation rental platform model that creates new destinations from underutilized land rather than discovering and listing existing vacation properties. The platform combines land acquisition, infrastructure development, and cooperative booking into a single pipeline, distinguishing it from discovery-based platforms (AirBnB, VRBO) that only list existing properties.

### 6c. Lot-Contract Crowdfunding Integration
A method for using real estate lot-sale contracts (fixed annual payments over a defined term) as the underlying asset for equity crowdfunding campaigns, where the cooperative commerce platform provides the transaction infrastructure, quality scoring (ADAPT), and investor-facing metrics.

### 6d. ADAPT-Scored Destination Nodes
A quality measurement system for destination development nodes that scores each location on Adaptability, Delivery, Activation, Performance, and Threshold metrics, where the score determines resource allocation priority within the cooperative network and serves as the investor-facing quality signal for crowdfunding campaigns.

### 6e. Multi-Stream WaterWheel Recycling
A revenue recycling mechanism that collects a percentage from multiple distinct commerce streams at a single geographic destination (lot sales, vacation bookings, retail, restaurant, camping, vehicle rental) and feeds them into a unified cooperative housing fund, where the recycled revenue finances infrastructure improvements that increase property values for all participants at the destination.

### 6f. Touring Route Destination Threading
A method for connecting multiple cooperative destination nodes along established motorcycle, RV, and recreational vehicle touring routes, where each destination provides standardized services (lodging, dining, fuel, vehicle maintenance) and cross-destination booking allows travelers to plan multi-stop itineraries through a single cooperative platform.

## 8. Risk and Mitigation

| Risk | Mitigation |
|---|---|
| Remote land fails to attract buyers | Phase infrastructure first via crowdfunding; sell lots into existing destination appeal rather than empty land |
| Seasonal demand (Montana winters) | Winterize for snow sports, hunting season; year-round general store serves permanent residents |
| Regulatory barriers (zoning, short-term rental rules) | Local SOP framework handles Montana-specific county regulations; engage county planning early |
| Infrastructure cost overruns | Crew Call labor at cooperative rates; phased build (general store first, restaurant second, cabins third) |
| Crowdfunding campaign underperformance | Hybrid model allows direct lot sales to proceed independently of crowdfunding |
| AirBnB/VRBO competitive pressure on vacation bookings | AirBnB Revenue Subsidy (#1928) ensures competitive pricing; destination creation (not discovery) differentiates |

## 9. SEC Considerations

- Lot-sale contracts structured as real estate purchases, NOT securities. Buyer purchases land, not a share of revenue.
- Crowdfunding campaigns through registered platforms (StartEngine, Wefunder) with full Regulation CF or Regulation A+ compliance.
- LB cooperative services are opt-in operational support, not investment returns. No lot buyer is promised returns from LB platform activity.
- Marks generated through commerce are earned through transactions, not granted as investment returns.
- All language in external-facing materials (Cue Card) avoids investment-return framing. Revenue projections are based on contract terms, not platform performance.
