## TL;DR

A cooperative membership architecture in which documentation of the origin, methodology, or lived experience behind a member's contribution to a cooperative platform is **strictly opt-in**, never required for full cooperative membership, and is paired with a graduated bundle of concrete benefits (named attribution, co-authorship credit, Service Allocation Authority accrual legibility, eligibility for research-layer participation under a living-laboratory framework, and durable public record inclusion) that are available to opting-in members on a per-contribution granular basis. **The architecture refuses the two-tier system** whereby documented members are treated as first-class and undocumented members are treated as second-class; both classes receive full membership rights. Benefits are the reward for documentation work, not a penalty for its absence.

---

## Problem Solved

Existing platforms that solicit member-contributed content face a binary choice: (a) require documentation of provenance, which creates a compliance burden and filters out members who lack the time or writing facility to document, or (b) skip documentation entirely, which loses the provenance-rich material that makes the platform defensible against drift, extractive reconstruction, or historical revision. Neither choice is cooperative: (a) filters on skill-that-has-nothing-to-do-with-membership, and (b) abandons the structural commitment device that keeps the platform honest.

The invention resolves the binary by decoupling documentation from membership entirely and making documentation a voluntary benefit-bearing activity that composes with membership rather than conditioning it.

## Independent Claim

**Claim 1.** A computer-implemented method for accepting and associating member documentation with member contributions in a cooperative platform, comprising:

(a) Receiving, by a cooperative platform from a member in good standing, a contribution comprising one of: a business listing, an innovation proposal, a service offering, a guide, a photograph, a product, a vehicle refurbishment record, a family recipe, or an equivalent member-originated cooperative artifact;

(b) Storing the contribution in a cooperative database and associating it with the member's account;

(c) Offering, through a user interface presented to the member at the time of contribution or at any subsequent time, an optional opt-in flow in which the member may elect to document one or more of: the origin moment of the contribution, the specific context in which the need was observed, a photograph of the originating circumstance, and a first-person narrative account, wherein the opt-in flow explicitly discloses that participation is voluntary and that full cooperative membership and all associated rights are preserved regardless of whether the member opts in;

(d) For members who opt in, activating one or more benefits from a benefits bundle comprising: persistent named attribution on the artifact, co-authorship credit on any derivative platform content that cites the artifact, increased legibility to the cooperative's Service Allocation Authority governance layer, eligibility for research-layer citation under a platform-wide research substrate framework, and durable public record inclusion within the cooperative's member-readable archive;

(e) For members who do not opt in, preserving all membership rights without modification, ensuring that contribution count, governance voting weight, cooperative resource allocation eligibility, and all other membership entitlements remain identical to opted-in members;

(f) Maintaining per-contribution granular consent such that a member who opts in for one contribution may opt out for another without affecting either;

(g) Providing a withdrawal mechanism whereby a member who previously opted in may retract consent for any specific contribution at any time, triggering removal of the associated documentation from platform public surfaces while preserving the underlying contribution and membership.

**Dependent Claim 1.1** — The method of Claim 1, wherein the opt-in flow presents the benefits bundle to the member as a concrete enumeration of gains rather than as a description of obligations avoided.

**Dependent Claim 1.2** — The method of Claim 1, wherein the Service Allocation Authority legibility benefit of (d) comprises making the opted-in contribution visible to cooperative governance mechanisms that calculate SAA accrual, such that documented contributions can accrue SAA weight based on demonstrated provenance that undocumented contributions cannot.

**Dependent Claim 1.3** — The method of Claim 1, wherein the research-layer eligibility benefit of (d) comprises including the opted-in contribution in a pool available for citation by external academic researchers under a Liana Banyan Living Laboratory framework, subject to per-contribution granular consent.

**Dependent Claim 1.4** — The method of Claim 1, further comprising publishing, prior to soliciting member opt-in, a founder-first corpus of documented contributions by the cooperative's founder covering a substantial fraction of all innovations in the cooperative's active registry, as a demonstration that the documentation road has been walked by the founder before any member is asked to walk it.

**Dependent Claim 1.5** — The method of Claim 1, wherein the non-opt-in membership rights explicitly include: equal governance vote weight, equal resource allocation eligibility, equal Family Table seating, equal cooperative currency earning rates, equal marketplace access, and equal eligibility for all cooperative benefits that exist at the date of the contribution.

**Dependent Claim 1.6** — A system comprising a processor, a persistent database, and instructions stored on a non-transitory computer-readable medium which, when executed, cause the processor to perform the method of Claim 1 through Claim 1.5.

## Prior Art Distinction

Existing cooperative and platform architectures that collect member documentation fall into two classes: (1) mandatory documentation systems (user-generated content platforms, academic databases, institutional repositories) which treat documentation as a precondition of participation, and (2) optional testimonial systems (review sites, social media) which treat documentation as promotional content with no concrete benefit linkage. **Neither class combines (a) strict voluntariness with (b) a benefit bundle that rewards documentation without penalizing its absence, (c) per-contribution granular consent, (d) explicit preservation of full cooperative membership rights for non-opting members, and (e) a founder-first-before-member-ask pedagogical sequence.** The combination is novel.
