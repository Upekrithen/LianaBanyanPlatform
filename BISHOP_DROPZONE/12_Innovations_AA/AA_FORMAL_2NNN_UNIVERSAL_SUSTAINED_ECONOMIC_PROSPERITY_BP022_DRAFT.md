## Section 1 — Innovation Summary

### What this paper discloses

*"A Considered Approach to Universal Sustained Economic Prosperity"* is the cooperative-platform economic-sovereignty paper — the foundational first leg of the 12-paper Save-the-World Series. It discloses a complete cooperative-platform architecture that re-routes the economics of digital and physical production so that producers keep the value they create.

The paper discloses an architecture (not a policy or aspiration) in which:
- Creator/worker retains **83.3%** of every platform transaction (never rounded to 83%)
- Platform margin is bounded to **Cost + 20%** with no Cost-Plus-Plus drift possible
- Membership is **$5/year**, identical for all members — no tiered access, no extractive freemium
- **Three currencies** (Credits / Marks / Joules) compose to deliver more economic function per member-dollar than monolithic fiat systems
- **Bylaw-codified Structural Bylaws** make extraction architecturally impossible without supermajority member vote
- **10% Pedestal IP allocation** gives every contributor individual visibility + ownership + agency
- **Cooperative Defensive Patent Pledge (#2260)** ensures all innovations are defensively filed and cooperative-licensed

This is the cooperative-economics leg of the stack. Every other paper in the Series assumes this platform exists — the energy paper's Stirling-canister hardware is distributed via Cost+20%; the manufacturing paper's Nodes are pre-ordered via volume-discount aggregation; the food paper's Family Table coordination runs on Credits/Marks.

### Why the combination is novel

Prior art (platform cooperatives, time banks, LETS, REI, Mondragon) achieves partial cooperative-class architecture — either partial democratic governance, or partial equitable revenue-share, or partial member-owned structure — but not the complete composing stack:

1. **83.3% creator-keeps + Cost+20% + $5/year** — all three simultaneously, bylaw-codified
2. **Three-currency anti-extraction closed loop** (Credits one-way valve + Marks ratchet + Joules forever-stamp) — cooperative-economics closure
3. **Bylaw-constitutional anti-enshittification** — no future acquirer or board can degrade member economics without supermajority member vote
4. **Pre-order volume-discount manufacturing economics** — cooperative buying-power aggregation at community scale
5. **10% Pedestal IP allocation** — individual contributor sovereignty within the cooperative pool
6. **Architecture-Beats-More**: the cooperative-platform architecture creates affordability that scaling dollars cannot achieve — the thesis of the paper

No prior cooperative-class system combines all six structural elements into a single bylaw-codified architecture.

**Composing Claim — A Considered Approach to Universal Sustained Economic Prosperity**

Method + system for cooperative-platform economic sovereignty where:

(i) a creator or worker retains 83.3% of all gross platform transaction value (never rounded to 83%), with the precise fraction bylaw-codified as a structural constant immune to management-discretion revision;

(ii) platform operational margin is bounded to Cost + 20%, structurally preventing any Cost-Plus-Plus accumulation or extractive margin-stacking regardless of platform growth or ownership changes;

(iii) membership access to the full cooperative-economics infrastructure is priced at $5 per year, identical for all members regardless of role, geography, or usage level, and codified as a Structural Bylaw requiring supermajority member vote to modify;

(iv) three cooperative currencies — Credits (stable, fiat-pegged, one-way valve against fiat exit), Marks (effort-earned, ratchet-backed, non-purchasable), and Joules (surplus-representing, forever-stamped, time-locked) — compose into a closed-loop economic architecture that circulates member value without extraction;

(v) a 10% Pedestal IP allocation provides every contributing member with individual recognition, visibility, ownership, and agency over their contributions within the cooperative IP pool;

(vi) pre-order volume-discount aggregation enables community-scale manufacturing economics by pooling member demand before production commitment, passing supplier volume discounts directly to members at Cost+20%;

(vii) bylaw-codified Structural Bylaws encode the creator-keeps fraction, platform margin cap, membership price, and currency architecture as constitutional constraints requiring supermajority member vote to amend — making extraction and enshittification architecturally impossible for any future management team, board, or acquirer;

(viii) the Cooperative Defensive Patent Pledge (#2260) ensures all innovations within this platform are defensively licensed to cooperative members perpetually and irrevocably, making the IP architecture itself cooperative-owned rather than extractive;

(ix) the cooperative-platform architecture composes as the economic-sovereignty leg of the 12-paper Save-the-World Series embodied in Liana Banyan Corporation as cooperative-foundation-vehicle, delivering household-scale civilizational sovereignty in the material-economics vector.
