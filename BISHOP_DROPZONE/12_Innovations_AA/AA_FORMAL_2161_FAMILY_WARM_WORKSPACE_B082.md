# A&A FORMAL — Innovation #2161
## Family Warm Workspace (Age-Appropriate Platform Economics)
## Bishop B082 | April 5, 2026

---

## STATUS: Crown Jewel Candidate

## SOURCE: K314 V2 Family Table Hub (B080)

---

## DESCRIPTION

A household coordination workspace that enforces age-appropriate economic participation by architectural constraint. Children see XP (experience points for task completion) but NEVER see ADAPT scores (adult reputation/reliability metrics). The activity feed uses human-language narration ("Maya completed her reading goal") rather than system log format ("user_id 42 updated record"). Family fund displays use savings-jar visual language (not bank ledger aesthetic). The system creates a cooperative household economy where children participate meaningfully without exposure to adult economic pressure.

## MECHANICS

1. **XP-only child display**: When a member profile has `is_minor: true`, the ADAPT score component is architecturally hidden — not just CSS-hidden, but conditionally unrendered. Children earn XP through task completion. They never see reliability scores, peer ratings, or economic metrics.
2. **Human-language activity feed**: Every activity entry is rendered as natural language narration. Template: "{Name} {verb} {object}" — "Maya completed Task," "Carlos earned 5 XP for his math sheet." Never "user_id updated," never system log format.
3. **Kanban-lite task board**: Household tasks in three columns (To Do / Doing / Done). Intentionally simplified from full project management. Children can drag tasks. Parents can assign.
4. **Family fund savings jar**: Shared household savings displayed as a visual jar with fill level, not as a bank ledger with columns and balances. Warm aesthetic signals cooperation and shared progress, not financial management.
5. **Privacy controls**: Each family member controls their own visibility. Toggle-switch privacy settings per data type. Children's defaults are maximally private.
6. **No ADAPT leakage**: Even if a parent views the Family Table, child rows show XP badges, never ADAPT arcs or scores. The constraint is at the data-fetch level, not the display level.

## PRIOR ART DISTINCTION

- **FamZoo / Greenlight / GoHenry**: Children's financial apps. Focus on spending/saving with real money. Do NOT have a cooperative task economy. Do NOT distinguish XP from reputation scores. No activity feed in human language.
- **Cozi / OurHome / Homey**: Family chore/task apps. Point systems for chores. No distinction between child-visible and adult-visible metrics. No warm-language activity feed. No cooperative economic model (Marks/Credits/Joules).
- **Notion / Trello for families**: Generic project tools used by families. No age-appropriate economic constraints. No XP/ADAPT distinction. No human-language feed.
- **No prior art found** for: (a) architectural enforcement of age-appropriate economic metric visibility (XP shown, ADAPT hidden for minors by data-fetch constraint, not CSS), (b) a family coordination workspace combining human-language activity narration with cooperative task economics, (c) savings-jar visual metaphor integrated into a multi-currency cooperative platform.

## CLAIMS

1. A family coordination workspace within a cooperative platform comprising: age-appropriate metric visibility enforced at the data-fetch level, wherein minor members see experience points but adult reputation scores are architecturally unrendered; a task board enabling meaningful child participation in household economics without exposure to adult economic metrics.

2. A human-language activity feed system comprising: natural language narration templates that render platform events as conversational sentences rather than system log entries; said templates using member names and action verbs to create relational rather than transactional communication within a household coordination context.

3. A family economic visualization comprising: a shared household fund rendered as a savings-jar metaphor with visual fill level rather than a financial ledger format; integrated within a multi-currency cooperative platform where the savings-jar aesthetic signals cooperative participation rather than financial management.
