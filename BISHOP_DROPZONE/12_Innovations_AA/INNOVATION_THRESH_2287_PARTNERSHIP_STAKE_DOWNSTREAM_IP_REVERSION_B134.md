## Method-claim language (provisional grade — refines at conversion)

A method for cooperative defensive enclosure of platform-derivative intellectual property, comprising:

1. **Substrate-Dependency Classification** — at Research Partner enrollment, the Cathedral substrate operator classifies prospective patent claims into three reversion-eligibility tiers via the **Substrate-Dependency Classifier (#2289 — transparent algorithmic on substrate-dependency-graph; Founder-ratified B134 turn 16; full spec at `BISHOP_DROPZONE/03_BishopHandoffs/SUBSTRATE_DEPENDENCY_CLASSIFIER_SCOPE_MEMO_B134.md`):**
   - **Tier A (Substrate-Dependent)**: claim cannot reduce to practice without LB substrate primitives (Cathedral Effect / Wrasse / Pheromone / Stone Tablet / Pre-Staging / Three Fates / etc.). **20% reversion mandatory** for smaller partners; reduced rate (5-10%) for major-vendor partner class (Anthropic / OpenAI / Google) per Symmetric-Offer Principle. (Industry-term partner-class-tier, membership-orthogonal — pricing identical for all members at $5/year, unchanged.)
   - **Tier B (Substrate-Augmenting)**: claim improves a specific LB substrate primitive but operates through it. **Same partner-class-tier reversion rate as Tier A; reduced 50% if augmentation is contributed back to LB substrate as open improvement under Pledge.**
   - **Tier C (Substrate-Orthogonal)**: claim derives from Research Partner's pre-existing IP or unrelated R&D. **0% reversion.** Partner retains full ownership.

2. **Reversion Window** — Tier A and Tier B classifications apply during the Window: Pledge filing date + 12 months. After Window expiry, partner retains future filings 100% (unless renewed).

3. **Reversion Mechanism** — at filing time, Partner files USPTO assignment recording 20% (or 10% Tier-B-contributed) interest to LB Corporation. Recording is condition precedent to Partner's USPTO filing; substrate access conditioned on assignment being recorded.

4. **Cooperative Pledge Inheritance** — LB-reverted share automatically inherits #2260 Pledge framework (free-forever to nonprofits/cooperatives/academics on EIN verification). Partner cannot subvert Pledge by laundering through Tier-A/B substrate.

5. **Non-Exclusivity Preservation** — multiple Research Partners may all hold reversion-clause agreements simultaneously; reversions accumulate to LB without exclusivity to any partner.

6. **Tier-Classification Dispute Process** — disputes over Tier A/B/C classification resolve via the Substrate-Dependency Classifier's transparent algorithmic output FIRST (both sides see same input → same output via algorithmic walk + reasoning trace + dependency-subgraph render). Appeal mechanism: human review (Three Fates arbitration backup — Clotho/Lachesis/Atropos) starts FROM the algorithm's reasoning trace, with reviewer either confirming algorithm or identifying specific edge case the algorithm missed (which then feeds back into algorithm refinement for future classifications). LB Corporation governance per Structural Bylaws is the final-appeal-of-last-resort. (Industry-term arbitration framework, membership-orthogonal.)

7. **Conversion-Time Continuation** — when Partner converts provisional to utility patent, reversion percentage carries forward to all child claims and continuations until Pledge framework explicitly releases.

## Distinguishing from prior art (preliminary, Pawn investigation pending)

- **University tech-transfer reversion** (e.g., Bayh-Dole) — federally-funded research has government march-in rights. Different mechanism: reversion is to government, not to commons. #2287 reversion is to a cooperative defensive Pledge, fundamentally distinct legal framework.
- **Patent pools** (e.g., MPEG-LA) — patents pooled for cross-licensing. Different mechanism: pool members each contribute their own; #2287 reversion is involuntary at Tier-A/B classification, not voluntary pool contribution.
- **Joint-venture IP-sharing** — typically negotiated case-by-case with bespoke terms. #2287 is a **standardized structural clause** that enables N-vendor concurrent partnerships without bespoke negotiation.
- **Open-source contribution agreements (CLAs)** — copyleft applies to derivatives of contributed code. #2287 is **patent-side** parallel: derivative claims revert proportionally rather than being copyleft-bound.

## Why patentable + load-bearing

1. **Solves a real problem unique to Cathedral-substrate ecosystems**: how to admit AI-corporation partnership without losing the cooperative-defensive moat. Existing IP frameworks force a binary (give-it-away open-source OR closed proprietary); this is a third path.

2. **Reduction-to-practice already exists in canon**: #2286 Partnership Stake (Anthropic-anticipated B121), #2260 Pledge (filed under in every Prov), #2314 Dual-License (B127). #2287 is the missing 8th structural property that completes the framework.

3. **Cross-vendor non-exclusivity** — multiple Research Partners can each carry reversion clauses simultaneously without conflict. Standard JV-IP doesn't compose this way.

4. **Brand-canon coherent**: 20% reversion ratio matches Founder-creator slice (`project_ip_load_balancing_v2.md`). Symmetry argument is itself patentable as part of the structural design rationale.

5. **Defensive AND offensive value**: defensive (prevents fast-follower distillation per Founder's "16-to-3 components" concern) AND offensive (creates a structural hook so Partner-developed IP automatically grows the cooperative's defensive moat).
