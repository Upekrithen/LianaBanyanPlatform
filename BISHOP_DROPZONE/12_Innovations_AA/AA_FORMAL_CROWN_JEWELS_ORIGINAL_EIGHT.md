## Description

The Cost+20% Economic Model is the **constitutional pricing floor** of the Liana Banyan cooperative. Every transaction on the platform — every storefront sale, every service rendered, every product listed — follows a single, transparent, non-negotiable rule: the seller's cost plus exactly 20%.

This is not a suggestion. It is not a guideline. It is a structural lock, protected by the DNA Lock (CJ#5), that **cannot be altered by any governance action short of dissolving the cooperative itself**.

### Comparison to Traditional Models

| Model | Creator Share | Platform Share | Transparency | Race to Bottom? |
|-------|-------------|---------------|-------------|-----------------|
| **Amazon Marketplace** | Varies (65-85%) | 15-35% + hidden fees | Opaque | Yes — algorithmic price wars |
| **Uber/Lyft** | ~60-75% | 25-40% + surge manipulation | Opaque | Yes — driver pay erosion |
| **Etsy** | ~80% after fees | ~20% layered (listing + transaction + payment) | Partially transparent | Yes — fee creep over time |
| **App Stores (Apple/Google)** | 70% | 30% | Fixed but extractive | No race, but rent-seeking |
| **Liana Banyan Cost+20%** | 83.3% | 16.7% (constitutional lock) | Fully transparent | Impossible by design |

The critical distinction: in every other platform, the platform's take can change. Fees increase. New charges appear. Terms shift. The Cost+20% model is **immutable**. The 16.7% is not a business decision — it is a constitutional provision. The cooperative cannot vote to raise it. The board cannot amend it. It is locked by CJ#5 (DNA Lock) and enforced computationally.

## Mechanics

### The 20% Breakdown

The 20% margin above cost is allocated as follows:

- **~13.3% to cooperative operations**: infrastructure, hosting, legal, insurance, platform development, and reserve fund. This is the cooperative's operating budget.
- **~3% to onboarding credit (Backed Marks)**: the member who originally onboarded the selling business receives Backed Marks — not cash, not equity, but allocation authority within the cooperative. This is Innovation #1897 in its mature form.
- **~2% to steward allocation**: the member serving as steward for the selling business receives allocation authority (also as Backed Marks) for their ongoing management work.
- **Remainder to infrastructure fund**: any surplus beyond the above categories flows to long-term cooperative infrastructure — legal defense (CJ#8), technology development, and expansion reserves.

### Price Floor Enforcement

- Sellers set their own prices, but the platform **enforces** a minimum of Cost+20%
- If a seller attempts to list below cost+20%, the system rejects the listing
- "Cost" is self-declared by the seller but subject to Star Chamber review if challenged
- The Cost+20% floor prevents predatory undercutting — no member can destroy another member's market by selling at a loss

### Constitutional Lock

- The 20% margin is a **DNA-Locked provision** (see CJ#5)
- It cannot be raised by governance vote
- It cannot be lowered below the minimum required for cooperative operations
- Any attempt to modify the margin triggers a DNA Lock challenge requiring supermajority plus constitutional review
- This prevents the most common failure mode of cooperative platforms: gradual fee creep driven by growth pressure

### Creator Retention Math

At Cost+20%, for every $1.00 of margin generated:
- Creator receives $0.833 (83.3%)
- Cooperative retains $0.167 (16.7%)

For a product with $10.00 cost:
- Sale price: $12.00
- Creator receives: $10.00 (cost recovery) + $1.67 (margin share) = $11.67
- Cooperative retains: $0.33
- Of that $0.33: ~$0.22 operations, ~$0.05 onboarding Backed Marks, ~$0.03 steward, ~$0.03 infrastructure

## Patentable Elements

2. **Automated cost+margin enforcement** — computational rejection of listings that violate the pricing floor, with self-declared cost subject to community audit
3. **Margin decomposition into cooperative functions** — the fixed margin is algorithmically split into operations, onboarding credit, steward allocation, and infrastructure, with each sub-allocation itself governed by constitutional provision
4. **Anti-predatory undercutting mechanism** — the pricing floor prevents loss-leader strategies that destroy peer businesses within the cooperative
5. **Transparent margin visibility** — every transaction displays the exact breakdown of where the 20% goes, making the cooperative's economics visible to every member in real time

## SEC/Legal Notes

- The 83.3% creator share is **earned income** (1099-reportable), not investment return.
- The 3% onboarding credit generates **Backed Marks** (allocation authority), not cash distributions. This is critical for Howey test compliance: there is no "expectation of profit from the efforts of others" because Backed Marks are not profit — they are cooperative resource allocation rights.
- The constitutional lock on margin prevents the cooperative from later increasing extraction — this is a consumer protection feature, not a securities feature.
- The pricing floor does not constitute price-fixing under antitrust law because: (a) members voluntarily join the cooperative and accept its terms, (b) the floor is a minimum, not a fixed price — sellers can price above Cost+20%, and (c) cooperative pricing rules are protected under the Capper-Volstead Act and state cooperative statutes.

## Description

The Three-Gear Currency System is Liana Banyan's **interlocking monetary architecture**. It replaces the single-currency model used by every other platform with three distinct instruments, each serving a different economic function, connected by one-way valves that prevent gaming and ensure stability.

The three gears:

- **Credits** — the spending currency. Cash in, goods out. $1 = 1 Credit. Closed-loop: Credits cannot be converted back to cash. They are the medium of exchange within the cooperative.
- **Marks** — the earning currency. Generated through effort differentials, restricted to essential goods and services. Marks represent work done that has not yet been compensated in cash — they are effort-debt instruments that the cooperative honors.
- **Joules** — the storage currency. Surplus value locked into long-term cooperative investment. The "forever stamp" mechanic: once value enters a Joule, it can never decrease. Joules are not owned by members — they are owned by the cooperative, and members direct their allocation.

### Comparison to Traditional Models

| System | Currencies | Conversion | Gaming Risk | Value Stability |
|--------|-----------|------------|-------------|-----------------|
| **USD Economy** | 1 (dollars) | N/A | Inflation, speculation | Central bank dependent |
| **Crypto Platforms** | 1+ tokens | Open market exchange | Extreme speculation | Volatile |
| **Loyalty Programs** | Points (1 type) | One-way (earn→redeem) | Inflation, devaluation | Platform-controlled |
| **Game Economies** | Gold + premium currency | Cash→premium, grind→gold | Pay-to-win, RMT | Developer adjusts |
| **Liana Banyan** | 3 interlocking gears | One-way valves, no reversal | Structurally prevented | Constitutional lock |

The Three-Gear System prevents every known failure mode of platform currencies: inflation (Credits are 1:1 pegged to USD), speculation (Joules cannot decrease in value), exploitation (Marks are restricted to essentials), and extraction (no currency can be converted to cash).

## Mechanics

### Credits (Gear One — Exchange)

- **Creation**: Member deposits cash. $1 = 1 Credit. No fractional creation. No credit on credit.
- **Use**: Purchase any good or service on the platform. Universal acceptance required — no storefront can refuse Credits.
- **Destruction**: Credits are consumed upon purchase. The seller receives Credits (not cash), which they use for their own purchases within the cooperative.
- **Cash-out**: **Impossible by design.** Credits are a closed loop. Once cash enters the cooperative as Credits, it stays. This is the fundamental difference from every other platform currency. The cooperative uses the underlying cash for operations, legal defense, and infrastructure.
- **Transparency**: Every Credit has a traceable origin (which member deposited the underlying cash) and a traceable destination (which purchase consumed it).

### Marks (Gear Two — Effort-Debt)

- **Creation**: Marks emerge from **differentials only**. They are never granted as gifts, never airdropped, never distributed without corresponding work. A Mark represents: "This member performed work that created value for the cooperative. The cooperative owes them."
- **Restriction**: Marks can ONLY be spent on essential goods and services — food, shelter, transport, basic necessities. They cannot be used for luxury items, entertainment, or discretionary purchases. This restriction ensures Marks function as a safety net, not a parallel economy.
- **Clearing**: Marks are cleared (destroyed) when the cooperative compensates the member through goods/services. A cleared Mark means: "The cooperative's debt to this member for this unit of work has been honored."
- **Backed Marks**: A special sub-type. Joule-collateral backed. Used exclusively for project sponsorship. When a member holds Backed Marks, they have the authority to allocate cooperative resources to specific projects — but they do not own those resources. Backed Marks represent demonstrated judgment, not wealth.
- **Pledged Marks**: Own Marks escrowed for a specific project. If the project succeeds, Pledged Marks are released back to the member. If the project fails, Pledged Marks are absorbed by the cooperative. This is skin-in-the-game without financial risk — the member risks their effort-credit, not their money.

### Joules (Gear Three — Storage)

- **Creation**: Surplus value from cooperative operations that exceeds immediate needs is converted to Joules. This is a one-way valve — once value becomes a Joule, it never reverts.
- **The "Forever Stamp" Mechanic**: Joules can increase in value as the cooperative grows, but they can **never decrease**. This is enforced by the Forex Ratchet (CJ#7). If the cooperative's total value increases, Joule value increases proportionally. If the cooperative's total value decreases, Joule value remains at its previous high-water mark. The cooperative absorbs the loss, not the Joule holders.
- **Ownership**: **The cooperative owns Joules. Members direct them.** This is the most important distinction in the entire currency system. A member with 100 Joules does not "have" 100 Joules of wealth. They have the authority to direct 100 Joules of cooperative resources toward projects and initiatives. This is allocation authority, not ownership.
- **SAA Connection**: A member's SAA (demonstrated judgment score) determines how much allocation authority they can exercise. High SAA = more Joules can be directed. This is earned through successful project sponsorship, not through accumulation.

### One-Way Valves

The gears connect through one-way valves that prevent gaming:

```
CASH ──→ Credits ──→ [purchases] ──→ consumed
                                         │
                    effort ──→ Marks ──→ [essentials] ──→ cleared
                                  │
                           Backed Marks ──→ [project sponsorship]
                                                    │
                              surplus ──→ Joules ──→ [allocation] ──→ cooperative growth
                                            ↑
                                     NEVER DECREASES (Forex Ratchet)
```

- Cash can become Credits. Credits cannot become cash.
- Work can generate Marks. Marks cannot become Credits or cash.
- Surplus can become Joules. Joules cannot become Marks, Credits, or cash.
- Each valve is one-way. No reversal. No arbitrage. No extraction.

### Equal Value Peg

All three currencies maintain equal face value: 1 Credit = 1 Mark = 1 Joule. This is not a market-determined exchange rate — it is a constitutional peg. The peg prevents speculation between currencies and ensures that a member who earns Marks through labor has the same purchasing power (within the Marks restriction to essentials) as a member who deposits cash for Credits.

## Patentable Elements

1. **Triple-currency cooperative system with one-way valves** — three distinct economic instruments (exchange, effort-debt, storage) connected by irreversible conversion pathways that prevent extraction and gaming
2. **Effort-debt currency restricted to essential goods** — a labor-backed instrument (Marks) that can only be redeemed for necessities, functioning as a cooperative safety net rather than a parallel economy
3. **Forever-stamp storage currency with ratchet floor** — a surplus storage instrument (Joules) whose value can increase but never decrease, with the cooperative absorbing any downturn losses
4. **Allocation-authority-not-ownership model** — a currency (Joules) owned by the cooperative but directed by members based on demonstrated judgment, separating resource control from resource ownership
5. **Backed Mark collateralization for project sponsorship** — Joule-backed allocation authority tokens that enable members to fund cooperative projects without financial risk, with authority earned through successful prior allocations

## SEC/Legal Notes

- **Credits** are not securities. They are prepaid stored-value instruments, analogous to gift cards. They do not appreciate in value, pay dividends, or represent ownership. Governed by state money transmitter laws — the cooperative may need state-level licenses depending on jurisdiction.
- **Marks** are not securities. They are internal cooperative labor credits — analogous to IOU instruments within a closed community. They represent work performed, not investment made. No expectation of profit. 1099-reportable when cleared against goods/services.
- **Joules** are the most legally sensitive instrument. Key defenses against securities classification:
  - Members do not "own" Joules — the cooperative owns them. Members direct allocation.
  - Joule value increase is tied to cooperative growth, but members cannot sell or extract Joules. There is no "expectation of profit" because there is no mechanism to realize profit.
  - The forever-stamp mechanic protects members from loss but does not promise gain.
  - The Howey test requires an "investment of money" — Joules are created from cooperative surplus, not from member investment.
- **Backed Marks** are not securities because they represent allocation authority (the right to direct cooperative resources), not ownership or profit rights. They are more analogous to voting rights than financial instruments.
- The closed-loop nature of all three currencies (no cash-out for any of them) is the strongest SEC defense: without a mechanism to extract value as cash, the "expectation of profit" prong of Howey is not met.

## Description

IP Load Balancing is the system by which **intellectual property revenue is distributed** across the Liana Banyan cooperative. It solves a problem that no other cooperative or platform has addressed: how do you fairly compensate creators for their innovations while simultaneously funding collective innovation infrastructure?

Traditional IP models are binary: either the creator owns everything (patents, copyrights), or the employer/platform owns everything (work-for-hire). Both fail cooperatives. Creator-owns-all means the cooperative has no innovation funding. Platform-owns-all means creators have no incentive.

IP Load Balancing is a **60/20/20 split** that funds both.

### Comparison to Traditional Models

| Model | Creator Share | Collective Share | Innovation Funding | Dual Participation? |
|-------|-------------|-----------------|-------------------|-------------------|
| **Traditional Employment** | 0% (work-for-hire) | 100% to employer | Employer decides | No |
| **Freelance/Independent** | 100% | 0% | Self-funded | No |
| **University Research** | Varies (0-50%) | University keeps rest | Grant-dependent | No |
| **Open Source** | 0% revenue (reputation only) | 0% revenue | Donation/sponsorship | No |
| **Liana Banyan IP LB** | 60% | 20% global + 20% bucket | Self-funding cycle | Yes — create AND sponsor |

## Mechanics

### The 60/20/20 Split

When an innovation generates revenue (through licensing, product sales, or service delivery):

- **60% to the creator**: Direct compensation for the innovator. This is earned income, not passive royalty. The creator did the work; the creator gets the majority.
- **20% to the global sponsor pool**: Distributed to ALL members who have contributed Backed Marks to innovation sponsorship across the cooperative. This creates a collective incentive to fund innovation — even members who are not themselves innovators benefit from the cooperative's innovation output.
- **20% to the targeted patent bucket**: Each innovation belongs to a domain-specific patent bucket (e.g., "Commerce Innovations," "Governance Innovations," "Social Innovations"). The 20% flows to members who specifically sponsored innovations in THAT bucket. This creates domain expertise incentives — members who consistently sponsor successful commerce innovations receive more from the commerce bucket.

### Patent Bucket System

- Innovations are grouped by domain into buckets
- Each bucket accumulates IP revenue from its member innovations
- Members who sponsor (via Backed Marks) innovations in a specific bucket earn from that bucket's revenue
- Buckets are created organically as innovation domains emerge — they are not pre-defined
- A member can sponsor innovations in multiple buckets, creating a diversified sponsorship portfolio

### Dual Participation

This is the key architectural innovation: **a member can earn from both sides**.

- As a **creator**: invent something, earn 60% of its revenue
- As a **sponsor**: back someone else's invention with Backed Marks, earn from the 20% global pool and the 20% bucket pool

A member who creates Innovation A and sponsors Innovation B earns:
- 60% of Innovation A's revenue (as creator)
- Their share of the global pool (which includes 20% from Innovation B)
- Their share of Innovation B's bucket pool (if they sponsored in that bucket)

This creates a **virtuous cycle**: successful creators earn Backed Marks (from the onboarding credit and steward systems), which they can use to sponsor other creators, which generates revenue, which generates more Backed Marks.

### Revenue Flow

```
Innovation generates $100 revenue
  │
  ├── $60 → Creator (direct compensation)
  │
  ├── $20 → Global Sponsor Pool
  │         └── distributed to all members with active sponsorship
  │             (weighted by Backed Mark contribution)
  │
  └── $20 → Patent Bucket (e.g., "Commerce Innovations")
            └── distributed to members who sponsored in THIS bucket
                (weighted by Backed Mark contribution to this bucket)
```

### Connection to SAA

A member's SAA (demonstrated judgment score) is built through successful sponsorship. When a member sponsors an innovation (via Backed Marks) and that innovation generates revenue, the member's SAA increases. Higher SAA means more Joule allocation authority (CJ#2), which means more influence over cooperative direction. This connects IP innovation directly to governance influence — but through demonstrated judgment, not through wealth.

## Patentable Elements

1. **60/20/20 IP revenue split across creator, global pool, and domain bucket** — automated revenue distribution that simultaneously compensates individual creators and funds collective innovation infrastructure
2. **Patent bucket system with domain-specific revenue accumulation** — innovations grouped by domain, with sponsorship revenue flowing to domain-specific contributors
3. **Dual-participation IP economics** — a single member can earn from both creation (60% as inventor) and sponsorship (proportional share of 20% pools as backer), creating a self-reinforcing innovation cycle
4. **Backed Mark sponsorship of IP with SAA feedback** — allocation authority tokens used to fund innovation, with successful sponsorship generating governance influence through demonstrated judgment scoring
5. **Self-funding innovation cycle** — IP revenue generates Backed Marks (via onboarding/steward allocation from CJ#1), which fund more innovation sponsorship, which generates more IP revenue, creating a perpetual innovation engine

## SEC/Legal Notes

- The 60% creator share is **earned income** from intellectual property. Standard IP licensing/royalty tax treatment.
- The 20% global pool distribution could be viewed as passive income — this is the highest SEC risk in the IP Load Balancing system. Key defenses:
  - Members earn pool shares through **active sponsorship decisions** (allocating Backed Marks), not through passive investment
  - Backed Marks are earned through labor (onboarding, stewardship), not purchased with cash
  - The "common enterprise" prong of Howey may apply, but the "investment of money" prong is weaker because Backed Marks originate from effort, not cash
- The patent bucket system strengthens SEC defense by requiring **domain-specific knowledge** — members must actively choose which buckets to sponsor, demonstrating the kind of active participation that distinguishes cooperative membership from passive investment
- All IP revenue stays within the cooperative as Credits/Marks — the closed-loop nature (no cash-out) significantly weakens any securities characterization
- The cooperative should maintain clear records showing that sponsorship decisions require active member judgment, not passive allocation

## Description

Compass Governance is the **political architecture** of the Liana Banyan cooperative. It is built on two interlocking principles: **fixed capacity** and **directional balance**.

Fixed capacity: there are exactly 300 governance seats. Not 299. Not 301. Three hundred, like the Spartans at Thermopylae. This number is a DNA-Locked constitutional provision. The cooperative cannot create more seats to dilute existing governance power. It cannot reduce seats to concentrate power. Three hundred is the number, and three hundred it remains.

Directional balance: the 300 seats are organized along four compass directions, each representing a different governance philosophy:

- **North (Innovation)**: advocates for new systems, new initiatives, new approaches. The "build more" faction.
- **South (Stability)**: advocates for reliability, maintenance, and proven systems. The "protect what works" faction.
- **East (Expansion)**: advocates for growth, new markets, new members, broader reach. The "grow outward" faction.
- **West (Conservation)**: advocates for sustainability, resource preservation, and debt reduction. The "steward resources" faction.

No direction is "right." All four are necessary. The genius is in the tension.

### Comparison to Traditional Models

| Model | Capacity | Balance Mechanism | Takeover Protection | Authority Basis |
|-------|----------|------------------|--------------------|-----------------| 
| **Corporate Board** | Small (5-15) | None — majority rules | Poison pills (defensive, not structural) | Shares owned |
| **US Congress** | Fixed (535) | Two chambers | Constitutional amendments (slow) | Election |
| **Platform Cooperative (typical)** | Unlimited | One member one vote | None — majority can capture | Membership |
| **DAO** | Unlimited | Token-weighted | Token concentration = capture | Tokens held |
| **Liana Banyan Compass** | Fixed (300) | Four-directional balance | DNA Lock + SAA requirement | Demonstrated judgment |

## Mechanics

### Seat Allocation

- 300 total seats, distributed across the four directions
- Distribution is not necessarily equal — it responds to cooperative needs
- Seats are **earned** through demonstrated contribution (SAA), not purchased or elected
- A member must achieve minimum SAA thresholds to be eligible for a governance seat
- Seat holders can be challenged at any time via Red Queen (CJ#6)

### One Member, One Vote

Every cooperative member has one vote. This is non-negotiable and DNA-Locked. No member gets more votes because they have more Credits, more Marks, or more Joules. The vote is the democratic floor.

However: **SAA determines allocation authority**. A member with high SAA (proven through successful project sponsorship) can direct more cooperative resources. This is not a vote — it is an operational authority earned through demonstrated judgment. The distinction is critical:

- **Vote**: "Should we build this?" — one member, one vote, equal weight
- **Allocation**: "How much should we allocate to building this?" — weighted by SAA

A member with 1,000 SAA and a member with 10 SAA have the same vote on whether to pursue a project. But if the project is approved, the member with 1,000 SAA can direct more resources toward its execution. This separates democratic legitimacy (voting) from operational competence (allocation).

### Directional Tension

The four directions create structural tension that prevents any single philosophy from dominating:

- North wants to build new things → West asks "can we afford it?"
- East wants to expand → South asks "will quality survive?"
- South wants to maintain → North asks "are we stagnating?"
- West wants to conserve → East asks "are we shrinking?"

Decisions require **cross-directional support**. A proposal that only North supports will fail. A proposal that North AND East support (innovation + expansion) still faces scrutiny from South AND West (stability + conservation). The most successful proposals earn support from at least three directions.

### The Spartan Constraint

Why 300? Because fixed capacity forces quality over quantity. In an unlimited-seat governance model, the path to power is recruitment — flood the seats with allies. In a 300-seat model, every seat is precious. Earning one requires demonstrated excellence. Losing one (via Red Queen challenge) means someone better took your place.

The 300 also prevents governance bloat. Decision-making bodies grow less effective as they grow larger. Three hundred is large enough for diverse representation but small enough for meaningful deliberation.

### DNA Lock Integration

The following governance provisions are DNA-Locked (see CJ#5):

- Total seats: 300 (cannot be increased or decreased)
- One member, one vote (cannot be made unequal)
- Four-directional structure (cannot be collapsed to fewer directions)
- SAA-based allocation authority (cannot be replaced with wealth-based authority)
- Red Queen challenge mechanism (cannot be disabled)

## Patentable Elements

1. **Fixed-capacity cooperative governance with constitutional seat lock** — a governance system with an immutable number of seats (300) that prevents dilution and concentration of governance power
2. **Four-directional governance balance model** — compass-based philosophical alignment (Innovation/Stability/Expansion/Conservation) that creates structural tension preventing any single ideology from capturing the cooperative
3. **Separated democratic vote and operational allocation authority** — one-member-one-vote for decisions, SAA-weighted allocation for resource direction, maintaining democratic legitimacy while rewarding demonstrated competence
4. **SAA-gated governance seat eligibility** — governance participation requires demonstrated judgment (earned through successful sponsorship and contribution), not financial investment or popularity
5. **Cross-directional support requirement** — proposals must earn support from multiple philosophical directions, preventing factional capture

## SEC/Legal Notes

## Description

The Red Queen is the **continuous authority verification system** of the Liana Banyan cooperative. Named after the Red Queen hypothesis from evolutionary biology — "you have to keep running just to stay in the same place" — it ensures that every person holding authority within the cooperative is **continuously earning** that authority.

In traditional organizations, authority is granted and then largely unmonitored:

- A CEO is hired and remains until fired or retired
- A board member is elected and serves their full term regardless of performance

The Red Queen inverts this. Authority is not a thing you have. It is a thing you are continuously demonstrating.

## Mechanics

## Description

Crown Jewel #7 is actually three interlocking mechanisms that together solve the **valuation and voting problem** in cooperative governance:

1. **HIVI (History-Indexed Value Integration)**: Deterministic asset pricing based on transaction history — not market sentiment, not speculation, not appraisal.
2. **Forex Ratchet**: One-way value ratchet for Joules — value increases but never decreases.
3. **Quadratic Voting**: Voting cost that increases quadratically, preventing plutocratic capture of governance decisions.

These three mechanisms are presented as a single Crown Jewel because they are architecturally inseparable. HIVI determines what things are worth. The Forex Ratchet ensures stored value never decreases. Quadratic Voting ensures that valuation power does not translate to governance capture.

### Comparison to Traditional Models

| Mechanism | Traditional Approach | LB Approach | Key Difference |
|-----------|---------------------|-------------|----------------|
| **Asset Pricing** | Market-based (supply/demand, speculation) | HIVI (transaction-history-based, deterministic) | No speculation possible |
| **Value Storage** | Market-dependent (can lose value) | Forex Ratchet (floor never decreases) | Member protected from loss |
| **Governance Voting** | One-person-one-vote OR one-share-one-vote | Quadratic (cost of influence increases quadratically) | Prevents both plutocracy and mob rule |

## Mechanics

### HIVI: History-Indexed Value Integration

HIVI is a **deterministic pricing algorithm**. It prices cooperative assets based on their transaction history — not on what someone thinks they are worth, but on what the historical record of transactions proves they are worth.

How it works:

- Every asset (product, service, IP, storefront) accumulates a transaction history
- HIVI analyzes this history: volume, frequency, consistency, trend
- The HIVI price is a weighted function of historical transaction data
- The price updates deterministically as new transactions occur
- There is no bid/ask spread. There is no market-maker. There is no speculation.

Example: A storefront has sold 500 items over 6 months at an average of $15 per item. HIVI prices the storefront's value based on this transaction history — not on what a buyer might pay, but on what the data proves. If the storefront's sales increase, its HIVI value increases proportionally. If sales decrease, HIVI value decreases (but the Forex Ratchet prevents Joule-denominated values from decreasing — see below).

HIVI eliminates the fundamental problem of cooperative asset valuation: who decides what things are worth? In traditional cooperatives, asset valuation requires appraisals, disagreements, and political battles. HIVI makes it mathematical.

### Forex Ratchet: The "Forever Stamp"

The Forex Ratchet is a **one-way value mechanism** for Joules (CJ#2, Gear Three). It works like a ratchet wrench: it can turn in one direction (up) but not the other (down).

How it works:

- Joule value is calculated based on total cooperative value divided by total Joules outstanding
- When cooperative value increases, Joule value increases proportionally
- When cooperative value DECREASES, **Joule value stays at its previous high-water mark**
- The cooperative absorbs the loss, not the Joule holders
- Joule value can NEVER decrease. Once a Joule is worth $1.50, it is worth at least $1.50 forever.

This is the "forever stamp" mechanic. Just as a "forever stamp" purchased at $0.55 remains valid when postage rises to $0.75, a Joule purchased (through surplus creation) at any value remains valid at least at that value, regardless of cooperative fluctuation.

The Forex Ratchet is DNA-Locked (CJ#5). The cooperative cannot vote to reduce Joule value. The ratchet is permanent.

### Cooperative Loss Absorption

When cooperative value decreases but Joule value cannot:

- The cooperative's operating reserve absorbs the difference
- If the operating reserve is insufficient, new Joule creation is suspended until value recovers
- During suspension, surplus accumulates as Credits (which do not have a ratchet floor)
- Once cooperative value recovers past the previous high-water mark, normal Joule creation resumes and accumulated surplus converts

This prevents the cooperative from collapsing under the weight of guaranteed Joule values during downturns while preserving the promise to existing Joule holders.

### Quadratic Voting

Quadratic Voting prevents plutocratic capture of cooperative governance. In a standard one-person-one-vote system, every vote costs the same. In a one-share-one-vote system, wealth equals power. Quadratic Voting finds the middle ground:

- Every member gets one vote at no cost (the democratic floor)
- Additional influence on specific proposals costs Credits, but the cost increases **quadratically**:
  - 1 additional vote = 1 Credit
  - 2 additional votes = 4 Credits
  - 3 additional votes = 9 Credits
  - 4 additional votes = 16 Credits
  - 10 additional votes = 100 Credits

This means a member who cares deeply about a specific proposal can invest more influence — but the cost escalates rapidly. A wealthy member who wants to dominate a vote must spend exponentially more than a poor member who wants to add a single extra vote. This makes buying governance outcomes prohibitively expensive while still allowing passionate members to express strong preferences.

Key constraints:
- Quadratic voting only applies to **specific proposals**, not to general elections or governance seat allocation
- Credits spent on quadratic votes are consumed (returned to the cooperative treasury)
- One-member-one-vote on governance seats and constitutional questions is preserved (no quadratic voting on fundamentals)
- The quadratic cost function is DNA-Locked

## Patentable Elements

1. **Deterministic transaction-history-based asset valuation (HIVI)** — an automated pricing algorithm that values cooperative assets based on historical transaction data rather than market sentiment, appraisal, or speculation
2. **One-way value ratchet for cooperative surplus currency (Forex Ratchet)** — a floor mechanism that allows stored-value instruments (Joules) to increase in value but never decrease, with the cooperative absorbing downward pressure
3. **Cooperative loss absorption with Joule creation suspension** — during cooperative value decreases, new surplus currency creation suspends to preserve the ratchet floor for existing holders, resuming when value recovers
4. **Quadratic voting cost function in cooperative governance** — additional governance influence on specific proposals costs Credits at a quadratically increasing rate, preventing plutocratic capture while allowing preference expression
5. **Integrated valuation-ratchet-voting system** — HIVI (pricing), Forex Ratchet (floor), and Quadratic Voting (governance) operating as a single interlocking mechanism where deterministic pricing feeds the ratchet floor which operates within quadratic-voting-governed parameters

## SEC/Legal Notes

- **HIVI pricing** strengthens SEC defense by making asset valuation transparent and deterministic. There is no speculative "expectation of profit" when value is mathematically derived from transaction history. Members can see exactly how HIVI calculates value.
- **Forex Ratchet** creates a unique SEC consideration: the guarantee that Joule value cannot decrease could be construed as a promise of value preservation. Defense: (a) Joules cannot be sold or converted to cash, so the "value" cannot be realized as profit, (b) the ratchet protects against loss but does not promise gain, and (c) the cooperative absorbs losses, which is standard mutual insurance, not securities.
- **Quadratic Voting** is a governance mechanism, not a financial one. Credits spent on votes are consumed, not invested. There is no expectation of return.
- The integrated system should be documented carefully in cooperative filings to demonstrate that HIVI, Ratchet, and Quadratic Voting are governance and operational tools, not financial instruments.

## Mechanics

The two layers work together: the Star Chamber reduces internal legal costs (members settle disputes within the cooperative rather than suing each other), which leaves more resources for Harbor Defense (defending against external threats).

Harbor Defense serves a deterrence function beyond its direct legal defense:

- An individual cooperative member with $500 in savings is a tempting legal target for a corporation with a $10M legal budget

- The mere existence of Harbor Defense changes the cost-benefit analysis for potential legal aggressors
- This is the "harbor" metaphor: ships in harbor are protected from storms. Members in the cooperative's legal harbor are protected from legal storms.

Harbor Defense covers:

- Lawsuits arising from cooperative commerce activity
- Regulatory investigations related to cooperative participation
- Intellectual property disputes involving cooperative innovations
- Cease-and-desist responses from competitors threatened by cooperative success
- Contract disputes arising from cooperative business relationships

Harbor Defense does NOT cover:

- Personal legal matters unrelated to cooperative activity
- Criminal defense (except where criminal charges arise from cooperative activity — e.g., regulatory charges)
- Matters where the member acted against cooperative interests (fraud, theft, deliberate harm to other members)
- Legal matters arising before cooperative membership
