## #2244 — Opt-In Member Documentation with Benefits

### Key Mechanism

Cooperative members who contribute to the platform (businesses, innovations, services, guides, photographs, etc.) are presented with an optional opt-in flow at contribution time or any subsequent time. The flow offers the member the choice to document the origin moment of the contribution — what happened, what they observed, a photograph of the context, a first-person account — in exchange for a concrete bundle of benefits: named attribution, co-authorship credit on derivative content, Service Allocation Authority accrual legibility, research-layer citation eligibility, and durable public record inclusion. Members who decline the opt-in receive full cooperative membership rights identically — equal governance vote, equal resource allocation, equal family-table seating, equal currency-earning rates. The architecture refuses any two-tier distinction between documented and undocumented members. Per-contribution granular consent is preserved; a member may opt in for one contribution and out for another, and may withdraw any specific opt-in at any time.

## #2249 — ROM-First AI Inference Cost Architecture (70/25/5 Model)

### Key Mechanism

Before any AI inference query is routed to a frontier language model, the query is first fingerprinted (normalized + cryptographically hashed) and checked against a persistent read-only-memory store of previously-computed query-to-output mappings. If the fingerprint is present, the cached output is returned immediately — no model invocation, near-zero cost. If the fingerprint is absent, the query is routed to a primary frontier model (Claude Opus 4.6 or equivalent) for fresh inference; the output is returned to the user AND written to the cache with a time-to-live parameter appropriate to the query's volatility class. Approximately five percent of cache-miss queries (high-stakes categories: financial, legal, governance, patent-related) are additionally routed to a cross-reference secondary model (Gemini 3.1 Pro or equivalent) for verification against hallucination or model-specific blindspots. The operational target ratio is 70% cache-served / 25% primary-model / 5% primary-plus-cross-reference, yielding a blended per-query cost of approximately 10–15% of a naive pipeline where every query reaches the frontier model directly. Cache invalidation is triggered by detected changes in the underlying source of truth, using standard cache-invalidation patterns from the CDN and database-query-cache disciplines.

### Why This Is Novel (Enumerated)

1. Application of cache-first architecture specifically to AI inference output caching at hyperscale (most prior art caches KV states within a single inference, not across invocations)
2. The specific 70/25/5 operational ratio as the numerical signature of the model, derived from Zipfian query distribution analysis
3. Integration of a cross-reference verification layer at 5% of query volume for high-stakes categories
4. Canonicalization of semantically-equivalent queries to the same fingerprint (not just exact-match caching)
5. Volatility-class-based time-to-live assignment, distinguishing time-sensitive queries from stable factual queries
6. Integration with a cooperative platform patent governance layer exposing cache hit rate as a governance metric
7. Integration with a regulatory compliance reporting module (Claim 1.10, composing with #2250)

## #2252 — Pioneer Proposal Rewards (Rolodex Recruitment Pipeline)

### Key Mechanism

A cooperative member who discovers an external party (vendor, creator, service provider) and proposes they join the cooperative submits a proposal timestamped and ordered by the proposal's chronological position relative to other proposals for the same external party. When the proposed party actually joins LB, rewards distribute on a diminishing scale: 1st proposer receives 100% of the base reward, 2nd receives 50%, 3rd receives 25%, 4th and beyond receive a 10% floor — no effort is wasted. Time decay applies independently: 7 days old or less yields 100%, 30 days yields 25%, 90+ days yields 10%. Each proposer's final reward is the minimum of the order-based percentage and the time-based percentage. Rewards are denominated in Marks (cooperative currency, one-way valve — cannot convert to fiat), and are strictly one level deep — the joining member's subsequent proposals earn rewards to themselves, not to the original proposer, as an anti-MLM structural guarantee. The first proposer becomes the joining member's automatic Rolodex connection, and the joining member receives a fill-in-the-blank business plan template pre-populated from the original proposer's research.

### Why This Is Novel (Enumerated)

1. Multi-proposer ordered attribution with a diminishing-with-floor structure (no existing referral program honors every proposer after the first)
2. Independent time-decay applied across all proposers
3. Strict one-level-deep enforcement as an anti-MLM structural guarantee
4. Cooperative currency denomination with one-way-valve constraint (not cash-based)
5. Automatic Rolodex connection formation and business plan template delivery to the joining party

## #2256 — Chess-Piece Librarian Doublets

### Key Mechanism

Four parallel pipelines — Bishop (indigo, prose-first semantic), Knight (silver, schema-first structural), Rook (slate, numerical-first statistical), Pawn (amber, provenance-first citation) — ingest the same source corpus through intentionally different parsers, classifiers, and indexers. Each pipeline produces its own independent truth snapshot. The four snapshots are compared pairwise at every comparable index. When all four agree at an index, the value is treated as triangulated truth. When they disagree, the disagreement itself is classified diagnostically: a disagreement between the prose pipeline and the schema pipeline indicates narrative-versus-schema drift; a disagreement between the numerical pipeline and the others indicates numerical drift; a disagreement between the provenance pipeline and the others indicates citation drift. The disagreement classification is then escalated to the cooperative governance layer for human review if the severity exceeds a configured threshold.

### Why This Is Novel (Enumerated)

## #2259 — The Scrambler (Eager Pairwise Consistency Verification) — K407 LIVE

### Key Mechanism

A background Python process runs continuously, sampling pairs of platform subsystems (4 Corps subsystems + 4 Librarian subsystems = 8 total, producing C(8,2) = 28 unordered pairs). The sampling order is a deterministic-but-non-sequential permutation of length 28 — reproducible for debugging, but non-sequential so that a drifting subsystem cannot align on predictably-checked indices. At each sampled pair, the Scrambler runs a set of deterministic invariant checks (count matches, hash matches, schema matches, cross-reference edge verification). Results are written to an append-only ledger with severity classification. Upon completing a full 28-cycle, the permutation itself rotates via a seeded shuffle to produce a new non-sequential permutation for the next cycle. The seed rotates every 7 full cycles, preventing long-horizon gaming by any subsystem that has learned the current permutation. The Scrambler contains zero AI inference capacity — it is pure deterministic predicate evaluation. ## #2261 — The Representative Scorecard (Power to the People)

### Key Mechanism

Cooperative members create Scorecards targeting specific elected representatives on specific policy issues. When a Scorecard is created, the system automatically populates it with data from external sources: the representative's voting record on related legislation (from Congress.gov and state legislature APIs), public statements on the issue (from press releases and social media), a list of facilities or infrastructure within the representative's jurisdiction relevant to the issue (e.g., datacenters in their district), and constituent impact data (e.g., local ratepayer costs). Members fact-check the data, add evidence, and vote on whether the Scorecard represents a consistent or inconsistent stance by the representative. A member-consensus rating accumulates. When the rating exceeds a configured escalation threshold (e.g., 100 members voting "needs attention"), the Scorecard routes to the cooperative's Political Expedition coordination layer as an input to coordinated-action queues — letter-writing, coordinated testimony, petition filings, or Red Carpet treatment for the representative. The Scorecard persists across election cycles, accumulating historical context on the representative's record over time.
