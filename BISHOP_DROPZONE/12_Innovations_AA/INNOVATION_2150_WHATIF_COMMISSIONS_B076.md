## The Problem

Founder is about to be in conversations with high-value people (makers, creators, potential Crown holders, partners) where the RIGHT move mid-conversation is to say: *"You know what, can you be in charge of that? These are essentially commissions."*

Currently: Founder would have to manually draft a letter, describe a role, set up the platform routing, and hand it off via email after the call. **The window for that kind of on-the-spot deployment closes fast.**

The fix: **pre-drafted, anticipated role assignments** with formal contracts, pre-configured platform routing, and personalized Red Carpet entries — ready to hand out during a conversation.

## The Mechanism — What-If Commissions

A **What-If Commission** is a pre-drafted role-assignment package that the Founder can hand to a specific person during a live conversation, on-the-spot. It contains:

1. **Opening line (always)**: *"We Need What You're Good At."* This signals: we anticipated you might have unusual ability; we are grateful to recognize it; this is in addition to any other role you hold.
2. **Scope of the commission** — role description, domain of responsibility, flexible boundaries (can be fractional, time-limited, or scoped to a specific guild/business subset)
3. **Flexibility clauses** — "2 hours each Tuesday"? That's a valid commitment. Commission adapts to what the person is willing to give.
4. **Formal What-If Commission Contract** — the lightweight legal document confirming mutual commitment
5. **Platform routing shift** — upon acceptance, the platform's internal routing automatically updates to reflect the new role (even if scoped)
6. **Personalized Red Carpet entry** — pre-configured welcome for this specific person, this specific commission

## The Analogy — Battlefield Commission

In military history, a battlefield commission was granted on-the-spot to soldiers demonstrating unusual leadership in active combat. The commander had pre-authorized authority; the paperwork caught up later. The **critical element**: recognition of unexpected merit, in the moment.

What-If Commissions are the civilian-cooperative equivalent. The paperwork exists in advance; the conversation is when they get activated.

## Why "What-If"

The name itself conveys: *"We thought: what if we meet someone of unusual ability who would meet higher expectations? We are grateful to be able to extend this additional benefit/position in recognition and request."*

It is a humble pre-authorization, not a predestination.

## Flexibility Structure — Based on Netherlands Fractional Job Policy

The What-If Commission system borrows from the Netherlands' fractional employment framework. A person commits to **what they are willing to do**, not a full role they cannot sustain. Commitments can be:

- **Time-fractional**: "2 hours Tuesday" / "one afternoon per month"
- **Scope-fractional**: "up to 10 businesses" / "one guild only" / "Pacific timezone only"
- **Project-fractional**: "for this one Kickstarter arc" / "through end of 2026"
- **Decision-fractional**: "advisory veto only, not day-to-day"

The commission accommodates the person; the person does not have to conform to a fixed job description. This is the foundation of .net — **versatile network formed through fractional commitments**.

## Platform Integration

A What-If Commission upon acceptance triggers:

1. **Member record update** — new role/title added to member profile with scope metadata
2. **Routing rule update** — platform-wide routing rules shift (e.g., messages about topic X now CC this person)
3. **Guild/Tribe membership update** — if scoped to a guild, membership added
4. **Red Carpet activation** — personalized welcome flow fires on their next login
5. **Founder's contact record update** — this person now appears with their commission metadata in Founder's contact dashboard
6. **Calendar + MoneyPenny sync** — commission meetings auto-scheduled based on the committed cadence

## Claims

1. A role-assignment system where role definitions are pre-drafted ("anticipated"), stored in platform infrastructure, and activated on-demand by a designated principal (Founder) during a live interaction with a named recipient.
2. A flexible-commitment contract architecture where the person commits to their available scope (time / domain / project / decision level) rather than conforming to a fixed job description.
3. An automatic platform-routing update mechanism where commission acceptance immediately shifts internal message routing, guild membership, and Red Carpet personalization without manual administrative configuration.
4. A "We Need What You're Good At" opening-line pattern as canonical signal of meritocratic recognition — distinguishable from standard recruitment or hiring language.
5. A pre-authorization model where the Founder (or designated principal) carries pre-approved commission authority, with paperwork and platform state updates completing the legal and operational transfer automatically.
