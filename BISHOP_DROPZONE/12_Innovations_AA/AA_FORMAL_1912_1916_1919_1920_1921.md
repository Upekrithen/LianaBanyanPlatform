## Description

Out of Bounds is a **strategic external discussion pipeline** that converts genuine research questions into multi-platform outreach. The cooperative regularly encounters real legal, technical, and economic questions during platform development. Rather than resolving these questions internally only, OOB posts them on Reddit, Discord, academic forums, and other public discussion venues — simultaneously obtaining expert feedback, generating organic interest, and driving qualified traffic back to the Pnyx governance forum.

The critical distinction: these are **authentic questions**, not marketing copy disguised as discussion. The cooperative genuinely needs answers about cooperative securities law, multi-currency accounting, proximity-based labor matching, and dozens of other real problems. OOB systematizes the process of asking those questions in public.

Five post templates have been written covering: legal/regulatory questions, technical architecture questions, economic model questions, governance design questions, and cooperative precedent questions. Each template includes framing language that contextualizes the question within cooperative design without promotional intent.

## Mechanics

### Template Selection
- Five template categories map to common question types arising from platform development
- Each template provides structure (context paragraph, specific question, invitation for critique) without prescribing content
- Templates are guides, not fill-in-the-blank — the poster adapts to the specific question

### Platform Targeting
- **Reddit**: r/cooperatives, r/legaladvice, r/startups, r/economics, topic-specific subreddits
- **Discord**: Cooperative-focused servers, legal tech communities, platform cooperative channels
- **Academic**: ResearchGate discussions, SSRN comment threads, university cooperative research groups
- Each platform has different norms for post length, tone, and engagement — the pipeline accounts for this

### Post Composition
- Question is extracted from an active platform development problem
- Template provides structural framing
- Post is composed with platform-appropriate formatting
- Cross-reference to related Pnyx threads is included where organic (not forced)

### Thread Tracking
- Each posted question is logged with platform, URL, date, and originating internal question
- Response monitoring occurs on a regular cadence
- Valuable responses are harvested and cross-referenced to internal development threads

### Response Harvesting
- Expert answers are summarized and linked back to the relevant Pnyx discussion
- Contributors who provide substantial value are noted for potential future outreach
- Answers that resolve genuine platform questions are incorporated into development

## Patentable Elements

1. **Systematized research-to-outreach pipeline** converting internal cooperative development questions into structured multi-platform discussion posts — limited patent relevance as this is primarily a workflow/strategy rather than a novel mechanism
2. **Template-driven question composition** with platform-specific adaptation — low novelty, standard content strategy

Document for completeness. Primary value is strategic, not mechanical.

## SEC/Legal Notes

- Posts must remain genuinely informational — no investment solicitation language
- No promises of returns, value appreciation, or financial benefit in any external post
- The cooperative's existence can be mentioned; its financial model should not be detailed in ways that could constitute an offer
- Reddit and Discord posts are public — assume SEC staff could read any post
- Academic forum posts carry implicit credibility; ensure claims are supportable

## Description

Initiative Patron Tiers replace the platform's generic participation tier names (Match, Flame, Torch, Blaze) with **initiative-specific themed tier names** that reflect the identity and culture of each initiative. A member advancing through Let's Make Bread earns the titles Table Setter, Sous Chef, Head Baker, and Breadwinner — not generic gamification labels. A member advancing through Defense Klaus earns Lookout, Shield Bearer, Knight Commander, and Shield Master.

This is a **naming and identity system**, not a mechanical innovation. Its value is cultural: members who identify with an initiative's language are more likely to deepen engagement. The tier names create narrative coherence — a "Guardian Angel" in LifeLine carries meaning that "Tier 4" does not.

Each initiative's four tiers correspond to the same underlying participation thresholds (measured in XP, completed bounties, active time, and peer recognition within that initiative). The tiers are cosmetic labels atop a unified progression engine.

## Mechanics

### Tier Structure (Universal)
- **Tier 1**: Entry-level participation (completed onboarding + first bounty within initiative)
- **Tier 2**: Sustained engagement (threshold of XP + minimum active weeks)
- **Tier 3**: Leadership behavior (mentoring, Crew Table leadership, Shepherding within initiative)
- **Tier 4**: Anchor status (long-term, high-impact contribution recognized by peers)

### Initiative-Specific Names (Examples)

| Initiative | Tier 1 | Tier 2 | Tier 3 | Tier 4 |
|-----------|--------|--------|--------|--------|
| Let's Make Bread | Table Setter | Sous Chef | Head Baker | Breadwinner |
| LifeLine | First Responder | Crisis Counselor | Station Chief | Guardian Angel |
| Defense Klaus | Lookout | Shield Bearer | Knight Commander | Shield Master |
| Rally Group | Passenger | Navigator | Road Captain | Convoy Commander |
| Didasko | Student | Tutor | Professor | Dean |
| The Forge | Apprentice | Journeyman | Master Smith | Forge Keeper |
| JukeBox | Roadie | Session Player | Bandleader | Maestro |
| Shield Knight | Recruit | Patrol | Watch Commander | Sentinel |
| Stocked Local Larder | Stocker | Pantry Lead | Warehouse Chief | Provision Master |

Remaining initiatives follow the same pattern — each set of four names is drawn from the initiative's metaphor and culture.

### Profile Display
- Member profile shows tier name per initiative, not a single global tier
- A member can be a Head Baker (Tier 3) in Let's Make Bread and a Lookout (Tier 1) in Defense Klaus simultaneously
- Cross-initiative tier display creates a composite identity: "Head Baker / Lookout / Navigator"

### Tier Privileges
- Each tier unlocks initiative-specific privileges (e.g., Tier 3 in Let's Make Bread can propose new storefront partnerships; Tier 3 in Defense Klaus can nominate Star Chamber cases)
- Privileges are functional, not honorary — they gate real platform actions

## Patentable Elements

1. **Initiative-specific themed tier naming system** within a cooperative platform — limited novelty (gamification tiers are well-established)
2. **Multi-initiative composite identity display** showing simultaneous tier levels across different cooperative service areas — modest novelty in cooperative context

Document for completeness. Primary value is cultural and engagement-oriented.

## SEC/Legal Notes

- Tier names must not imply financial status, investment level, or equity position
- "Patron" in the innovation name refers to participation patronage (cooperative sense), not financial patronage — language should be monitored
- Tier advancement must be clearly tied to participation and labor, never to financial contribution
- No tier should gate access to financial returns or profit-sharing mechanisms

## Description

The OOB Plug Architecture mechanizes Innovation #1912 (Out of Bounds) by embedding multi-platform posting capability directly into the Founder Portfolio Helm interface. A post composed once in Helm auto-propagates to Reddit, Discord, Pnyx, and any future platform through a **Plug-based dispatch system** — the same architectural pattern used for Calendar Plugs (built in Knight Session 82).

Each destination platform is a Plug: a self-contained module with its own credential storage, format adapter, posting API integration, and reply collection mechanism. The Helm dashboard provides a unified thread view where replies from all platforms are aggregated into a single conversation stream, regardless of origin.

This is architecturally significant because it extends the Plug pattern beyond calendars into content distribution. The Plug registry becomes a general-purpose integration framework: Calendar Plugs for scheduling, OOB Plugs for posting, and future Plugs for any platform-to-platform bridge. The pattern is reusable and extensible.

## Mechanics

### Plug Registry
- Each platform (Reddit, Discord, Pnyx, future platforms) is registered as a Plug in the existing Plug registry table
- Plug registration includes: platform name, API endpoint pattern, credential requirements, format constraints, rate limits
- New platforms are added by creating a new Plug entry — no core code changes required

### Credential Storage
- OAuth tokens, API keys, and session credentials are stored per-Plug in Supabase with encryption at rest
- Each Plug manages its own credential lifecycle (refresh, revocation, re-authentication)
- Credentials are scoped to the Helm operator — no shared platform accounts

### Format Adaptation
- Each Plug includes a format adapter that transforms a canonical post (title, body, tags, links) into platform-appropriate format
- Reddit adapter: handles subreddit targeting, flair selection, markdown formatting, character limits
- Discord adapter: handles server/channel targeting, embed formatting, mention conventions
- Pnyx adapter: handles category assignment, governance tagging, internal cross-references
- Adapters are bidirectional — they also normalize incoming replies into a canonical format

### Post Dispatch
- Composer in Helm produces a canonical post object
- Operator selects destination Plugs (checkboxes: Reddit, Discord, Pnyx, etc.)
- Dispatch engine sends the post through each selected Plug's format adapter and API
- Each dispatch returns a platform-specific post ID stored for thread tracking

### Reply Collection
- Each Plug periodically polls its platform for new replies on tracked threads (or uses webhooks where available)
- Replies are normalized through the format adapter and stored in a unified reply table
- Reply table links: original post ID, platform, author, timestamp, content, parent reply (for threading)

### Unified Thread View
- Helm dashboard displays all replies across all platforms in a single chronological stream
- Each reply is tagged with its origin platform (Reddit badge, Discord badge, etc.)
- Operator can respond from Helm — the response is dispatched back through the appropriate Plug to the correct platform and thread

## Patentable Elements

1. **Plug-based multi-platform content dispatch system** where each destination platform is a self-contained module with credential management, format adaptation, and reply collection — extends the Calendar Plug pattern into content distribution
2. **Bidirectional format adaptation** that normalizes both outgoing posts and incoming replies into a canonical format for unified display
3. **Unified cross-platform thread aggregation** in a cooperative management dashboard, enabling single-pane reply management across Reddit, Discord, governance forums, and extensible future platforms
4. **Reusable Plug registry architecture** that serves both calendar integration and content distribution through the same registration and credential framework

## SEC/Legal Notes

- Auto-posting to external platforms carries SEC risk if post content includes investment-related language — all posts must pass through the same SEC-safe content guidelines used for MoneyPenny social posts
- Platform Terms of Service must be respected per-Plug — automated posting may violate some platforms' TOS if not properly disclosed
- Reddit specifically prohibits coordinated inauthentic behavior — OOB posts must be genuine questions, not orchestrated campaigns
- Credential storage must comply with each platform's API usage policies
- Reply harvesting must respect platform data ownership terms

## Description

The Guided Discovery Configurator is a **Dell-style interest routing system** for cooperative member onboarding. It replaces the overwhelming "here are 16 initiatives, pick one" experience with a 3-to-5 question configurator that narrows a new member's path to their highest-impact entry point.

The critical design distinction: this is a **configurator**, not a quiz. A quiz tests knowledge. A configurator routes preference. There are no wrong answers. Each choice narrows the option space until the system can recommend a specific Treasure Map, pre-load a relevant first bounty, and configure a tailored Beacon Run — all before the member has to understand the full platform.

The configurator solves the cold-start problem for complex cooperatives. A platform with 16 initiatives, hundreds of bounties, and multiple currency types is paralyzing for a new member. The configurator reduces cognitive load to a sequence of simple self-identification questions: "Are you a maker or a helper? Do you want to work with food, transport, or technology? Do you prefer solo tasks or team projects?" Three questions, and the member has a personalized pathway.

## Mechanics

### Question Tree
- 3 to 5 branching questions, each with 2 to 4 answer options
- Questions are identity-based, not knowledge-based: "What describes you best?" not "What do you know about cooperatives?"
- Each answer eliminates initiative branches and narrows toward a cluster of 1 to 3 initiatives

### Example Flow
```
Q1: "What gets you going?"
  → "Making things" → Forge / Arena / JukeBox cluster
  → "Helping people" → LifeLine / Mission ONE / Stocked Local Larder cluster
  → "Running a business" → Let's Make Bread / Rally Group cluster
  → "Learning and teaching" → Didasko / Academy cluster

Q2 (if "Making things"): "What kind of making?"
  → "Physical products" → Forge path
  → "Design and art" → Arena path
  → "Music and media" → JukeBox path

Q3: "How do you like to work?"
  → "Solo, at my own pace" → Bounty-first pathway
  → "With a team" → Crew Table pathway
  → "I want to lead" → Steward pathway
```

### Treasure Map Selection
- Configurator output maps to one of the 12 Treasure Maps (Breakfast through Service Runner)
- The selected map becomes the member's default dashboard view
- Map is a recommendation, not a lock — member can switch at any time

### Bounty Pre-Load
- Based on configurator output, the system pre-selects 1 to 3 starter bounties that align with the member's declared interests
- Bounties appear in a "Recommended For You" section on the member's first dashboard load
- Pre-loaded bounties are low-barrier (completable in under 30 minutes) to generate early momentum

### Beacon Run Configuration
- The member's first Beacon Run (guided tutorial sequence) is configured to highlight features relevant to their chosen path
- A "maker" gets a Beacon Run through the Arena and Forge
- A "helper" gets a Beacon Run through LifeLine and Mission ONE
- Beacon Runs are the existing Wildfire Beacon system with configurator-driven targeting

### Profile Seeding
- Configurator answers seed the member's profile with interest tags
- Interest tags influence future recommendations, Shepherd matching, and Crew Table suggestions
- Tags are visible to the member and editable — no hidden profiling

## Patentable Elements

1. **Cooperative onboarding configurator** that dynamically generates personalized service pathways from interest mapping — the configurator pattern applied to multi-initiative cooperative membership is novel
2. **Treasure Map auto-selection** based on branching interest questions, pre-loading initiative-specific bounties and tutorial sequences
3. **Beacon Run dynamic configuration** where the guided tutorial adapts its content path based on configurator output rather than following a fixed sequence
4. **Profile seeding from onboarding configurator** creating immediate personalization without requiring the member to manually explore the platform

## SEC/Legal Notes

- Configurator must not route members toward financial products or investment-like features
- Interest mapping data must be disclosed to the member (no hidden profiling)
- "Recommended" bounties must be clearly labeled as suggestions, not obligations
- The configurator must not collect data beyond what is needed for pathway selection — no demographic harvesting
- COPPA compliance if minors could access the configurator (platform age gate applies)

## Description

Innovation #1921 is the **documentation of the Liana Banyan AI collaboration methodology as a replicable framework**. The methodology — which produced 1,935 innovations, 8 patent applications, and a deployed production platform using a single human founder coordinating four AI agents — is itself the innovation. The academic paper "How to Bake AI Cake" formalizes this methodology so that other organizations can replicate it.

The core concepts documented:

**AI Tuner**: The human role is not "programmer" or "manager" but "tuner" — drawn from Anne McCaffrey's *Crystal Singer*. The Founder does not write code or prose; he tunes the AI outputs through corrections, redirections, and quality gates. The AI Tuner concept reframes human-AI collaboration from delegation ("do this task") to calibration ("adjust this output").

**Chess Set Roles**: Four AI agents with distinct specializations — Rook (Gemini, strategic analysis), Knight (Cursor, code implementation), Bishop (Claude, architecture and writing), Pawn (Perplexity, research and validation). The roles are not arbitrary; they map to the chess pieces' movement patterns (Knight jumps to implementation, Bishop moves diagonally across domains, Rook holds long lines of strategic analysis, Pawns advance through territory).

**Separation Principle**: No AI agent performs its own quality control. Bishop writes; Founder corrects. Knight builds; Bishop reviews architecture. Pawn researches; Rook validates. The separation prevents confirmation bias and creates adversarial tension that improves output quality.

**GRAFTING Cycle**: The production loop — THRESHING (extract innovations from session work) followed by POLLINATION (propagate statistics, update handoffs, synchronize state across agents). GRAFTING ensures that no innovation is lost between sessions and that all agents operate from current state.

**Correction Ratchet**: Every Founder correction is recorded and persists across sessions via MEMORY.md and handoff documents. Corrections accumulate monotonically — once corrected, an error never recurs. This creates a system that improves over time without retraining.

## Mechanics

### Role Assignment
- Each AI agent is assigned based on capability match to role requirements
- Role boundaries are explicit: Knight does not write papers, Bishop does not deploy code
- Cross-role consultation occurs through structured handoff documents, not ad-hoc conversation

### Handoff Protocol
- Every session produces a handoff document capturing: what was built, what changed, what is next
- Handoff documents are the inter-session state transfer mechanism
- The BISHOP_DROPZONE directory is the physical handoff point — documents are written there for Knight to consume, and vice versa

### GRAFTING Cycle
- **THRESHING**: At session end, all innovations produced are extracted, numbered, classified, and added to the Innovation Bag
- **POLLINATION**: Innovation counts, system states, and key decisions are propagated to MEMORY.md, handoff documents, and the Milestone file
- GRAFTING runs at the boundary between sessions — it is the commit point

### Correction Ratchet
- Founder corrections are stored in MEMORY.md under specific headings (e.g., "Founder Corrections (NEVER use wrong versions)")
- Each correction includes the wrong version and the right version
- AI agents load MEMORY.md at session start — corrections are active from first interaction
- The ratchet is append-only: corrections are never removed

### Output Multiplication
- The paper documents how a single founder produced output equivalent to a mid-size development team
- Key metric: 1,935 innovations, 8 patent applications, full production platform, 39+ academic papers, 11+ Crown Letters — all from one human coordinating four AI agents over approximately 90 sessions

## Patentable Elements

1. **GRAFTING Cycle** (THRESHING + POLLINATION) as a formalized inter-session state management process for multi-agent AI collaboration — modest process patent potential
2. **Correction Ratchet** as a monotonically accumulating error-correction mechanism using persistent memory files — the specific implementation pattern has some novelty
3. **Chess Set role assignment methodology** with enforced Separation Principle preventing self-review — limited novelty (separation of concerns is well-established), but the specific AI agent application may be documentable

Primary value is academic and reputational, not patent-driven. The full paper is the deliverable.

## SEC/Legal Notes

- The paper describes methodology, not financial products — minimal SEC exposure
- Claims about output volume (1,935 innovations, etc.) must be verifiable — all innovations are numbered and documented in the Innovation Bag
- The paper must not imply that the methodology guarantees commercial success or financial returns
- If published in academic venues, standard academic disclosure requirements apply
- The AI Tuner concept does not create an employment or contractor relationship with AI providers
