## INNOVATION #2150 — WHAT-IF COMMISSIONS

### Description

A pre-drafted, anticipatory role-assignment system designed for on-the-spot deployment during live conversations between a principal (Founder) and a prospective role-holder. What-If Commissions are formal, lightweight contracts carried in advance by the principal, with pre-configured platform-routing rules that activate automatically upon recipient signature. The opening line — *"We Need What You're Good At"* — signals meritocratic recognition distinct from standard recruitment or hiring language.

The system borrows its structural metaphor from military battlefield commissions (on-the-spot recognition of unexpected leadership) and its flexibility model from the Netherlands fractional-employment framework (commitment to what the person can actually deliver, not to a fixed job description).

### Mechanics

**Pre-authored Commission Templates**:
- Commission templates are drafted and stored in platform infrastructure in advance of any specific recipient
- Each template specifies: title, commission type, domain scope, authority description, default duration, platform-routing rules, guild memberships granted, and associated Red Carpet personalization
- Templates are reusable and parameterizable — the same "Business Onboarder" template can be issued to many recipients with different scope-limits

**Live-Issuance Flow**:
1. Principal identifies a prospective recipient during a conversation
2. Principal pulls up pre-drafted commission template with recipient name pre-populated via contact dashboard
3. Principal and recipient negotiate scope-limits in-conversation (time-fractional, scope-fractional, project-fractional, decision-fractional)
4. Recipient signs; contract is captured digitally or on paper
5. Platform routing updates fire automatically upon signature

**Fractional Commitment Architecture**:
Four dimensions of scope the recipient may set:
- Time-fractional: e.g., "2 hours each Tuesday"
- Scope-fractional: e.g., "up to 10 businesses" or "one guild only"
- Project-fractional: e.g., "for this one Kickstarter arc"
- Decision-fractional: e.g., "advisory veto only, not day-to-day"

The commission accommodates the recipient; the recipient does not conform to a fixed role description.

**Automatic Platform Activation**:
Upon acceptance signature, the system automatically:
1. Updates recipient's member record with scoped role and title
2. Updates platform routing rules so scoped messages/decisions reach the recipient
3. Adds recipient to relevant guilds/tribes per template
4. Activates personalized Red Carpet flow for this commission on recipient's next login
5. Notifies relevant Crowns, Captains, and other stakeholders of the new authority
6. Creates Medallion accumulation path for commissioned work

### Prior Art Distinction

**Versus traditional recruitment letters**: Standard executive recruitment is a negotiation starting from a fixed job description. What-If Commissions start from recipient capability and let scope-limits form the role around the person. The written role is emergent, not pre-fixed.

**Versus fractional hiring platforms (Upwork, Toptal, Catalant)**: These match task-to-worker for fixed-scope gigs. What-If Commissions grant ongoing scoped authority with platform-level routing updates, not transactional tasks.

**Versus OpenBadges / reputation systems**: Badges recognize past achievement. What-If Commissions grant forward-looking authority based on anticipated competence.

**Versus contractor-to-employee conversion pathways**: These escalate engagement depth over time. Commissions are scoped at issuance and stay at that scope unless renegotiated.

**Versus military battlefield commissions**: Military commissions grant commissioned-officer rank on-the-spot based on demonstrated combat leadership. What-If Commissions adapt this for cooperative platforms: pre-authorized recognition, conversation-triggered activation, automatic organizational-state updates.

**Versus Netherlands fractional employment law**: Netherlands law protects the worker's right to scoped hours. What-If Commissions extend this into scope-dimensions beyond time (domain, project, decision level), AND integrate with platform infrastructure so the scope is operationally enforced, not merely legally protected.

### Claims

1. A pre-drafted role-assignment system where role definitions are authored, stored, and parameterized in advance by a platform principal, then activated on-demand during live interactions with named recipients whose identity and scope are bound at activation time rather than authoring time.

2. A flexible-commitment contract architecture where the commission recipient selects scope-limits across four orthogonal dimensions — time, domain/scope, project/duration, and decision-level — rather than conforming to a pre-fixed job description.

3. An automatic platform-routing update mechanism where commission acceptance triggers immediate shifts in internal message routing, guild/tribe membership, role-based access control, and personalized onboarding flows without requiring manual administrative configuration by any third party.

4. A recognizable signaling pattern — specifically the opening phrase *"We Need What You're Good At"* — functioning as a semantic marker distinguishing meritocratic commission offers from standard recruitment, hiring, or contractor-engagement communications.

5. A pre-authorization model where a designated principal carries authority to grant scoped commissions without board, committee, or hiring-manager approval, with platform infrastructure completing the legal, organizational, and operational state transfer automatically upon recipient acceptance.

6. An integration architecture where Commission issuance is coordinated with a Founder Contact Dashboard that, per scheduled conversation, surfaces applicable Commission templates alongside the recipient's Red Carpet personalization, Treasure Maps, past conversation history, and external calendar synchronization — enabling live commission handoff during the conversation itself.

7. A contract-length and complexity discipline where the commission contract is intentionally kept to one-to-two signed pages, with the weight of the commitment residing in the role scope and platform-state-change semantics rather than in legalese.

### Open Design Parameters

- Expiration and renegotiation cadence for active commissions
- Escalation path from scoped commission to full Crown seat
- Commission revocation procedure and notification protocol
- Audit trail for principal-granted authorities (who was granted what, by whom, under what terms)
- Delegation: can commission-holders themselves issue sub-commissions within their scope?
- Cross-principal commissions (if/when roles beyond Founder gain commission-granting authority)

## Summary

What-If Commissions solve a workflow-critical problem for cooperative platforms: how to deploy the right person into the right scoped role **during the conversation where that person's capability is first surfaced**. The system combines pre-authored templates, fractional-commitment scope dimensions, and automatic platform-state updates to turn high-value conversations into on-the-spot deployments — without manual admin overhead, committee approval, or paperwork delay.

The opening phrase *"We Need What You're Good At"* is the canonical semantic signal. The four scope dimensions (time / domain / project / decision-level) are the canonical flexibility architecture. The automatic routing update is the canonical operational guarantee.

No prior art combines all five claim elements (pre-authoring + live activation + fractional scope + automatic platform routing + pre-authorization model) into a single coherent workflow system.
