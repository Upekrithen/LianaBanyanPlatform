## Definition

**The Spice Rack** is a content and skill taxonomy that categorizes every Spoonful, BST Episode, and Skipping Stone by its business-skill type using a culinary spice metaphor. Each spice represents a distinct skill category needed for a cooperative business to succeed. The taxonomy serves three simultaneous functions:

1. **Content grading**: Every distributable content piece gets a spice tag (Salt, Garlic, Paprika, etc.) indicating what skill domain it covers
2. **Audience filtering**: Followers can tune into the spices they care about ("show me all the Garlic Spoonfuls" = "show me the financial mechanics")
3. **Skill language**: The same spice names become the vocabulary for project skill-matching — "this project needs Garlic and Cinnamon" means "this project needs accounting and design"

## Architecture

### The Ten Spices

### Why These Ten

The ten spices map to the ten functional domains that every viable business needs:

1. Operations (Salt) — keeping things running
2. Finance (Garlic) — keeping things honest
3. Marketing (Sugar) — getting people in the door
4. Design (Cinnamon) — making it feel right
5. Legal (Pepper) — keeping it protected
6. Innovation (Ginger) — solving new problems
7. Engineering (Cumin) — building the foundation
8. Leadership (Paprika) — setting the direction
9. Creative (Basil) — telling the story
10. Coordination (Oregano) — connecting the pieces

These are not arbitrary. They map roughly to the functional areas a cooperative project needs covered. A member storefront needs Salt (operations), Garlic (pricing), Cinnamon (design), and Sugar (marketing) at minimum. A manufacturing project adds Cumin (engineering) and Pepper (compliance). The Spice Rack tells you what's in the recipe and what's still needed.

### Content Grading

Every distributable content piece gets a primary spice tag and up to two secondary tags:

```
SP-097-03 [GARLIC] [salt]
"Extraction requires the ability to increase the take rate. When the rate is hardcoded, extraction is structurally impossible."

SP-102-03 [GINGER] [cumin]
"AI agents can detect presence but cannot detect absence. The Blizzard lives in the should-have."

SP-100-05 [PAPRIKA]
"Founded by a veteran of no particular note who enlisted at sixteen."

EP-034 [PAPRIKA] [sugar]
"No problem big guy — you're still the best! I figure the way I speak to you is how I would speak to any person."
```

Primary tag is CAPS. Secondary tags are lowercase. Maximum three tags per piece.

### Audience Navigation

The Viewing Schedule (#2141) gains a spice filter:

- "Show me all GARLIC content today" → financial mechanics across all channels
- "Show me PAPRIKA on LinkedIn" → founder narrative on professional network
- "Show me GINGER anywhere" → innovation/diagnostic content wherever it posts

Followers can subscribe to spice channels. A finance professional follows Garlic. A designer follows Cinnamon. A founder follows Paprika. Each person gets a personalized content stream from the same distribution grid.

### The Stone Soup Connection

The Stone Soup fable is foundational to Liana Banyan's narrative. The Spice Rack makes it operational:

| Fable Element | Platform Equivalent |
|--------------|-------------------|
| The pot | The Project (any member's project) |
| The stone | The platform ($5/year infrastructure) |
| The water | The cooperative ecosystem (members, marketplace, currency) |
| Each villager's ingredient | Each member's skill (their spice) |
| The soup | A viable cooperative business |
| The bread | Income earned and kept at 83.3% |

"Bring your Garlic to our Pot" = "Bring your accounting skills to this project."
"This recipe needs more Cinnamon" = "This project needs a designer."
"We have plenty of Cumin but no Sugar" = "We have engineers but no marketing."

### The Little Red Hen Connection

The Little Red Hen asks: "Who will help me plant the wheat? Who will help me grind the flour? Who will help me bake the bread?"

The Spice Rack answers: "I will bring Garlic. She will bring Cinnamon. He will bring Cumin. Together we bake the bread — and everyone who contributed eats."

The LRH fable's moral (those who don't contribute don't eat) maps directly to the cooperative model (effort-based Marks, participation-gated governance). The Spice Rack names the contributions.

## Claims

**Claim 1 (Independent, System).** A computer-implemented system for content and skill classification in a cooperative platform, comprising: one or more processors and a memory storing instructions; a taxonomy store maintaining a plurality of taxonomy tags, each taxonomy tag associated with a named skill domain and a metaphor identifier; a tagging module configured to associate taxonomy tags with content items and with member profiles according to subject-matter and skill correspondence; a filter module configured to return content items or member profiles matching one or more selected taxonomy tags; and a presentation module configured to render tagged content items in a user interface using the metaphor identifiers as visual labels.

**Claim 2 (Independent, Method).** A computer-implemented method for dual-purpose taxonomy classification, the method comprising: maintaining a taxonomy of tags, each tag corresponding to both a content category and a member skill domain; assigning taxonomy tags to content items distributed via one or more channels; assigning taxonomy tags to member profiles based on declared or measured skills; and returning, in response to a query specifying one or more taxonomy tags, both content items and member profiles sharing the specified tags such that a single taxonomy vocabulary serves content discovery and member-project matching.

**Claim 3.** The system of claim 1, wherein the plurality of taxonomy tags comprises exactly ten tags corresponding to ten functional business-skill domains including at least operations, finance, marketing, design, legal, innovation, engineering, leadership, creative, and project coordination.

**Claim 4.** The system of claim 1, wherein the tagging module is configured to permit a member profile or content item to carry multiple taxonomy tags with associated weights indicating degree of match.

**Claim 5.** The method of claim 2, further comprising aggregating taxonomy tag frequency across content items to generate a skill-emphasis report for the cooperative platform.

**Claim 6.** The method of claim 2, wherein the metaphor identifiers comprise culinary spice names that serve as shared vocabulary for both content filtering and project skill declarations.
