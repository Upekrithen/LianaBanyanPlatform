## DESCRIPTION

A formal architectural doctrine comprising 10 canonical rules that are codified directly into the component library, design token system, and page templates of a cooperative platform. These rules are not guidelines or style suggestions — they are enforced through component constraints, token values, and architectural patterns that make violations structurally difficult.

### The 10 Rules

1. **FocusShell = public/conversion** (hero owns viewport, no sidebar)
2. **AppShell = member workspaces** (sidebar, operational density)
3. **Hero pattern**: eyebrow, headline, body, primary CTA, secondary CTA, proof strip
4. **Never SaaS language**: no "upgrade/premium/unlock features"
5. **Progressive disclosure**: preview, recommend action, drill-down
6. **One dominant focal element per viewport**
7. **Mobile-first**: sticky bottom CTA after first scroll
8. **Informative locks**: "Members can respond, launch, and transact here" (never punitive)
9. **No red for negative states**: amber for overdue, "Room to grow" not "Declining"
10. **83.3% / Cost+20% = trust anchor**, not promotional flourish (hero + one transparency panel only)

Plus two securities-compliance rules enforced across all copy:
- No equity/shares/dividends/ROI/invest language
- "Liana Banyan CORPORATION" / "Founder & General Manager" / no demographic intake

## MECHANISM

1. Design tokens in `v2-tokens.css` encode cooperative values: amber (not red) for attention states, trust-anchor typography for economic figures.
2. Component primitives (`InformativeLock`, `ProofStrip`, `HeroSection`) enforce doctrine by construction — the InformativeLock component physically cannot render "upgrade" or "unlock" language.
3. Shell selection (FocusShell vs AppShell) prevents topology violations.
4. Hero pattern components enforce the eyebrow/headline/body/CTA/proof-strip order.
5. The doctrine README at `src/components/v2/README.md` serves as the canonical reference, with each rule traceable to specific components and tokens.

## PRIOR ART ASSESSMENT

**No known prior art for principle-driven architecture in cooperative platforms.**

Design systems (Material Design, Ant Design, Carbon) encode visual consistency but not ethical/economic principles. No known design system enforces rules like "no SaaS language," "no red for negative states," or "trust anchor positioning" through component constraints. The combination of cooperative economic principles with UI architecture governance is novel.

## FORMAL CLAIMS

1. A design governance system for a cooperative platform comprising a set of canonical rules encoded as component constraints, design tokens, and architectural patterns, wherein the rules enforce cooperative economic principles — including anti-SaaS language restrictions, non-punitive access messaging, and trust-anchor positioning — through structural constraints that make violations architecturally difficult.

2. A method of enforcing cooperative platform values through a component library wherein lock components are constrained to render informative cooperative messaging rather than punitive paywall language, attention-state colors exclude red in favor of neutral tones, and economic trust figures are architecturally positioned as anchors rather than promotional elements.

3. A cooperative platform design system comprising a doctrine document, design tokens, and component primitives that together enforce at least five distinct categories of cooperative governance rules — topology separation, language constraints, progressive disclosure patterns, attention-state color governance, and economic figure positioning — through component-level structural constraints rather than guidelines.
