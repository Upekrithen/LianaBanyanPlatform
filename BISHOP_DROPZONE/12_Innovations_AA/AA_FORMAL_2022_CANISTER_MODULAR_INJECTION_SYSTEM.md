### Innovation #2022 — The Canister System

**Category:** Decentralized Manufacturing — Tooling Infrastructure
**Origin:** Founder's direct experience with lithographic mold-making for HexIsle terrain pieces

---

### Problem Statement

Current small-scale injection molding requires:
- Custom-built containment for every mold (Lego walls, putty seals, weighted boards)
- Oversized containment relative to part size (a small part gets a 10x board)
- Complete teardown and rebuild between runs
- Hydraulic or pneumatic pressure systems for injection
- No standardization between different part sizes

The Founder's direct experience: building containment boxes from Lego walls, sealing with putty, clamping with oversized boards and weights, then starting from scratch for every new part. Inefficient, non-repeatable, wasteful of time and materials.

---

### Solution: The Canister System

A modular, stackable, gravity-pressure injection molding system built from 3D-printed components. No hydraulics. No custom containment. Standardized sizes. Infinitely stackable.

---

### Component Architecture

#### 1. A/B Mold Canisters (The Core Unit)
- **Shape:** Cylindrical, like soup cans — available in multiple standard diameters (small/medium/large/XL)
- **A canister:** Twice as long as B canister. A slides OVER B like a telescoping sleeve.
- **Keying mechanism:** Hexagonal vertex system
  - 3 vertices have GROOVES (female)
  - 3 alternating vertices have BARS (male)
  - A and B are keyed so A's grooves match B's bars and vice versa
- **Locking:** A slides over B, then the assembly is inserted into the Sleeve and twisted 60° to lock
- **Cavity:** The mold cavity sits in the center between A and B, where the part forms
- **Material:** 3D printed (SLA for precision, SLS for durability) — the molds themselves ARE the canisters

#### 2. Sprue System (Bottom of B / Top of A)
- **Bottom of B:** Half-sphere HOLLOW with a sprue path leading to the central cavity
- **Half-sphere WHOLE:** A separate piece that fits "like a glove" into the hollow of B
  - Has a tube through its center connecting to the piston/pressure area below
  - This is the injection point — melted plastic flows UP through the tube, into the sprue, into the cavity
- **Top of A (outside):** IDENTICAL to the half-sphere WHOLE
  - This means another A/B canister can stack on top, connecting via the same sprue interface
  - The chain continues vertically: plastic flows from the base up through every canister in the stack

#### 3. The Sleeve (Clamping System)
- Cylindrical sleeve that receives the locked A/B canister assembly
- A/B locks into the sleeve via twist mechanism
- Sleeves lock to each other vertically (for stacking multiple A/B pairs)
- Sleeves can also be adjacent to each other horizontally (for parallel injection)

#### 4. The Base (Pressure System)
- Larger-diameter containing sleeve at the bottom of the stack
- Contains the melted plastic (or slurry, salt, resin — any injectable material)
- **Pressure mechanism:** A weighted piston pushes DOWN on the material
  - The weight above creates downward force
  - Material is forced UP through the sprue system into all canisters in the stack
- **Vacuum assist:** The top of the sleeve stack is capped with a dual-diameter cap (larger OD + smaller ID)
  - The cap doesn't move
  - As the weighted piston moves down, air is pulled OUT from the top
  - This creates negative pressure above the material, ASSISTING the positive pressure from below
  - Dual pressure: gravity push from below + vacuum pull from above

#### 5. The Cap (Top Seal)
- Larger diameter portion seals the top of the outermost sleeve
- Smaller diameter portion extends into the sleeve to maintain alignment
- Stationary while the piston moves — provides the reaction surface for vacuum generation
- Air exits through a controlled vent (one-way valve or bleed hole)

---

### Operating Procedure

1. **LOAD:** Insert 3D-printed A/B mold pair with part cavity
2. **BIND:** Slide A over B, insert into sleeve, twist 60° to lock
3. **STACK (optional):** Add more A/B pairs on top, lock sleeves together
4. **INJECT:** Place melted material in base, apply weight to piston
5. **WAIT:** Material flows up through sprue system into all cavities; gravity + vacuum do the work
6. **UNBIND:** Twist 60° to unlock, separate A from B
7. **RELEASE:** Remove finished part(s)
8. **REPEAT:** Reload same molds or swap in different A/B pairs

### Cooperative Significance

The Canister System is a PRODUCT that the cooperative can manufacture and sell. It's also INFRASTRUCTURE that cooperative makers can use to produce parts without expensive equipment. This is the Decentralized Factory Node (#1939) made physical:

- A maker buys a Canister System starter kit (sleeves, caps, base, weights)
- They 3D print custom A/B mold pairs for their specific parts
- They inject materials using gravity pressure — no hydraulics, no compressor, no expensive equipment
- They can scale by stacking more canisters or running parallel sleeves
- The cooperative can sell standard A/B mold pairs for common HexIsle terrain pieces

**This turns every member with a 3D printer into a potential injection mold operator.**
