[SUBSTANCE PARA 1 — Founder voice. Structural argument: cooperative-substrate as architectural choice complementing public-purpose function rather than competing with it. The "complement, not replace" beat is the load-bearing pre-emption of Mazzucato's debate-prone instinct.]

Liana Banyan is a cooperative platform with a Cost+20% margin — locked structurally in the operating agreement, not by policy choice that a future board could revise. Creators retain 83.3% of every transaction. The platform's IP allocation runs 60/20/10/10 across patent buckets / founder-creator allocation / global sponsor pool / individual creator pedestals, with every innovation filed under the Cooperative Defensive Patent Pledge — not gatekept, not enclosed, licensed for cooperative use by structure.

What we are *not* claiming, and what bears stating directly because your prior published work makes it the natural debate to have: the cooperative-substrate is not a substitute for state public-purpose function. *The Entrepreneurial State* and *Mission Economy* operate at the level of *who organizes the foundational risk-bearing investment* — and the answer at that layer has been, empirically and durably, the state. The cooperative-substrate operates at a different layer: *who captures the value that the foundational investment generates downstream, after the risk-bearing phase*. The two are architecturally complementary, not substitutional. The state bears the foundational risk; the cooperative-substrate ensures the value flowing from that public investment lands with the workers who do the downstream work, rather than with extractive platform intermediaries who happen to sit between the state-funded substrate and the worker who uses it.

[SUBSTANCE PARA 2 — Founder voice. Empirical anchor — the K528 cross-architecture benchmark + K533 verification harness make the architectural claim falsifiable.]

The architectural claim is empirically falsifiable, and the harness above is how. The K528 cross-architecture benchmark (1,170 graded responses across 16 vendor and architecture conditions, 200 sealed questions across six knowledge categories, $206.33 vendor API spend) found that the same indexed-retrieval substrate, with six different language models behind it, produces accuracy scores clustering within three percentage points across the models at a 180-times cost spread. The architectural property: *index coverage is the binding constraint on retrieval quality, not model capability*. Same substrate, different models, equivalent retrieval — at compute-cost-per-correct-answer 76× lower than the cheapest vendor-native AI memory product. That finding is replicable on your machine, your corpus, your vendor keys, in five minutes for fifty cents (vendor API). The harness doesn't ask you to trust us; it verifies architecturally.
