## §0 PREAMBLE

**0.1 Purpose**
This term sheet sets out the proposed structure for a Regional Co-Founder
relationship between Liana Banyan Corporation ("LB Corp"), the cooperative
entity it anchors ("the Cooperative"), and an individual accepting a Regional
Co-Founder designation ("Regional Co-Founder" or "RCF").

**0.2 Draft Status**
This is a DRAFT document authored for Founder review at BP089
(session date 2026-06-20). No version of this document should be transmitted
to any prospective Regional Co-Founder until:

  (a) The Founder has reviewed and ratified the final version.
  (b) An attorney licensed in the governing jurisdiction has reviewed it.
  (c) A version appropriate to the specific recipient's region and circumstances
      has been prepared.

**0.3 Background: What Liana Banyan Cooperative Is**
Liana Banyan Cooperative is a worker-builder-creator cooperative structured
to return economic value to its members rather than to external shareholders.
Its central product is the Cooperative Substrate: a persistent, member-owned
AI-memory and peer-coordination layer that cures the AI amnesia problem
(context lost at every conversation boundary) and replaces extractive
platform economics (SaaS subscriptions, 30% delivery-app take, advertising)
with Cost+20% cooperative margins that keep 83.3% of transaction value with
the worker, builder, or creator who did the work.

The Cooperative is governed cooperatively. It is not a startup seeking
venture-capital exit. It is built to run indefinitely as a member-owned
institution.

**0.4 Why Regional Co-Founders**
The Cooperative's twelve-city go-to-market structure requires trusted,
operationally capable leaders in each geographic region. A Regional Co-Founder
is not a franchisee and not an employee. They are a founding partner in the
growth of the Cooperative within their region, compensated through a share
of the Founder's patent royalty portfolio and through the cooperative
profit-sharing structure, not through salary.

**0.5 Definitions Used Herein**

- "LB Corp" means Liana Banyan Corporation, a Wyoming corporation.
- "Cooperative" means the Liana Banyan Cooperative entity, the member-owned
  institution anchored by LB Corp.
- "Founder" means the founder and General Manager of LB Corp and the Cooperative.
- "Regional Co-Founder" or "RCF" means the individual executing this agreement
  in the role described herein.
- "Region" means the geographic area assigned to the RCF, as specified in
  the executed agreement.
- "Substrate Market" means the Cooperative's peer-to-peer commerce and
  coordination layer operating at Cost+20% margins.
- "Sweet 16" means the sixteen founding initiatives of the Cooperative,
  including but not limited to Let's Make Dinner, Let's Get Groceries,
  Larder, Family Table, and the LB Card.
- "Pledge #2260" means the Cooperative's founding patent pledge, to which
  all Co-Founders are signatories.
- "PROV Portfolio" means the Founder's international provisional patent
  portfolio, currently comprising PROV_22, PROV_23, PROV_24, and subsequent
  filings, covering 300+ countries under PCT/international treaty.

## §1 ROLE DEFINITION

**1.1 Title**
Regional Co-Founder of Liana Banyan Cooperative for [REGION].

**1.2 Nature of the Role**
The Regional Co-Founder is a founding partner, not an employee, contractor,
or franchisee. The RCF operates with significant autonomy within the
cooperative governance structure. The RCF is accountable to the Cooperative's
members in their region and to the Founder in matters of cooperative-wide
strategy and canonical governance.

**1.3 Regional Scope**
The RCF's operational mandate covers [REGION], defined as [to be specified
in the executed agreement]. Regional scope may be adjusted by mutual
written agreement. No exclusivity is implied unless explicitly stated in
the executed agreement.

**1.4 Relationship to Cooperative Governance**
The RCF holds a Director seat on the Cooperative's regional governing
body (see §3). The RCF participates in cooperative-wide governance
votes on matters that affect all regions, weighted per cooperative bylaws.

**1.5 Relationship to Founder**
The RCF reports to the Founder on strategic matters and maintains a
monthly cadence with the Founder (see §5.3). The RCF is not subordinate
to the Founder in day-to-day operational decisions within their region;
the cooperative governance structure governs those decisions.

**1.6 Plurality**
Multiple Regional Co-Founders may be appointed, each for a distinct
region. This term sheet governs each individual RCF relationship.
Co-Founders in different regions are peers, not competitors. The
Cooperative is designed for inter-regional cooperation, not rivalry.

## §2 PATENT ROYALTY STAKE

**2.1 Carve-Out Structure**
The Founder has carved out five percent (5%) of the total royalty interest
in the PROV Portfolio for distribution to Regional Co-Founders, Crown
holders, and other strategic partners ("the Co-Founder Pool"). This
carve-out is separate from the Founder's retained interest and from the
Cooperative's institutional interest in the portfolio.

**2.2 Individual Stake**
The RCF shall receive a royalty stake of between 0.5% and 1.0% of the
PROV Portfolio's total royalty interest, allocated from the Co-Founder
Pool. The specific percentage shall be negotiated and specified in the
executed agreement based on region size, strategic importance, and the
RCF's prior contributions.

For the avoidance of doubt: a 1.0% stake represents 1.0% of the entire
PROV Portfolio royalty stream, not 1.0% of the 5% Co-Founder Pool.

**2.3 Vesting Schedule**
The RCF's patent royalty stake vests over four (4) years from the
Commencement Date, with the following schedule:

  - Months 1-12: No vesting (the "Cliff").
  - Month 13: 25% of the total stake vests at the Cliff.
  - Months 14-48: The remaining 75% vests in equal monthly installments
    (approximately 2.08% of the total stake per month).

**2.4 Cliff Rationale**
The one-year cliff is designed to confirm mutual fit before any royalty
interest transfers. The RCF who departs or is terminated for cause before
the one-year anniversary vests nothing. The cliff is waived in the
circumstances described in §8.3 (involuntary departure not-for-cause).

**2.5 Milestone Accelerators**
In addition to time-based vesting, the following milestones each
accelerate vesting by six (6) months (meaning six months of unvested
stake vests immediately upon achievement):

  - Milestone A: [X] member-businesses live and transacting on the
    Substrate Market within the Region. (X to be specified in the
    executed agreement.)
  - Milestone B: [Y] individual members enrolled in the Cooperative
    within the Region. (Y to be specified in the executed agreement.)
  - Milestone C: [Z] restaurants live on the Substrate Market within
    the Region, with at least five items listed at Cost+20% pricing
    per the canonical food-node pricing structure. (Z to be specified
    in the executed agreement.)
  - Milestone D: Three (3) active Let's Make Dinner chapters operating
    within the Region, each with at least one confirmed cook and one
    confirmed order in the prior calendar month.

No single milestone accelerator shall cause total vested percentage to
exceed what would have been vested under a fully accelerated three-year
schedule. Milestones may be achieved in any order. Achieving all four
milestones results in a maximum of twenty-four (24) months of acceleration.

**2.6 Royalty Payment Mechanics**
Royalty payments shall be made to the RCF according to the payment
schedule established in the PROV Portfolio licensing agreements, as those
agreements are executed with licensees. The RCF understands that royalty
income is contingent on the Portfolio being licensed and on licensees
making payments. LB Corp makes no guarantee of royalty income amount
or timing.

**2.7 Tax Treatment**
The RCF is responsible for all tax obligations arising from receipt of
the patent royalty stake and any royalty payments. LB Corp will provide
documentation as required by law. This document does not constitute tax
advice; the RCF should consult qualified tax counsel.

**2.8 Transfer Restrictions**
The patent royalty stake is personal to the RCF and may not be sold,
assigned, pledged, or transferred without prior written consent of the
Founder. Unvested stake is non-transferable. Vested stake may be
transferred to a revocable living trust or similar estate-planning vehicle
with written notice to LB Corp.

**2.9 Dilution**
The Co-Founder Pool is fixed at 5% of the PROV Portfolio royalty interest.
Appointment of additional Regional Co-Founders does not dilute existing
RCF stakes; additional appointments draw from the remaining unallocated
portion of the pool. Once the pool is exhausted, no further stakes shall
be allocated without Founder ratification and written amendment.

## §3 COOPERATIVE DIRECTOR SEAT

**3.1 Director Designation**
The RCF shall serve as a Director of the Cooperative's Regional Governing
Body for [REGION] during the term of this agreement.

**3.2 Voting Rights**
As a Director, the RCF holds one (1) governance vote on regional matters
per the Cooperative's bylaws. On cooperative-wide matters put to a
full Director vote, the RCF holds one (1) vote weighted per the
Cooperative's inter-regional governance protocol (to be established
in the Cooperative's final bylaws prior to execution).

**3.3 Profit-Share Participation**
The RCF participates in the regional Cost+20% margin pool for transactions
originating within their Region, per the Cooperative's bylaws and the
margin-distribution schedule to be specified in those bylaws. This
profit-share is in addition to the patent royalty stake.

The Cost+20% margin structure means the Cooperative retains a margin
of at least 20% above cost on all Substrate Market transactions within
the region. A portion of that margin is distributed to regional Directors
per the distribution schedule. The specific percentage allocated to the
RCF director profit-share shall be specified in the Cooperative's
regional bylaws supplement.

**3.4 Director Duties**
As a Director, the RCF owes fiduciary duties to the Cooperative's
members within their region, including duties of care and loyalty.
The RCF must act in the interest of regional members, not solely in
their own personal interest.

**3.5 Director Term**
The Director seat is co-terminus with this agreement. Upon termination
of this agreement (see §8), the RCF's Director seat and associated
voting rights terminate simultaneously.

## §4 RESPONSIBILITIES

**4.1 Sweet 16 Initiative Ownership**
The RCF has primary responsibility for the regional implementation of
the Cooperative's Sweet 16 initiatives. This includes:

  - Identifying and recruiting restaurants and food merchants to the
    Substrate Market for the Let's Make Dinner initiative.
  - Recruiting and supporting home cooks for LMD chapter formation.
  - Coordinating with the Founder on rollout sequencing and timing.
  - Reporting regional progress at the monthly cadence.

**4.2 Restaurant and Merchant Onboarding**
The RCF shall lead the onboarding of restaurants and merchants to the
Substrate Market within their Region. This includes:

  - Presenting the Cooperative model to prospective food-node members.
  - Coordinating with LB Corp's technical team on Stripe Connect Express
    setup for new merchants (application fee = 16.7%; merchant retains
    83.3%).
  - Ensuring merchants understand the Cost+20% pricing structure and
    the canonical food-node pricing rails (Tab-class and Kit-F-class).
  - Following up on merchant activation and first-transaction completion.

**4.3 Member Recruitment**
The RCF shall recruit individual members to the Cooperative within
their Region. This includes:

  - Representing the Cooperative at community events, gaming gatherings,
    Discord and Reddit community bridge opportunities, and other
    appropriate venues.
  - Maintaining an accurate count of enrolled members for milestone
    tracking purposes.
  - Supporting member onboarding and first-transaction completion.

**4.4 Chapter Formation**
The RCF shall lead the formation of Cooperative chapters within their
Region. This includes:

  - Identifying anchor community partners (gaming communities, Discord
    servers, Reddit subreddits, neighborhood associations, and similar).
  - Coordinating chapter launch per the Cooperative's chapter-formation
    protocol.
  - Supporting CREW formation within chapters for Bounty Wall work.
  - Maintaining chapter health reporting at the monthly cadence.

**4.5 Regional PR and Press**
The RCF serves as the Cooperative's primary public face in their Region.
This includes:

  - Conducting press interviews and media appearances on behalf of the
    Cooperative in the Region.
  - Representing the Cooperative at regional conferences, panels, and
    public events.
  - Coordinating with the Founder on any press coverage that reaches
    beyond the Region (national or international outlets).
  - Adhering to canonical messaging and branding at all times.
    The RCF shall not make statements about the Cooperative's technology,
    economics, or governance that contradict canonical eblets without
    prior Founder approval.

**4.6 Reporting**
The RCF shall provide:

  - Monthly written status reports at the monthly cadence with the Founder.
  - Milestone tracking documentation when milestones are achieved.
  - Annual review participation per §5.4.

**4.7 Canonical Compliance**
The RCF shall familiarize themselves with the Cooperative's canonical
governance documents, including the closing liturgy, the Cost+20%
margin structure, the Substitution Rail taxonomy, and other foundational
documents as provided by the Founder. The RCF shall represent the
Cooperative accurately and shall not improvise governance positions.

## §5 COMMITMENT

**5.1 Time Commitment**
The RCF commits to a minimum of twenty (20) hours per week dedicated
to Regional Co-Founder responsibilities. This is a substantial commitment
and the Founder asks prospective RCFs to be honest with themselves
about their capacity before executing this agreement. The role is not
suitable for a person who cannot reliably provide this time.

**5.2 Term**
The initial term of this agreement is four (4) years from the
Commencement Date ("Initial Term"). At the conclusion of the Initial Term,
the parties may renew by mutual written agreement for successive one-year
terms or for such other period as the parties agree.

**5.3 Monthly Cadence**
The RCF and Founder shall meet (in person, by video, or by phone) at
least once per calendar month throughout the term. The Founder may
designate an alternate point of contact for specific operational
matters, but the monthly principal-to-principal cadence is a material
term of this agreement.

**5.4 Annual Review**
The parties shall conduct a formal annual review of the RCF's regional
progress, milestone achievement, and the health of the relationship.
The annual review is an opportunity to adjust targets, discuss
challenges, and confirm mutual commitment to the next year of the term.

**5.5 Good Faith**
The RCF commits to act in good faith toward the Cooperative, its members,
and the Founder at all times. Good faith includes honest reporting,
candid communication of concerns, and proactive escalation of problems
rather than concealment.

## §6 COMPENSATION

**6.1 No Salary**
The Regional Co-Founder role carries no salary. LB Corp will not pay the
RCF wages, hourly compensation, or guaranteed periodic cash payments.
The Founder is transparent: this is a founder role, not an employment role.
The compensation is equity-like (patent royalty stake) and cooperative
profit-share, both of which are contingent on the Cooperative's success.

**6.2 Patent Royalty Stake**
The RCF's primary compensation vehicle is the patent royalty stake
described in §2. The stake is meaningful and the Founder believes it
represents significant potential value as the PROV Portfolio is licensed
internationally. However, it is illiquid, vesting-contingent, and
dependent on market outcomes the Founder cannot guarantee.

**6.3 Cooperative Profit-Share**
The RCF participates in the regional Cost+20% margin pool as described
in §3.3. This profit-share grows as regional transaction volume grows.
In the early period (Year 1), this profit-share may be minimal. The
Founder is honest about this. The expectation is that the profit-share
becomes meaningful as the region reaches operational scale.

**6.4 Expenses**
Reasonable, documented out-of-pocket expenses incurred in the performance
of Regional Co-Founder duties shall be reimbursed per an expense policy
to be agreed in writing between the parties. The RCF shall obtain prior
written approval for any single expense exceeding [THRESHOLD to be specified
in the executed agreement].

**6.5 No Benefits**
The RCF is not an employee and is not entitled to employee benefits,
including but not limited to health insurance, retirement contributions,
paid leave, or workers' compensation. The RCF is responsible for their
own benefits, insurance, and retirement planning.

**6.6 Transparency Acknowledgment**
By executing this agreement, the RCF acknowledges that they have read
and understood the compensation structure, that they are not entering
this role with the expectation of salary or guaranteed income, and that
they accept the contingent nature of the compensation in exchange for
the co-founding opportunity and the patent royalty stake.

## §8 TERMINATION

**8.1 Termination for Cause**
LB Corp may terminate this agreement immediately, without notice, for cause.
"Cause" includes but is not limited to:

  (a) Material breach of this agreement that remains uncured for thirty
      (30) days after written notice.
  (b) Fraud, dishonesty, or willful misconduct in the performance of
      Regional Co-Founder duties.
  (c) Conviction of a felony or crime involving moral turpitude.
  (d) Material violation of the Cooperative's canonical governance
      documents or Code of Conduct.
  (e) Breach of the Non-Compete provisions of §10.
  (f) Breach of the Confidentiality provisions of §11.

Upon termination for cause, all unvested patent royalty stake is
forfeited. Vested stake is retained.

**8.2 Termination by Mutual Agreement**
The parties may terminate this agreement by mutual written agreement
at any time. The terms of any such mutual termination, including
vested stake treatment and transition responsibilities, shall be
specified in the written termination agreement.

**8.3 Involuntary Departure Not-For-Cause**
If LB Corp terminates this agreement without cause (i.e., not for any
of the reasons listed in §8.1), the following acceleration applies:

  - All unvested patent royalty stake accelerates and vests immediately
    upon the effective date of termination.
  - The RCF retains their profit-share entitlement through the end of
    the calendar quarter in which termination occurs.
  - The one-year cliff described in §2.3 is waived; if the termination
    occurs before the twelve-month anniversary, the RCF vests the
    full stake as if the cliff had been satisfied.

This provision is designed to protect the RCF from arbitrary removal
after they have committed time and effort to the Region.

**8.4 Voluntary Departure**
If the RCF resigns voluntarily before the end of the Initial Term,
vesting stops as of the effective date of resignation. No acceleration
applies. Vested stake is retained. Unvested stake is forfeited.

**8.5 Transition Obligations**
Upon termination for any reason, the RCF shall:

  (a) Provide a written transition memorandum summarizing the status
      of all active regional initiatives, member relationships, and
      merchant relationships within thirty (30) days of the effective
      date of termination.
  (b) Return all LB Corp confidential information and materials.
  (c) Cooperate in the transition of their responsibilities to a
      successor Regional Co-Founder or to the Founder.
  (d) Remain bound by §10 (Non-Compete) and §11 (Confidentiality)
      for the periods specified therein after termination.

**8.6 Director Seat**
The RCF's Cooperative Director seat terminates simultaneously with
this agreement regardless of the reason for termination.

## §10 NON-COMPETE

**10.1 Scope**
During the term of this agreement and for twelve (12) months following
its termination, the RCF shall not:

  (a) Found, co-found, or serve as a principal (including as a director,
      officer, general partner, or controlling shareholder) of any
      entity that operates a cooperative substrate platform -- meaning
      a member-owned, AI-assisted peer coordination and commerce platform
      -- that directly competes with the Cooperative in the Region.

**10.2 What Is NOT Restricted**
The Non-Compete does not restrict the RCF from:

  (a) Owning, operating, or participating in any non-substrate business,
      regardless of industry.
  (b) Serving as an employee, contractor, or advisor of any non-substrate
      business.
  (c) Starting or joining a traditional SaaS company, a conventional
      cooperative (credit union, housing cooperative, food cooperative,
      worker cooperative) that does not operate a substrate platform,
      or any other business that does not directly replicate the
      Cooperative's substrate architecture.
  (d) Writing, speaking, or publishing about cooperative economics,
      AI, or related subjects, provided such activity does not disclose
      LB Corp confidential information in violation of §11.

**10.3 Rationale**
The Non-Compete is deliberately narrow. The Founder believes the
Cooperative is better served by a culture of openness than by
aggressive non-compete enforcement. The restriction is limited to
the specific harm of a departing RCF directly replicating the
Cooperative's substrate architecture in the same region.

**10.4 Geographic Scope**
The Non-Compete applies within the Region. It does not apply to activity
outside the Region.

**10.5 Reasonableness**
If any court of competent jurisdiction finds any provision of this
section to be unenforceable in whole or in part, such court is
authorized to modify the provision to the minimum extent necessary
to make it enforceable, and the remaining provisions shall remain
in full force and effect.

## §11 CONFIDENTIALITY

**11.1 Definition of Confidential Information**
"Confidential Information" means all non-public information disclosed
by LB Corp or the Cooperative to the RCF in connection with this
agreement, including but not limited to:

  (a) The contents of the PROV Portfolio prior to public filing.
  (b) Member data, merchant data, and transaction data.
  (c) Technical specifications of the Substrate.
  (d) Financial information, including revenue, margins, and cost data.
  (e) Strategic plans, roadmaps, and initiative sequencing.
  (f) The terms of this agreement itself.
  (g) Crown letter recipients and the terms of any Crown letter.
  (h) Any information designated in writing as confidential at the
      time of disclosure.

**11.2 Obligations**
The RCF shall:

  (a) Hold all Confidential Information in strict confidence.
  (b) Not disclose Confidential Information to any third party without
      prior written consent of the Founder.
  (c) Use Confidential Information only in the performance of Regional
      Co-Founder duties.
  (d) Protect Confidential Information using at least the same degree
      of care the RCF uses to protect their own confidential information,
      and in no event less than reasonable care.

**11.3 Permitted Disclosures**
The obligations of §11.2 do not apply to information that:

  (a) Is or becomes publicly known through no breach of this agreement
      by the RCF.
  (b) Was known to the RCF prior to disclosure by LB Corp, as evidenced
      by written records predating disclosure.
  (c) Is independently developed by the RCF without use of Confidential
      Information.
  (d) Is disclosed with prior written consent of the Founder.
  (e) Is required to be disclosed by law, regulation, or court order,
      provided the RCF gives LB Corp prompt written notice and cooperates
      in seeking a protective order.

**11.4 Duration**
Confidentiality obligations under this section survive termination of
this agreement for a period of five (5) years. Obligations with respect
to trade secrets survive indefinitely.

**11.5 Return of Materials**
Upon termination of this agreement, the RCF shall promptly return or
destroy (and certify destruction of) all materials containing
Confidential Information, including all copies in any medium.

## §12 GOVERNING LAW AND DISPUTE RESOLUTION

**12.1 Governing Law**
This agreement shall be governed by and construed in accordance with
the laws of the State of Wyoming, without regard to conflict-of-law
principles.

**12.2 Incorporation State**
LB Corp is incorporated in the State of Wyoming. Wyoming has been
selected as the governing law state because it is the state of LB Corp's
incorporation and because Wyoming law is generally favorable to
cooperative and entrepreneurial structures.

**12.3 Dispute Resolution**
Any dispute arising out of or related to this agreement shall be
resolved first through good-faith negotiation between the parties.
If negotiation fails to resolve the dispute within thirty (30) days,
the parties agree to submit the dispute to non-binding mediation
before a mutually agreed mediator. If mediation fails, the parties
agree to submit to binding arbitration under the rules of the American
Arbitration Association, with the arbitration to be conducted in
[CITY, Wyoming] or by remote means as the parties agree.

**12.4 Costs**
Each party shall bear its own legal fees and costs in any dispute
resolution proceeding unless the arbitrator awards fees and costs
to the prevailing party as part of the arbitration award.

**12.5 Injunctive Relief**
Notwithstanding §12.3, either party may seek injunctive or other
equitable relief in a court of competent jurisdiction to prevent
irreparable harm, including but not limited to breach of §11
(Confidentiality) or §7 (IP Assignment).
