## § 2 — The 300-Plan Structure

The Pay-It-Forward 300 is a founding cohort of exactly 300 members who:

1. **Pay the standard membership fee**: $5/year — same as every other member. The 300 designation is not purchased; it is earned through relationship and vouching.

2. **Receive a cooperative IP allocation**:
   - 20% of every future patent filed by the Founder becomes co-owned by the 300 (split equally: ~1/1,500 each)
   - 5% Joules allocation from Founder's existing pool
   - Perpetual. Survives the Founder. Binding on the Founder's estate.

3. **Carry the recursive obligation**: If a PF300 member brings others into cooperative arrangements, they give the same deal (20% future patents + 5% Joules) to their own 300. Same deal, all the way down, forever. No decay at any layer.

4. **Form the critical mass for platform launch**: 300 members, even at $5/year, generates $1,500/year in base revenue. More importantly, 300 engaged founding members creates the human substrate for Captain recruitment — the tier that actually leads cooperative communities at the local level.

**The cold-start solution**: The Founding Circle of 300 pre-funds the AI infrastructure through the scale-up period, reaching the "inversion point" described in the Sipping Tea paper before the platform opens to the public.

## § 3 — The Moses + 70 Elders Governance Model

The structural connection is explicit and canon-worthy:

**Biblical precedent (Numbers 11:16-17, 24-25):**
> *"And the Lord said unto Moses, Gather unto me seventy men of the elders of Israel... and I will take of the spirit which is upon thee, and will put it upon them; and they shall bear the burden of the people with thee, that thou bear it not thyself alone."*

**The Moses pattern:**
- Moses alone cannot govern a multitude
- God instructs him to select 70 elders to whom authority is distributed
- The 70 carry the burden WITH Moses, not FOR Moses
- The authority flows from Moses → 70 → the camps (the broader community)

**The 300 specifically:**
The Book of Judges references Gideon's 300 (Judges 7) — the reduced force that achieved cooperative victory through quality over quantity. The PF300 draws on BOTH governance models simultaneously:
- Moses's 70 for the *governance layer* (Captains)
- Gideon's 300 for the *founding force* that achieves critical mass before broader deployment

The 300 is not arbitrary. It is the number at which cooperative critical mass becomes self-sustaining without external capital. Below 300, the platform needs the Founder's own capital to operate. At 300+, the distributed contributions of the founding cohort cover operations, and every new member after that reduces per-capita cost.

## § 4 — Connection to the Captain System

The Captain governance architecture (A&A Formals #1963–1966) is the operational expression of the Moses 70-elders model:

- Captains are community leaders who manage local cooperative units
- They are nominated by community activity, not appointed by headquarters
- Their authority derives from the community they serve, distributed from the Founder
- The Founders' role: set the rules, protect the architecture, get out of the way

The PF300 members are the pre-Captain cohort — the people who receive the founding IP allocation because they are trusted enough to carry the recursive obligation forward. Many PF300 members will become Captains. Not all Captains need to be PF300 members. The distinction matters: PF300 is about founding IP ownership; Captains are about community governance. They compose but are not identical.
