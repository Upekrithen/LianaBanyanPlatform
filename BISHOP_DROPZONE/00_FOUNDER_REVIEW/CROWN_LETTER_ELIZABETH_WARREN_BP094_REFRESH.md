**The problem you identified is real and getting worse.**

Data centers built to run frontier AI models draw extraordinary power. They consume cooling water that surrounding communities cannot afford to lose. They drive up home energy costs in exactly the neighborhoods that are least able to absorb the increase. They face permitting battles from municipal governments who understand the infrastructure burden but feel economic pressure to approve anyway. And they are being proposed at a scale -- new nuclear plant partnerships, municipal water rights applications, transmission line expansions -- that will lock in energy commitments for the next thirty years.

A levy on data center energy consumption addresses this problem by making it more expensive to build and operate these facilities. It does not prevent new data centers from being built. It does not eliminate the water consumption, the grid strain, or the municipal infrastructure costs. It taxes the harm after the harm is done. That is a reasonable policy response. But there is a better one.

**Substrate Replaces New Data Centers.**

This is not a theoretical claim. The mechanism is this: existing consumer hardware -- laptops, desktops, phones, small servers -- is already on, already cooled by its existing thermal envelope, already connected to the grid through existing residential and commercial service. When AI inference is routed through cooperative substrate rather than centralized mega-scale compute, it uses hardware that would otherwise be idle. No new data center footprint required. No new water rights application. No new nuclear plant permitting. No new municipal opposition. Zero new capital expenditure on infrastructure that will draw power for thirty years.
