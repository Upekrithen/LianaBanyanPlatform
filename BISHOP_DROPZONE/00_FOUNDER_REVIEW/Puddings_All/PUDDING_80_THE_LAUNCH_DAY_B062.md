# Pudding #80 — The Launch Day

*What happens when you send 100 letters to people who have never heard of you — and why you do it anyway.*

---

April 2, 2026. Opening Gambit fires.

Fifteen Crown Letters go out first. Warren Buffett. Mackenzie Scott. Sal Khan. Craig Newmark. Peter Seibel. Bret Simon. Dale Dougherty. John Glenn. Roderick Williams. Tim Kaiser. Trebor Scholz. Olaf Scholz. And others whose names the cooperative values enough to write to individually, by hand, one at a time.

None of these people asked for a letter. None of them know who Jonathan Jones is. A veteran of no particular note, father of eight, who spent nine years and $525,000 building something that probably sounds too good to be true.

That is the fundamental challenge of the Opening Gambit: credibility before reputation. The letters are substantive, specific, and SEC-clean. They reference real systems, real patent numbers, real economic models. They link to a platform that is live and running. They do not ask for money — the cooperative does not take outside investment. They do not promise returns — there are no returns to promise.

What they ask for is attention. Read this. Look at the platform. See if it works the way we say it does. If it does, consider what it means for your work.

That is a big ask from a stranger. Most of these letters will be filtered by assistants, filed in "interesting but not urgent" folders, or deleted unread. That is expected. The Opening Gambit is not a sales campaign. It is a credibility deployment — placing the platform's existence into the awareness of people who are positioned to validate or amplify it.

The math is straightforward. If 100 letters yield 10 responses, and 10 responses yield 3 meetings, and 3 meetings yield 1 partnership or endorsement — that single outcome justifies the entire effort. One credible voice saying "I looked at this and it's real" changes the trajectory of everything that follows.

The Battery Dispatch runs in parallel: 15 social media posts over 15 days, each building on the last. LinkedIn and X, with targeted posts to Reddit communities for the manufacturing and terrain-building verticals. Every post links to the previous day's, creating a thread. Every post contains a Golden Key — a hidden word that earns cooperative currency for readers who pay attention.

The Opening Gambit is the private channel: individual letters to specific people. The Battery Dispatch is the public channel: social media posts to anyone who will listen. They fire simultaneously because the message is the same. The platform exists. It works. Come see.

What happens after launch day is not up to the Founder. It is up to the letters, the platform, and the people who receive them. The cooperative was built for nine years in relative silence. Today it speaks.
