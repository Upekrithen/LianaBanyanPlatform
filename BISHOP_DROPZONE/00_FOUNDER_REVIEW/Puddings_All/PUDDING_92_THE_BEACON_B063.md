# Pudding #92 — The Beacon

*Location-based discovery that turns a walk through your neighborhood into an economic act.*

---

Geocaching proved that people will walk miles to find a hidden container in the woods. Pokemon Go proved that people will walk miles to catch a digital creature on their phone. Both demonstrated the same principle: attach a reward to a location, and people will go there.

Liana Banyan's Beacon system applies this principle to local economic discovery. A Beacon is a location-based point tied to a real business, a real product, or a real service. Walk within range, and your phone registers the discovery. The Beacon is not a coupon. It is not an advertisement. It is a signal that something exists at this location that a member of the cooperative placed there.

Beacon Runs gamify the walk. A run is a curated path through a set of Beacons — a coffee shop, a bookstore, a barbershop, a bakery — with a leaderboard tracking who completed the route first, who discovered all the stops, who came back twice. The competition is friendly, the prizes (if any) are small, and the real output is foot traffic to local businesses that cannot afford digital advertising.

Wildfire Runs are the viral variant. A member drops a Beacon. Anyone who discovers it can chain a new Beacon from that location. The chain spreads outward like a wildfire — each new Beacon placed by a different member, each new location a different local business. The founder of the chain does not control where it goes. The community does.

Treasure Maps layer knowledge on top of geography. A Map is a quiz-driven path through Cephas content — the platform's educational library. Walk to a location, answer a question about cooperative economics or local history or manufacturing processes, and advance to the next node. Treasure Maps are how the platform teaches without lecturing: the content meets you where you are, literally.

The Beacon system does not collect demographic data. It does not build behavioral profiles. It does not sell location history to advertisers. It records that a member discovered a Beacon at a timestamp, and nothing else. The member's walking patterns are not the platform's product.

Discovery should benefit the discoverer and the discovered. The Beacon system is designed so that a walk through your neighborhood generates value for the businesses you pass, knowledge for the questions you answer, and reputation for the effort you invest — without generating surveillance data for a platform that profits from watching you move.
