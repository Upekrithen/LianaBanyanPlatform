# Pudding #90 — The Storefront

*A five-step wizard that builds a cooperative marketplace listing in under ten minutes.*

---

Setting up a shop on Etsy takes about 45 minutes. Shopify takes longer — domain configuration, theme selection, payment integration, shipping rules. Amazon Seller Central can take days, with bank verification, tax interviews, and category approvals. Each platform has built a moat out of complexity, and the complexity serves the platform more than the seller.

Liana Banyan's Storefront Builder is a five-step wizard. Step one: what do you sell? Step two: describe it. Step three: set your price. Step four: add photos. Step five: review and publish. The live preview pane shows exactly what your listing will look like as you build it. No themes to choose. No payment integration to configure — the platform handles that. No shipping rules unless you sell physical goods that ship.

The pricing step is where the cooperative economics become tangible. You set your price. The platform shows Cost+20% in descriptive form — not as a promotional claim, but as a structural explanation. On a $50 product, you keep $41.67. The platform takes $8.33. This is not negotiable, not variable, and not subject to change based on your sales volume or seller tier.

There are no promoted listings. There is no pay-to-play visibility. The storefront appears in the marketplace based on relevance and recency, not on advertising spend. A new seller with one product has the same visibility mechanics as a seller with fifty products. The difference is in what they sell, not in what they pay to be seen.

The storefront connects to every other system on the platform. Coalition membership pools purchasing power with other storefronts. The Captain dashboard gives business owners analytics without surveillance. The Cue Card system lets members recommend your storefront to their networks with single-hop attribution. The Red Carpet brings high-value creators whose audiences discover your products.

Five steps. Under ten minutes. No monthly fee beyond the $5 annual membership. No per-listing fee. No advertising budget required. The storefront is a tool for selling, not a mechanism for extracting from sellers.
