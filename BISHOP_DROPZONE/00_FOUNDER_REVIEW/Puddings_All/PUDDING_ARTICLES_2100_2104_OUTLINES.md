## ARTICLE 1: "Zero Storage, Full Income: The Content Economy That Costs Nothing to Run"

**Hook:** What if a platform could host 10 million photos and pay $1.25/month for storage?

**Core argument:** Traditional content platforms spend millions on CDN and storage. LB spends nothing because the content lives on members' existing social media. The platform is a registrar (who made what, where it lives, who gets paid) — not a host.

**Covers:** #2100 (Bounty Photography), #2101 (Pearl Diver), zero-storage metadata architecture

**Key stat:** 10 million content items = ~10 GB of metadata = $1.25/month. Same content on AWS S3 + CloudFront = $30,000-$50,000/year.

**Reader takeaway:** Platform cooperatives don't need VC infrastructure budgets to compete with Big Tech content platforms. They need smart architecture.

**Length:** ~1,500 words
**Cephas slug:** `pudding/zero-storage-full-income`

## ARTICLE 2: "Pearl Diver: Your Neighborhood Knows More Than Google"

**Hook:** Google can tell you a store's hours. It can't tell you that the veteran's discount stacks on top of the Tuesday 50% off, and the best inventory arrives Monday afternoon.

**Core argument:** Physical-world deal intelligence — discount schedules, stacking combinations, clearance timing, hidden sources — exists only in human heads. No app tracks it. No algorithm discovers it. Pearl Divers are the human intelligence layer that couponing apps can't replace.

**Covers:** #2101 (Pearl Diver), subscription model, Let's Get Groceries integration

**Key example:** Diana knows that Goodwill on Bandera has 50% off color-tag Tuesdays, AND veteran's 20% stacks (= 60% total), AND the best inventory arrives Monday afternoon. That knowledge saves $50-100 per trip × 50 trips/year = $2,500-5,000/year. One person sharing that saves hundreds of families the same amount.

**Reader takeaway:** The most valuable data isn't on the internet. It's walking around in your neighbor's head. Pearl Divers get paid to share it.

**Length:** ~1,200 words
**Cephas slug:** `pudding/pearl-diver-neighborhood-intelligence`

## ARTICLE 3: "The $5 Classroom: What Happens When Teachers Keep 83.3%"

**Hook:** Udemy takes 63 cents of every dollar a teacher earns. What if a platform took 16.7 cents — and could never raise it?

**Core argument:** The difference between a "side gig" and a "career" for online teachers is often just the platform's take rate. At Cost+20%, a teacher earning $100/session keeps $83.30 instead of $37. That's $22,224/year more — enough to make teaching from home a real career instead of a hobby.

**Covers:** #2103 (Cooperative Classroom), Cost+20% economics, dual revenue (subscriptions + per-session), Zoom-as-infrastructure

**Key comparison table:**

| Platform | Teacher Keeps | On $3,000/month gross |
|----------|-------------|---------------------|
| Udemy | 37% | $1,110 |
| Preply | 67% | $2,010 |
| iTalki | ~85% | $2,550 |
| **LB Cooperative** | **83.3%** | **$2,499** |

But LB is the ONLY one offering: group classes + individual tutoring + multi-currency payment + cooperative ownership + Guild support. At the same or better take rate.

**Reader takeaway:** The classroom of the future isn't a building. It's a living room, a Zoom account, and a $5 cooperative membership.

**Length:** ~1,500 words

## ARTICLE 4: "Why the First 10 Matter: How Pioneers Prove a Career Exists"

**Hook:** Nobody will try a job that doesn't exist yet. So how do you prove it exists? You pay the first 10 people to try it — and publish what happens.

**Core argument:** Every new economic role faces a cold-start problem. The Pioneer Program solves it by incentivizing the first adopters with diminishing rewards (generous for the first 10, tapering to zero at 1,000) in exchange for being showcased as case studies. Their real stories — real names, real photos, real income numbers — become the proof that recruits everyone who comes after.

**Covers:** #2104 (Pioneer Program), TasteTester pattern, diminishing-reward economics, the flywheel from pioneer to proof

**Key framework:**
```
Pioneer tries the role → succeeds → story published → 
  → 10 more try it → they succeed → their stories published →
    → 100 more try it → statistical proof emerges →
      → 1,000+ → role is proven → pioneers needed for NEXT role
```

**Reader takeaway:** You don't need a million-dollar marketing budget to launch a new type of work. You need 10 brave people and a system that turns their success into everyone else's confidence.

**Length:** ~1,200 words
**Cephas slug:** `pudding/why-the-first-ten-matter`

## ARTICLE 5: "Four Currencies, One Subscription: How Cooperative Money Actually Works"

**Hook:** What if you could subscribe to your favorite creator by paying with effort instead of cash?

**Core argument:** LB's Universal Member Subscription system accepts four currencies at permanent parity: Marks (earned through contribution), Credits (purchased), Joules (stored surplus), and Dollars (Stripe). This means a member who earns Marks through photography can spend them on a Spanish class subscription — without any real money changing hands. The cooperative's internal economy circulates value between members at zero payment processing cost.

**Covers:** #2102 (Universal Member Subscriptions), three-currency system, Marks economy flywheel, TasteMaker integration

**Key insight:** Every subscription paid in Marks is value that stays INSIDE the cooperative. No Stripe fees. No payment processor taking 2.9% + $0.30. No bank in the middle. Two members exchanging value directly, mediated by a ledger entry that costs a fraction of a penny.

As the Marks economy grows, the cooperative becomes increasingly independent of external financial infrastructure. That's not a side effect — it's the design.

**Reader takeaway:** Cooperative currency isn't theoretical. It's a subscription you pay for with the effort you already put in. And it makes the whole system cheaper to run.

**Length:** ~1,500 words
**Cephas slug:** `pudding/four-currencies-one-subscription`
