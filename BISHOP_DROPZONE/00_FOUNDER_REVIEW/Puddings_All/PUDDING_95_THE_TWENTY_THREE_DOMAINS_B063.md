# Pudding #95 — The Twenty-Three Domains

*How a solo founder and four AI agents migrated an entire platform architecture in five months.*

---

In November 2025, the Liana Banyan platform was a monolithic React application. Pages lived in a flat directory. Components were organized by convenience, not by function. A change to the housing system might touch files in three unrelated folders. Finding all the pieces of the commerce engine required searching the entire codebase.

By April 2, 2026 — five months later — the platform had been reorganized into 23 distinct domains, each with its own pages, components, hooks, libraries, routes, and type definitions. Commerce. Housing. Governance. Currency. Beacon. Calendar. Manufacturing. Defense. Twenty-three self-contained modules that can be understood, modified, and tested independently.

The migration was not performed by a team of engineers. It was performed by one founder and four AI agents: Knight (Cursor, the builder), Bishop (Claude, the coordinator), Rook (Gemini, the analyst), and Pawn (Perplexity, the researcher). The founder — who calls himself the AI Tuner — does not write code. He writes prompts. Each prompt is a detailed specification for a domain migration: here are the v1 assets (tables, functions, pages, components), here is the v2 structure (domain folder, file tree, naming conventions), here are the rules (AppShell for operational pages, FocusShell for public landing pages, no securities language, 83.3% in descriptive form).

Knight executed 230 sessions. Each session produced a discrete unit of work — a domain migration, a feature build, a bug fix, a deployment. Bishop coordinated the sequencing, wrote the prompts, processed research from Pawn, and maintained the knowledge base through the Librarian MCP system. Rook provided analysis. Pawn delivered research, page design specifications, and legal review.

The result is 658 v2 files across 23 domains, backed by 580 database tables, 139 edge functions, 470 pages, and 455 Cephas content entries. The entire knowledge base is indexed and searchable through 15 index files maintained by the Librarian.

Five months. One founder. Four AI agents. Zero employees. Twenty-three domains. The platform is not a prototype. It is a production system with 35 live subsystems, 11 filed provisional patents, and 2,130 documented innovations.

The v2 migration is not a technical achievement worth celebrating for its own sake. It is proof that a cooperative platform can be built without venture capital, without a payroll, and without compromising on architectural quality — if the builder is willing to treat AI agents as genuine collaborators rather than autocomplete tools.
