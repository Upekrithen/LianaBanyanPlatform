# Pudding #105: The Tooth

## The Article

My daughter has teeth coming in. Which means the other teeth need to go out.

We explained to her that it would be a little bit of discomfort — brushing hard enough to be effective, wiggling the loose tooth a little, keeping at it a little at a time. Not because we enjoy watching a seven-year-old wince. Because she wants to eat on that side of her mouth without pain. And the only way to get there is through a little discomfort now.

She didn't love hearing it. But she understood it. Because the thing she wants — eating without pain, a smile that works — is worth more than the temporary sting of brushing where it hurts.

---

Starting something new is the same.

Starting a business on Liana Banyan — or anywhere — involves discomfort. You have to describe what you do in fifteen seconds on a Cue Card. That's uncomfortable if you've never said it out loud. You have to price your work using real numbers, not feelings. That stings when you've been giving it away. You have to post your first listing, share your first link, hand your first card to a stranger and say: "This is what I built."

None of that is easy. And we don't pretend it is.

---

What the cooperative does is remove as much of that discomfort as possible. Not all of it. Some of it has to stay — because the discomfort is the growth.

The Cue Card doesn't write your pitch for you. But it gives you a template, a constraint (fifteen seconds), and a campaign library of examples. The discomfort of finding your words is still yours. The discomfort of formatting, designing, and distributing is removed.

The Treasure Map doesn't build your business for you. But it gives you five steps, real market data, and connections to local demand. The discomfort of making decisions is still yours. The discomfort of figuring out where to start is removed.

The Cold Start Hub doesn't guarantee your first sale. But it matches your craft to a pathway, shows you who nearby is already looking for what you offer, and connects you to Captains who've walked this route before. The discomfort of selling is still yours. The discomfort of being alone in it is removed.

---

There's a difference between hard and unnecessarily hard.

Brushing where it hurts is hard. Doing it without a toothbrush would be unnecessarily hard. Wiggling a loose tooth is hard. Doing it without knowing that a better tooth is coming in underneath would be unnecessarily hard.

The cooperative is the toothbrush. The Treasure Map is the knowledge that something better is growing underneath. The Cue Card is the gentle wiggle. The Captain is the parent who says: "I know. Keep going. It's worth it."

---

The discomfort doesn't go away entirely. If it did, the thing you built wouldn't mean anything. The sting of pricing your work honestly, of asking someone to pay for what you made, of putting your name on something and saying "this is mine" — that sting is what makes it real. That's the tooth coming loose. That's the new one pushing through.

We try to make it easier. Not easy. Easier. Because the difference between giving up and pushing through is usually not courage. It's having the right tool at the right moment — a toothbrush, a template, a five-step plan, a neighbor who says "I did this too."

---

My daughter brushed where it hurt. The tooth is loose now. She wiggles it every day. She's not happy about the wiggling. But she smiles when she talks about what she's going to eat when the new tooth comes in.

That's what starting a business feels like. A little discomfort. A lot of wiggling. And a wonderful smile at the end.
