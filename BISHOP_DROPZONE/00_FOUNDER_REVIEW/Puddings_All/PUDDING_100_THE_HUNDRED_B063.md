# Pudding #100 — The Hundred

*One hundred articles about a cooperative that exists because one person decided to build it.*

---

This is the hundredth Pudding article. The first was written to explain a single feature. The hundredth is written to explain why there are a hundred.

The name "Pudding" comes from the phrase "the proof is in the pudding" — itself a corruption of the older saying "the proof of the pudding is in the eating." You cannot know whether the pudding is good by looking at the recipe. You have to taste it. These articles are the tasting.

Each article explains one piece of a cooperative platform that has grown to 23 domains, 580 database tables, 139 edge functions, 2,130 documented innovations, 168 Crown Jewels, 11 provisional patents, and 35 production systems. The platform costs members $5 per year. Creators keep 83.3% of every transaction. The platform takes Cost+20%. These numbers do not change.

The articles were written by an AI agent — Bishop, one of four agents coordinated by a single founder who calls himself the AI Tuner. The founder does not write code. He does not write articles. He writes prompts. He describes what needs to exist, and the agents build it. Knight writes the code. Bishop writes the documents and coordinates the team. Rook analyzes. Pawn researches. The founder tunes.

One hundred articles is not a milestone worth celebrating for its own sake. A hundred bad articles is worse than ten good ones. But these hundred articles cover the full breadth of a platform that was built from scratch over two decades of thinking and five months of building — cooperative economics, three-currency systems, location-based discovery, desktop manufacturing, dispute resolution, housing accountability, vehicle sharing, gamified education, patent filing, civic engagement, creator tools, family coordination, and the AI architecture that made it all possible.

The proof of the pudding is in the eating. One hundred articles describe what the pudding tastes like. The platform itself — live, deployed, and serving its first members — is the pudding.

Liana Banyan Corporation. Wyoming C-Corp. Founded by a veteran of no particular note who enlisted at sixteen, served as Infantry and then Aviation, raised eight children, and spent over two decades building a cooperative that nobody asked for and everybody needs.

$5 per year. 83.3% to the creator. Cost+20%. The math on the label.

One hundred articles. One platform. One founder. Four agents.

For the Keep.
