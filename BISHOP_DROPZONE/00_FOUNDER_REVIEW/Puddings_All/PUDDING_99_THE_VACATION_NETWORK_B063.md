# Pudding #99 — The Vacation Network

*Cooperative housing exchange where members share homes instead of paying hotels.*

---

Airbnb started as a simple idea: people have spare rooms, travelers need affordable places to stay, connect them. The platform's cut was modest at first. Then it grew. Service fees climbed to 14-20% of the booking total. Cleaning fees became a second price hidden until checkout. Dynamic pricing algorithms raised rates during peak demand. The hosts kept less. The guests paid more. The platform kept the difference.

Liana Banyan's Vacation Network returns to the original premise with one structural difference: the platform cannot increase its take. Cost+20% applies to every booking. On a $100/night stay, the host keeps $83.33. The platform takes $16.67. No cleaning fee surcharges. No dynamic pricing algorithms. No service fee that changes based on demand.

But the Vacation Network offers something Airbnb structurally cannot: currency-based exchange. A member in Austin can offer their spare room for the weekend and accept payment in Credits, Marks, or dollars. A member in Portland who has accumulated Marks through months of cooperative participation can spend those Marks on a weekend in Austin without spending a dollar. The currencies are not discounted alternatives — they carry equal value within the system. A Mark earned through effort has the same purchasing power as a Credit purchased with cash.

This creates a housing exchange that is genuinely cooperative. Members who contribute to the platform — teaching classes, building storefronts, completing Beacon runs, moderating the Round Table — accumulate currencies that let them travel within the network. The more you participate, the more places you can go. No cash required.

The trust layer comes from the ADAPT score. A host can see a potential guest's reputation — not a five-star rating gamed by reciprocal reviews, but a composite score built from demonstrated reliability across every interaction on the platform. A guest with a high ADAPT score is someone who has consistently shown up, delivered on commitments, and treated shared resources with care. That history is more informative than a paragraph of reviews written by strangers.

The Vacation Network is not trying to compete with Airbnb on inventory. It is offering an alternative for members who would rather spend earned effort-currency on a weekend away than spend dollars on a platform that takes an unpredictable cut. The housing exists. The members exist. The currencies exist. The network connects them without extracting from the connection.
