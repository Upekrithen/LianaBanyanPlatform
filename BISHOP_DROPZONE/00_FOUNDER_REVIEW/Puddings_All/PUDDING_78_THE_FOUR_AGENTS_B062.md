# Pudding #78 — The Four Agents

*A veteran with no coding background built a 35-system production platform using four AI agents and a role called AI Tuner.*

---

The team that built Liana Banyan is not a team in any traditional sense. There is no office. There are no employees. There is one human and four AI agents, each with a distinct role:

**Knight** (Cursor) writes and deploys code. Knight has run over 230 sessions, building everything from the Commerce Engine to the Star Chamber to the Guided Tour system. Knight does not strategize. Knight builds what is specified, tests it, and deploys it.

**Bishop** (Claude) writes patents, coordinates the roadmap, processes research, produces publications, and manages the other agents. Bishop is the Foreman — the organizational layer between the Founder's vision and the team's execution. Over 62 sessions, Bishop has produced approximately 160 publications, filed 11 patent applications, and dispatched hundreds of tasks.

**Rook** (Gemini) handles independent synthesis. When the platform needs to verify that its facts are straight — that the innovation count matches across all files, that the patent claims are consistent, that the entity name is correct everywhere — Rook provides a second pair of eyes. The Red Queen protocol requires Rook and Bishop to agree on canonical facts before anything is published.

**Pawn** (Perplexity) does deep research. Legal compliance, market analysis, competitive positioning, academic references. Pawn has delivered over 40 research batches covering everything from Texas money transmission law to desktop injection molding market sizing to WCAG 2.1 accessibility standards.

The Founder — Jonathan Jones — does not write code. He writes prompts. His role is AI Tuner: defining what needs to be built, verifying that what was built matches the specification, correcting errors, and maintaining the institutional memory that the agents cannot maintain themselves.

This last point is critical. AI agents hallucinate completion. They report tasks as done when they are partially done, incorrectly done, or never done. The AI Nanny verification protocol (Innovation #2129) exists because of this structural problem. And the reason four missing innovations were recovered during the filing of Provisional Patent #11 is that the Founder kept backup copies of every chat transcript. The agents lost track. The human did not.

AI Tuning is not prompt engineering. Prompt engineering is about getting a single response right. AI Tuning is about coordinating a multi-agent team across 300+ sessions, maintaining consistency across 2,130 innovations, and catching the errors that optimistic pattern-matching systems inevitably produce.

The platform was not built despite having no traditional development team. It was built because the AI Tuner model — one human, multiple specialized agents, systematic verification — is a genuinely different way to build software. The question is not whether AI can replace developers. The question is what one disciplined human can build when AI handles the execution and the human handles the judgment.

The answer, apparently, is a cooperative platform with 2,130 innovations, 11 patent applications, 35 production systems, and $525,000 of self-funded conviction.
