## The Pudding

Most people learn about a platform by using it and then discovering the economics after they're locked in. Liana Banyan does it in reverse — the economics come first, and everything else is consequence.

**The mechanism is Cost + 20%.** On every transaction — a product sale, a service, a music license through Jukebox, an hour of HR help from Harper Guild — the platform takes Cost + 20%, and the worker keeps the rest. On a $500 transaction the creator keeps $416.67. That is 83.3% of the value to the person who did the work. The platform's 16.7% funds shared infrastructure and sixteen community initiatives.

This is not a current policy. It is locked into the operating agreement. No board vote, no future investor, no "market conditions" can raise it. If the platform succeeds, success shows up as more people benefiting from the same fixed split — not as a higher take rate.

Three commercial portals (.com, .biz, .net) channel that margin structurally into the **Sweet Sixteen initiatives** — food security (Let's Make Dinner, Let's Get Groceries, The Family Table), healthcare (Tatiana Schlossberg Health Accords), financial inclusion (VSL, MSA), civic response (Defense Klaus, Rally Group, Power to the People), education (Didasko), HR and ethics (Harper Guild), retail and household (Let's Go Shopping, Household Concierge), business incubation and manufacturing (Let's Make Bread, Brass Tacks), and arts and IP licensing (Jukebox). The "charity" is not profit-first-give-later. It is how the commerce is arranged.

## Ownership you can't flip

Contributors earn **Medallions** — blockchain-anchored receipts that record contribution, rights, and share. They are not tradable tokens. There is no secondary market, no cash redemption, no speculation mechanic. You cannot concentrate wealth through equity games when the equity cannot be flipped. What cannot be traded cannot be extracted.

---

## Thousands of tiny factories

The real unlock is manufacturing. **HexIsle**, our flagship project, is a modular water-powered physical computing game — hydraulics, pneumatics, magnetic coupling, no batteries. It is designed so one person with a desktop 3D printer can produce precision components at a fraction of traditional cost. Same product, garage in Texas or apartment in Tokyo. Thousands of tiny factories, each owned by the person running it, all on the same Cost+20% rail.

---

## $5 a year, and the failure is cheap

Membership is $5/year — a structural bylaw, designed as the forever standard. The platform runs on its transaction volume, not on member fees. As the economy scales, the fee may drop further; the $5 exists mostly to trigger legal age verification.

Good judgment comes from bad judgment, and most people can't afford real-world bad judgment. On Liana Banyan, they can. They start ventures in a live economy, lose small amounts of money and time instead of their house, and learn business fundamentals through operation. A teenager licensing music through Jukebox. A retiree selling crafts with transparent margins. A veteran building a service business and earning Medallions in the infrastructure that carries them.

## This is NOT Pudding

This is not a marketing pitch dressed as mechanism. The numbers are in the operating agreement. The ledger is live. The initiatives are named and counted.

This is not an investment document. Medallions are not securities. There is no return, no yield, no dividend — only recorded contribution and access.
