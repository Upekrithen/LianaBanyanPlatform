## The Pudding

Most platforms require you to apply. You fill out a form. You wait. A committee reviews. A role gets posted. You apply again for the posted version. Someone hires you into the job description that was written before anyone knew you existed.

Liana Banyan does it backwards.

The Founder carries pre-drafted **What-If Commissions** — formal role-assignment contracts authored in advance, each one waiting for the right person to show up. When that person shows up in a conversation, the Founder doesn't say "let me write something up and email you later." The Founder says: *"We Need What You're Good At."*

That phrase is a signal. It means we anticipated this moment. We thought — what if we meet someone of unusual ability who would meet higher expectations? That person is you. The commission in our hand exists because we were ready for you to arrive.

## The Battlefield Commission Model

In military history, a battlefield commission was granted on-the-spot to soldiers demonstrating unusual leadership in combat. The commander had pre-authorized authority; the paperwork caught up later. The critical element was **recognition of unexpected merit, in the moment**.

What-If Commissions are the cooperative-platform civilian equivalent. The paperwork exists in advance. The authority is pre-delegated. The conversation is the activation moment.

## The Scope You Choose

A What-If Commission is flexible where a job description is rigid. You don't conform to the role — the role accommodates you. Four dimensions you set yourself:

- **Time-fractional**: "2 hours each Tuesday" is a valid commitment
- **Scope-fractional**: "up to 10 businesses" or "one guild only" is a valid commitment
- **Project-fractional**: "for this one Kickstarter arc" is a valid commitment
- **Decision-fractional**: "advisory veto only, not day-to-day" is a valid commitment

This borrows from the Netherlands fractional-employment framework — commit to what you're actually willing and able to do. LB extends it across four dimensions, not just time. The commission accommodates; you do not have to sacrifice other roles, businesses, or platforms to hold one.

## The Automatic Activation

When you sign, the platform fires. Your member profile updates to reflect your new scoped role. Platform routing rules shift — messages and decisions in your domain start reaching you. Guild and tribe memberships adjust. Your personalized Red Carpet for this commission activates on your next login. Relevant Crowns and Captains are notified. Your Medallion accumulation path opens for commissioned work.

No admin runaround. No HR onboarding maze. The platform updates because you said yes.

## Why This Matters

Traditional organizations lose extraordinary people in the gap between "I think this person is special" and "the paperwork is ready." Weeks pass. The person's calendar fills. Their interest cools. The job description gets written by someone who wasn't in the original conversation.

What-If Commissions close that gap to zero. The window of "we just had the conversation where I realized you were right" is also the window where the authority transfers. The recognition and the deployment are the same act.

## This is NOT Pudding

This is not instant-hire. Acceptance still requires signature, scope-negotiation, and the recipient's genuine commitment. Commissions can be declined or renegotiated or withdrawn.

This is not unlimited authority. Each commission is scoped by the template it derives from and by the commitment the recipient sets. No commission grants open-ended power.

This is not a replacement for Crown Letters. Crown Letters are planned invitations to initiative leadership or C-suite seats. What-If Commissions are spontaneous recognitions of unanticipated ability, at any scope level.

This is not a hiring system. LB does not "hire" in the traditional sense. Commissions grant scoped cooperative authority; Medallions accumulate; 83.3% of commissioned-work transaction value flows to the person doing the work. The economics are the same as every other role on the platform.

*We Need What You're Good At.*
