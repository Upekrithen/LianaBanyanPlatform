## The Old Status Symbol

FoundersCard markets itself to 250,000 "entrepreneurs and business professionals." For $495 per year, you get discounts at Hilton, United, Virgin Atlantic, Waldorf Astoria, and Wynn. Forbes called it "a community for leaders." The Wall Street Journal said it "soothes stress with elite access."

What does it build? Nothing. What does it produce? Nothing. What do the 250,000 members own? A membership card and a slightly cheaper hotel room.

It is a toll booth dressed as a country club.

## The New Status Symbol

Liana Banyan costs $5 per year. For that, you get:

- **Your own storefront** — sell what you make, keep 83.3% of every dollar
- **Three currencies** — Credits (dollar-pegged), Marks (earned by effort), Joules (never expire)
- **A patent portfolio** — 2,125 innovations, 11 filings, 2,097 formal claims — and you own a share
- **16 charitable initiatives** funded automatically by the 20% margin — not by your guilt
- **A cooperative card** — not for hotel points, but for economic participation

FoundersCard members pay $495 to *access* status. Liana Banyan members pay $5 to *create* it.

## What Status Actually Means

Status used to mean *what you could afford to consume.* A $495 card. A first-class lounge. A hotel upgrade.

But consumption is a treadmill. You never own the treadmill. You just keep running.

Liana Banyan flips the frame. Status isn't what you consume — it's what you build. Your Marks are earned, not bought. Your Joules are permanent, not expiring. Your storefront is yours, not rented. Your cooperative share grows as the platform grows — not because you paid more, but because you contributed more.

**Making affordability a status symbol** means that the person who paid $5 and earned 1,000 Marks through effort has more standing than the person who paid $495 for a hotel discount. Because Marks represent work. Work represents value. Value represents the actual economy — not the simulacrum of one.

## The Candle and the Light

FoundersCard is a better candle. Nicer wax. Fancier wick. Still just fire.

Liana Banyan is the wiring.

$5 to plug in. 83.3% of every watt goes to the person who generated it. The 20% margin lights the streets for everyone.

The FoundersCard asks: *"What perks can we give you for $495?"*

Liana Banyan asks: *"What can you build for $5?"*
