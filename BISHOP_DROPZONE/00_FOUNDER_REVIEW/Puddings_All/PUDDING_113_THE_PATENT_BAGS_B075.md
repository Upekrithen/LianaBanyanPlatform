## The Pudding

Somewhere in a vault, filed under a system invented by four AI agents and one Founder, there are twelve numbered bags.

Bag 5 is a water table. Not a metaphorical one. A physical one — three nested hexagonal reservoirs where gravity drives water between oscillating chambers, creating continuous pressure waves without electricity. Golden Lotus pistons with Tesla valve cups convert that motion into rotation. Rooster teeth serve as air pistons that make miniature plants physically grow when pressed. A five-year-old can play the game that runs on this table. The entire system operates without electronics.

Bag 6 is diceless combat. No dice. No stat sheets. Push the character — that is the attack. The character falls — that is the damage. The physics IS the game mechanic. The rules are embedded in the physical interaction, not written in a rulebook. A child and a veteran gamer play the same game at different strategic depths.

Bag 7 is jewelry that fights back. A wrist bangle that looks like fashion but converts to a self-defense tool through a mechanical transformation. Integrated evidence collection. A distributed legal defense fund you join with minimal contact. Emergency response built into the platform with false-positive training signals that keep responders sharp without real emergencies.

Bag 8 is three cooperative businesses in one filing — Let's Make Dinner (shared meal coordination at Cost+20%), JukeBox (creative licensing without labels or agents), and Very Short Lending (micro-duration loans collateralized by Joules, interest-free because cooperative members do not charge each other).

Bag 9 is three more — Let's Make Bread (business incubator, no equity taken), community-first shopping, and municipal treasury management.

Bag 10 is Ghost World — every platform feature exists in both simulation and reality. New members start in the simulation. Full access. No real money at risk. When ready, one toggle. The simulation IS the onboarding.

And then Rook — the Gemini agent — expanded the portfolio from Bags 21 through 26: decentralized quality assurance, community engagement, operational efficiency, patent structure meta-systems, community innovation, and 3D user experience.

Twelve bags. Ninety innovations between them. Over three hundred claims. Filed across eleven provisional patent applications. Built to last.
