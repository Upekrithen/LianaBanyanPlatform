# Pudding #44: The Solidarity Stack
## Five Layers of Cooperative Infrastructure in One Platform

---

### At a Glance (~50 words)
The Solidarity Stack is a concept from the platform cooperativism movement: a horizontal ecosystem linking cooperatives, social movements, and recuperated factories. Liana Banyan builds all five layers — governance, currency, production, IP, and AI — into a single integrated platform. The concept is an idea. This is the implementation.

---

### More Info (~300 words)

Platform cooperativism has spent a decade building the theoretical case for cooperative alternatives to extractive platforms. The movement produced essential scholarship, conferences, pilot projects, and policy frameworks. What it has not yet produced — at scale — is a fully integrated implementation that spans the full stack from governance to manufacturing.

Liana Banyan builds five layers:

**Layer 1: Cooperative Governance.** Marks-weighted Backer Elections where members vote on platform priorities. A Star Chamber with four AI judges for dispute resolution. Chain voting, Senate halls, and an Areopagus appeal system. Governance is not advisory — it's operational. Votes lead to builds.

**Layer 2: Multi-Currency Economics.** Credits ($1=1) for exchange. Marks for effort-differential recognition. Joules for surplus allocation. Three currencies that separate the functions money normally bundles together, so the cooperative can value labor that market pricing ignores.

**Layer 3: Distributed Manufacturing.** 100 Pioneer Nodes with seven equipment types. Design Democracy where the community votes on what gets produced. A six-stage factory pipeline from design to shipping. Physical production governed by digital democracy.

**Layer 4: Intellectual Property Infrastructure.** 2,129 innovations. 167 Crown Jewels. 10 patent provisionals with 2,097 formal claims. The cooperative's ideas are documented, protected, and attributed. IP serves the community rather than extracting from it.

**Layer 5: AI-Mediated Operations.** Four specialized AI agents (Knight, Bishop, Rook, Pawn) operating under human governance. AI builds the infrastructure, coordinates the work, and amplifies the Founder's judgment across more domains than any single human could manage. The AI is a tool of the cooperative, not its owner.

Each layer depends on the others. Governance without currency is symbolic. Currency without production is speculative. Production without IP is unprotected. IP without AI is unscalable. AI without governance is unaccountable. The stack works because the layers are integrated, not because any layer is individually novel.

### Full Detail

**Why "stack" and not "platform"**

The word "stack" implies layers that build on each other, like a technology stack (database, server, application, interface). A platform is a single surface. A stack is a vertical integration of capabilities where each layer makes the one above it possible.

Liana Banyan is a platform, but calling it only a platform misses the structural depth. The marketplace function sits on top of currency mechanics. The currency mechanics sit on top of governance rules. The governance rules sit on top of AI-mediated coordination. Remove any layer and the others weaken or collapse.

This is why incremental approaches to platform cooperativism have struggled. A cooperative that adds governance to an existing marketplace inherits the marketplace's economic assumptions. A cooperative that adds currency to an existing governance structure inherits the governance structure's limitations. Building the full stack from scratch — as Liana Banyan did — is harder but produces an integrated system where each layer reinforces the others.

**Layer 1 in detail: Governance**

Cooperative governance is not voting on a website. It's the operational system that determines what gets built, who resolves disputes, and how power flows.

Liana Banyan's governance operates through four interconnected mechanisms:

- **Backer Elections** use Marks-weighted voting. Members stake Marks (effort-differential currency) to express governance commitment. Higher stakes reflect demonstrated contribution, not wealth. Proposals that clear the threshold enter the production pipeline.
- **Star Chamber** provides dispute resolution through four AI judges that analyze cases against cooperative principles. Human override exists at every level. The system handles member disputes, content appeals, and economic disagreements.
- **Chain Voting** allows sequential, linked votes on related proposals — so the community can make complex, multi-step decisions rather than binary yes/no choices.
- **Areopagus** is a four-level appeal system: internal review, community review, cooperative ombudsperson, and external AAA arbitration. No member is ever trapped in a decision with no recourse.

This is governance with teeth. Votes produce real changes to the platform. Appeals lead to real reversals. The system has memory — every decision, every vote, every appeal is recorded.

**Layer 2 in detail: Multi-Currency Economics**

Most cooperatives use dollars. Dollars are useful for exchange but terrible for recognizing effort that doesn't have a market price. How much is a community member's volunteer time worth? How much is a creator's reputation contribution worth? Dollars force everything into a single metric that systematically undervalues non-market contributions.

Three currencies solve this by separation of functions:

- **Credits** ($1=1) handle exchange. When you buy a product, subscribe to a channel, or pay for a service, you use Credits. They behave like money because they are money. Credits never cash out to fiat — the one-way valve is irrevocable.
- **Marks** recognize effort differentials. When you contribute labor, expertise, governance participation, or community building, you earn Marks. Marks are not exchangeable for dollars. They represent your accumulated contribution to the cooperative and give weight to your governance vote. Sponsorship Marks are ONE LEVEL ONLY — not multi-level.
- **Joules** allocate surplus. When the cooperative generates surplus above operating costs, it distributes Joules. A Joule is a permanent stake — a "forever stamp" that represents your share of the cooperative's accumulated value. Joules can collateralize Backed Marks for governance, creating a link between surplus and governance power.

The three currencies don't compete. They cover different functions. A member might earn Credits through sales, Marks through community work, and Joules through the cooperative's success — all simultaneously, all visible on their Wallet dashboard.

**Layer 3 in detail: Distributed Manufacturing**

The cooperative doesn't just sell digital goods. It produces physical products through a 100-node Pioneer Network governed by Design Democracy.

The pipeline has six stages: DESIGN → SLA (prototype) → VALIDATE → SLS (production body) → INJECT (injection molding) → SHIP. Each stage has checkpoints. Products that pass community vote in Design Democracy enter the pipeline. Products that don't pass are archived, not deleted.

Pioneer Nodes register with seven equipment types (SLA, FDM, resin, CNC, laser, injection, desktop). The system routes production requests to nodes with the right capabilities. A design requiring SLA prototyping goes to SLA nodes. A design requiring injection molding goes to nodes with the Canister System.

Manufacturing at Cost+20%. No one undercuts anyone on price — quality, speed, and reliability are the differentiators.

**Layer 4 in detail: Intellectual Property**

2,129 innovations. Each one documented, categorized, and attributed. 167 Crown Jewels — innovations designated as particularly significant to the cooperative's identity and competitive position. 10 patent provisionals filed with 2,097 formal claims protecting the full combination.

The IP layer exists because cooperatives have historically been bad at protecting their ideas. Open-source principles are valuable, but unprotected innovations get captured by extractive companies. Liana Banyan's approach is protective without being extractive: the cooperative owns the IP collectively, patents prevent competitors from capturing the innovations, and creators retain attribution throughout.

**Layer 5 in detail: AI-Mediated Operations**

Four AI agents, each specialized:

- **Knight** (Cursor): Writes all production code. 222 sessions. Has built 15 of 23 operational domains.
- **Bishop** (Claude): Coordinates all agents. Strategy, roadmap, letters, quality control, dispatching.
- **Rook** (Gemini): Deep analysis. Vault management. Long-context document processing.
- **Pawn** (Perplexity): Live research. Market analysis. Regulatory compliance. Page design specs.

The Founder holds every final decision. No code deploys, no letter sends, no architecture changes without human approval. The AI agents amplify human capacity without replacing human judgment.

This is the fifth layer because it makes the other four scalable. Governance at 23 domains requires coordination no single human can maintain alone. Currency design across thousands of transactions requires analysis beyond manual calculation. Manufacturing coordination across 100 nodes requires logistics AI can track. IP documentation at 2,129 innovations requires institutional memory AI can maintain.

**The integration is the innovation**

Any individual layer could exist independently. Cooperatives with good governance exist. Multi-currency experiments exist. Distributed manufacturing networks exist. Patent portfolios exist. AI coding assistants exist.

What doesn't exist elsewhere — yet — is all five operating as a single integrated system where governance directs manufacturing, currency values the labor, IP protects the output, and AI maintains the whole thing under human authority. The Solidarity Stack as concept. Liana Banyan as implementation.
