## THE PROOF

Every technology platform shows you what it wants you to see. The feed, the dashboard, the recommendations, the price. What it never shows you is the machine underneath — how the price was calculated, why that recommendation appeared, where your data went, who profits from the transaction.

We built a button that turns the walls transparent.

---

## THE PUDDING

It's called X-Ray. It's a toggle on every page. When you flip it on, annotation blocks appear next to every element — explaining what it is, where the data comes from, how the economics work, and what you can do about it.

Not a help page. Not a tooltip. Not a "Learn More" link that opens a new tab. Inline, contextual, right-there-next-to-the-thing-it-explains annotations that appear on the actual surface you're already looking at.

Here's what happens when you turn on X-Ray on a marketplace listing:

The price says $50. The X-Ray annotation says: "Cost to produce: $41.67. Platform operating margin: $8.33 (Cost+20%). Creator receives: $41.67 (83.3%). Platform retains: $8.33 for infrastructure, moderation, and member services. No advertising revenue. No data sale. No hidden fees."

That's not marketing copy. That's a financial X-ray of a single transaction, rendered in real time, from the actual data.

Or you're looking at a Beacon — a local business promotion. X-Ray says: "This beacon was created by [Storefront Name]. It runs for 72 hours. Distribution: Battery Dispatch grid across 4 channels. Creator keeps 83.3% of any resulting transactions. Platform take: Cost+20% on the transaction, nothing on the beacon itself. The beacon is free to create."

Or you're in the Star Chamber watching a case. X-Ray says: "Four AI judges are reviewing this case. Oracle and Morpheus run on Claude (Anthropic). Red Queen and Dredd run on Perplexity. Judge responses are displayed in a reasoning matrix. Founder override is an annotation, not a command — judge reasoning is preserved regardless of override."

Every page. Every component. Every economic transaction. The X-Ray sees through all of it.

This matters because the default state of technology is opacity. Not malicious opacity — just the natural result of building complex systems where the economics happen in backend services that nobody designed a UI for. The economics exist. The data flows exist. The profit margins exist. They just don't have a front door.

X-Ray gives them a front door.

And it's not just economics. X-Ray annotations cover:
- **Structure**: What this component is and why it's here
- **Data flow**: Where this information comes from and where it goes
- **Economics**: Who pays, who earns, what the margin is
- **Governance**: Who can change this, and through what process
- **Connection**: What other platform surfaces this connects to

Every annotation has a glossary entry. The glossary is structured — not a flat dictionary, but a navigable map of how the platform works. Click an X-Ray term and you get the definition, the related surfaces, and the governance path for changing it.

The mascot character changes state when X-Ray is on. Relaxed posture becomes alert. The goggles come down. The cyan tint activates. It's a visual signal: you are now looking at the platform differently. Not because the platform changed — because your perspective did.

X-Ray is not a debugging tool. It's not a developer feature hidden behind a flag. It's a member-facing transparency system that assumes every member has the right to understand how their cooperative works. The barista has the right to see the margin on the coffee. The photographer has the right to see the economics of the bounty. The parent has the right to see what data their child's XP score touches.

The technical term is "platform-wide DOM annotation system with structured glossary providing member-facing transparency overlays." The human term is: you can see through the walls.

We built it because we believe transparency isn't a feature. It's architecture. And architecture that can't be inspected by the people who live in it isn't cooperative — it's a landlord who won't show you the lease.

## THIS IS NOT PUDDING

X-Ray Instrumentation System is Innovation #2154, classified as a Crown Jewel. Tour Target Annotation System (#2157) provides the build-time DOM hooks that X-Ray reads at runtime. Together they create a dual-layer transparency architecture: X-Ray for "how does this work?" and Guided Tour for "what should I try next?"

Prior art includes browser developer tools (for developers, not members), accessibility inspectors (structural, not economic), and "how it works" marketing pages (static, not contextual). No prior platform has shipped a member-facing, toggle-activated, inline annotation system that exposes economic mechanics, data flows, and governance pathways on every surface simultaneously.
