# Pudding #87 — The Subscription Channel

*Creator premium content where the creator keeps 83.3% and subscribers pay in any of four currencies.*

---

Patreon takes 5-12% of creator earnings plus payment processing fees. YouTube channel memberships take 30%. Substack takes 10%. In each case, the creator's share is determined by the platform, and the subscriber pays in exactly one currency: dollars.

Liana Banyan's Subscription Channels flip both of those constraints. The creator keeps 83.3% of subscription revenue. The platform takes Cost+20% — the same structural margin applied to every transaction on the platform. And subscribers can pay in any of four currencies: Credits, Marks, Joules, or dollars.

Why four currencies? Because different subscribers have different relationships to the cooperative. A new member might pay in dollars. A member who has been contributing effort to the platform has accumulated Marks and might prefer to spend those. A member with Joules — the surplus currency earned when the cooperative generates more value than it consumes — might use those. Credits are the simplest: one dollar equals one Credit.

The creator does not need to manage four payment rails. The platform handles the currency routing. What the creator sees is: 14 active subscribers, $X in revenue this month. The economics block appears near the subscription options — not buried in a FAQ, but visible as the answer to the question every potential subscriber asks: "Why should I subscribe here instead of somewhere else?"

The answer is the math. On a $10/month subscription, the creator keeps $8.33 here versus $7.00-$9.50 on conventional platforms. Over a year with 100 subscribers, that difference is $996-$1,596. For a creator earning their living from content, that is rent.

Preview content is the conversion engine. Some posts are visible to everyone. Some are locked. The mix is controlled by the creator, not by the platform. The subscribed view replaces the preview on the same page — no redirect, no separate portal, no second login. You subscribe, and the content appears where the lock icon used to be.

The channel is the creator's. The subscribers are the creator's. The 83.3% is structural, not promotional. It does not change when the platform decides it needs more revenue.
