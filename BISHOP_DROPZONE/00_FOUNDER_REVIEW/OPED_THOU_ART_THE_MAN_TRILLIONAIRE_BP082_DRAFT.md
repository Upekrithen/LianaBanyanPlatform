# Thou Art the Man

*Elon Musk just became the world's first trillionaire. I don't want to be that man. So we built the platform that refuses to make me one.*

---

The headlines this week named Elon Musk the world's first trillionaire. A thousand billion dollars. The number is so large it stops meaning anything. It's not money anymore. It's something else.

When I read it, I thought of the prophet Nathan, and a king named David, and a poor man's one little ewe lamb.

Most people skim past the parable, but it lands hard if you read it slow. There was a rich man, says Nathan, with very many flocks and herds. And there was a poor man, who had nothing — *save one little ewe lamb, which he had bought and nourished up: and it grew up together with him, and with his children; it did eat of his own meat, and drank of his own cup, and lay in his bosom, and was unto him as a daughter.* A traveler came to the rich man. The rich man, instead of taking from his own abundance, took the poor man's one lamb and dressed it for his guest.

David, hearing this, was outraged. *As the LORD liveth, the man that hath done this thing shall surely die*. Whoever did this — whoever could be so without pity — deserved the worst that could be given to him.

And Nathan said unto David, *Thou art the man.*

I read this passage during a season of building. I had been working on what would become Liana Banyan for what was then about thirty-five years. I was not a king. I had no flocks and herds. I had a wife, eight children, a $200 used car when I could swing it, and a $5000 used computer when the parts went on sale the Monday after Black Friday. I was, by every measure that matters in this country, an unremarkable man trying to do an unremarkable thing for ordinary people.

But that passage stopped me anyway. Because the parable is not just about David. It's a warning about the trajectory you can be on without seeing it. About the man you can become if you build a system that rewards you for being the rich man who takes the poor man's only lamb. About the slow, almost imperceptible compounding of advantage that turns into a structure that turns into a fortune that turns into — at some point — an indictment.

I don't want to be the man with no pity. I have never wanted to be that man. I do not believe Elon Musk wanted to be that man either, when he was a boy in South Africa. I do not believe most of the men who become billionaires set out, in their twenties, to become billionaires. I think most of them set out to build something they thought the world needed. And then the system rewarded them so disproportionately for building it that the reward itself became the problem.

The reward is the problem.

So we built a platform that refuses to give that reward. Liana Banyan is the **Capitalist Cooperative**. We do it fairly, for the benefit of all. *For He maketh his sun to rise on the evil and on the good, and sendeth rain on the just and on the unjust.* That is Matthew 5:45. That is the inclusion clause. Everyone gets the rain. Everyone gets the sun. Nobody is gatekept from prosperity because of who they are or where they started.

How does the structure refuse the reward?

By naming it. By writing it into the bylaws. By making it so the founder — me — *cannot become the man with no pity even if I wanted to*.

**Creators keep 83.3 percent of every dollar.** That is not generosity. That is structural refusal. It means the company cannot accumulate the kind of margin that produces a trillionaire. The math is locked. On a $500 transaction the creator takes home $416.67. The platform takes 16.7%. Twenty percent above actual cost. No more. Audited. Written in plain English. Pledged for the life of the cooperative.

**Membership is $5 a year.** Not a tier. Not a trial. Not a pricing experiment. Five dollars. Once. The dues fund the commons. They are not the revenue model. We are not optimizing the dues upward.

**The currencies cannot be converted to dollars.** Credits, Marks, and Joules — the three coins of the cooperative — are explicitly non-fungible to fiat. There is no IPO path. There is no exit liquidity that turns the founder into a billionaire. The structure refuses it.

**Influence is earned, not bought.** You cannot purchase votes on what the cooperative does. You earn standing through demonstrated judgment. The plutocrat path is closed.

These four constraints are not aspirations. They are bylaws. They will outlive me.

I am not pointing at Elon Musk and saying *thou art the man*. I am pointing at the structure that produced him and saying — *we refuse it*. The cooperative refuses it on behalf of every founder who, twenty years from now, might otherwise be standing where he stands. The cooperative refuses it on behalf of the poor man's one little ewe lamb. The cooperative refuses it because *I have read the parable, and I know who is at risk of being the rich man.*

It is me. It is every one of us. Without structure, it is always us.

So we built the structure.

You can join the cooperative for five dollars a year. Or you can read the bylaws and the math, and fork the whole thing, and build your own. We made the patents free. *For freely ye have received, freely give.* That is Matthew 10:8.

It rains on the just and the unjust.

That is the point.
