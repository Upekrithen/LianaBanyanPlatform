**The Substrate-Replaces-Data-Centers Argument**

Alphabet is building data centers to support the inference load of three billion Google Search users, hundreds of millions of Gemini users, and the Google Cloud enterprise inference market. Every data center requires land, power infrastructure, cooling, hardware procurement, and operating staff. The lead time from commitment to live compute is 18–36 months.

Cooperative substrate routes a portion of inference load to existing peer machines  -  cooperative members' devices, enrolled as substrate nodes. The compute already exists. The power is already purchased. The cooling already runs. The substrate routes the workload; the cooperative compensates the node operator; the data center buildout for that fraction of inference is deferred or eliminated.

At Alphabet's scale, even a 1% shift in inference routing to cooperative substrate reduces data-center demand meaningfully. At $175–185B in annual capex with 60% in compute, 1% of compute spend is $1.05B–$1.11B in annual infrastructure. The cooperative substrate routing does not have to absorb the full inference load to generate material savings. It only needs to absorb the fraction that runs efficiently on distributed peer hardware.
