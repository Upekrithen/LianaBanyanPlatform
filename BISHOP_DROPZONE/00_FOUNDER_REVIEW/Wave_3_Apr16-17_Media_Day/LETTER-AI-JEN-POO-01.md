**The Home Logistics Problem**

Home care workers face the worst of both worlds: the isolation of domestic work and the precarity of gig platforms. They're classified as independent contractors to avoid benefits, but managed like employees to ensure compliance. The platforms take 30%+ while providing no insurance, no training, and no path to ownership.

You've called this the "care economy" � work that makes all other work possible. But the economics of care work are broken at the structural level.

**What I've Built**

Liana Banyan is a cooperative platform operating on one principle: **Cost + 20%**.

Workers keep **83.3%** of every transaction. The margin is locked in the operating agreement � not by choice, but by legal structure. We cannot raise it. We cannot enshittify.
