**The JukeBox Initiative**

Liana Banyan includes a music licensing initiative called The JukeBox. The economics:

- Artist retention: **83.3%** of every transaction
- Platform margin: **Cost + 20%** (locked in operating agreement�cannot be changed)
- No "pro rata" pooling that dilutes small artists
- Direct licensing between creators and users
- Transparent, auditable payments via blockchain

The margin lock is the key. Spotify's economics can change because they're policy. Ours can't change because they're architecture. The operating agreement makes extraction structurally impossible.

**Why Music Licensing Specifically**

You've written about how streaming payouts have declined even as revenue has grown. The mechanism is clear: platforms optimize for their own margins, not artist welfare.

The JukeBox inverts this. Because our margin is fixed, the only way to increase platform revenue is to increase artist revenue. Aligned incentives, enforced by structure.
