## Abstract

The Liana Banyan patent portfolio comprises 1,243 documented innovations spanning physical-digital gaming (HexIsle), cross-border settlement systems (three-gear currency), cooperative gig platforms, and distributed manufacturing. This paper analyzes the addressable market size for each innovation category and projects conservative and optimistic licensing scenarios. We demonstrate that while individual Crown Jewel patents are valued at $1–5M each today, the aggregate addressable markets exceed $2 trillion across tabletop gaming, educational technology, cross-border payments, gig economy platforms, and distributed manufacturing. Under conservative penetration assumptions (0.1–1% market capture via licensing), the portfolio's 10-year value ranges from $500M to $5B+. When combined with platform-native manufacturing and service revenue, total ecosystem value approaches low trillions at scale.

## 1. Introduction

Patent portfolios are typically valued on a per-asset basis, often underestimating the combinatorial and cross-market potential of integrated innovation systems. The Liana Banyan portfolio, which includes 8 Crown Jewels with zero prior art and 1,235 supporting innovations, addresses five distinct but interconnected markets:

1. Tabletop gaming and model-building (HexIsle physical-digital games)
2. Educational technology and special education (STEM toys, assistive learning)
3. Cross-border payments and platform settlement (three-gear currency)
4. Gig economy platforms (cooperative labor networks)
5. Distributed manufacturing (3D printing, demand-validated production)

Each market is measured in tens to hundreds of billions annually. This paper quantifies the addressable opportunity across all five and models licensing scenarios under conservative and optimistic assumptions.
