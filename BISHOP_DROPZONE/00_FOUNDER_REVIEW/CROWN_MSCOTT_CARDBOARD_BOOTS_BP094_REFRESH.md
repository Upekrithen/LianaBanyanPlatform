# Cardboard Boots: An Open Letter to MacKenzie Scott

Dear Ms. Scott,

**I don't want your money. I'm asking for something far more valuable: your reputation, and one word of introduction to operators you trust.** Everything below is why I think the ask is worth your fifteen minutes, and what is already on the table -- built, signed, pledged -- whether or not you ever reply.

---

## 1. The Two Suits

In college, as a need-based scholarship student, I had two roommates. One was wealthy -- his family owned ten homes. The other was poor like me. When I needed a suit for an interview and couldn't afford one, my poor roommate immediately gave me one of the two he owned. Only afterward did my wealthy roommate offer one of his fifteen. That sacrifice thirty years ago still makes me cry, and it exemplifies the truth you wrote about which gift means more -- $100 from someone earning $50,000 or $100,000 from someone with millions.

I don't have money. I may have something more valuable.

---

## 2. What's Already on the Table (Plain)

As of today we have **22 USPTO provisional patents filed**, with the most recent lodged in June 2026. In controlled benchmarking our context-persistence architecture (the Romulator 9000, Prov 13, April 2026) measured **92.7% accuracy against 3.6%** for the same model without the preload, at comparable marginal cost. For a 200-person nonprofit running AI agents at production scale, that delta is hundreds of thousands of dollars a year -- and better work. Beyond that: on 2026-06-15, a single cooperative substrate node scored 97.1% on 70 MMLU-Pro questions with zero paid API calls. Single-node ceiling, sealed receipt. The cooperative mesh architecture goes further from there.

Under our **Cooperative Defensive Patent Pledge** -- signed into the operating agreement, not aspirational -- that capability is given permanently to a commons, free for every nonprofit, cooperative, and academic institution with an IRS-verified EIN. No application. No committee. No gatekeeping. Every organization your foundation has ever funded is eligible on day one. **The majority of the 22-patent portfolio is pledged permanently into the commons.**

30/30 trial waves passed. 28/28 structural proofs sealed. The substrate is not aspiration; it is empirically confirmed architecture.

That is what I bring to your table, alongside the ask.

---

## 3. Why You, Specifically

Three reasons, in order of weight:

1. **You have spent a decade dismantling the assumption that money flows top-down through gatekeepers.** $19 billion, no application process, no naming rights demanded, recipients chosen on the merits of the work. The cooperative substrate is the same instinct moved one layer down: into the platform itself, codified in the bylaws, so the instinct survives any single founder, any single donor, any single decade.
2. **You wrote the line about which gift means more.** That line is the architecture of our $5/year membership and our medallion-sponsorship seed: a no-strings $100 sponsorship each to ten waitresses, bus drivers, and laborers, who then forward into ten $10 memberships each. Geometric, not extractive.
3. **You read your own inbox.** That matters more than the other two.

---

## 4. The Nine and a Half Years

Over the last nine years and six months I've built a public, community-owned safety net of **sixteen charitable initiatives** designed to sustain themselves far beyond our lifetimes -- focused on economic mobility, food security, and cooperative healthcare for people who experience the sharpest side of inequality. The Sweet Sixteen is now structurally complete; each Initiative has a named Crown candidate, and second-wave Crown invitations are in dispatch this week.

I'm seeking a word from you to experienced leaders in your network who could help these Initiatives succeed at scale. **I pray for potatoes at the end of a hoe handle, but your endorsement would exponentially multiply every ask I make.** I'm prepared to hand any or all of these Initiatives -- and even the entire platform -- to leaders you trust.

Even one connection to someone experienced in food security, cooperative healthcare, or platform-scale governance -- who can vet the pitfalls I don't know about yet -- could prevent costly mistakes and multiply impact. I know enough to know I don't know enough.

## 5. The Specific Ask

If our work aligns with your values, would you point operators from your network my direction -- people who excel at scaling community-driven solutions, or ethical CTO, COO, or operational candidates, even fractional, who could steward this platform beyond my limited capacity?

This is not charity TO the People but infrastructure BY the People FOR the People -- *"nothing about us without us"* -- which applies your principle that communities removing obstacles for different groups become better for everyone. Member-owned. Member-governed. Member-benefiting.

Every member can start a business for $5/year, build their own designs or products, or get a job in another member's business -- all under the same cooperative terms. Three commercial websites sustainably fund the sixteen charitable initiatives on a fourth, through a baked-in **20% "Cost of Doing Good"** margin. The infrastructure is backed by **2,270 documented innovations** -- 99% utility patents, not design -- and **228 Crown Jewels** survived rigorous prior-art searches against the U.S. patent office with no prior art found. **What we need is people and leadership; the money will follow.**

## 6. The Medallion Seed

I'm launching the alpha by using half of my family's emergency savings to seed the launch through our medallion sponsorship program: a no-strings-attached $100 sponsorship each to ten waitresses, bus drivers, and laborers who would never otherwise see any of these opportunities to change their lives. Each recipient can share their sponsorship forward into ten $10 memberships -- or more, with involvement. One bus driver becomes ten transit workers becomes hundreds. Under the Pledge, that same cooperative architecture is available to every nonprofit your foundation has ever funded, on the same terms.

The medallion cascade distributes ownership broadly from the outset. Surplus distributions are allocated proportionally across all medallion-holders -- weighted by contribution record. A founding medallion-holder's larger distribution reflects their larger total contribution record, not a passive claim on others' labor. This mirrors how established worker cooperatives distribute patronage dividends: in proportion to each member's active participation in the cooperative's economic activity.

Our economics show 500 members in any locale reach break-even, around 1,000 sustainably profitable, with surplus dedicated to local initiatives.
