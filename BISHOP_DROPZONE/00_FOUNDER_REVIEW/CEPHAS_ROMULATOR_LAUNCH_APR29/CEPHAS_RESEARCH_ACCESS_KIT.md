In the same period, users of Gemini 3.1 Pro's 1M-token context window filed formal complaints of progressive context degradation — the model "forgetting" established details by prompt 21–25 in long sessions, with users forced to manually rebuild context as a workaround. The Romulator addresses a different architectural layer than either: not compression, not window size, but verified deterministic pre-loading that eliminates re-ingestion entirely. Researchers studying the Romulator architecture will find it useful to examine these parallel developments alongside the session archive — they document the same problem arriving at our door from three different directions simultaneously.

## How to Request Access

Send a one-page note describing:

1. **Who you are** — affiliation, research focus, prior relevant work
2. **What you want** — which archive slices, what time window
3. **What you intend** — publication venue, expected timeline, whether collaboration or independent coverage
4. **Terms** — standard research agreement, case-specific NDA, or something else

Email: **research@lianabanyan.com**

**Response target**: 72 hours.

## What We Ask in Return

- **Cite the source.** The commons depends on attribution.
- **Share findings.** Send a pre-print or link when you publish.
- **Reciprocate where you can.** If your institution produces related data (agent benchmarks, economic studies), consider contributing to the commons under the same terms we offer here.
- **Be honest.** If the architecture doesn't deliver on a specific claim under your independent testing, we want to hear it first, in writing, before you publish. We will respond with corrections or concessions in under 72 hours.

## Why This Exists

Most companies treat research access as a marketing surface. Controlled briefings. Approved quotes. Embargo dates.

We are building a commons. Commons don't work that way.

If the Romulator architecture, the cooperative economics, or the #2260 Pledge framework only works when filtered through public relations, it does not work. We are betting that full access makes the work stronger, not weaker — and the six months of archive is the test.
