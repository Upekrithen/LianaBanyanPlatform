## Licensing, in Plain English

Every AI company has three choices about the Romulator.

**Tier 1 — Reference Implementation.** They take the code. Direct support. "Romulator" product branding. Highest cost. Fastest time to value.

**Tier 2 — Spec + Conformance.** They build their own to the spec. Submit to the TCK. If they pass, they earn the "Romulator-Compliant 9000" certification mark for their product. Per-seat or per-inference royalty. Plus a reciprocal pledge — their related IP joins the commons. This is the tier for Microsoft, Google, Anthropic, Meta, OpenAI.

**Tier 3 — Pledge-Only.** Academics, nonprofits, strategic allies. Patent grant. Commons membership. No mark, no royalty. Just the reciprocal pledge back into the commons.

**Why the shape matters**: a Tier-2 licensee is not paying a toll to a competitor. They are buying a seat at a commons that makes the whole industry safer. Every licensee's pledge strengthens every other licensee's protection. The more join, the stronger everyone's position becomes.

**The 80% give-away is the bootstrap mechanism.** If the Founder retained everything, no one would join the commons. If the Founder gave everything, no one would pay. The 80/20 split is the tuning point where the incentives align for all three tiers at once.
