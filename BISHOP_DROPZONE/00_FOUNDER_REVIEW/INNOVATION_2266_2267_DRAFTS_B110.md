## Innovation #2266 (was #2244) — Opt-In Member Documentation with Benefits

**Category:** Member Engagement / Growth Infrastructure / Open Water Architecture
**Session:** B097 (originally) / B109 (formalization)
**CJ status:** Candidate (pending Founder ratification)
**Parent cluster:** #2240 Open Water (7-Tier Vessel Growth Advisory)

### The problem it solves

Members who grow through the Open Water advisory process (Levels 0→6) carry valuable lived knowledge — *what worked, what didn't, what the unspoken assumption turned out to be* — but that knowledge dies with the engagement unless someone captures it. Traditional mentorship platforms either lose this layer entirely (the match ends, the tacit knowledge walks away) or capture it extractively (the platform owns the member's story and monetizes it without sharing).

### The mechanism

**Every Member in an Open Water engagement is invited to document their journey as it unfolds.** Participation is opt-in, never required. What they document, they own. The platform captures:

- The brief they published (public or private per their choice)
- The Patron they selected and why
- Quarterly check-ins: what worked, what surprised, what they'd tell themselves at the start
- Resolution: target vs. outcome; Level transition or no-fault exit
- Retrospective: one-paragraph what-they-know-now that they didn't know when they started

**Benefits to opt-in documenters:**
1. **Guide contribution credit (#2245)** — documented journeys become part of the Member-Generated Guide Corpus that the next cohort uses to select Patrons and write better briefs. Contributors earn SAA on every future engagement that cites their documented journey.
2. **Reputation flag** — "Contributes to the commons" becomes a visible flag on the Member's profile, making them a more-trusted Patron candidate when they step into that role at the next Level.
3. **Mark reward** — a fixed allocation of Marks per documented milestone (first brief, first quarterly check-in, resolution retrospective), capped to prevent gaming.
4. **Priority Patron access** — opt-in documenters get first-look at new Patron volunteer pools when they publish their next brief.

### The epistemology

This is the member-side of the "No Unwitnessed AI Output" principle applied to human mentorship: Open Water's claims about what *works* must be verified by the people who walked the path, documented by them, and held by the commons. The platform does not own their stories; the platform pays them to share.

- #2241 Ripple (members who back Open Water engagements without being the primary Patron)
- #2242 Peer-Proximity Expertise Matching (the thesis that context-proximate mentors outperform altitude-expert ones)
- #2245 Member-Generated Guide Corpus (the downstream corpus this feeds)
- No Effort Is Wasted (#Pudding 24, #2140) — documentation is never wasted even if the engagement exits early

### Crown Jewel case

Every other mentorship platform extracts member stories for marketing (LinkedIn success stories, podcast appearances, case studies) without paying the members who lived them. #2244 pays, credits, and renders visible the lived knowledge — and feeds it back into the selection mechanism for the next cohort. The combination of *opt-in* + *paid in Marks* + *credited to a public corpus* + *visible as a reputation flag* is the novel system.

## Innovation #2267 (was #2245) — Member-Generated Guide Corpus

**Category:** Commons Infrastructure / Collective Intelligence / Open Water Architecture
**Session:** B097 (originally) / B109 (formalization)
**CJ status:** Candidate (pending Founder ratification)
**Parent cluster:** #2240 Open Water (7-Tier Vessel Growth Advisory) + #2244 Opt-In Member Documentation

### The problem it solves

The canonical playbooks for scaling a business — the YC startup school, the Stanford MBA case library, the Shark Tank archive — are written by people who already scaled, looking back. What's missing is the playbook written by people *in the middle of walking the path, step by step, one rung at a time*. A Level 0 Member looking to activate their first paper route does not need to read about how Warby Parker scaled from $1M to $100M; they need to read what the Member one Level ahead of them did in the first month.

### The mechanism

**The Member-Generated Guide Corpus is the aggregated, editorialized, searchable body of documented journeys (fed by #2244).** Each entry is:

- **Indexed by Level transition** — Level 0→1, Level 1→2, …, Level 5→6
- **Indexed by Cold Start Pathway** — Food, Manufacturing, Service, Local Business, Guild, Tribe
- **Tagged by industry and geography** — so a Member in rural Montana filtering for "paper route 0→1" gets content from rural paper-route journeys, not urban ride-share ones
- **Editorialized by the Librarian** — the SP-15-equivalent editorial layer distills each documented journey into: what happened, what was decided, what the contributor would tell themselves at the start
- **Continuously refreshed** — new documented journeys flow in automatically; stale entries (≥ 3 years old with no refresh) are moved to an archive tier, not deleted

**Access is tiered:**
- **Pledge-Only (free)** — nonprofits, cooperatives, academic institutions use the full corpus with no fee under the Cooperative Defensive Patent Pledge
- **Members (included in $5/yr)** — any LB Member can query the corpus
- **Commercial use** — per-query or subscription under Spec+Conformance Tier of the Pledge

### The epistemology

This is Peer-Proximity Expertise Matching (#2242) operating at corpus-scale: instead of pairing a Member with one Patron who walked the adjacent rung, #2245 lets a Member read *hundreds* of people who walked the adjacent rung, filtered to their exact context. The network effect is asymmetric: every new documented journey makes the corpus more useful to every future reader, while the documenter is paid in Marks for the contribution.

### Related
- #2240 Open Water (parent cluster)
- #2242 Peer-Proximity Expertise Matching (the thesis)
- #2244 Opt-In Member Documentation with Benefits (the feeder mechanism)
- #2260 Cooperative Defensive Patent Pledge (the legal structure that keeps the corpus free for the commons)
- SP-15 Editorial Archaeologist (architectural analogue — the editorial-distillation layer the Librarian would run on contributed journeys)

### Crown Jewel case

No other mentorship or scale-advisory platform has built a peer-contributed, peer-paid, peer-indexed, commons-licensed corpus of documented growth journeys. The individual components exist in fragments (LinkedIn journeys, Y Combinator blog posts, Substack founders' letters), but none of them are (a) indexed by Level transition, (b) filtered by Cold Start Pathway, (c) editorialized by a canonical layer, (d) paid-in for contribution via Marks, AND (e) commons-free for nonprofit/cooperative/academic use under a Patent Pledge. The combination is the CJ.
