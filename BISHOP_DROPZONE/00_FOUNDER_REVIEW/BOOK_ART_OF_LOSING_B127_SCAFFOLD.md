## Reframed thesis (B127 v2)

**The book is about how Founder built Liana Banyan, told through the productive-failure arc.** The losses are not the spine — the building is the spine. Each loss earned a piece of the LB design. The Art of Losing is the title because the wisdom that became LB is wisdom from the losses.

Founder framing B127 (verbatim):
> "I think The Art of Losing has enough on its own to actually be a book about how I made Liana Banyan. Which is not really what Ordinary Worlds is about, so that can be my second book - a novel. Seriously. I have many notebooks about that as well, to transcribe. In my spare time."

So the structural question shifts. v1 organized by failure-class (loss → lesson). v2 organizes by LB-architecture-build (LB primitive → loss that taught it).
