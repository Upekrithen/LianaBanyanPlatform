# How to Save the World in Six Easy Steps

## A Business Plan

*Not left or right. A more effective team.*

---

*A little girl told her mother she wanted to be an astronaut.*

*Her mother, trying to be helpful, said: "Well, sweetheart, you'll need advanced college degrees. You'll need to serve in the military and get piloting hours. And then three years of experience leading others."*

*The girl looked at her mom and shrugged.*

*"No problem. That's just, like, three things."*

---

Figure out how to end World Hunger. Tell Everyone. And give them a selfish reason to do it.

That is, in full, the business plan. What follows is the machinery.

The selfish reason is not charity. It is not an appeal to conscience. It is mathematics. When you can pick apples and your neighbor can deliver them, and the platform pays each of you fairly -- Creators AND Workers keep **83.3%** of every transaction, every time, locked by constitutional bylaw -- cooperation becomes the rational choice. Not the noble choice. The smart choice.

That is the insight the cooperative economy has been waiting for. Not a better appeal to altruism. A better deal.

This is a business plan with six steps. Each step is a self-contained economic engine that feeds the next. Each step starts with one person doing one thing in one place. Each step scales without permission, without venture capital, and without anyone's approval but the people doing the work.

What you will find is a cooperative commerce platform built from a six-month pivot out of a hydraulic gaming platform project. When the Founder saw the real need, he dropped the original plan and shipped. The result: **2,270 documented innovations**, **22 USPTO provisional patent applications**, **16 live initiatives**, and a three-currency economic system with mathematical solvency proofs. Built by one veteran, four AI agents, and nine years of obsession.

That is just, like, six things.

---

**The economics are the same every step.** Cost+20%. That is the platform margin. Not "up to" -- exactly. Locked in the operating agreement. No board vote changes it. Creators AND Workers keep **83.3%** of every transaction. Membership: **$5 per year**. That is the stake. That is all you can lose. 500 members equals break-even. 1,000 equals profitable. In any locale.

---

**Step 1: Level the Field.** The biggest cost in deploying AI today is not the model. It is rebuilding knowledge from scratch on every call. MnemosyneC is the cooperative's answer. One AI answers a question; the answer becomes shared by every AI that asks it next. We proved it: 97.1% Trial_01 sealed at BP083 single-node Plow -- 68 of 70 questions correct on one consumer laptop, zero paid API calls, free open-weights model. The substrate moat is the architecture, not the parameters. Every peer who joins makes the cooperative smarter for every other member.

**Step 2: Feed Your Neighbors.** My grandmother was a waitress for fifty years. When she worked, everyone at her table was her responsibility -- no one left hungry, no one left poorly served. She kept barely a fraction of what her work was worth to the restaurant and less again to the platform economy that had not yet been invented. Let's Make Dinner coordinates families cooking for one another on rotating schedules. Let's Make Bread is the local incubator where neighbors turn recipes into co-owned food businesses at Cost+20%. A cook keeps 83.3%. A delivery driver keeps 83.3%. A food photographer who images the menu keeps 83.3%. Nobody was rescued. Everybody benefited. Food is the first node in every economic network. When the first real dollar flows, everything else becomes possible.

**Step 3: Employ the World.** Everyone has something useful to do. The problem is not a shortage of work -- it is a shortage of fair arrangements for doing it.

Rally Group dispatches community crews when a wildfire hits before FEMA arrives. Defense Klaus gives workers a shared legal defense fund and community safety infrastructure.

In January 2026, KSAT News documented San Antonians using informal peer ridesharing through Facebook groups -- cash upfront, no background checks, no insurance. A driver was shot when a passenger refused to pay the fare. The cooperative infrastructure exists to make that demand safe to meet.

The Mirror Clause goes further: large organizations licensing the platform must staff one cooperative member for every internal role they fill. Technology adoption becomes a hiring engine.

**Step 4: Build Businesses and Make Things.** Anyone can try any venture for $5. Treasure Maps: $5 try-out. Eight ready-made business plans on the back. Pick one. Start. Employment gives people a job. Business ownership gives them a stake. The cooperative is designed to produce both.

**Step 5: Power to the People.** This is the Switzerland step. By the time a community has shared food, shared work, and shared ownership, it has built the legitimacy to share governance. Political Expedition is the civic-action arm: nonpartisan transparency tools and accountability infrastructure built by the people it governs. A community that has fed itself, employed itself, and built its own businesses has earned the right to govern itself.

**Step 6: Belong Together.** A Guild is professional -- what you do. A Tribe is personal -- who you are. The Muses is the creative community layer, the guild for every starving artist who is done starving. Didasko is the learning and teaching arm where knowledge passes from those who have it to those who need it, at Cost+20%, with the teacher keeping 83.3%. Belonging is the last to arrive and the one that makes everything else sustainable. People do not fight for platforms. They fight for communities. The platform is the substrate. The community is the point.
