**Why Cooperative Substrate and Stargate Compose**

Stargate is a hardware-layer solution: build more compute, reduce per-unit cost at scale. Cooperative substrate is a routing-layer solution: shift inference workloads to cooperative peer machines that already exist, reducing the need for new compute at each inference event. These are not competing bets. They compose.

The Stargate buildout assumes the current inference-routing model  -  centralized cloud, cost-per-token billing  -  continues. Cooperative substrate introduces a second routing rail: cooperative-mesh inference, where compute is provided by the cooperative's peer fleet and compensated through the Substitution Rail (Credits/Marks/Joules  -  no fiat extraction). The fee to OpenAI as a licensee comes from the savings generated on the first rail when workloads shift to the second.

Put differently: cooperative substrate does not compete with Stargate. It reduces the fraction of inference that needs to flow through Stargate at all. The fraction that does flow through Stargate is now more profitable because the substrate is absorbing the high-frequency, lower-complexity inference load that does not require flagship compute.
