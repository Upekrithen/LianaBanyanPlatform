### 1. The Moratorium Act (March 25, 2026)

**What it is:** Sen. Bernie Sanders (I-VT) and Rep. Alexandria Ocasio-Cortez (D-NY-14) jointly introduced the **Artificial Intelligence Data Center Moratorium Act of 2026** on **March 25, 2026.**

**What it does:** Imposes a nationwide halt on constructing or upgrading data centers with a power demand of 20 MW or more until "strong national safeguards" are in place.

**AOC's own words (March 25, 2026):** "Congress has a moral obligation to stand with the American people and stop the expansion of these data centers until we have a framework to adequately address the existential harm AI poses to our society." Also: "We must choose humanity over profit."

**Stated concerns per press release:**
- AI data centers increase electricity and utility prices for consumers
- Environmental harm to communities near facilities
- Economic gains flow only to wealthy tech owners, not workers
- No federal framework to address ICE surveillance partnerships, deepfakes, or other AI harms

**Legislative prospects:** Near-zero in the current Congress. The bill is a messaging vehicle and a marker, not a near-term floor vote.

**Pushback from within the Democratic Party:** Sen. John Fetterman (D-PA) publicly called the moratorium "China First" in a Fox News interview, signaling the bill has opposition from moderate Democrats. This is a live political landmine (see Section 7 below).

Sources: [Sanders press release](https://www.sanders.senate.gov/press-releases/news-sanders-ocasio-cortez-announce-ai-data-center-moratorium-act/) | [Al Jazeera](https://www.aljazeera.com/economy/2026/3/26/us-lawmakers-push-for-pause-in-data-centres-until-ai-safeguards-in-place) | [Scripps News](https://www.scrippsnews.com/politics/sanders-ocasio-cortez-push-bill-to-impose-ai-data-center-moratorium)

### 2. The Brown Water Hearing (May 20, 2026)

**What happened:** At a House Energy and Commerce Subcommittee on Oversight and Investigations hearing on **May 20, 2026**, Ocasio-Cortez held up two jars of visibly brown, contaminated water from **Morgan County, Georgia**, near Meta's Stanton Springs data center campus east of Atlanta.

**AOC's exact words at the hearing:** "I have a jar right here, this is the current drinking water in Morgan County, Georgia. Neither one of these things are drinkable, right?" She also stated that "These families now have to ship water to their house in order to cook and bathe themselves."

**The specific allegation:** Meta's Stanton Springs data center consumes **10% of Morgan County's daily water supply.** The county is projected to reach a total water deficit by 2030. Residents report brown tap water they cannot drink or cook with.

**Meta's defense:** Commissioned an independent groundwater study that found data center operations had no impact on residents; Meta says all water is sourced from the local utility, not groundwater.

**EPA response:** Jessica Kramer (EPA water office) committed under oath to investigate: "I will be looking into exactly what you've just talked about... it is a priority to ensure the water quality standards established by EPA are being met." EPA promised an immediate investigation.

**Why this matters for the letter:** This is the single most visceral, viral moment of AOC's AI data center advocacy. Two jars of brown water on a hearing table is a stronger lede than the Ashburn aerial photo in V02. The letter should reference it.

Sources: [CBS Atlanta](https://www.cbsnews.com/atlanta/news/aoc-presses-epa-over-morgan-county-drinking-water-concerns-tied-to-georgia-data-center-development/) | [Tom's Hardware](https://www.tomshardware.com/tech-industry/big-tech/meta-data-center-allegedly-muddies-georgia-towns-drinking-water-investigation-underway-epa-promises-immediate-investigation-after-congresswoman-brings-dirty-jars-of-water-to-hearing) | [HuffPost](https://www.huffpost.com/entry/alexandria-ocasio-cortez-epa-data-centers-water-pollution_n_6a0f8ecfe4b084c012e4d5d1)

### 3. The Grid/Energy Case

**Electricity bill projections:** Electric bills are on track to rise an average of **8% nationwide by 2030** to cover the costs of grid expansion driven by data center load. Data centers are estimated to account for **44% of new U.S. electricity demand by 2028.**

**DOE study (secured by AOC):** The FY26 Energy and Water Appropriations Act includes a study on demand-response and load flexibility as potential solutions to ease the energy burden of AI data centers on consumers and prevent blackouts. AOC secured this provision specifically because of data center concerns.

**The regulatory framing AOC is making:** She is not arguing AI is bad. She is arguing the *externalities of the current data center architecture* -- power draw, water consumption, home energy bill increases -- are being imposed on communities without consent or compensation.

Sources: [Food and Water Watch](https://www.foodandwaterwatch.org/2026/03/25/sanders-aoc-ai-data-center-moratorium-act-is-desperately-needed-as-americans-energy-bills-skyrocket/) | [Ocasio-Cortez.house.gov DOE study press release](https://ocasio-cortez.house.gov/media/press-releases/ocasio-cortez-secures-department-energy-study-impact-ai-data-centers-americans)
