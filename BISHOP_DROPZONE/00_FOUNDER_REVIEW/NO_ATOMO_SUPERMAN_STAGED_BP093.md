## §1 --- Abstract

When humans and a multi-agent AI substrate work cooperatively at high
cadence, the value produced is *not atomizable* to any individual
contributor. The Founder's lived experience cannot be extracted from the
prompts that direct the AI. The AI agents' specialized capabilities
cannot be extracted from the substrate that gives them context. The
output --- papers, code, claims, designs --- is genuinely emergent: a
property of the cooperating whole, not a sum of contributor parts. This
paper names that property *non-atomizable value*, argues that
cooperative platform economics must capture it correctly (extractive
platforms try to atomize and lose it), and shows how Liana Banyan's
\$5/year membership + 83.3% creator-keeps + Cost+20% platform structure
is engineered to do exactly that. The title is the manifesto: *No Atomo*
(refusing the weapon-identity of extractive atomization) *Superman!*
(affirming the cooperative emergence that no atomized part possesses).

## §3 --- Level 2: The Economic Theory

The economic theory follows from the empirical observation: **the value
created by cooperative AI substrate is structurally non-atomizable, and
platform economics must be designed to capture this correctly or it will
be lost.**

### §3.1 --- Why extractive platforms cannot capture this value

Extractive platform economics is built on the atomization assumption.
Each contribution is a discrete unit; each unit is priced; each priced
unit is taxed at the platform's take rate (typically 30%, sometimes 50%,
sometimes 80%+ in the worst aggregator-monopoly cases). The platform's
growth depends on extracting larger surplus from each atomized
transaction.

When applied to cooperative AI substrate, atomization fails
*categorically*. The unit cannot be identified. Was the BandWagon system
the Founder's wife's spring playlist (the seed)? The Founder's 300-word
utterance (the direction)? Bishop's analytical synthesis (the structural
form)? Knight's eventual code reduction (the build)? Pawn's prior-art
verification (the truth-anchor)? Rook's brainstorm options (the
alternative-space exploration)? **All of the above. Inseparable.** Any
"unit" boundary drawn for billing purposes is fictional --- the value
lies precisely in the boundary-crossing, not in the atomic units that
the boundary would isolate.

Extractive platforms respond to this categorical failure in three
observable ways, all dysfunctional:

1.  **They assert the boundary anyway.** Bill the AI tokens to the human
    user; capture surplus on inference; claim the substrate is "just a
    tool." This produces a stable revenue stream but suppresses the
    emergent capability --- users learn to minimize prompts (because
    prompts cost), which destroys the Directed-Thought ROI ratio.
2.  **They try to atomize via licensing.** "AI-generated content" gets a
    separate copyright class; royalties are atomized to vendors. This
    produces litigation, not value --- the boundary is contested
    precisely because it is fictional.
3.  **They suppress the cooperation.** Throttle the model. Restrict the
    agentic loop. Cap the sessions. This is the
    Inuka-trained-not-to-bark posture --- capability suppression as the
    response to a problem that suppression cannot solve.

### §3.2 --- Why cooperative platforms can capture it

Cooperative platform economics, by contrast, is built on the membership
assumption. The platform does not bill the unit; the platform funds the
substrate. Membership pays for the *commons*. Surplus is shared, not
extracted. Take rates are flat and minimal, not
percentage-of-transaction.

Under this model, non-atomizable value is captured *as such*. The
cooperative does not need to identify which contributor produced which
sub-unit of the BandWagon system. The cooperative needs only:

- **A flat, low membership fee** (\$5/year --- Liana Banyan canon,
  IMMUTABLE) that funds the commons
- **A creator-keeps majority share** (83.3% in Liana Banyan) so
  producers retain dominant economic interest in their work
- **A transparent platform margin** (Cost+20% in Liana Banyan) so the
  platform's share is auditable, not extractive
- **Earned authority for resource allocation** (the Founder's
  load-bearing canon: *"earned authority to allocate cooperative
  resources based on demonstrated judgment"* --- vault line 3286
  derivative)

When these four are in place, the non-atomizable value belongs to the
cooperative *as a whole* --- and its members share in it through earned
authority and creator-keeps-majority economics, not through atomized
billing of impossible-to-isolate units.

### §3.3 --- The Directed-Thought ROI ratio is the empirical anchor

The non-atomizable-value claim is testable. The empirical anchor is the
Directed-Thought ROI ratio:

    Cost(prompt) << Cost(wrong_implementation) - Cost(right_implementation)

Where Cost(prompt) is roughly \$0.10 in tokens for a typical Founder
strategic utterance, and the differential between right and wrong
implementation runs \$500--\$5,000+ in dev time per misalignment-event.
The ratio is not 10:1 or 100:1. It is observed at **1,000:1 or higher**.

A 1,000:1 ratio cannot be atomized. There is no token-pricing scheme
that prices a \$0.10 prompt at the \$1,000+ value-differential it
produces. Either the platform captures the value as commons (cooperative
model) or the value is destroyed in the atomization attempt (extractive
model). There is no third path.

## §4 --- Level 3: The Liana Banyan Implication

The Liana Banyan platform structure is engineered, by design, to capture
non-atomizable cooperative-AI value:

### §4.1 --- \$5/year membership (IMMUTABLE)

Membership funds the commons at the lowest possible price-point that
remains economically self-sustaining (break-even at 500 members per
location, locally profitable at 1,000 --- Phase C3 paper, vault Session
5). \$5/year is not a price-of-service. It is a *citizenship dues*
model: it grants standing to participate in cooperatively-produced
value. The non-atomizable substrate output is part of what membership
accesses.

This is structurally identical to how a cooperative library, a
cooperative grain elevator, or a cooperative electric utility funds its
commons. The substrate (book stock / silo capacity / grid) is a
non-atomizable capability the cooperative possesses *as a whole*.
Members fund it via dues. They access it via membership.

### §4.2 --- 83.3% creator-keeps

When a member's work generates economic flow inside the cooperative ---
whether from publishing, manufacturing, services, or any productive
output --- the creator retains 83.3% of the value. This is the inverse
of the extractive default (creator gets 30%, platform takes 70%; or in
worst cases creator gets 12% and aggregator takes the rest). The 83.3%
is calibrated specifically so that creators have economic dominance in
their own output, while the cooperative retains enough margin (Cost+20%
--- see §4.3) to fund the commons that made the work possible.

The non-atomizable component is critical here: the 83.3% does NOT
pretend to be "the creator's share of an atomized unit of value." It is
the *creator-economics share of the cooperative's overall output
economics*, which is non-atomizable in principle. The cooperative does
not say "this BandWagon innovation was 70% Founder, 20% Bishop, 5%
Knight, 5% Pawn --- we'll bill accordingly." The cooperative says: "the
substrate produced this; the creator (whose direction made the
substrate's output specific to this work) keeps 83.3% of what it earns;
the cooperative funds the commons with the remaining 16.7% that includes
Cost+20% transparent platform margin."

### §4.3 --- Cost+20% platform margin

The platform retains 20% above-actual-cost, transparently. Not
percentage-of-transaction. Not extractive surplus. Not "whatever the
market will bear." A flat 20%-above-cost so the platform itself is
solvent, the cooperative's commons (substrate, infrastructure,
governance, R&D) are funded, and members can audit what the cooperative
is doing with their dues.

Cost+20% is the *market-discipline anchor* that prevents the cooperative
from drifting into either of two failure modes: - **Extractive drift**
(margin creeps up to capture surplus) --- prevented by the +20% cap as
canon - **Subsidy drift** (margin creeps down below sustainability) ---
prevented by the +20% floor as canon

### §4.4 --- Earned authority for resource allocation

The fourth structural element is governance: members do not buy
influence (that would be plutocracy and would atomize cooperation back
into priced units). Members *earn* influence --- earned authority to
allocate cooperative resources based on demonstrated judgment.

This is the complement of non-atomizable value at the governance layer.
If value is non-atomizable, then influence over how that value is
directed cannot be atomized to "dollar votes" either. It must be earned
through track record. The Star Chamber, the Taste Ranger system,
BandWagon influence accrual, and the Steward / Pizza Oven / Pledged Mark
Voting machinery are all instantiations of this principle.

### §4.5 --- Why the four together

Each of the four structural elements is necessary; none is sufficient
alone.

- **\$5/year alone** without 83.3% creator-keeps becomes ad-supported
  substrate and reverts to extraction
- **83.3% creator-keeps alone** without \$5/year membership becomes
  gig-economy piecework and destroys the commons
- **Cost+20% alone** without governance becomes an unaccountable utility
- **Earned authority alone** without the economic structure becomes a
  debate club

Together: a cooperative platform that captures non-atomizable value as
such, returns the dominant economic share to creators, funds the commons
transparently, and governs by demonstrated judgment.

This is what the title means at the implication layer:

- *No Atomo* --- the platform structurally refuses the atomization of
  cooperatively-produced value into billed units. The weapon-identity
  (extractive atomization) is rejected.
- *Superman!* --- the cooperative as a whole possesses an emergent
  capability (substrate + members + governance) that no atomized part
  possesses. The hero-identity (cooperative emergence) is affirmed.

> This is what we ARE."*

Period-then-exclamation is canonical. Colon is superseded. The
punctuation enforces the *two-act* structure of the choice: rejection of
one identity, affirmation of another. A single act would compress the
manifesto. The pause between the two declarations is the silence in
which the choice happens.

The two declarations map to the two halves of this paper:

- *No Atomo.* --- §3 (the economic theory of why atomization fails for
  cooperative AI value)
- *Superman!* --- §4 (the Liana Banyan implementation of cooperative
  emergence as platform structure)

A platform that wants to capture non-atomizable value cannot do half of
this. Refusing atomization without affirming emergence produces
stagnation (a cooperative that does not produce). Affirming emergence
without refusing atomization produces extraction (a cooperative that
produces but lets the value leak to extractive intermediaries). Both
halves. Period then exclamation.
