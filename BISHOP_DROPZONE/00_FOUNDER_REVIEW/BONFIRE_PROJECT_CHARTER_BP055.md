# Bonfire Project — Research Arm Operational Charter
## Independent Research into Cooperative Economics, AI Substrate, and Social Impact
**BP055 · May 24, 2026 · Knight**

---

## § 0 — What the Bonfire Project Is

The **Bonfire Project** (CAI Bonfire Project™) is the platform's independent research arm — a cooperative-class research organization that produces the intellectual infrastructure for the platform and the broader cooperative-AI ecosystem.

Named for the bonfire as a gathering point: people come from different directions, contribute fuel, and share warmth and light. No one person owns the fire. Everyone benefits from it. The research Bonfire produces is similarly: contributed from different domains, available to all, owned by no single investor.

The Bonfire Project operates under SSPL licensing (Server-Side Public License), which means its research outputs remain open for cooperative use and cannot be proprietary-captured by extractive commercial actors.

---

## § 1 — Mission

> **To conduct independent research into cooperative economics, cooperative AI substrate architecture, and the social impact of member-owned digital platforms — and to publish those findings in ways that grow the cooperative-class intellectual commons.**

**Not**: a marketing arm. Not a content farm. Not a competitive intelligence function.

**Yes**: serious research, publishable findings, patent prior art, canonical Eblets, and white papers that make the cooperative-class argument better than anyone else is making it.

## § 3 — Governance

**Research Council (3 persons):**
- **Seat 1**: Founder (Jonathan Jones) — final voice on research priorities and canonical alignment
- **Seat 2**: Academic liaison (TBD — targeted to cooperative economics academic community)
- **Seat 3**: Community representative (elected from Captain tier; 1-year rotating term)

**Operating principles:**
- Research priorities are proposed by any member, filtered through the Research Council, approved by member vote above a threshold of significance
- No research agenda item can be funded without a clear articulation of how it benefits the cooperative membership
- All research outputs are published under SSPL licensing; the cooperative reserves no proprietary claim over research outputs (only over platform implementations)

**Quorum**: Any two of three for routine approvals; all three for budget allocations over $500 or for decisions to publish research with direct competitive implications

---

## § 4 — Funding

**Source**: Cooperative surplus allocation — drawn from the 10% infrastructure portion of the 60/20/10/10 surplus distribution formula

**Budget structure (at scale)**:
- $0–2,500 members: Research budget funded by Founder discretionary allocation; Bonfire operates on sweat equity
- 2,500–10,000 members: 10% infrastructure surplus generates meaningful Bonfire budget (est. $500–2,000/year at modest transaction volume)
- 10,000+ members: Bonfire operates as a self-sustaining research division funded by cooperative surplus; may seek external cooperative-aligned grants

**No VC, no extractive sponsors**: The Bonfire Project does not accept funding from venture capital firms, extractive platform corporations, or any entity whose interests conflict with cooperative-class outcomes. Cooperative-aligned foundations and academic partnerships are eligible.
