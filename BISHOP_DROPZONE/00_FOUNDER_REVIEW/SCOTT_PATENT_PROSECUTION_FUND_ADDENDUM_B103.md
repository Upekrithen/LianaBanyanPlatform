## The Patent Prosecution Fund

On April 12, 2026, I filed Provisional Patent Application 64/036,646 — the thirteenth in a series — covering the Romulator 9000, an AI coordination architecture we are placing into a public cooperative patent commons. I have a conversion deadline of November 26, 2026. Converting thirteen provisionals into full utility patents costs approximately $3-6 million in attorney fees at standard rates.

We have established a **Patent Prosecution Fund** housed in Upekrithen LLC, a wholly-owned subsidiary of Liana Banyan Corporation.

The Fund:

- Pays patent counsel only. Not salaries. Not operations. Not overhead.
- Is governed separately from the cooperative's revenue-sharing structure (the 60/20/10/10 IP split that governs licensing income)
- Dissolves when the conversion is complete. It is not an endowment.
- Never touches my personal finances
- Is auditable at any time against invoices from patent counsel of record

A grant to the Fund is a grant to the commons — specifically, to the legal work required to make 2,412 patent claims defensible before the November deadline. After that date, any unconverted provisional becomes public domain. The clock is real.

## Why This Preserves the Frame

You have a pattern: unrestricted grants, no conditions, the recipient decides how it serves the mission. This ask is narrower than your usual pattern, not broader. The Fund has a single legal purpose and a defined end state.

"I don't want your money" is still true for me personally. What I'm asking is whether you would fund the legal infrastructure that converts a private innovation archive into a permanent public commons — protecting 222 no-prior-art innovations for every researcher, cooperative, and community organization that will run on this architecture for the next twenty years.

If the Fund never receives a dollar, we pursue the pro bono route, the micro-patron campaign, the DIY filings. We will convert what we can convert and let the rest expire into the public domain gracefully. We don't need the Fund to survive. We need it to do this right.
