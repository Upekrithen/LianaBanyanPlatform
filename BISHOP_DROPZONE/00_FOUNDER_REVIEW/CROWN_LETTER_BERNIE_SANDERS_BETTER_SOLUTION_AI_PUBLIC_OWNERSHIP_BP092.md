### WHERE THE BILL IS VULNERABLE

The *American AI Sovereign Wealth Fund Act* faces structural vulnerabilities that will be exploited in litigation and in legislative maneuvering. These are not arguments against your goal  -  they are arguments for a complementary mechanism that delivers the same goal while the bill fights through:

- **OpenAI is not profitable  -  no dividends to distribute.** Your op-ed acknowledges this directly. A sovereign wealth fund holding OpenAI equity at IPO receives shares in a company that has never posted a profit. The Alaska Permanent Fund model distributes investment returns. There are no returns yet. The fund distributes nothing to your constituents until OpenAI profitability  -  which is unresolved.
- **50% mandatory equity transfer → 5+ years of constitutional litigation.** The Takings Clause challenge will be filed within hours of passage. Corporate lawyers will argue this is an uncompensated taking of private property. This does not mean you lose  -  but the mechanism delivers nothing while the case winds through courts.
- **Companies will offshore IPOs or restructure to avoid equity reach.** xAI is already offshore-friendly. OpenAI's capped-profit restructuring creates ambiguity about what equity even means. The legislative trigger (IPO this year) may not land cleanly on companies that restructure to avoid it.
- **Board representation creates governance conflicts.** Who votes Treasury's shares in OpenAI against an acquisition that benefits public-shareholders but threatens national strategic interest? Who controls that decision? The fund manager becomes a political actor  -  exactly the centralized-power problem the Alaska Permanent Fund avoids by distributing cash rather than holding governance stakes.
- **Sam Altman / Anthropic / Musk endorsing "concepts" ≠ endorsing 50% equity transfer.** Your op-ed notes their support for public wealth funds as a concept. These are not endorsements of the *American AI Sovereign Wealth Fund Act*'s specific mechanism. They will fight the equity transfer provision while supporting the abstract goal. Don't count on their endorsements surviving the specific ask.
- **One-time transfer doesn't capture future growth.** The bill locks public share at companies' current valuation at IPO. If these companies grow 10x over the next decade  -  which analysts project  -  the 50% stake is locked at the IPO price. The public doesn't capture the growth curve.
