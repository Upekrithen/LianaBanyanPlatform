## §2 WHAT LIANA BANYAN COOPERATIVE IS

The shortest honest version: I built a cure for AI amnesia, and I
wrapped it in a cooperative.

Here is what that means.

Every AI conversation you have today starts from zero. The AI does not
remember you. It does not remember what you talked about yesterday, what
you decided last week, or what your business context is. You are a
stranger to it every time. That is not a limitation of the AI's
intelligence -- it is a structural failure of how AI systems are deployed.
I call it the context-loss problem. Everything I have built is designed
to solve it.

The Cooperative Substrate is the solution. It is a persistent,
member-owned memory and peer-coordination layer that travels with you
across every conversation, every platform, and every vendor. When the AI
vendor changes their pricing, deprecates their model, or goes offline,
your context -- your history, your preferences, your relationships, your
business data -- stays with you. We call this continuity-lift across
vendor churn. Intelligence-lift is what gets people in the door.
Continuity-lift is why they stay.

The economic model is just as important as the technology. The
Cooperative charges Cost+20% margins on all transactions through the
Substrate Market. That means the cooperative retains a 20% operating
margin -- enough to run the infrastructure, pay for coordination, and
distribute profit-shares to regional directors. The remaining 83.3% of
every transaction goes to the worker, builder, cook, merchant, or creator
who did the work. We do not take 30% the way delivery apps do. We do not
take your data and sell it to advertisers. The members own the substrate.
The members set the rules.

The cooperative is not a startup. I am not building this to sell it to
a private equity firm in five years. I am building it to run for fifty
years as a member-owned institution. The architecture -- the technology,
the economics, the governance -- is designed for permanence, not exit.

## §5 WHAT I AM NOT ASKING

I want to be explicit about what this is not, because I do not want you
to say yes to the wrong thing.

I am not asking for passive investment. There is no check to write.
The Cooperative does not take venture capital and does not want it.

I am not asking for endorsement-only participation. If you sign on as
a Regional Co-Founder, you are actually doing the work. You are in the
region. You are meeting the merchants. You are showing up at community
events. The role is operational.

I am not offering a token board seat. A token board seat is a title
in exchange for a name. I am not interested in that, and I do not think
you would be either. If I wanted names on a masthead, I would hire a
publicist.

I am not asking you to quit whatever you are currently doing. Many of
the people I am writing to have jobs, projects, and obligations that
matter to them. The Regional Co-Founder commitment is twenty hours per
week minimum. That is real time. But it is not your whole life.

I am not asking you to take my word for anything. I am asking for a
conversation, and then the materials speak for themselves.

Twenty hours per week minimum. Four-year initial term. Monthly calls
with me personally.

I will not tell you that is a small ask. It is not. Founding something
real takes real time. The Cooperative's first year in a new region is
the hardest year. You will be building from zero -- first merchants,
first members, first chapters, first press. It gets easier as the
flywheel turns, but the first year requires sustained effort.

I ask you to think carefully about whether you have twenty hours per week
to give to this before you respond. If the honest answer is no, I would
rather hear that now than twelve months in. I respect a no that comes
from genuine self-knowledge more than a yes that does not last.

The four-year term is designed to align with the patent royalty vesting
schedule. The Cooperative needs Regional Co-Founders who are committed
to the long arc, not to a sprint. Four years is enough time to build
something real in a region.
