# How to Save the World in Six Easy Steps

## A Business Plan

*Not left or right. A more effective team.*

---

*A little girl told her mother she wanted to be an astronaut.*

*Her mother, trying to be helpful, said: "Well, sweetheart, you'll need advanced college degrees. You'll need to serve in the military and get piloting hours. And then three years of experience leading others."*

*The girl looked at her mom and shrugged.*

*"No problem. That's just, like, three things."*

---

Figure out how to end World Hunger. Tell Everyone. And give them a selfish reason to do it.

That is, in full, the business plan. What follows is the machinery.

The selfish reason is not charity. It is not an appeal to conscience. It is mathematics. When you can pick apples and your neighbor can deliver them, and the platform pays each of you fairly -- Creators AND Workers keep **83.3%** of every transaction, every time, locked by constitutional bylaw -- cooperation becomes the rational choice. Not the noble choice. The smart choice.

That is the insight the cooperative economy has been waiting for. Not a better appeal to altruism. A better deal.

This is a business plan with six steps. Each step is a self-contained economic engine that feeds the next. Each step starts with one person doing one thing in one place. Each step scales without permission, without venture capital, and without anyone's approval but the people doing the work.

What you will find is a cooperative commerce platform built from a six-month pivot out of a hydraulic gaming platform project: when the Founder saw the real need, dropped the original plan, and shipped. The result: **2,270 documented innovations**, **21 USPTO provisional patent applications**, **36 live production systems**, and a three-currency economic system with mathematical solvency proofs. Built by one veteran, four AI agents, and nine years of obsession.

That is just, like, six things.

---

## The Economics (same engine, every step)

**Cost+20%.** That is the platform margin. Not "up to" -- exactly. Locked in the operating agreement. No board vote, no investor pressure, no quarterly earnings call can change it. Creators AND Workers keep **83.3%** of every transaction. The platform takes what it needs to operate and nothing more.

**Three internal currencies.** Credits (participation instrument -- not convertible to fiat). Marks (effort-differential -- what you DID matters, not just what you paid). Joules (surplus stored value, like forever stamps -- locked at the rate when you contributed). None of them convert to fiat. Members earn fiat through actual hiring and payment. The internal currencies make work that has already been done more valuable through Substitution.

**$5/year membership.** That is the stake. That is all you can lose. **500 members = break-even. 1,000 = profitable.** In any locale.

**50-year charter dissipation.** The cooperative does not flip in three years. It is structured to outlast the Founder.

The math IS the selfish reason. When cooperation always pays fairly and transparently, self-interest and the common good stop being opposites. They align.

---

## Step 1: Level the Field

The biggest cost in deploying AI today is not the model. It is the context: rebuilding state from scratch on every call, re-reading the same documents, reconstructing the same understanding, paying for the same tokens again and again.

MnemosyneC is the cooperative's answer to that waste. When one AI resolves a question and deposits the answer into the shared substrate, every other AI that asks the same question gets the answer back for free -- or close to it. The work is done once. The benefit accumulates. The substrate compounds.

We measured this. **Gemma 4 12B + LianaBanyan substrate: 100% (25/25), Banyan Metric 90.45, kappa 1.000, $0 marginal cost per query** once the substrate is populated. A two-machine LAN test ran 20/20 hash-verified cross-node queries. Accuracy went from **0% to 100%** when the mesh was engaged. The network-effect proof -- more of us is better -- is empirical, not theoretical.

MnemosyneC is a rung, not the destination. The destination is cooperation at scale. Sell the ladder.

---

## Step 2: Feed Your Neighbors

A restaurant owner in San Antonio lists her menu on the platform. A neighbor photographs the food. A delivery driver brings breakfast tacos to the office park down the road. The cook keeps 83.3%. The photographer earns Marks. The driver accumulates toward vehicle ownership. The office worker gets fresh food at her desk.

Nobody was rescued. Everybody benefited.

**Let's Make Dinner** coordinates families cooking for one another on rotating schedules or simply meal-prepping together for a week at a time at volume prices. **The Family Table** (#5) connects through the cookbook system. **Let's Get Groceries** (#2) is cooperative purchasing: when 12 families buy flour together, they pay wholesale.

**Why this is Step 2:** Food is the universal need. It is the first node in every economic network. When the first real dollar flows through the Commerce Engine, everything else becomes possible.

## Step 3: Employ the World

Everyone has something useful to do. The problem is not a shortage of work -- it is a shortage of fair arrangements for doing it.

**Rally Group** (#9) is community resilience made operational. When a wildfire hits a neighborhood, before FEMA arrives, the Rally Group has already dispatched a Crew Call. Local members with trucks, chainsaws, and first aid kits are on-site in hours. They earn Marks for their contribution. The community does not wait for someone else to save them. They save each other.

Defense Klaus is personal safety and legal defense for workers, for caregivers, for anyone moving through the world alone. It is a wearable bangle or bracelet that transforms into personal defense claws when needed, while giving the wearer access to a shared legal defense fund and support network. You buy it for someone you love for six dollars and an email address that is never used for marketing, only to register that this person is covered.

The rideshare stack sits alongside that safety layer: payment processed before the trip, tiered background verification with community vouching, the Earn-Down vehicle ownership program, panic buttons, and legal defense for drivers working alone. The demand is proven: in January 2026, KSAT News documented the practice San Antonians call "Hood Uber" -- informal, unregulated peer ridesharing arranged through Facebook groups, cash upfront, no background checks, no insurance. A driver was shot when a passenger refused to pay the fare. The cooperative infrastructure exists to make that demand safe to meet.

**Let's Go Shopping** (#3) and **Household Concierge** (#4) do the same for birthdays, Christmas, and Mother's Day as well as errands and home services: the same Cost+20% rails, the same 83.3% kept by workers and creators, but pointed at all the small tasks and gifts that keep a household and a family running.

The Mirror Clause takes this one step further. When a large organization installs MnemosyneC at scale, the license requires that for every person they staff to operate the system, a Liana Banyan cooperative member fills a matching role. One of theirs, one of ours. This is the Employ-the-World thesis written directly into the contract: the act of adopting the platform is, by design, an act of hiring from the community it serves. Technology deployment becomes a job-creation engine.

**Why this is Step 3:** Once food is flowing, the next universal need is a productive livelihood. Employment is not just income -- it is dignity, identity, and membership in the economy.

## Step 4: Build Businesses and Make Things

Anyone can try any venture for $5. A new member browses the 8 Treasure Maps: each one a different business category with demand signals on the front and a mini business plan on the back. The Cold Start Hub walks her through registration, first Cue Card, first customers. The Captain System kicks in at 20 orders.

**VSL** (#10) is the video sales letter engine: cooperative marketing infrastructure that any member can run without a marketing budget. **Brass Tacks** (#16) is the operations layer: the paperwork, the back-office, the compliance structure that makes a side hustle into a legitimate business.

**Why this is Step 4:** Employment (Step 3) gives people a job. Business ownership gives them a stake. These are different things. The cooperative is designed to produce both.

---

## Step 5: Power to the People

*Not left or right. A more effective team.*

This is the Switzerland step.

By the time a community has shared food, shared work, shared ownership, and shared infrastructure, it has also built the legitimacy to share governance.

**Political Expedition** (#15) is the civic-action arm: nonpartisan civic transparency, accountability tools, the machinery of representative self-governance built by the people it governs. It does not import ideology. It exports the structure that lets communities make their own choices, fairly. **The Orchard** is the civic-discourse arm: a separate spinout-class entity where reason and faith and politics can meet across difference, judged by truth rather than tribal membership. You can bring any worldview. You must bring your evidence.

**Tatiana Schlossberg Health Accords** (#6) and **MSA/Tereno** (#7) are the policy and structural arms: health access at Cost+20% and regional economic organization that gives communities collective negotiating weight.

**Why this is Step 5:** Power must be earned, not seized. A community that has fed itself, employed itself, and built its own businesses has earned the right to govern itself.

---

## Step 6: Belong Together

A Tribe is personal. Your neighborhood. Your faith community. Your running club. Many Tribes per member. A Guild is professional: what you DO. A Tribe is personal: who you ARE.

**The Muses** is the creative-community layer: the guild for every starving artist who is done starving. **JukeBox** (#13) is the music and cultural-expression arm: a cooperative performance and distribution layer that puts Creators AND Workers first. **Didasko** (#14) is the learning and teaching arm: the cooperative where knowledge passes from those who have it to those who need it, at Cost+20%, with the teacher keeping 83.3%.

Belonging is the sixth step because it is the hardest to engineer and the last to arrive -- but when it arrives, it is the one that makes everything else sustainable. People do not fight for platforms. They fight for communities. The platform is the substrate. The community is the point.

## The Point

A little girl wanted to be an astronaut. Her mother listed everything it would take. Years of work. Enormous sacrifice. A path that most people would look at and say: impossible.

The girl looked at the list and saw three things.

Liana Banyan is the same list. Level the Field. Feed Your Neighbors. Employ the World. Build Businesses and Make Things. Power to the People. Belong Together.

That is just, like, six things.

The platform is built. The door is open. The kitchen is ready.

I am one Army ant among many: a U.S. Army National Guard veteran of no particular note, a father of eight, an amateur engineer and economist, a repentant sinner and hopeful Christian, and the founder and general manager of Liana Banyan Corporation.

We can help each other help ourselves.

For the keep.
