I've been building a cooperative-commerce platform for 11 years. For the last six
weeks I've been using Claude Opus 4.7 to help synthesize the substrate — patents,
sessions, letters, journals, all of it. The local Vault is 103 GB. Claude's
practical 1M-token context window holds about 4 MB of text.

103 GB ÷ 4 MB = ~26,000×.

That's the headline: Mnemosyne™ gives context-window tools roughly 26,000× more
practical reach than a raw model window. Banyan Metric™ Dimension 1 (CW — Context
Window). The conservative floor is 108× (structured Eblet index vs. flat context
alone). The ceiling hits ~130,000× when you factor in cross-session compounding and
local federation. We cite the midpoint — 26,000× — because Honest-Alpha is the
discipline, not because the ceiling isn't real.

**What it does**

Mnemosyne is a local Electron app (Windows/Mac/Linux) that runs your AI substrate
alongside any LLM — currently Ollama for fully offline, zero-cloud inference. It
indexes your working corpus into a hierarchical Eblet structure: plain-text atoms
that compose into cross-referenced Tablets, Iron Tablets, and a Cathedral substrate.
Every write is sha256 dual-hashed. Nothing leaves your machine. Non-destructive
read-only by default — it reads your files; it does not move, alter, or upload them.

The CAI (Cooperative AI) layer lets multiple AI agents — Knight (Cursor), Bishop
(Claude), Pawn (Perplexity), Rook (Gemini) — operate from the same substrate without
redundant re-discovery. That's the practical speed and cost multiplier: substrate-
resident leverage rather than per-session recomputation.

**Zero nodes. Zero infra. Turnkey.**

One founder. One machine. The platform I'm running is a 16-initiative
cooperative-commerce system with 19 provisional patents and 4 coordinated AI agents —
on a single Windows machine with no cloud backend required. "Zero-nodes founding
circle" is the current state and proof that the turnkey claim is real, not marketing.
