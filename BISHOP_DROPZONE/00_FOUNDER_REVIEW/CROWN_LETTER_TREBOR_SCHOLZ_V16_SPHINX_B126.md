## IV. The architecture, in one sentence and one technical paragraph

**One sentence:** A planet-scale voluntary federation of cooperative-AI-discipline-enforcement cells, structurally sovereignty-preserving at every tier of composition, free in perpetuity to nonprofits and academics under the Cooperative Defensive Patent Pledge, brand-named *The Sphinx Project*, founded jointly by Liana Banyan Corporation and (I am asking) the Platform Cooperativism Consortium.

**One technical paragraph:** Each Federated Discipline Cell performs two operations — *enforce-locally* (block, warn, enrich, substitute, or allow AI interactions per user-defined Augur rules at the cell's scope) and *federate-upward* (compose with peer cells under a parent cell, contributing signal aggregation, rule promotion, audit roll-up, and policy receipt). Cells compose recursively without bound. The Augur primitive is implemented via client-side hooks at the layer external to the underlying AI model (browser extension, daemon middleware, IDE extension, settings.json hook) — the AI's *advice* about whether to consult substrate is replaced with the *harness's enforcement* that it must. Cathedral substrate (the canonical-memory layer at the heart of the Cathedral Effect, empirically validated at +86 percentage points mean accuracy lift across eight cloud AI vendors) feeds the consultation operation at every tier. Federation transport between cells uses the Cathedral Federation Protocol — described internally as *"basically TCP/IP for cooperative AI memory."* Sovereignty preservation is structural, not contractual: lower-tier cells cannot have their rules modified by higher-tier cells without consent, and adding new tiers above existing tiers does not redistribute lower-tier authority downward.
