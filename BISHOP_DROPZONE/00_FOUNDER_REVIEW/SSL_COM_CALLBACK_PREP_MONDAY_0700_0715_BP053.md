**Bishop authored 2026-05-23 · Founder ratify before Monday wake.**

## §0 — The one sentence to remember

> **You're calling to get either Option A (cert at standard price now) or Option B (refund + non-interference with alternate vendor). Both close the matter. You have no preference. The rest is detail.**

If the conversation drifts, return to that sentence.

Both are Saladin's-Pattern-clean. Both name the situation factually. Neither is hostile.

## §3 — What to ask for (the three outcomes · ordered by Bishop pref)

### Outcome A · Standard-turnaround completion at standard price (best for SSL.com to offer · simplest for you)

> *"I'd like the certificate issued at the original standard price, with no expedite charge — even though it's running late. Can you do that today or tomorrow?"*

If they say YES → confirm in writing (request follow-up email confirming the cert issuance commitment). **Done.** Letter not sent. Move on.

If they say "we can expedite for $600 today" → that's NOT Option A. **Push back gently:**

> *"The standard turnaround already passed without contact. I'm not paying the expedite fee for service your published SLA committed to deliver at the standard price. If standard issuance is no longer possible, I'd rather pursue Option B — refund + alternate vendor — than pay a premium to get the original deal."*

That's the structural pivot. The merciful first move is asking for Option A; the second move (in-call) is naming Option B as the alternative they can choose if A is off the table.

### Outcome B · Refund + non-interference with alternate vendor

> *"Alternatively, I'd accept a full refund on the order with confirmation that I'm free to obtain the certificate from another CA without any non-compete or wait period implication. I'll go to DigiCert or Sectigo and have it in hand by midweek."*

If they say YES → confirm the refund timeline (typically 5-10 business days back to original payment method) + ask for a written confirmation email. **Done.** Letter not sent. Start alternate-vendor application same day.

### Outcome C · Some third resolution they offer

If they offer something not-A and not-B (e.g., "we'll waive the expedite fee as a courtesy" · "we'll discount your next certificate" · "let me escalate to my manager and call you back today"):

**Listen.** Then evaluate against this rubric:

- Does this deliver the cert at no extra cost? → Accept
- Does this deliver the cert with some real concession (small discount, credit, free OV cert for the relationship)? → Accept · note the relationship value
- Is this just "let me get back to you" with no timeline? → Push for a SPECIFIC commitment with a SPECIFIC deadline ("I need either A or B confirmed in writing by end of business Tuesday — what can your manager commit to by then?")
- Is this vague apology with no resolution? → Politely move toward Option B + plan to send the breach letter Tuesday if no real response

## §4 — Tone discipline (Saladin's Pattern™ in real-time)

- **No threats.** Don't mention chargebacks, BBB, AG complaints, or the breach letter unless the rep asks "what are my options if we can't resolve this?" — even then, name them factually, not as threats.
- **No anger.** Anger gives them permission to dismiss the substance. Cold-factual + cooperative-class voice does the work.
- **No long monologue.** 15-minute call · keep your statements to 30-60 seconds at a time · let them respond.
- **Heart of Peace toward the rep.** They probably did not personally cause the silence. They got handed a ticket Monday morning. They want to help if they can.
- **Brick Wall on the outcome.** Stay on Option A or Option B. Don't let "let me check with my manager and call you back next week" become the resolution.

## §5 — Documentation during the call

While talking, have a doc open. Write down verbatim:

- Rep's name + title + direct extension (ask for these)
- Any commitment they make + the deadline they commit to
- The follow-up email address they will send written confirmation to
- Any reference number they generate for this call

If they don't offer a follow-up email or reference number, ASK:

> *"To make sure we're on the same page, can you send me a written summary of what we've agreed to, with the timeline? My email is [FILL] and I'd like that within the next hour or two so we both have the record."*

That written record IS the canonical artifact — Library-of-Congress preservation discipline applies. If they balk, that's a structural signal to move toward Option B.

## §7 — What NOT to do in the call

- **Don't accept "we'll get back to you" without a date.** Always pin to a date.
- **Don't volunteer the breach letter's existence.** It's leverage by being unsent; mention only if directly asked about escalation options.
- **Don't agree to pay the expedite fee retroactively.** That validates the structural pattern this whole exchange is trying to refuse.
- **Don't sign anything new on the call.** Verbal commitments + written follow-up · no on-call contract amendments.
- **Don't apologize for needing the cert.** You're a customer. The standard turnaround is what they sell. You're not asking for a favor.
