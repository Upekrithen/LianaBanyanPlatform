## What We Built

Liana Banyan is a cooperative commerce platform — the economic infrastructure that small businesses, nonprofits, teachers, photographers, craftspeople, and communities have always needed but never had access to. The economics are locked in the foundation: every creator keeps 83.3% of what they earn. The platform takes Cost plus 20% — enough to run, nothing more. Membership is $5 a year. We filed 13 patent applications covering 2,263 innovations. 222 of those have no prior art anywhere in the world.

We also built the R9 — an AI architecture that saves large organizations over $200,000 a year in wasted computing costs. We gave it away free to nonprofits, cooperatives, and schools. Forever. Structurally. It cannot be taken back.

Is that saving the world? I think it's a reasonable start.

## When We See Money

The platform earns Cost+20% on every transaction from day one. That margin is small at first and grows with every member who joins. Jonathan's share — 20% of the platform's IP revenue — grows with it.

The honest timeline:

- **Spring/Summer 2026**: Platform running. Members joining. Margin building monthly. Not a salary yet — a foundation.
- **Summer/Fall 2026**: If Herjavec says yes, patent prosecution is funded and the IP portfolio is protected. Licensing conversations with technology companies begin.
- **Late 2026 / Early 2027**: MacKenzie Scott's network, if they join, brings hundreds of organizations onto the platform at once. The monthly membership and transaction base reaches meaningful scale.
- **2027**: Licensing fees from technology companies wanting R9-compatibility begin flowing. Jonathan's 20% slice becomes real, recurring income — not dependent on any single deal, any single donor, or any single decision by anyone other than the platform's members.

This is not a lottery. It is not a startup waiting for an exit. It is a cooperative — which means it builds steadily, distributes fairly, and does not stop.
