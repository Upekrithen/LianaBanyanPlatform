## 1. The Concept, in One Sentence

A monthly subscription to Liana Banyan that gives a member a Newton-grade runway — housing, transportation, food, clothing, entertainment — paid for partly with money up front and partly with effort worked off in Marks under the existing Liana Banyan rules, scaled to the recipient's vessel tier.

## 4. The Mr. Cloyd Anchor — SSSS *is* a Voucher Subscription (Founder-ratified B109)

**[hook: Founder writes the Lewistown / Sears / $200 lawn mower / $20/month story verbatim — this is the Pudding #186 anchor "What Your Hand Finds To Do." The version where Mr. Cloyd extends $200 of credit to a 14-year-old on reputation alone, the lawn mower pays for itself in income before the layaway closes, and the Founder ends up with the tool *and* the income. The whole SSSS is that loop, scaled.]**

What Mr. Cloyd did at the Lewistown Sears in the early 1980s is the entire architecture of SSSS in miniature. He did not invent a new financial instrument. He extended **credit** on reputation, against the income the credit would *enable*.

**The onramp Marks in the 3-month runway are Vouchers, not subsidies.** A Voucher extended by the platform (or a Patron / sponsor) to the subscribing member, with a defined repayment schedule in Marks earned through Cold Start Pathways. This is a load-bearing structural choice, ratified by Founder in B109:

- The runway is mechanically a **commercial credit extension**, not an investment contract. Pawn B62 concluded this class of Voucher transaction is "legally uncomplicated" at the $50–$500 scale (Level 0 member entry) and scales cleanly up to larger envelopes at higher vessel tiers.
- The **3-month accountability ladder (§5) = Voucher repayment schedule.** Month 1 warning, Month 2 deadline, Month 3 grace — the standard Voucher performance ladder, applied to subsistence.
- After graduation the Voucher closes. The member earns Marks normally through productive output under the standard $5/yr membership. No permanent dependency; no open-ended subsidy.

Mr. Cloyd's deal was a Voucher avant la lettre. **SSSS is a Voucher Subscription — the Cloyd mechanism wired into a recurring product.**

## 5. Mechanism

**Sign-up.** Member subscribes via LB Card (or, in the interim before Stripe/Lithic approval, via a personal credit card). Subscription tier sets the runway envelope.

**The runway (3-month onramp).** Member receives access to housing, transportation, basic food, clothing, and entertainment within their tier envelope. Subsidy is paid out in Marks against the member's ledger — they can use what's there.

**The accountability ladder.** Per Founder's design:
- **Month 1** — produce or be warned. Warning is documented; no penalty yet.
- **Month 2** — produce by end of month. Deadline. Failure to produce = formal status change.
- **Month 3** — grace month. Member retains housing/transport but Marks subsidy halts. Must produce.
- **End of Month 3** — produce or exit. Member who has produced rolls into the standard membership tier with their accumulated Marks intact.

"Produce" = measurable output through any of the Six Cold Start Pathways: Food (a meal served, a Pudding posted), Manufacturing (a unit shipped, a Canister built), Service (an hour delivered, a Steward stamp earned), Local Business (a transaction closed), Guild (a professional Mark earned), Tribe (a community Mark earned).

**Graduation.** A member who completes the 3-month onramp and continues producing converts to standard membership ($5/yr) with full Pedestal-eligibility. Their accumulated Marks become genuine working capital in their hands.

## 6. The Property Coupling — Destination Montana / Destination Anywhere

The cost problem (rent charges for something already built — *the Magie / Landlord's Game critique*) is solved by **owning the property**, not renting it. Cooperative Housing Acquisition (#1927) is the existing mechanism; the Housing WaterWheel (#1929) is how it self-funds.

**Mechanism:** SSSS is structured to flow into property purchase, not rental. Member's Marks contribution + LB's WaterWheel revenue + (optionally) external grant capital = capital stack for acquiring residential property in the cooperative's name. Housing is then provided to subscribers at *cost-of-maintenance + 20%*, not at market rent. Across enough properties, the WaterWheel pays the mortgage and the maintenance from the member-population's productive output.

**This also solves the premises-liability problem** flagged in §3 of the prior risks pass: the cooperative owns the housing, the cooperative carries the insurance, the cooperative sets the safety standards. Standardized across the property network. Not a partner-network workaround — a structurally clean solution.

**"Destination Montana"** is the original frame (anchored to Lewistown). **"Destination Anywhere"** generalizes — the same mechanism deployed in any geography that supports the Cooperative Housing acquisition model under local law.

## 8. Risks (compressed; mitigations in §6)

1. **Tax / securities law.** Marks-as-subsistence + housing-as-benefit creates imputed-income and possible "investment contract" surface. **Mitigation:** counsel review pre-launch (already standard for LB launches); the Magie / cost-of-maintenance + 20% structure keeps the offering on the *services* side of the line, not the *investment* side. Founder's instinct on the Voucher side (Path B abandoned, Open Water Path A only) means we already have counsel-vetted Voucher rails.
2. **Adverse selection.** **Mitigation:** the 3-month accountability ladder. Self-selection happens by month 2; no long-tail liability.
3. **Premises liability.** **Mitigation:** §6 — cooperative owns the housing, not partners. Standardized insurance, code, safety.
