## The suggestion (from SP-16 recombiner, B109 first run)

> **What if the Courtesy SSL were explicitly named in the Cardboard Boots Scott letter?** v014f uses Pratchett Boot Theory as framing but never cites the Courtesy SSL as its operational answer. Adding one sentence ("the Courtesy SSL is Boot Theory operationalized") would close the metaphor-to-mechanism loop.

## Canonical anchor

From `CANONICAL_LAWS_AND_FRAMEWORKS.md` §IV:

> **The Courtesy SSL (Super Short Loan)** — First $50 of any borrowing available with no credit check, no questions, no vouchers. Interest is transparent: days borrowed = percent interest (5 days = 5%). Replenished by members who remember needing help.
> *Why:* USAA covered the Founder's gas when he had $1 in checking. Assumes goodness is default. Specifically designed to stop the desperate from entering predatory lending cycles.

## The proposed insertion — location

**Insert immediately after the existing sentence ending "...the chain reaction that can go on for years."** in the medallion paragraph (v014f line 34). This places the Boot Theory → mechanism bridge at the exact point where the letter is arguing that the cooperative architecture *breaks* the poverty premium.

**Current v014f text at the insertion point:**

> "...One bus driver becomes ten transit workers becomes hundreds understanding they can have their own part of what they themselves and others work to achieve — the chain reaction that can go on for years. And under the Pledge, that same cooperative architecture is available..."

## Three drafts of the insertion sentence — pick one, rewrite, or reject

**Draft A (minimal, names both):**
> The medallion is one answer to what Pratchett called Boots Theory — that being poor costs more over time than being rich; the Courtesy SSL, our $50 no-questions-asked loan, is another.

**Draft B (compound, keeps the Boot metaphor implicit):**
> The medallion breaks the poverty premium at the front end; the Courtesy SSL — a $50 loan, no credit check, replenished by members who remember needing help — breaks it at the lending end, where it does the worst damage.

**Draft C (single clause, tightest):**
> Every mechanism in this cooperative — medallion, Courtesy SSL, Voucher chain — exists because a cheap pair of boots costs more over time than a good pair.

## Why this is a real edit, not cosmetic

The letter's title is *Cardboard Boots* — a Pratchett Boot Theory reference. The body never explicates the metaphor or names a specific mechanism that answers it. Scott's own giving philosophy is Pratchett-adjacent ("$100 from someone earning $50,000…"). Making the metaphor-to-mechanism loop explicit gives a skeptical reader (or op-ed editor) one concrete operational noun — "Courtesy SSL" — to anchor to. It also earns the title.

## Why it's *also* fine to decline the edit

v014f is working. The medallion-as-chain-reaction already implies the Boot answer without naming it. The title carries the metaphor. The Courtesy SSL is not a mechanism Scott needs to know about to say yes to the ask. If the letter is already sending, don't touch it.
