### What This Story Should Accomplish

A pitch-class artifact using a restaurant as the lens for cooperative economics. The restaurant is a natural cooperative-economy vehicle because:

1. **The labor is local**: The cook, the server, the dishwasher — all community members
2. **The margin is thin**: Restaurant economics are notoriously tight; every dollar matters
3. **The community benefits directly**: A good restaurant becomes a community anchor
4. **The cooperative alternative is legible**: The difference between a franchise (extract margin to headquarters) and a cooperative restaurant (keep surplus in the community) is visible and concrete

### The Intended Argument

A cooperative restaurant named "La Ma" — or using "La Ma" as a reference — serves as the pitch illustration for how cooperative economics works differently from extractive economics. The restaurant version of the argument is accessible to anyone who has ever eaten at a neighborhood place they loved and watched it close because the owner couldn't keep up with rent or franchise fees.

Cooperative infrastructure is the difference between that restaurant closing and that restaurant surviving. The cooperative pools purchasing, handles shared back-office functions, provides access to capital that would otherwise require a bank loan, and keeps the margin in the community rather than sending it to a distant headquarters.

**The math, applied to a restaurant:**
- Traditional franchise: owner keeps ~15-20% of revenue after fees, food costs, labor, rent
- Cooperative restaurant member: may earn up to 83.3% of the value they generate, with cooperative pooling handling the costs that used to go to franchise fees
