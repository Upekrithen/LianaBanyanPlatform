# The Art of Losing
### *An Op-Ed by Jonathan R. Jones*

*Founder & General Manager, Liana Banyan Corporation*
*Capitalist · Cooperative · Patriotic Interdependentalist*

---

> *"The art of losing isn't hard to master;*
> *so many things seem filled with the intent*
> *to be lost that their loss is no disaster."*
>
> - Elizabeth Bishop, "One Art" (1976)

Elizabeth Bishop mastered losing as resignation. Keys. A river. Two cities. A continent. Her villanelle builds with wry restraint until the final stanza breaks into confession. She writes "disaster" anyway, in parentheses, because the art of losing always costs more than it should.

I do not read it as resignation.

---

On the morning of June 18th, 2026, the power was cut to my house. I had been awake for approximately twenty-four hours already, deep in a technical sprint building cooperative infrastructure, when the lights went out. A simple administrative consequence: the utilities were in a friend's name. The cut was how I learned he had died.

His name was Alford Ray Hunter. He was my namesake - I am Jonathan *Ray* Jones - lifelong minister, Director of the Rally Group, the best Christian example in real life I ever personally knew. His funeral had been held more than a week before I discovered he was gone.

I sat with that. I prayed. I talked with my wife.

Then I fixed the utilities, and I kept working.

---

I told my sister, who is navigating her own displacement, what I tell my children: *Never despair at the immediacy of your circumstance.*

The advice is not optimism. It is structural. It removes quitting from the option set.

*And you might fail. A lot, and lose a lot, but it's not over until you win - because Either You WIN or you KEEP TRYING. And that is why you will win. Because you chose to.*

---

Babe Ruth retired holding two records simultaneously: the most home runs in MLB history (714, held until Hank Aaron in 1974) and the most strikeouts in MLB history (1,330, held for decades after). Those records were not in tension. The home runs were enabled by the strikeouts. The same swing class that produced the most strikeouts produced the most home runs.

I have played over 25,000 games of chess on a single account. My highest rating reached 2118, placing me in the top 0.4% of chess players worldwide. My win rate is 46%. I lose more than I win. In the past year alone I played 7,825 games - 3,910 as White (45.9% wins) and 3,915 as Black (43.7% wins). I am in the top half-percent of chess players on earth, and I lose more than I win. How? Because every loss taught me something. Because the losses get harder, which means the education gets deeper.

Chess is also my daily instrument for measuring cognition. If I lose to a simple tactic I saw and dismissed from fatigue, that is my beacon to stretch and have a coffee. Inherently unstable systems are flown by continuous micro-adjustment. So is a mind under load. So is a cooperative under construction.

Losing, at this scale, is not misfortune. It is curriculum.

*Nothing in the world can take the place of persistence. Talent will not; nothing is more common than unsuccessful men with talent. Genius will not; unrewarded genius is almost a proverb. Education will not; the world is full of educated derelicts. Persistence and determination alone are omnipotent.* - Calvin Coolidge

---

What I built in those forty hours - and in the years of losing that preceded them - is called MnemosyneC. It is the cooperative orchestration layer for Liana Banyan: a platform with sixteen charitable initiatives, a fixed Cost+20% platform margin locked by operating agreement, and a membership that costs $5/year. Not five hundred dollars. Five. Because the business model of the cooperative is not extraction - it is participation. Workers, Builders, and Creators keep 83.3%. The members are not the product.

The platform is live at mnemosynec.org/ai. On the morning of June 19th, 2026, the cooperative mesh - four machines, one shared substrate - passed its first full test: Trial 02, seventy questions, 70/70 answered on the anchor pass using both Claude and Gemma4:12b independently. A follow-on cooperative trial, Trial 02b, returned 81.9% accuracy across three active peers with 100% inter-peer agreement - zero disagreement on any question. A 4-peer Frontier mesh confirmed. The underlying cooperative design is captured in PROV_22, now 100pp USPTO-ready.

This is what preparation looks like. Not absence of loss. Preparation through loss.

---

The history of my family: a namesake friend whose name I carry in the middle of mine, and to whom I dedicate every receipt that follows.

The history of the nation: forty years watching the carnival game stay rigged - and deciding the correct response is not a shrug but a free carnival. The cooperative is that carnival.

The history of the world: the architecture of extraction is not inevitable. It is a design. Designs can be redesigned. The Substrate - the cooperative mesh, the member-owned platform - is the counter-design. Built in public. Priced at cost plus twenty percent. Owned by no one. Accountable to everyone.

Elizabeth Bishop knew loss. She wrote its grammar with precision and restraint. I do not argue with her craft. I argue with her conclusion.

The art of losing is not resignation. It is preparation.

And I am prepared.
