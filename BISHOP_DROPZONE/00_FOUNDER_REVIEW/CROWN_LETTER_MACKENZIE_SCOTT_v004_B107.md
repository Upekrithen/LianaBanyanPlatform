## Who Gets In — And How

Any organization with verifiable government registration joins automatically. In the United States: a valid 501(c) EIN — public record, verified programmatically against the IRS database. Internationally: equivalent registered charity status in their jurisdiction. Cooperatives registered under applicable law. Accredited academic institutions.

The IRS already vetted them. We verify the number. No staff review. No application. No committee.

For organizations doing the work without formal registration yet — mutual aid networks, land trusts still forming, international organizations without clean registry equivalents — there is a one-page application reviewed by the existing member council. The community vets the community.

Your grantees skip both tracks. You already vetted them. The seat is waiting.

One structural note worth stating plainly: the distribution is equal per organization regardless of size. A food bank with 300 staff and a two-person housing collective receive the same quarterly share. This means the dividend is not worth gaming. Nobody fabricates a 501(c) for $100 a year. The architecture selects for mission alignment without requiring active fraud detection. This is by design, not accident — and it is written into the program rules as a declared principle, not a quiet side effect.
