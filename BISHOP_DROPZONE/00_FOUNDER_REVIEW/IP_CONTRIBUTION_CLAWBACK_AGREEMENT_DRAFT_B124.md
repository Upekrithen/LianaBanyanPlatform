## DRAFT CLAUSE TEXT (counsel-review pending)

### § X.Y — Good-Faith Filing and External-Assertion Clawback

**X.Y.1 — Good-Faith Filing Warranty.** Each Member contributing intellectual property ("Contributed IP") to the Liana Banyan IP allocation pool warrants and represents that:

(a) The Contributed IP is filed in good faith for the bona fide purpose of supporting the Liana Banyan cooperative ecosystem and the Cooperative Defensive Patent Pledge (Innovation #2260).

(b) The Contributed IP is not filed primarily or substantially for the purpose of establishing prior art, patent assertion entity ("PAE"), or non-practicing entity ("NPE") strategy outside the Liana Banyan ecosystem.

(c) The Contributed IP is not encumbered by any prior assertion campaign, licensing demand, or pending litigation in which the Member or any party affiliated with the Member is acting as the asserting party against a third party outside the Liana Banyan ecosystem.

**X.Y.2 — External Assertion Clawback.** If, within ten (10) years following the date of contribution, the Member or any party that derives rights from the Member ("Successor Party") asserts the Contributed IP, or any patent claim derived in substantial part from the Contributed IP, against a third party in any forum outside the Liana Banyan ecosystem in a manner that:

(a) Seeks monetary damages, injunctive relief, or licensing fees from the third party; AND

(b) Is unrelated to the operation, defense, or expansion of the Liana Banyan cooperative ecosystem; AND

(c) Targets a third party that is not a participant in any Cooperative Defensive Patent Pledge program;

then the following clawback consequences apply:

**(i) Forfeiture of Pedestal allocation.** Any Pedestal-allocated stake (10% bucket) the Member received in connection with the Contributed IP shall be forfeit to the Liana Banyan global sponsor pool (10% bucket) for redistribution to other members.

**(ii) Forfeiture of Founder-creator allocation.** Any Founder-creator-allocated stake (20% bucket) the Member received in connection with the Contributed IP shall be forfeit to the patent buckets (60% bucket) for collective use.

**(iii) License obligation.** The Member shall grant Liana Banyan Corporation, and all members of the Cooperative Defensive Patent Pledge program, a perpetual, royalty-free, irrevocable license to the asserted IP for use within the Liana Banyan ecosystem and Pledge participants' operations.

**(iv) Recovery of LB-funded prosecution costs.** If Liana Banyan Corporation funded the prosecution, examination, or maintenance of the Contributed IP, the Member shall reimburse Liana Banyan Corporation for those costs, indexed for inflation from the date of expenditure.

**X.Y.3 — Permitted External Assertions.** This clause does not restrict the Member from:

(a) **Defensive assertion.** Asserting the Contributed IP defensively against a party that has first sued the Member or any Liana Banyan Member for patent infringement.

(b) **Pledge-aligned assertion.** Asserting the Contributed IP against a party operating in bad faith against the Pledge framework (e.g., a patent troll attempting to extract licensing fees from Pledge members).

(c) **Post-clawback-period assertion.** After the ten-year clawback period elapses, the Member retains all rights to the Contributed IP not otherwise restricted by the underlying patent law or the Pledge framework. The clause is intentionally time-limited; lifetime restrictions would be both legally questionable and ethically excessive.

**X.Y.4 — Broad-Claim Review.** Liana Banyan Corporation, through counsel, shall review each IP contribution for unusually broad or abstract claim language that may indicate prior-art-cover intent. Members may be asked to narrow claim language as a condition of acceptance into the IP allocation pool. Members retain the right to withdraw their contribution and pursue independent filing if they decline to narrow claim language. Acceptance into the LB IP pool is not automatic; the cooperative reserves curatorial authority over what becomes part of the collective patent infrastructure.

**X.Y.5 — Why This Matters.** The Liana Banyan IP allocation framework (60% patent buckets / 20% Founder-creator / 10% global sponsor pool / 10% individual Pedestals) and the associated Cooperative Defensive Patent Pledge (#2260) exist to redistribute IP value cooperatively rather than concentrate it in PAE/NPE assertion strategies. A member who uses the LB filing apparatus to seed prior art for future external assertion exploits the cooperative's good-faith infrastructure for non-cooperative ends. This clause makes that exploitation costly enough to deter without being so restrictive as to chill genuine member contribution.

The architectural backing is the same as the affinity-facade clause (§ X.Y above): *a good name is rather to be chosen than great riches* (Proverbs 22:1). The cooperative's IP good name depends on its filings being trustworthy as cooperative defenses, not as ammunition for external troll campaigns.

**X.Y.6 — Severability.** If any provision of this clause is found unenforceable in a particular jurisdiction, the remaining provisions remain enforceable. Specifically: if the ten-year clawback period is found excessive in a given jurisdiction, the clawback period shall be reduced to the maximum enforceable period in that jurisdiction; the rest of the clause survives.

## NOTES FOR COUNSEL REVIEW

1. **Patent law considerations.** The clawback provisions interact with patent law's basic principle that patents are alienable property. Counsel should confirm that the contractual restriction on assertion is enforceable as a *use restriction* on a license/contribution, not as an attempt to alienate underlying patent rights. Wyoming and Delaware contract law should both support this; specific federal patent-law considerations may apply.

2. **Antitrust considerations.** A clause restricting how members can use their IP could be characterized as an anticompetitive arrangement. Counsel should confirm the Cooperative Defensive Patent Pledge framing — the clause restricts external aggressive assertion, not external use generally — survives any antitrust scrutiny. The Pledge's defensive-cooperative nature is the safe-harbor.

3. **Ten-year window.** Bishop chose ten years as a defensible middle-ground (long enough to deter cynical short-game filings; short enough to not chill genuine contribution). Counsel may shorten to five or seven if jurisdiction-specific concerns apply, or extend to fifteen if Founder prefers the longer commitment.

4. **Definition of "Liana Banyan ecosystem."** The clause references "the Liana Banyan ecosystem" as the safe harbor. Counsel should ensure the definition (e.g., "Liana Banyan Corporation, its affiliates, the Cooperative Defensive Patent Pledge program, and any party operating under a Pledge license") is precise enough to be enforceable.

5. **Withdrawal-of-contribution path (X.Y.4).** Critical that members can withdraw their contribution if they decline to narrow claim language. Without this, the clause becomes a coercive accept-our-narrowing-or-lose-everything frame. The withdrawal path preserves the voluntary nature of the contribution.
