## I. The Three-Gear Currency Differential

Mundell's 1961 theory of optimal currency areas established that economic regions benefit from shared currencies when they exhibit factor mobility, price flexibility, and synchronized business cycles. Every global platform since then has faced the inverse problem: participants from radically different economic zones must transact together, but their underlying economies remain permanently desynchronized.

No platform has solved this. The traditional responses each sacrifice something essential.

Equal nominal pricing -- a $10 fee -- excludes weak-currency participants entirely. Ten dollars is an hour of income in some economies and a week of income in others. Purchasing power adjustment creates arbitrage: sophisticated actors find the seam and exploit it. Regional segregation -- Fiverr's pricing tiers, Upwork's currency conversion, Amazon's marketplace divisions -- fragments network effects and creates administrative overhead. Every existing platform accepts one of these three failures as the cost of operating globally.

The three-gear currency differential resolves all three at once. It is the first formalized mechanism in platform economics to do so.

The system uses three internal currencies, each with a distinct function and acquisition method:

**Credits** are the primary platform currency for all standard transactions. They are acquired with fiat, earned through work, or received as payment. They are internal only -- they do not convert back to fiat. Members earn fiat through actual hiring and payment. Credits are the universal medium of exchange inside the platform. Everyone uses them. Everyone has equal purchasing power with them.

**Marks** are the effort-differential currency for participants from weak-currency economies. When a participant's local purchasing power is below the platform baseline, the gap does not exclude them and does not require a subsidy. It becomes Marks -- recognizing what they did, not what they paid. Marks clear through participation (completing work orders, verified votes, positive reviews, referrals). If a participant cannot or chooses not to clear their Marks obligation within one year, the Marks forfeit to Liana Banyan. Liana Banyan offers the forfeited Marks to other members at the Joules rate (1.2 Credits per Mark). The purchasing member receives the Marks and clears them through their own participation. Liana Banyan retains a 0.2-Credit margin per Mark. The original holder may redeem themselves at the Credit rate (1 Credit per Mark) within that one-year window. No equity transfers. No ownership changes hands. No profit expectation is created. Marks do not convert to fiat.

**Joules** are the surplus-storage currency for participants from strong-currency economies. When a participant contributes more than the baseline transaction requires, the surplus does not vanish into platform margin. It becomes Joules, locked at the acquisition rate -- the Forever Stamp mechanic. A stamp bought today is still good tomorrow even if postage rates rise. Mary's 40 Joules redeem at the same internal value they held when she earned them, which means as the platform economy grows, her locked-rate Joules purchase more than they would have at a later acquisition price. Joules do not convert to fiat.

The differential mechanism in formal terms: let B equal the platform baseline value (initially $1 USD equivalent), P(i) equal participant i's local currency purchasing power relative to baseline, and C equal Credits desired.

When P(i) is less than 1 (weak-currency economy): participant pays C times B in local currency equivalent; participant receives C Credits plus [(1 minus P(i)) times C] Marks.

When P(i) is greater than 1 (strong-currency economy): participant pays C times B in local currency equivalent; participant receives C Credits plus [(P(i) minus 1) times C] Joules.

The worked examples are direct. Bob's currency carries P equal to 0.8. To acquire 100 Credits: Bob pays $80 equivalent in his local currency, platform receives $80 equivalent, Bob receives 100 Credits plus 20 Marks. Bob participates at full 100-Credit purchasing power immediately. Mary's currency carries P equal to 1.4. To acquire 100 Credits: Mary pays $140 equivalent in her local currency, platform receives $140 equivalent, Mary receives 100 Credits plus 40 Joules locked at today's rate.

Bob in Nigeria and Mary in Switzerland participate on equal internal footing. Neither converts Credits back to fiat. Both earn fiat through actual hiring and payment. The structural gap in their external economic starting points is absorbed into Marks and Joules -- equal participation, different adjustment instruments, complete fairness. No subsidy. No arbitrage seam. No network fragmentation.

The system equilibrium condition: Total Credits issued equals total Credits backed by value received plus Marks obligations plus Joules reserves. Marks represent deferred value cleared through participation or converted to equity. Joules represent stored value held for future redemption. Credits circulate at face value regardless of origin. The solvency proof in Section 4.2 of the CAUSEP paper establishes that no participant composition scenario -- 100% weak-currency, 100% strong-currency, or any mixed state -- can produce insolvency. The mechanism is mathematically complete.

No platform has resolved the international purchasing-power trilemma before this one. This is the macro innovation that earns the first paragraph of this letter.
