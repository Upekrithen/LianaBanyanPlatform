## What They Do
NIH-funded research network that solves medical mysteries through collaborative team science. Patients with undiagnosed conditions submit cases through the UDN Gateway. Researchers across multiple institutions use model organisms (fruit flies, nematodes, zebrafish), genomic analysis, and cross-disciplinary expertise to find answers.

## Why Liana Banyan
The Health Accords' **Six Medical Degrees of Separation** is the crowdsourced complement to what UDN does institutionally. UDN uses elite research teams across institutions. LB builds the public knowledge board — peer-to-peer — where members with badges and reputations record what works and what doesn't, creating breadcrumb trails so the one-in-a-million patient can find the one-in-a-million provider who's seen their condition before.

**The founder's eye condition anecdote** is the founding story: an optometrist could only diagnose a rare one-in-a-million condition because he happened to have it himself. Six Medical Degrees of Separation eliminates the "happened to" — it builds the path deliberately.

**Dr. Lisa Sanders' *Diagnosis* (Netflix/NYT)** shows the same principle works: crowdsourcing diagnoses through column readers. LB systematizes this with reputation-backed knowledge boards, verification stamps, and discovery breadcrumbs.

## Integration Proposal
- LB members experiencing undiagnosed symptoms can be routed to UDN's case submission gateway
- UDN researchers can access LB's aggregated symptom/condition knowledge boards (anonymized) for pattern detection
- LB's Six Medical Degrees of Separation acts as the crowdsourced triage layer that feeds cases TO expert networks like UDN
- Joint publication opportunity: demonstrate that crowdsourced symptom aggregation can identify rare condition patterns before institutional diagnosis
