## The three amendments requested

### Amendment 1 — Purpose Clause tied to the Pledge

**Proposed text (counsel to refine):**
> The Corporation exists to operate under the Cooperative Defensive Patent Pledge (Innovation #2260) in perpetuity, granting royalty-free member licenses as established in these bylaws. Any corporate action inconsistent with the Pledge — including but not limited to revocation of member licenses, sale of assets that would render the Pledge inoperable, or amendments that narrow the Pledge's grants — exceeds the Corporation's legal authority and shall be voidable upon motion by any member in standing.

**Legal effect:** Makes Pledge-inconsistent actions *ultra vires* (beyond corporate authority). A buyer who acquired the corporation intending to strip the Pledge would buy a legal tangle — the moment they act against purpose, any member can sue to void. This is the bylaw equivalent of a suicide pill for bad-faith acquirers.

### Amendment 2 — Unanimous-Member-Consent for Pledge-Ending Transactions

**Proposed text (counsel to refine):**
> Any transaction or corporate restructuring that would cause the Corporation to cease operating under the Cooperative Defensive Patent Pledge requires unanimous written consent of all members in standing at the time of the proposed transaction. This provision may not be amended except by unanimous written consent of all members.

**Legal effect:** Practical impossibility of sale without member buy-in. The "amendments to this provision require unanimous consent" nested clause prevents a future board from simply re-amending the bylaws to remove the restriction.

### Amendment 3 — Golden Key Steward Role

**Proposed text (counsel to refine):**
> The Corporation hereby establishes the role of Golden Key Steward, held initially by the Founder, Jonathan Jones, and subsequently by such successor(s) as designated in the Founder's personal capacity or, absent such designation, by unanimous vote of members in standing. The Golden Key Steward holds a non-economic, non-transferable golden share granting perpetual veto power over: (a) any Pledge-ending transaction, (b) any bylaws amendment touching Amendments 1 or 2 above, (c) dissolution of the Corporation. The golden share carries no economic rights, no voting rights on ordinary business, and no claim on assets; its sole function is structural protection of the Pledge. The golden share may not be sold, assigned, or encumbered.

**Legal effect:** Creates a structural veto independent of economic ownership. Echoes the existing Deck Card / Golden Key concept per [project_deck_card_system.md](../../.claude/projects/C--Users-Administrator-Documents/memory/project_deck_card_system.md). Similar precedents: Tesla's "Twitter Exception" share structures, Patagonia's 2022 Purpose Trust + Holdfast Collective restructuring, Mozilla Foundation's relationship to Mozilla Corporation. Counsel should cite all three in drafting.
