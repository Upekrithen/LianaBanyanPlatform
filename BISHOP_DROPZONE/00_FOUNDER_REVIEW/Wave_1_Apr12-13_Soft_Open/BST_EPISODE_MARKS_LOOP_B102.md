## Episode Summary

This episode tells the story of Pudding #182 in real time — the day the Founder's son's car broke down, the shop fixed it same-day on a handshake, and a nine-month Marks-and-Joules loop closed in a single phone call. The episode uses this story to teach the four-currency vocabulary (Credits, Marks, Joules, Backed Marks) and connects it to historical precedents (WIR Bank, Sardex, Amish barn-raising, Radford's POW camp paper).

## Episode Outline

### Opening (5 min)
- Cold open: "My son's car broke down today. By the time I finish recording this episode, he'll be driving it home."
- Set the stakes: this isn't a parable. This happened today. The timestamps are real.

### Act 1: The Phone Call (10 min)
- The call to the shop. "We'll work something out."
- No invoice. No deposit. No credit application.
- What did the shop just do? They fronted the tow, the parts, and a day of skilled labor on a handshake.
- Why would anyone do that?

### Act 2: Nine Months of Network Cable (15 min)
- The IT buildout. Computers, network segmentation, VoIP, security cameras, diagnostic software.
- Never took a dollar of cash.
- The deal: keep my family's vehicles running, the way I keep your shop running.
- This is a four-currency relationship. Map it:
  - Credits = parts and tow (cash at cost)
  - Marks = labor discount + priority turnaround (the differential)
  - Joules = nine months of IT work (the reservoir)
  - Backed Marks = the shop fronting everything (trust you can see)

### Act 3: You Already Have One (15 min)
- Direct address to the listener: you have this relationship too.
- The neighbor, the sister-in-law, the friend who's a nurse.
- These are the most durable economic relationships in your life.
- They don't require a credit score. They don't disappear when the app gets sold.
- The only thing missing: scale. You can't have this with a stranger. Until now.

### Act 4: Ninety-Two Years of Proof (10 min)
- WIR Bank (Switzerland, 1934). Sardex (Sardinia, 2009). Radford's POW camp (1945). Amish barn-raising.
- This is not new. This is ancient. We just named the pieces.
- Liana Banyan adds one thing: a way to carry your reservoir with you.

### Close (5 min)
- "My son drove his car home today because I wired a network nine months ago."
- That's Marks. That's how it works. That's the whole thing.
- Call to action: think about who in your life you already have this relationship with. Now imagine having it with someone you've never met.
- Sign off: "You build the Features. We're building the Board."
