---

## WHAT YOU BUILT

In 1976, you lent $27 to 42 women in Jobra. They used it to buy bamboo to make stools. They repaid every cent. You realized something that economists had missed: poor people aren't poor because they're incapable. They're poor because they're excluded from systems that could help them succeed.

You built Grameen Bank on that insight. 9 million borrowers. 97% repayment rate. $30 billion in loans. The 2006 Nobel Peace Prize.

More than the numbers: you changed how the world thinks about poverty. You proved that trust works. That small loans can unlock big lives. That the poor are the best judges of what they need.

You called it "social business." I call it common sense. Either way, you were right.

---

## WHAT I'VE BUILT

Liana Banyan is a cooperative platform in which commercial activity funds charitable initiatives — sustainably, by design, beyond any one person's lifetime.

VSL is one of 16 initiatives. It's modeled directly on your principles:

- **Trust the borrower.** No collateral. No credit score gatekeeping.
- **Start small.** Loans as low as $50.
- **Build community.** Group accountability, not bureaucratic enforcement.
- **Tell stories.** Lenders connect to borrowers as humans, not data points.

Jessica Jackley heard you speak and built Kiva. I read about both of you and designed VSL.

The lineage is clear: Grameen → Kiva → VSL.

If you were to bless what we're doing, VSL would carry the spiritual authority of its original source. That matters — not for marketing, but for integrity. I want to build on your foundation, not pretend I invented it.

---

## WHY I'M WRITING

I'm not asking you to join anything. I'm not asking for money, time, or endorsement.

I'm writing to:

1. **Acknowledge the debt.** VSL exists because Grameen existed. I wanted you to know.

2. **Inform you.** If someone builds on your life's work, you deserve to know about it.

3. **Invite your blessing.** Not publicly, not for press. Just — if you think what we're doing honors your principles, I'd like to know. And if you think we've misunderstood something, I'd like to know that too.

I've also written to Jessica Jackley to lead VSL as the Lender Mentor. If she accepts, the initiative would have both operational expertise and the moral continuity of someone who heard you speak and changed her life because of it.
