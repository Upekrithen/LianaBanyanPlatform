## WHAT I'VE BUILT

## THE 2nd SECOND INDUSTRIAL REVOLUTION

Here's what I believe:

The first Industrial Revolution centralized manufacturing. Factories. Assembly lines. Scale at the cost of craft.

The second one is supposed to decentralize it. 3D printers. CNC machines. Maker spaces. But so far, it's been a promise without infrastructure.

**HexIsle** is my contribution to that infrastructure � a modular hydraulic gaming table system designed to be manufactured at scale while remaining accessible to makers. It's physical computing. It's the Tereno Platform. It's what happens when a maker spends four decades thinking about how things fit together.

The real unlock is manufacturing. HexIsle proves a single person with an SLA 3D printer, molding materials or desktop extruders and elbow grease can produce precision components at 95% less cost than traditional manufacturing. Ask me how I know. And modular design means the same product can be made in a garage in Texas or an apartment in Tokyo. We're not building one factory � we're enabling thousands of tiny ones, each owned by the person running it. And all ancillary service businesses.

But HexIsle is just one product. **Let's Make Bread** is the system that helps other makers do the same thing � turn their ideas into products, and their products into businesses.

I call it the **2nd Second Industrial Revolution**. Not because I invented the concept � but because I'm building the rails. And it doesn't take much � a community of 1,000 makes it work in any location.
