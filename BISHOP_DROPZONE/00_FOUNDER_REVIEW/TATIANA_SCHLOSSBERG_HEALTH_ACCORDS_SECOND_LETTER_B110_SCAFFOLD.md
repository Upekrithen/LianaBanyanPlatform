## What She Did

Tatiana Schlossberg spent her career documenting environmental destruction. When her own diagnosis came — the one that meant her career had an ending date on it — she did not pivot to memoir. She did not use her remaining time to curate her own legacy.

**She kept documenting.**

And the subject she chose to document, with her remaining time, was the healthcare system that was failing her and failing so many others worse. She wrote about the medications that might extend her life, if she could afford them, if her insurance approved them. She wrote about the people for whom the answer to those ifs was always no.

She spent the last months she will ever have doing her job.

## What We're Asking of You

We are not asking you to advocate. You already do.

We are not asking you to witness. You already witness.

We are asking something smaller and larger at once: **document what is broken, so others can build what heals.** That is the Schlossberg Standard. Keep doing what you do, but leave a written record — because the record is the seed.

The infrastructure *we* built in response to her work — **The Tatiana Schlossberg Health Accords**, a named governance layer for every patent, procedure, and price we touch in healthcare — would not exist if she had chosen silence. She documented; we built. That is how this is supposed to work.

## What We Will Do

The Health Accords are structurally open. No foundation approval. No grant application. No gatekeeping. Every patent filed in Patent Bags 12, 14, 15, and 18 carries this dedication:

> *"The Tatiana Schlossberg Health Accords — in memory of the journalist who documented what was broken so others could build what heals. She wrote until the end."*

And every innovation under those bags operates under the Schlossberg Standard:

- **Transparency** — all costs visible; no hidden margins
- **Documentation** — everything written down for others to verify
- **Accessibility** — graduated payment; cost is never a barrier
- **Accountability** — workers paid in ownership, aligned with patients

If your work intersects any of that — if you see a case we should be measuring, a broken assumption we should be correcting, a patient pathway the Accords should be covering — **tell us.** You don't need permission. You don't need status. You need an EIN if your organization adopts the Pledge-tier commons, but you don't need one to send us a note.

The commons is open. It's open because she wrote the article we needed to read.
