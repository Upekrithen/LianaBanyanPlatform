## Infrastructure / Energy-Grid Cooperative Spinout
**BP055 · May 24, 2026 · Knight**

---

## § 1 — What Battery Dispatch™ Is

Battery Dispatch™ is the **infrastructure and energy-grid cooperative spinout** of the Liana Banyan platform: a cooperative-owned system for coordinating the dispatch of distributed residential and small-commercial batteries against grid signals. Members who own or lease batteries participate in collective grid-support operations. The cooperative manages the dispatch, handles the settlement, and distributes the value back to member-owners at Cost + 20% — the same margin structure that governs every other platform operation.

Sub-mark **Broadcast Scheduler™** is the dispatch-timing primitive: the compression mechanism that turns a grid signal into a cooperative broadcast pulse ("who dispatches, when, in what order"). **Say It Fast™** (BP048) is the discipline that shapes those pulses — cooperative decisions compressed into broadcast-able information, distributed at the speed of grid needs.

Battery Dispatch™ is "Power to the People" in its most literal form: kilowatts, cooperatively managed, returning value to the households and small businesses that generate them.

## § 4 — How It Relates to the Cooperative Platform

Battery Dispatch™ is Spinout #4 in the platform's canonical spinout architecture (BP052). It is not a separate company — it is a cooperative-owned sub-operation that runs on the same member-governance, same Cost+20% margin, and same surplus-allocation rules as the core platform.

The relationship is structural:
- **Anchor** (sponsor-circle cooperative) underwrites member battery purchase for those who cannot afford upfront hardware
- **Brass Tacks** (#16) provides cooperative labor for installation and maintenance
- **Bonfire Project** (research arm) publishes the Broadcast Scheduler research upstream, keeping it open-source and SSPL-licensed
- **Rally** (#9) activates emergency-grid pivot protocols during crises (hurricanes, grid failures, demand spikes)

Battery Dispatch™ is where cooperative economic principles meet physical infrastructure. A member who participates may earn from their battery through grid-support operations — the same principle as a creator earning from their content, just in kilowatts instead of transactions. The cooperative keeps the value local, distributes it fairly, and governs it democratically.
