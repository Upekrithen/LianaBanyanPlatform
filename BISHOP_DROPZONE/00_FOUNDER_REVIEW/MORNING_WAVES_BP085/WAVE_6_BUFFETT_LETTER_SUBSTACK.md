# What If the Answer Isn't Philanthropy?
## An Open Letter to Warren Buffett

---

## Before the Letter

I wrote this on January 24, 2026. I sent it. Warren Buffett has not replied.

I am not bitter about that. He receives letters by the thousands. I am one voice among many, and I said as much in the letter itself — if he isn't the right fit, I hoped he would say so, or simply stay quiet until the end of March. The deadline passed. The platform kept building anyway.

I am publishing it now because the arguments in it are real arguments, not pitch-deck theater, and they belong in public. The cooperative I described is not hypothetical — it is live and building. If Buffett's silence means someone else should fill the role I described, they should be able to read what that role is. This letter defines it better than any summary I could write after the fact.

He had it first. Now you have it.

**Date:** January 24, 2026

---

Dear Mr. Buffett,

I have the math to prove it is impossible to concentrate wealth using the infrastructure I've already built. A platform that distributes it by design, through self-funded sustainable economics far beyond our lifetimes, with no venture capital needed and scalable profitability at 1,000 users anywhere.

The proof is in the bylaws—83.3% to creators, makers, and workers on every transaction, locked permanently. No board can change it. No investor can override it. I gave away the mechanism for extraction before I launched. The details are at Cephas.LianaBanyan.org.

Here's what's supposed to be impossible:

- **Three-gear currency** that solves international equity without touching FOREX. The IMF has been trying since Bretton Woods.
- **Market penetration, price skimming, AND discount pricing simultaneously.** Economics says pick two.
- **Distributed manufacturing** that cuts production costs by 95%—margins that fund 16 charitable initiatives without donations.
- **HexIsle** — water-powered gaming that converts hydraulics to pneumatics and gear mechanics. No batteries. No screens. Kids learn physics by playing with actual physics.

This isn't theory. 611+ patent claims filed January 23rd at 11:52 PM—the latest (#63/967,200) with 292 claims alone. 47 years of engineering. 1,200+ CAD diagrams. 18 handwritten journals. Prototypes since 2003 on active duty. I didn't pitch this to VCs. I built it for my 8 kids and the world. Plus it's an engaging and challenging pastime. And a lot of fun.

## What You Get

A 10% equity stake in the only platform that structurally cannot extract.

Your return comes from volume, not extraction. At 1,000 users, the platform sustains. At 100,000, it thrives. At 1,000,000, your stake generates returns that dwarf traditional investments—not because we take more from each transaction, but because there are more transactions.

The pie grows. Your slice stays the same percentage. The math rewards patience—which I understand is your specialty.

Your stake can pass to your foundation. A perpetual giving machine that funds itself.

## The Window

I'm launching now. The window where capital matters most is the next 12 months.

After that, either we've failed and your money couldn't have saved us, or we've succeeded and your money would cost us equity we no longer need to sell.

This is the moment where investment has maximum leverage.

You're my first choice for this role. This letter is yours alone. I don't build escape tunnels, just arrows. If I haven't heard back by end of March, I'll assume this isn't the right fit and hope to not need it by then. But I wanted you to know: this offer, at these terms, is yours first.

## Why You — And What Role You'd Fill

You co-founded the Giving Pledge. You're one of the few who's actually done what you said you'd do.

You've said giving money away doesn't solve concentration—it flows out and concentrates again. Philanthropy is a patch. This is infrastructure. The commerce is the charity.

**I'm not asking you to be Washington. I'm asking you to fill the role of the French fleet.**

Washington won the war. But Cornwallis surrendered at Yorktown because the French fleet blocked his escape. France didn't fight every battle—they showed up. They signaled this wasn't a doomed rebellion. It was a revolution with backing.

I'm writing to MacKenzie Scott to ask her to fill a role only she can fill—connecting me to operators who know how to scale community solutions. I'm writing to Melinda French Gates to ask her to fill another role—validating that this works in Lagos, not just Louisville. José Andrés, Sal Khan, Muhammad Yunus—each could lead an initiative. They're the army. I'm running the campaign.

Each role is distinct. Each is essential. And each can be filled by someone else if the person I've chosen declines. But you're my first choice for this one—the seed capital and credibility that makes everything else possible.

Your involvement tells everyone else: this is real. This has weight. This is worth joining.

You don't have to fire a cannon. Your sails on the horizon change the calculation for everyone.

If this isn't for you, I understand. But if you know someone better suited to this role—someone who thinks like you do about sustainable economics—I'd welcome the introduction.

## How I Got Here

When I was a kid, I rode a ten-speed with no brakes down hills because I wanted to see how fast I could go. Not reckless—I never hit a car, a building, or a person. Only a dog that was tracking me.

Challenge practice works. Like Burrell Smith from the Mac team—he'd start Galaga by killing off his only two backups. He had a saying: "Make a mess, clean it up." He lost a lot, at first. In time, he had every high score on the machine because he developed the skill not to need backup.

Decades of playing chess where I've lost more than I've won still gives me an objectively high rating. Not luck. 47 years of losing, studying what went wrong, and playing again. The rating is just proof that plugging away builds real skill.

This platform is the same. I've eliminated thousands of ways things won't work, and kept what does. The structure you're looking at isn't my first draft, or my hundredth—it's the version that survived everything I learned; applied.

I don't think I need backup. But I've lost enough times to make sure.

My IFR helicopter rating means trusting instruments when there's no horizon. That's what I'm asking you to do: trust the instruments. The math is sound. The platform is built. I've already done the test flights and I've done hundreds of autorotations in the darkness. The role I'm asking you to take isn't to fly—it's to signal to everyone else that this aircraft is airworthy.
