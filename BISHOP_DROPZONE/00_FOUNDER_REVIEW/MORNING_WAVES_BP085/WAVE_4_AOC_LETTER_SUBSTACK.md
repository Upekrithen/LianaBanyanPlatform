# Power to the People: An Open Letter to Representative Ocasio-Cortez

**Status:** PUBLISH-READY · 70-Q MMLU-Pro Receipt · V04 · BP083 · 2026-06-15
**Author:** J. Jones · Founder & General Manager · Liana Banyan Corporation
**Initiative:** #15 Political Expedition · Door-Opening Crown Offer

Dear Representative Ocasio-Cortez,

You have spent your career arguing that the economy should work for the people who actually work in it. I spent the last nine years designing---and the last seven months feverishly building---an economy that does, now, today.

Liana Banyan is a cooperative commerce platform. It consists of three commercial websites with a constitutionally locked Cost+20% margin that sustainably funds sixteen charitable initiatives across food security, healthcare, education, and civic engagement. Workers, builders, and creators keep the lion's share of every effort they put in. They own the platform. Membership costs five dollars a year. No venture capital. No ads. No shareholders extracting value. No exit strategy---because the point is not to sell it, it is to sustain it until it makes something better than itself and dissolves at year fifty.

We call it what it is: a Capitalist Cooperative. We earn margin. We sustain ourselves. And then we give the majority back to the people doing the work. Rain falls on the just and the unjust alike. Help Each Other Help Ourselves.

To make this worker-owned economy capable of competing with tech monopolies, we needed an equalizer. I built a technology I call the Substrace Theorem, implemented as MnemosyneC---the Caithedral™ of verified memory. It makes an ordinary AI far more capable at no added cost. Here is what that equalizer looks like in practice, verified on a consumer laptop, no paid API keys, running a free open-weights model:

On 2026-06-15, using the v0.3.5 canonical cooperative-architecture pipeline on a consumer laptop with Ollama + gemma4:12b, the Substrace Theorem scored **68 of 70 questions correct on the canonical MMLU-Pro academic benchmark---97.1%---across all 14 academic domains, with 316 verified-cooperative substrate eblets grown, and 2 Andon-cord-correct quarantines**. Every domain the cooperative pipeline entered turned GREEN. Not three. Not nine. Fourteen of fourteen. The full run completed in approximately 70 minutes. Phase 9 baseline, before cooperative architecture: 36 of 70 (51.4%). Cooperative-architecture lift: **+45.7 percentage points**.

The 2 quarantines are not failures. They are proof the system refuses to hallucinate. When the cooperative pipeline cannot verify an answer with sufficient confidence, it quarantines the question rather than guess. That is cooperative-class self-policing---the architecture enforcing epistemic honesty without being told to.

That is not a laboratory result. That is the equalizer. The most powerful tool of our age running on the same hardware your constituents already own, trained by no corporation, paid for by no subscription, owned by no monopoly. If a tech giant's proprietary model produces the same result, it does so at a price point that keeps it in the hands of the powerful. The Substrace Theorem produces it on a consumer laptop because it was designed for the cooperative class, not for the investor class.

Under our Cooperative Defensive Patent Pledge, we are giving it to the world, free, for every cooperative and nonprofit. Step one: level the playing field so the most powerful tool of our age belongs to everyone, not just those who can pay.

I am not writing to ask for money, nor for a political endorsement. I am writing to you about Initiative #15: Power to the People, our Political Expedition.

The Political Expedition is not a PAC. It is civic engagement built on a cooperative backbone: voter education, community organizing, and political participation tools funded entirely by the same commerce margins that fund our food security initiatives. The goal is to make civic participation so accessible and well-supported that *not* participating becomes the harder choice. You understand better than almost anyone in American public life that civic participation and economic participation are the exact same fight.

Democracy is a muscle. Use it or lose it. I have been thinking about civic structure since I was a child of missionaries in Dar es Salaam, through sharing my parents with fostered siblings six at a time, and through enlisted Army National Guard Infantry service at sixteen. Over two decades of building systems and raising eight children, I have watched the tools of participation get harder to reach for the people who need them most. Too many people have been told their arm does not count.

You bartended. You organized. You ran. You won. And then you used your platform not to accumulate power, but to demonstrate that ordinary people can wield it. The cooperative is designed to make that exact story ordinary.

I know that House Ethics rules prohibit a sitting Member of Congress from holding a formal governance seat in a private enterprise. Therefore, I am not asking you to take the seat directly.

I am offering you the Door-Opening Crown for Power to the People by asking you to appoint a surrogate from your world-class grassroots organizing network---someone with the DNA of the Justice Democrats or your own organizing team---to hold the First Seat on the Political Expedition Council on your behalf.

We are not building toward a fight. We are building toward a table large enough that no one has to fight to get a seat. The architecture makes hostile capture structurally impossible: you cannot extract value from a cooperative without granting freedom of participation to the people who create it. That is not a policy position. It is a design constraint.

I ask nothing of your office and nothing that conflicts with your public duties. I ask only that the movement you helped build send one trusted person to the table where the next phase of that movement is being designed.

With respect and in hope,
