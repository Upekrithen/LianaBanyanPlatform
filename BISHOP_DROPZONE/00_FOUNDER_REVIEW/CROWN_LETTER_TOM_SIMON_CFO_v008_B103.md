## What I Built

Liana Banyan Corporation is a cooperative commerce platform -- sixteen charitable initiatives funded by three commercial websites, where every creator, and worker, keeps 83.3% of revenue generated and the platform margin is permanently locked at Cost + 20%. The operating agreement makes extraction structurally impossible.

We use blockchain strictly as an immutable ledger -- for intellectual property provenance, transaction audit trails, and internal valuation markers. Not on a public mainnet. Not tradeable. Not redeemable for cash. Never used to raise money. We call it "Test-Net By Design": our provenance layer lives permanently on a test network, not as a staging step before mainnet, but as a deliberate architectural choice to prevent speculation from ever being possible.

Our internal currencies (Credits, Marks, and Joules) are closed-loop service units -- they work like arcade tokens: you can earn them, spend them inside the system, but you cannot sell them, transfer them, or cash them out. Period.

The value of our blockchain medallions, in my view, is precisely that they have no intrinsic value. They only signify -- immutably -- the actual dynamic value of the intellectual property they represent. The medallion isn't the asset. The innovation is the asset. The medallion is the notarized receipt that proves the innovation exists, when it was documented, and who contributed to it. That receipt can't be traded because it *shouldn't* be traded. It's evidence, not currency.

**One related note:** We recently formalized the AI coordination layer that makes all of this possible at scale -- the **Romulator 9000**, filed on Provisional Patent Application 64/036,646 on April 12, 2026. Its architecture is built on the same receipt-chain principle: every scheduled operation leaves a dated, cryptographically-bound receipt; a watchdog catches any job that failed to fire. Silent failure is structurally impossible. A forensic accountant would recognize the design -- it is audit discipline applied to software. The CFO seat at Liana Banyan will own oversight of both the blockchain provenance chain and the Romulator receipt architecture.

And we treat it as "garbage in, garbage forever" if we're not careful -- which is why the CFO seat owns controls over what gets notarized in the first place.

I've attached a technical paper -- *"Blockchain Without Coin or Speculation"* -- that lays out exactly how we use the technology and, more importantly, what we deliberately do not do with it. I wrote it specifically for someone with your forensic accounting background to evaluate. I'm not asking you to validate it. I'm asking you to find the holes.
