## Law 1 — No Unwitnessed AI Output
### *Unifies:* Tom Simon's forensic stance (2026-02-09) + Star Chamber double-blind verification (2026-01-26) + Moneypenny Protocol (current canonical §V)

**Statement:** No AI-produced output is treated as canonical until it has been witnessed — verified, challenged, or ratified — by a non-AI agent. The witness may be a human (Founder, Founder-designated Member, external counsel, subject-matter expert) or a deterministic system (TouchStone, Scrambler, Moneypenny, a predicate-verifier). AI-on-AI verification does not qualify, because it shares the training-data blindspot the verification is meant to catch.

**Why:** Tom Simon (26-year FBI forensic accountant) observed that "garbage in, garbage forever" applies to AI in a way that ordinary auditing does not — once a hallucinated fact enters the canonical record, later AI sessions treat it as ground truth and compound the error. Star Chamber was drafted as a Crown Letter credibility layer around the same insight, using double-blind AI verification against deterministic ground truth. Moneypenny Protocol is the operational expression of the same rule applied to daily Founder work. All three arrived at the same constraint from different angles: **the verifier cannot be AI, or we have the same problem one level up.**

**How this law gets applied:**
- Every claim entering `innovation_log`, `canonical_values.yaml`, Crown Letter LOCKED versions, or CANONICAL_LAWS_AND_FRAMEWORKS is witnessed by a non-AI agent before lock.
- TouchStone and the Scramblers (Innovation #2263) are the system-side witnesses for file-system state.
- Founder and Founder-designated humans are the witnesses for Founder-voice artifacts, legal interpretations, and any claim that requires judgment.
- External experts (counsel, Simon as CFO, domain SMEs) are witnesses for regulatory, financial, or domain-specific claims.
- If no non-AI witness is available at the moment a claim would be canonized, the claim holds as **candidate** (marked in the relevant ledger) and is not treated as canonical until a witness arrives.

**What this law forbids:**
- AI-only consensus passing as verification (two models agreeing is not a witness)
- Session-to-session AI ratification ("the previous Bishop said it" is a citation, not a witness)
- SP-16 Creative Recombiner output becoming canon without Founder review (the Recombiner *surfaces* candidates; Founder *witnesses* them)

## Law 2 — The Inversion Principle
### *Names:* the structural pattern behind every major Liana Banyan innovation

**Statement:** Major Liana Banyan innovations are structurally *inversions* of the conventional wisdom they replace, not *improvements* on it. Where the conventional solution adds more of the same thing, the Liana Banyan solution inverts the load-bearing assumption and removes the need for the thing entirely.

**Why:** SP-16 B109 observed the pattern across at least six innovations, independently named by Founder at different times:
- **Cost+20% (C+20):** inverts opacity-as-competitive-advantage. Where the conventional market treats cost information as proprietary, C+20 treats it as required disclosure and earns margin through reciprocity instead of concealment.
- **Ghost Credits:** invert demand-validation-after-build. Where the conventional lean-startup playbook says *build a prototype and test demand*, Ghost Credits say *sell the commitment first and use the commitments to decide whether to build*.
- **Position Funding:** inverts capital-first. Where the conventional stance is *raise capital, then hire the team*, Position Funding flips this to *commit the team, then democratically fund the positions the team needs filled*.
- **Peer-Proximity Expertise Matching (#2242):** inverts expert-altitude. Where the conventional assumption is *the best mentor is the most successful*, Peer-Proximity says *the best mentor is one rung ahead of you, because they remember the rung you're on*.
- **Medallion valuation:** inverts intrinsic-value. Where the conventional currency derives value from scarcity or convertibility, the Medallion's value is explicitly *that it has no intrinsic value* — per Tom Simon letter: "its value is that it has no intrinsic value."
- **R9 architecture:** inverts dump-the-haystack. Where Google, Microsoft, and Anthropic all answer "how does AI remember what matters?" by adding more context to the prompt, R9 answers by pre-pulling the needles and loading them first.

The pattern was *not intentional at design time* (Founder-confirmed B109) but is structurally *consistent across the portfolio*. Recognizing it retroactively gives future innovations a claim-structure template: when proposing a new mechanism, the question becomes *what conventional assumption am I inverting, not just improving?*

**How this principle gets applied:**
- Any new innovation proposal at CJ-candidate scale should be able to state its inversion explicitly: "the conventional solution does X; this solution inverts the assumption that X is necessary and does NOT-X."
- Crown Letters can use the inversion framing as a rhetorical spine — it maps cleanly onto the "Cardboard Boots" / Pratchett / Boot Theory template (the poor don't need *better* cheap boots, they need to not-buy-cheap-boots).
- Academic and patent claims benefit from the inversion framing because inversion claims are easier to distinguish from prior art than incremental improvement claims.

**What this principle does *not* claim:**
- Every LB innovation is an inversion (some are genuine refinements, e.g., the WaterWheel 30/40/15/15 split is a calibration, not an inversion)
- Inversions are always better than improvements (context-dependent)
- Other platforms never invert (Segway vs. cars was an attempted inversion; it failed. The pattern is descriptive, not prescriptive of success.)

**What it does claim:**
- Across the LB portfolio, the *load-bearing* innovations — the ones that carry the CJ claims and the Crown Letter asks — are inversions, not improvements. This is a retrospective observation that future innovations can benefit from being evaluated against.
