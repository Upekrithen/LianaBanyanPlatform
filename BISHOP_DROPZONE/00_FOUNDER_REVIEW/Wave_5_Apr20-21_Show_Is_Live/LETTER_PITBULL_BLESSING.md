# 🌎 LETTER TO PITBULL
## A Recognition Letter — Not a Crown Offer
## Mr. Worldwide, Meet Worldwide Workers
### To Pitbull — and to anyone positioned to help
## Liana Banyan Corporation

---

Dear Mr. Pérez,

I listen to your version of "9 to 5" with Dolly Parton every single day while I work.

Not as background music. As a reminder.

*"Working 9 to 5, what a way to make a living / Barely getting by, it's all taking and no giving..."*

That song — the original, your remix, the whole message — is exactly why I've spent nine years building what I'm building.

---

## WHAT I'VE BUILT

My name is Jonathan Jones. I'm a {{founderAge}}-year-old father of eight, ARNG veteran, and the founder of Liana Banyan — a cooperative platform where workers keep 83.3% of what they earn instead of getting squeezed by middlemen.

I'm not famous. I'm not a billionaire. I'm just someone who got tired of watching people work hard and get barely enough to survive. The systems we have aren't designed to help workers — they're designed to extract from them.

So I designed different ones.

**Cost + 20%.** That's the whole philosophy. Creators keep 83.3%. The platform takes 20% to operate. No hidden fees. No algorithmic manipulation. No race to the bottom.

It's the opposite of "all taking and no giving."

---

## WHY I'M WRITING TO YOU

I'm not asking you to run anything. You've got SLAM schools to build and a world to conquer. I know you're busy changing lives through education — charter schools for kids who need a shot, in communities that get overlooked.

I'm writing because your work and mine are pointing the same direction.

You give kids a path through education.
I'm trying to give adults a path through fair work.

You built SLAM because you know what it's like to come from nothing.
I built Liana Banyan because I delivered newspapers in freezing Montana at 13 and learned what extraction feels like.

Different paths. Same destination: **opportunity for people who don't get handed anything.**

---

## THE 9 TO 5 CONNECTION

When you and Dolly dropped that remix, something clicked for me.

Here's Dolly Parton — country music royalty, wrote the song 40+ years ago about workers getting squeezed — and here's Pitbull — Mr. Worldwide, came up from nothing, built an empire — and you two are saying the same thing:

*The system isn't fair. But we keep working anyway.*

That's the spirit I'm trying to capture. Not just complaining about the system — building something better alongside it.

I've written to Dolly too. Told her the same thing I'm telling you: your song is on my daily playlist. It keeps me going.

Just a word to someone in your network: *"Look at what this guy is building."* They can reach me directly at the number below.

If Liana Banyan succeeds — if we build a platform where millions of people work from home, keep 83.3% of what they earn, and actually get ahead — your song will have been part of the fuel that made it happen.
