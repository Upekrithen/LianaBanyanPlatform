### Why the structure is "half of my personal slice" and not "20% off the top"

Let me be clear about what you're buying and where it comes from, because the architecture matters.

Every patent in the Liana Banyan portfolio has its licensing revenue split four ways: 60% to member-held Patent Buckets, 20% to the Creator (me, for founder-introduced IP), 10% to a diversified Global Sponsor Pool, and 10% to Individual Patent Pedestals that fund the platform's operations. That's the cooperative's architecture, and it's constitutional — it doesn't change, ever, for anyone, under any circumstances.

**Your 10% comes out of my 20%.** Not off the top of the whole pool. Not out of any member's stake. Not out of the platform's operations. It comes from my personal Founder Creator share, and it comes at exactly half of that share — I keep the other half for my family during the same 20-year window, and the full 20% reverts to my estate at year 20 when your slice retires.

Three reasons for structuring it this way:

**First, it has nothing to do with anyone else.** My 20% is mine to dispose of commercially. Selling half of it to you affects no cooperative member, no bucket stakeholder, no sponsor, no Pedestal holder. The transaction is fully contained in my personal economic rights. Whatever we agree to is between you and me, period.

**Second, half is the only number that means what it says.** I could have offered you 60% of my share, or 70%, or 80%, and kept the rest. Every asymmetric number invites the question *why not 50/50?* and the honest answer is always *because I want more*. Half removes that conversation. It makes this transaction about the bet, not the split. I'm telling you: I believe in this portfolio so completely that I'll split the upside with you evenly, just because you're the right person to partner with on funding my escape velocity. That's a statement I'm willing to make.

**Third, I mean what I said earlier in this letter — I'm not trying to become a billionaire.** Half of my Founder's share over 20 years is enough, by a very large margin, for the things I actually need to do for my family and for the Canister System launch. The other half is plenty for legacy. Keeping more wouldn't change my life in any meaningful way. It would only change yours. And I'd rather sell half of a potentially huge future to get the other half, than keep all of a small present.

### The Structure

This is a Patent Revenue Participation Agreement. Not equity. Not a security. A partial assignment of my personal Founder Creator share in a specific patent portfolio, for a bounded time window, to a single sophisticated commercial counterparty.

**What you get:**
- 10% of all patent licensing revenue, royalties, and enforcement proceeds from the Liana Banyan patent portfolio — sourced entirely from my 20% Founder Creator slice at exactly half of that slice
- For a period of 20 years from execution
- Quarterly reporting on all patent-related revenue
- Visibility into the portfolio's growth (currently adding 20-30 innovations per week)
- Inheritance by your estate / heirs at death within the 20-year window (the RPA is non-transferable except by death to your estate)

**What you don't get:**
- No equity in Liana Banyan Corporation
- No membership interest in Upekrithen, LLC (my personal holding company)
- No board seat
- No vote on patent prosecution or licensing decisions
- No operational control
- No securities — this is a commercial contract between you and me personally

**What I retain:**
- 100% ownership of all patents (Liana Banyan Corporation holds title; I never transfer ownership)
- The other half of my 20% Founder Creator slice (= 10% of total licensing revenue) for the same 20-year window, for my family
- Full 20% Founder Creator slice reverting to me or my estate at year 20 when your share retires automatically
- Full control over prosecution, enforcement, and licensing strategy
- The right to cross-license, donate, or open-source specific patents for cooperative purposes
- The ability to grow the portfolio without diluting your revenue percentage

Your $5 million buys a revenue stream from my personal Creator share for 20 years. My portfolio grows. Your 10% of a larger portfolio becomes worth more over time. You don't need to manage anything. You don't need to approve anything. You collect a check.
