## 5. Distinguishing Đ from Vietnamese Dong

> ⚠️ **Critical disambiguation for USPTO examining attorney review:**
>
> The canonical NotCents mark is a **coined design mark** for cooperative finance. It is NOT the Vietnamese Dong currency symbol (VND, ₫, Unicode U+20AB).
>
> Key distinctions:
> - Vietnamese Dong symbol: ₫ (U+20AB, lowercase; U+0111 đ "LATIN SMALL LETTER D WITH STROKE")
> - The closest Unicode analog for internal reference: U+0110 Đ (LATIN CAPITAL LETTER D WITH STROKE)
> - The canonical NotCents mark: a **custom drawn** double-barred reversed-C glyph per BP048 design spec — structurally distinct from both Đ and ₫
>
> **Recommended TEAS "Translation/Transliteration" field entry:**  
> *"The mark is not a recognized currency symbol. It is a coined design mark representing the cooperative-finance philosophy of the applicant's platform. It is not a translation, romanization, or representation of any foreign language word or existing currency symbol."*
>
> **Counsel note:** USPTO examining attorneys have flagged Vietnamese Dong character marks in financial-services applications before. The custom-drawing nature of the NotCents glyph (distinct from the Unicode standard glyphs) provides the strongest defense. Ensure the submitted drawing is the custom BP048 design, not a Unicode font render of Đ.
