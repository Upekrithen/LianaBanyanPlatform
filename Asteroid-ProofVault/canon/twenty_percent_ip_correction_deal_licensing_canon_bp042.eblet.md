## §9 — Why Cooperative-Class Instead of Adversarial-Class

Adversarial enforcement (cease-and-desist · sue-for-damages · DMCA takedown) is **expensive · slow · destroys cooperation · favors well-funded parties**. It is the wrong tool for a cooperative-class licensing model.

Cooperative-class correction-deal is the structural alternative:

- **Automated.** Auto-detection + Harper Guild panel review · no court calendar dependency.
- **Cheap.** No litigation overhead · correction cost is the reserve, already pre-allocated.
- **Preserves cooperation.** The licensee is not an adversary; they are a member who chose the wrong tier. Correction restores cooperation rather than ending it.
- **Neutral on funding asymmetry.** A well-funded licensee and a Tier 1 member-operator face the same Harper Guild rule-base and the same 20% reserve mechanism.
- **Scales with cooperative-substrate growth.** Each new operator's tier selection auto-establishes their own reserve; correction capacity scales linearly with operator count.

The mechanism composes with:

- **IP Attribution Ledger Federal Body Cam** (BP041 SAGA 6) — every adjustment is recorded permanently.
- **Tower of Peace cross-cooperative dispute resolution** (K533 #20 reservation) — for overage cases that exceed the reserve.
- **Harper Guild rule-base** (AGPL Free Forever · K533 #12 verification) — adjudication panel rules are open-source and auditable.

**Trust-builds-through-correct-behavior.** Reserves release over time as compliance is demonstrated. The system rewards correct-tier-selection retroactively just as much as it corrects wrong-tier-selection retroactively.
