## §1 CANONICAL DEFINITION

**MENU** = the canonical Liana Banyan name for a member's curated, named, versioned, shareable inventory of Eblets.

A MENU is a member's "Eblet shelf" — a collection of Eblets they have curated and are willing to share with other members (or with their own AI agents). Just as a restaurant menu shows what is available and lets a guest choose, a Liana Banyan MENU shows what Eblets a member or cooperator has curated and is prepared to share.

**MENUS** (plural) is the canonical term for the social/sharing layer of the Eblet ecosystem.

Founder direct (BP058 verbatim):
> *"Inventory of Eblets, which we need to call: MENUS, and I can add their Menu to MY Menus and Recipes in my Helm, and either view it as aggregate ... or as separated, like from Bob, from Mary, OR most usefully, as aggregate and separated into Fruits, Vegetables, Hard Candies, etc. and other categorical configurations I can customize as a user to fit my liking"*

## §2 MOTIVATION / FOUNDER VOICE

The Eblet substrate is a cathedral of canonical knowledge — but it is not inherently social. MENUS introduce the social/sharing layer: the mechanism by which members surface, offer, and receive curated Eblet inventories from one another (and from their own AI configurations).

The naming metaphor is intentional and precise:
- A menu shows what is available without giving everything away
- A menu is curated by the host, not randomized
- A menu can be seasonal (versioned), shared across tables (cooperative-class), and adapted to preference (user-configurable categorical views)
- Multiple menus can be combined — when you add someone else's MENU to your Helm, you see their shelf alongside yours

The "We Are Those Workers / Builders / Creators" tri-term identifies three MENU archetypes:
- **Workers** share operational Eblets: workflows, tools, task-execution primitives
- **Builders** share architectural Eblets: system designs, specs, structural decisions
- **Creators** share content Eblets: articles, media, creative IP, publishing primitives

## §3 MECHANISM

### §3.1 MENU Structure

A MENU is a named, versioned, shareable collection with these attributes:

| Attribute | Description |
|---|---|
| **Name** | Member-assigned · human-readable · displayed in Helm |
| **Version / Strain** | Mnemosyne Strain-class tracking · v1, v2, etc. — members subscribe to a MENU at a specific strain or always-latest |
| **Owner** | The cooperative-class member who curated it · attributed permanently |
| **Contents** | An ordered or categorized list of Eblet references (not copies — pointers) |
| **Visibility** | Public to all members · shared with specific members · private (not yet shared) |
| **Fork discipline** | Any member may fork a MENU with attribution · "Forked from [Owner]'s MENU v2" · cooperative-class attribution is permanent |

### §3.2 MENU vs. Substrate

| Layer | What It Is |
|---|---|
| **Substrate / Cathedral** | The underlying storage and retrieval engine · pheromone trails · Wrasse registry · all Eblets live here · not user-visible as a single surface |
| **MENU** | The public-facing, user-curated shelf · what the member has selected to surface and share · a named view into a subset of the substrate |
| **Helm** | The dashboard that aggregates MENUS into views · per §Helm canon |

MENU ≠ the substrate. The substrate is the engine; the MENU is the curated display.

### §3.3 MENU Lifecycle

1. **Mint** — Member creates a named MENU and begins populating it with Eblet references
2. **Share** — Member sets visibility (public / named members / private)
3. **Subscribe** — Other members add the MENU to their Helm; it appears alongside their own MENUs in aggregate or per-source views
4. **Fork** — Member forks a MENU to create their own variant (attribution preserved)
5. **Version** — When Eblet contents evolve, the MENU versions; subscribers can pin to a strain or follow always-latest
6. **Sunset** — Member marks MENU inactive; substrate attribution survives (Eblets remain in cathedral with original IP credit)

### §3.4 Cold Storage Capsule Composition

In the Mnemosyne P2P vision (BP058 §2.5), MENUS stored in the member's Asteroid Proof Vault Cold Storage Capsule become **RECIPROCAL PACKETS** — other members can READ (not take) what the member has allowed in that Cold Storage partition. The reciprocity rule: access proportional to what you share. This composes with the DECENT™ data center P2P architecture.

## §5 CONSTRAINTS + ANTI-PATTERNS

**MENU is NOT:**
- The substrate itself (the cathedral stores everything; MENU is a curated shelf, not the warehouse)
- A complete dump of all Eblets (a MENU is curated — the member chooses what to include)
- A copy mechanism (MENU references Eblets; it does not duplicate them — Eblets remain in their source cathedral with original attribution)
- A proprietary lock-in (fork discipline is canonical — cooperative-class forks are always allowed with attribution)
- A search interface (that is pheromone_query and the Detective team — MENU is curation, not search)

**Anti-patterns:**
- Calling a raw pheromone query result a "MENU" — it is not; a MENU is curated and named by a member
- Creating anonymous MENUs — every MENU has an owner; attribution is structural and permanent
- Creating MENU contents that bypass Eblet half-life — stale Eblets age out regardless of MENU inclusion
- Sharing MENUs that contain private/secret Eblets — visibility controls exist and must be respected

## §6 EXAMPLES

**Example 1 — Worker MENU ("Jonah's Workflow Stack")**
A cooperative member named Jonah curates a MENU called "Jonah's Workflow Stack" containing 12 operational Eblets: task-routing patterns, commit discipline rules, and deployment checklists he uses daily. He sets it public. Other members add it to their Helm and see it as a per-source view ("From Jonah") alongside their own MENUs.

**Example 2 — Builder MENU ("Mary's Architectural Primitives")**
Mary is a system architect. Her MENU contains 20 architectural Eblets: API design principles, data model patterns, scaling decisions. She versions it; when she updates an Eblet, her MENU increments to v3. Subscribers on always-latest automatically see the new content in their Helm.

**Example 3 — Creator MENU ("Lena's Publishing Kit")**
Lena is a Harper's Guild creator. Her MENU contains publishing templates, style guide Eblets, and IP contribution agreement references. She shares it with her local Harper's Guild chapter members via visibility: named members.

**Example 4 — Hard Candy MENU ("Founder's AI Config Stack")**
The Founder curates a MENU containing Hard Candy Eblets for Bishop, Knight, Pawn, and Rook configurations. Any cooperative member who wants to instantiate their own AI agents with the canonical Liana Banyan configuration can fork this MENU. Attribution remains with the Founder. The fork carries "Forked from Founder's AI Config Stack v1" permanently.

**Example 5 — Categorical View in Helm**
Bob adds 5 MENUs from 5 different cooperative members to his Helm. His Helm shows him these views:
- Aggregate: all Eblets from all 5 MENUs sorted by relevance
- Per-source: "From Bob," "From Mary," "From Jonah" as separate tabs
- Categorical: "Fruits / Vegetables / Hard Candies" — food-class taxonomy applied across all 5 MENUs
- Custom: Bob creates his own category filter "Deployment Tools" that selects Eblets tagged with deployment primitives regardless of source
