## Question 1 — Is the instrument a LICENSE, a COVENANT-NOT-TO-SUE, or a REVENUE-SHARE (or a hybrid)?

**Bishop-recommended structure (unchanged thesis): a HYBRID — a patent + know-how LICENSE that grants affirmative permission to practice, paired with a separate REVENUE-SHARE (gain-share) payment covenant, and backstopped by a defensive COVENANT-NOT-TO-ASSERT that mirrors Cooperative Defensive Patent Pledge #2260.** Pawn's pressure test does **not** overturn the hybrid, but adds two hardening fixes (1.5–1.6 below) without which the *Brulotte/Kimble* dodge is "relabeling, not curing."

Reasoning (for counsel to confirm):

1. **Why a license at all, not a bare covenant-not-to-sue.** A counterparty wants *affirmative permission* to practice the patented methods and use the know-how/specs — a defined field of use, quality/attribution terms, and a clean basis to compute and audit the gain-share. A license is the cleaner vehicle for a commercial deal between sophisticated parties.

2. **Why pair it with a covenant-not-to-assert (#2260 mirror).** Keep the broad defensive peace as a covenant (so the world, including this counterparty, is protected against offensive assertion), and layer the *commercial* license + payment on top for the counterparty that wants to monetize savings. This separates "we will not weaponize the patents" (covenant, runs broadly) from "you may commercially incorporate and you pay 20%" (license + revenue-share, runs to this counterparty). Per HL5 (BP048 §6 Q1), the covenant form avoids the licensee-estoppel and patent-misuse exposure a pure license carries.

3. **Why the 20% is a verified-savings PAYMENT COVENANT, not a running patent royalty.** Framing the 20% as a patent royalty invites (a) *Brulotte/Kimble* royalty-after-expiry misuse risk and (b) tying the fee to the *patent* rather than the *value delivered*. The recommended frame: the 20% is **a share of independently verified savings that would not exist without MnemosyneC** — a discount the counterparty elects, not a toll — measured on verified savings, with the know-how/trade-secret + ongoing-substrate-service carrying consideration even after any patent expires.

4. **Securities / Howey.** Draft so the counterparty's payment is **consideration for a license + a service/substrate it itself operates**, not a passive investment in LB. Include an express recital: commercial license-and-services arrangement between sophisticated commercial parties; not a security; not an investment contract; neither party expects profit from the managerial efforts of the other. **Counsel to confirm Howey is not implicated.**

5. **★ PAWN HARDENING FIX (Brulotte safe-harbor — confident drafting fix).** Semantic relabeling alone will likely be pierced: if the 20% stays flat after the underlying patent expires, a court may strike it as post-expiry royalty regardless of the "payment covenant" label. *Kimble* itself supplies the safe harbor — post-expiration payments are valid when **tied strictly to non-patent rights** (trade secrets / proprietary software). Two structural fixes, belt-and-suspenders:
   - **(i) Explicit severability clause** separating the patent-license consideration from the trade-secret / software-license consideration, **allocating the 20% to the latter** so it survives patent expiry on a non-patent basis.
   - **(ii) Structural step-down** of the patent-attributable portion of the fee **upon patent expiration**, so no patent-tied payment extends past patent life.
   - *Confident this is the right shape; the exact step-down percentage / allocation split is counsel-to-confirm.*

> **★ THE SPECIFIC POINT COUNSEL MUST CONFIRM (Q1):**
> Confirm the three-part hybrid — (a) commercial **license** (affirmative grant + field of use + the 20% payment covenant), (b) separate **covenant-not-to-assert** mirroring Pledge #2260, and (c) the 20% as a **verified-savings payment covenant, NOT a patent running royalty**. **Critically (Pawn):** confirm the *Brulotte/Kimble* cure is real, not cosmetic — i.e., add the **severability** (patent vs. trade-secret/software rights) **and the patent-expiry step-down**, and confirm the no-securities/Howey recitals suffice.

### §1A.1 — Why *Brulotte/Kimble* Do Not Apply (Pathway 2 Primary)

The *Brulotte* rule (*Brulotte v. Thys Co.*, 379 U.S. 29 (1964)) and *Kimble* (*Kimble v. Marvel Entertainment*, 576 U.S. 446 (2015)) prohibit patent royalties that extend beyond the expiration of the licensed patent. They operate on a simple premise: once a patent expires, the patentee cannot collect royalties for practice of that patent.

**The cooperative's primary legal position is that *Brulotte/Kimble* are inapplicable in the first place**, for the following structural reason:

The underlying intellectual property — all patents, patent applications, and patent-eligible inventions comprising MnemosyneC and the cooperative commons — were **irrevocably donated** by the inventor (J. Jones) to the Liana Banyan cooperative commons. The IP donation is recorded in the IP Donation Agreement (see §1A.4 below). This donation is not a license; it is an unconditional transfer to the cooperative entity for the benefit of all members past, present, and future.

Because the IP is now **cooperative property**, the Cooperative Margin (Cost+20%) and the 83.3% creator-and-worker keep are **cooperative platform economics and governance allocations** — not a payment by a licensee to a patentee. Specifically:

- The **Cost+20% Cooperative Margin** is the platform infrastructure fee that covers cooperative operating costs plus a 20% overhead for ecosystem maintenance, not a royalty extracted in exchange for a right to practice a patent.
- The **83.3% creator-and-worker keep** is the cooperative's governance-mandated allocation formula under Statute §5, distributing platform revenues to the people and entities who generate value on the platform.
- Neither figure is computed by reference to any patented claim, patent life, or patent coverage. Both are bylaw-level rules that would apply identically if every patent in the cooperative commons expired tomorrow.

**Conclusion (counsel to confirm):** Because the cooperative owns the IP outright (via irrevocable donation, not license), there is no licensor–licensee royalty relationship. *Brulotte/Kimble* address royalties a *patentee* collects from a *licensee* for use of a *specific patent*. The Cooperative Margin is none of those things — it is a platform fee from a member or commercial partner to their own cooperative, for platform services, governance, and non-expiring know-how. The structure holds for the full 50-year charter with **no operative step-down on patent expiry.**

### §1A.2 — Severability Step-Down Clause (Pathway 2 Belt-and-Suspenders Fallback)

Notwithstanding Pathway 2 (§1A.1 above), the instrument shall include the following severability clause as belt-and-suspenders protection in the event any portion of the Cooperative Margin is ever characterized as a patent royalty by a court or regulator:

> **Severability Step-Down (verbatim for counsel to place in instrument):**
> *"To the extent any portion of the Cooperative Margin is ever deemed a royalty for the use of patented technology, that portion shall automatically step down by [X]% upon expiration of the last-to-expire patent in the cooperative commons, with the remainder of the Margin strictly attributed to non-expiring intellectual property — software copyrights, trade secrets, trademarks — and ongoing platform services."*

The bracketed [X]% is counsel-to-confirm based on the IP donation delineation schedule (§1A.4). The baseline position is that the patent-attributable portion is **zero** (Pathway 2), making the step-down a null operation — but the clause ensures that if a court recharacterizes, the recharacterized amount steps down and the Margin survives on a non-patent basis.

### §1A.3 — Immutability of §5 Allocations on Patent Expiry

**The 83.3% creator-and-worker keep and the Cost+20% Cooperative Margin are immutable cooperative bylaws (Statute §5). They do not change on patent expiry under any scenario.**

Specifically:
- No step-down (Pathway 1 or Pathway 2 severability) touches the **83.3% allocation** — this is the cooperative's internal distribution formula for creator and worker members, which is wholly independent of any patent consideration.
- No step-down touches the **Cost+20% Cooperative Margin** as a whole — the step-down, if ever triggered, applies only to the sub-portion of the Margin that a court has characterized as patent-attributable, not to the entire Margin.
- The **Cost basis** (the "Cost" in Cost+20%) is a platform-services cost recovery, not a patent toll, and is unaffected by patent status at all times.

**Counsel to confirm:** that the instrument's severability clause is drafted to make this immutability unambiguous — the step-down is a surgical sub-portion adjustment, not a wholesale Margin reduction, and the §5 allocation formula is expressly carved out of any patent-expiry trigger.

### §1A.4 — IP Donation Delineation Schedule (Drafting Action)

The IP Donation Agreement (Upekrithen LLC → Liana Banyan Corporation) must include, as a schedule or exhibit, a **patent-vs.-know-how delineation schedule** that:

1. **Lists the donated patented inventions** (by patent number, application number, or disclosure reference) separately from the know-how, trade secrets, algorithms, governance mechanics, and trademarks also donated.
2. **Expressly attributes** the Cooperative Margin primarily to the non-expiring components (software copyrights, trade secrets, governance protocols, trademarks) and to ongoing platform services, with any patent contribution stated as subordinate and separately identified.
3. **Provides the basis** for counsel to set the [X]% step-down figure in §1A.2, by quantifying what fraction (if any) of the Margin is attributable to the patented inventions specifically.

**Purpose:** This schedule is the evidentiary backbone of both Pathway 2 (demonstrating the Margin is not patent-derived) and the Pathway 1 severability fallback (establishing what step-down percentage is supportable). Without the schedule, the instrument's Brulotte defense is asserted but unanchored.

**Action item for counsel:** confirm the delineation schedule form, advise on the standard of specificity required to make it court-defensible, and integrate it into the IP Donation Agreement as a formal exhibit.

## Question 2 — AUDIT RIGHTS: how does the counterparty let LB verify the "savings"?

**Bishop-recommended structure (revised per Pawn): (1) a DEFINED SAVINGS FORMULA anchored to an OBJECTIVE EXTERNAL BENCHMARK, (2) certified COUNTERPARTY SELF-REPORT on a fixed cadence, (3) a CLEAN-ROOM INDEPENDENT AUDIT (mutually-agreed Big-4, binary/top-line reporting only) as the verification backstop.**

Reasoning (for counsel to confirm):

1. **Layer 1 — Define "savings"; anchor it to an objective external benchmark, not vendor OPEX (★ Pawn fix).** A formula keyed to the counterparty's *internal, opaque* cost metrics is a target for a **void-for-uncertainty** defense and is unverifiable in practice. Anchor the baseline to an **objective, externally verifiable reference** — e.g., published per-token API pricing and/or industry-standard inference benchmarks — rather than the vendor's proprietary internal OPEX. Bishop-recommended baseline: *savings = (objective-benchmark per-unit inference/context cost WITHOUT the MnemosyneC substrate) − (the same WITH the substrate), × qualifying volume over the period, net of reasonable substrate-operating cost.* The cooperative's COLD-vs-HOT harness (`librarian-mcp/r10_cross_vendor/run_benchmark.py`) is the *template for how a baseline is established*; the contractual figure is the **counterparty's qualifying production volume applied to the objective benchmark**.

2. **Layer 2 — Certified self-report on cadence.** Counterparty reports qualifying volume + formula inputs quarterly (or counsel's period), with an officer's certification.

3. **Layer 3 — Clean-Room independent audit (★ Pawn fix; replaces direct-inspection right).** No hyperscaler will grant LB direct access to inference-cost data, even NDA'd, and a materiality cost-shift is useless if the vendor can block the audit on security grounds. Instead: a **Clean-Room Audit by a mutually-agreed Big-4 accounting firm** under NDA, which reviews the counterparty's data but reports to LB **only** a binary "compliant / non-compliant" plus a **top-line discrepancy percentage** — never the underlying raw cost data. **Cost-shifting:** LB bears the audit cost unless the audit finds an under-report exceeding a materiality threshold (e.g., 5%), in which case the counterparty bears the cost and trues-up plus interest. **Remove** any LB right to directly inspect vendor server logs.

4. **Proprietary-data posture (counsel's call).** The paper's aspiration ("a vendor cannot dispute the fee by claiming their numbers are proprietary") is honored by the **objective-benchmark formula + clean-room reconciliation**, which verifies the fee without exposing raw cost data. Counsel decides whether to still demand open methodology (Founder/paper preference, harder to sign) or accept the clean-room compromise (Bishop+Pawn pragmatic recommendation).

> **★ THE SPECIFIC POINT COUNSEL MUST CONFIRM (Q2):**
> Confirm the revised mechanic — **objective-external-benchmark formula → certified self-report → Big-4 clean-room audit with binary/top-line reporting, materiality-triggered cost-shifting + true-up, no direct log inspection.** Confirm the benchmark-anchored formula is precise enough to defeat **void-for-uncertainty**, and resolve the **open vs. confidential** data posture.

## Question 3 — The NONPROFIT / SMALL-BUILDER MERCY CLAUSE

**Bishop-recommended structure (revised per Pawn): a tiered, objective-threshold eligibility clause — FREE below a small-actor threshold; REDUCED RATE in a middle band; full 20% above a large-actor threshold — drafted NOT as a "free grant" but as a CONDITIONAL COVENANT-NOT-TO-SUE keyed to objective revenue/size caps, with automatic termination on acquisition/sublicense to a non-qualifying entity, an explicit MFL carve-out, and express no-implied-license / no-partnership / no-exhaustion language.**

Reasoning (for counsel to confirm):

1. **Eligibility by objective threshold, not LB discretion.** Eligibility turns on objective, verifiable criteria: (a) 501(c)(3)/recognized non-profit, (b) trailing-12-month gross revenue or AI-spend below a defined ceiling, (c) headcount below a defined size, or (d) academic/research use. Any one qualifying criterion → free tier; a middle band catches grown-but-not-large actors. Thresholds are counsel-and-Founder's call; the *objective-tier structure* is the recommendation.

2. **Why "mercy" must be structural, not discretionary.** Objective, automatically-applied tiers are cleaner to administer and avoid inconsistent-enforcement arguments. The paper's framing — *"not generosity… architecture"* — is legally correct for this second reason too.

3. **★ PAWN FIX — do not call it a "free grant"; structure as a Conditional Covenant-Not-to-Sue.** A bare "free grant" risks (i) **patent exhaustion / implied license** bleeding into commercial tiers, and (ii) triggering a hyperscaler's **Most-Favored-Licensee (MFL)** clause ("you gave a zero rate — we invoke MFL for zero"). Fixes:
   - Draft the small/non-profit benefit as a **Conditional Covenant-Not-to-Sue** constrained by objective caps (e.g., trailing-12-month gross revenue under $X), **not** as an affirmative free license.
   - **Automatic termination** of the covenant if the entity is **acquired by, or sublicenses to,** an entity exceeding the cap (see also §7 assignment-on-acquisition).
   - Express recital: **creates no implied license, no patent exhaustion, no partnership, no joint venture.**
   - **MFL carve-out:** any MFL clause granted to a large counterparty must **expressly exclude** the mercy/non-profit tier, so the free tier cannot be invoked to ratchet a commercial rate to zero.

4. **Consistency with Pledge #2260 / SSPL.** The defensive covenant-not-to-assert (Q1) already protects everyone defensively; the mercy clause additionally **waives the commercial 20% payment** for qualifying small/non-profit actors. Flexing only the payment (not the grant) keeps the instrument coherent and matches the public "No Ads · No Strings" posture.

> **★ THE SPECIFIC POINT COUNSEL MUST CONFIRM (Q3):**
> Confirm the **objective-threshold tiering** + the **Conditional-Covenant-Not-to-Sue** framing (vs. a free license), set/bless the thresholds, and confirm the clause (i) avoids patent-exhaustion/implied-license, (ii) creates no partnership/securities expectation, (iii) **auto-terminates on acquisition by a non-qualifying entity**, and (iv) is **carved out of any MFL clause**.

## Question 4 — SUBCHAPTER-T interaction: patronage-sourced or non-patronage income? (★ FOUNDER DECISION + the riskiest question)

**This is the question most squarely outside a non-lawyer's competence — FLAGGED FOR COOPERATIVE-TAX SPECIALIST.** Per §0.1, the Founder has (1) confirmed the counterparties are *currently* strictly third-party non-members, (2) **authorized converting them to $5/year member-patrons if that fixes the tax**, and (3) declared the cooperative **open to either** path. Counsel chooses the mechanism that fixes the tax without breaking the Subchapter-T posture.

Reasoning (for tax counsel to confirm):

1. **The patronage / non-patronage line.** Under IRC Subchapter T, a cooperative may deduct **patronage dividends** — amounts paid to members out of net earnings **from business done with or for its members**. Income not arising from member business is **non-patronage income**, generally **not** deductible as a patronage dividend and taxed at the cooperative level like ordinary C-corp income. (LB is a Wyoming **C-Corporation** under Subchapter T.)

2. **PATH A — Non-patronage (the conservative default).** As the instrument stands (counterparties = non-members), the 20% is a commercial license/services payment from outside enterprises → **non-patronage-sourced**, **not** deductible as a patronage dividend, **taxed at the LB C-corp level** (≈21% federal) before any downstream use. Consequence: the "20% funds the sixteen initiatives" narrative (Part IV) must be restated as funded from **after-tax** non-patronage earnings. Gain-share sits **outside** and is **walled off from** the §13 / §13.7 patronage + capital-credits mechanic.

3. **PATH B — Member-patron / patronage-sourcing (Founder-authorized to explore).** If the counterparties become **$5/year member-patrons**, the 20% *might* be recharacterized as business done **with or for a member** → patronage-sourced → eligible for the patronage-dividend deduction at the cooperative level.
   - **★ PAWN'S CIRCULARITY TRAP (must be resolved, not assumed away):** if a hyperscaler is a member-patron, then patronage treatment implies LB would owe **patronage dividends back to the vendor-member** in proportion to the business done with it — "absurd circular economics" (the vendor pays 20%, then receives patronage allocations back). Tax counsel must determine whether the deduction can be achieved **without** an economically perverse dividend-back obligation (e.g., via the member-class definition, allocation units, or by-law allocation rules), or whether Path B collapses under its own circularity.
   - **Securities/most-favored note:** making a class of large commercial counterparties "members" must not (i) disturb the Howey posture (Q1) or (ii) create governance/voting consequences the cooperative does not intend (see the ≤5% money-weighted-vote cap canon — members who pay must not thereby buy governance weight).

4. **UBIT / characterization wrinkles (either path).** Because the income is royalty/license-flavored and from outside business, counsel should check **unrelated-business-income** exposure, special-allocation rules, and interaction with the §13 Annual Allocation Statement and §13.7 currency mechanic. Confirm the chosen path does not jeopardize the cooperative's overall Subchapter-T posture (HL5 §3 item 2: "if §11–13 do not cohere as one mechanic, patronage tax treatment is at risk").

> **★ THE SPECIFIC POINT TAX COUNSEL MUST CONFIRM (Q4):**
> Decide between **Path A** (non-patronage; accept co-op-level tax; restate Part IV as after-tax; wall off from §13/§13.7) and **Path B** (counterparties as $5 member-patrons; patronage-sourced) — **and if Path B, resolve Pawn's patronage-dividend circularity** and the governance/Howey side-effects. Founder is path-agnostic; the constraint is "fix the tax without breaking Subchapter-T." Flag all UBIT/characterization issues. **Cooperative-tax-specialist question; the above is a non-lawyer's issue-spot, not an answer.**
