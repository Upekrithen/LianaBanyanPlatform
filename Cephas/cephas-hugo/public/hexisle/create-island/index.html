Conducted Intelligence — Island Creation#

“It’s HOW you use it”#

Founder direct (BP021 turn 129): “I want to put out a pretty big bounty — If you join LB, you can be part of making our own worlds in HexIsle using our new CAI enabled interface. MAKE your own island, that is part of the Ghost World/Real World that IS HexIsle!”

CAI ◌ NotCents is the Liana Banyan productized AI interface. You don’t interact with a raw vendor API. You interact with Conducted Intelligence — your design intent is routed by Conductor’s Baton (#2277) to the right model class, fanned out across specialist subagents, and assembled into a permanent piece of HexIsle lore.

What You’re Building#

A member-island is a permanent coordinate in the Multi-dim Twinning tensor:

Locations × Dimensions × Times

Three axes. Every island occupies a point in all three:

AxisWhat it means
LocationsA Real-World anchor — geographic, cultural, or structural
DimensionsWhich Ghost-World layer your island inhabits
TimesChronos timestamp — default: present through perpetuity

Your island is bidirectionally twinned: the Real World and Ghost World versions reference each other permanently. Neither can be un-written.

The CAI Interface#

The /hexisle/create-island interface is Conducted Intelligence in action.

How it works#

  1. You describe your island in natural language — no forms required to start
  2. Conductor’s Baton (#2277) routes your intent to specialist subagent threads:
    • Spec-author — structures your island spec into the canonical schema
    • Lore-author — develops the Ghost-World narrative
    • Mech-designer — identifies which of HexIsle’s 33 base innovations your island composes with
    • Real-World-anchor researcher — helps surface the right twinning point
    • IP-Ledger-stamp preparer — readies your creator attribution
  3. You see progress as each subagent thread completes (X-Ray Mode toggle: inspect the routing if you want to)
  4. Preview pane — live render of your island spec as you author it

What gets produced#

Your island spec includes:

  • Island name and lore summary
  • Real-World Twinning anchor (the bidirectional canonical link)
  • Ghost-World state (mechs / lore / inhabitants / events)
  • Tensor coordinate (Locations × Dimensions × Times)
  • 3-access-key configuration (Deck Card / Guide / Babylon Candle)
  • Pedestal Forum entry point (Mordecai-Esther decree-composition welcome mat)
  • IP Ledger creator-stamp

X-Ray Mode#

Every member using CAI can see through the interface to the routing:

  • Which model class handled your spec (Sonnet for structured spec-emit; Opus for narrative-class)
  • Which subagent threads fired
  • What each subagent contributed
  • The Conductor’s routing decision

This is the “It’s HOW you use it” philosophy made visible. CAI ◌ NotCents is not a black box.

3-Access-Key Gating (FLAG_MARKER FM-001)#

When you create your island, you configure how other members visit it:

Deck Card (Durable)#

A signed membership credential. Non-consumable — members present once and it’s valid across sessions. LB membership is the foundation; your island’s cohort-class eligibility criteria layer on top.

Guide (Mediated)#

A co-member vouches for a visitor. Routes through the Pheromone trust-anchor graph — the endorsement creates a visible, permanent social trail. Guide access is the trust-network in action: relationships matter.

Babylon Candle (Consumable Transient)#

Single-use Marks expenditure. The visitor burns a Candle. The Marks flow: 83.3% to you as creator (platform Cost+20%). Every Babylon Candle burn is an IP Ledger credit to your account. The Candle is transient — it burns once. The credit is permanent.

Mordecai-Esther Decree-Composition#

Once your island is submitted, its original spec is immutable — the Law of the Medes and Persians (Esther 8). No one, not even you, modifies it.

But other members can append co-equal-authority decrees to your Pedestal Forum:

  • extending — builds on your original
  • contradicting — challenges your original
  • composing — does both simultaneously

All three have the same standing as your original. Contradictions are legally valid alongside the original. The island grows through co-authorship without anyone touching the foundation.

MIC-003 (see Standing Bounties) specifically rewards members who design their island to attract high decree-composition density.

IP Ledger + Marks Economics#

Every member-island you create:

  1. IP Ledger stamp — permanent creator attribution. The record is in the substrate.
  2. Marks per visit — 83.3% creator-keeps per-visit; platform Cost+20%
  3. Tier 6 multiplier — 4.0× base Marks pay rate for the Member-Island-Creator class
  4. #2260 framework — your island inherits the Cooperative Defensive Patent Pledge

LB membership is $5/year — pricing identical for every member from day one through year fifty, lifetime guarantee at signup price. The Marks economics above are IP-Ledger-class, not membership-pricing. Membership-orthogonal.

Eligibility (Tier 6 Member-Island-Creator Class)#

To create a member-island:

  • Active LB membership
  • Pedestal active
  • IP Ledger registered
  • #2260 Cooperative Defensive Patent Pledge co-signature on file

The #2260 co-signature means every island you create enters the Cooperative Defensive Patent framework. Your creations are collectively protected by the whole Federation.

Canon-Class Promotion#

Your island starts as member_class. The canonical-7 islands (Harvest, Navigate, Engineer, Battle, Seek, Magic, Train) are permanently immutable — they are the Founder-authored original canon.

Member-N islands compose alongside the canonical-7. They never replace or modify the original seven.

If your island earns it, the Founder can promote it to canon_promoted status via Founder Fire Code authority. Canon-promoted islands gain:

  • Wrasse anchor priority (sub-ms substrate retrieval)
  • Cross-island federation routing default-on
  • IP allocation 60/20/10/10 framework engagement

Canon-class promotion is not automatic — it is a Founder decision. The fire code gate is intentional.
