I'm Just a Bill: Older Americans Act Reauthorization

👀 I’m Just a Bill — Older Americans Act Reauthorization A Liana Banyan “I’m Just a Bill” Bounty Poster What is this bill? Reauthorization of the Older Americans Act (OAA) in the 119th Congress. The OAA funds Area Agencies on Aging, congregate and home-delivered meals (Meals on Wheels), in-home services, family caregiver support, and elder-rights services. What does it actually do? Reauthorizes federal grant programs supporting older-adult community services through 2030. Updates the program structure to address gaps identified since the 2020 reauthorization. Caregiver support: Expands National Family Caregiver Support Program funding. Nutrition services: Modifies congregate-meal program structure to accommodate post-2020 service-delivery patterns. Elder-rights: Strengthens elder-abuse prevention and reporting infrastructure. Who votes when? House Education and Workforce + Senate HELP active consideration; reauthorization typically bipartisan. How does this affect cooperative-class families? Composes directly with #5 The Family Table ($11T unpaid-care-work + Ashton Applewhite Age Champion) and #6 Tatiana Schlossberg Health Accords.
