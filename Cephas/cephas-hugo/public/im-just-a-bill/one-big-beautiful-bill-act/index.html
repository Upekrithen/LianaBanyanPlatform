I'm Just a Bill: One Big Beautiful Bill Act (H.R.1, 119th Congress)

Active May 12, 2026

👀 I’m Just a Bill — H.R.1 “One Big Beautiful Bill Act”

A Liana Banyan “I’m Just a Bill” Bounty Poster

What is this bill?

H.R.1 is the comprehensive reconciliation package moving through the 119th Congress in 2025-26. It bundles tax provisions, social safety net changes, and federal spending priorities into a single budget reconciliation vehicle.

“Reconciliation” means it only needs a simple majority in the Senate (51 votes), not the usual 60-vote filibuster threshold. That’s why so much gets packed into one bill.

What does it actually do?

In plain language:

  • Tax provisions: Extends and modifies tax cuts, including changes to individual rates and pass-through deductions.
  • SNAP (food stamps): Adjusts eligibility rules and work requirements; potential reductions in benefit duration.
  • Medicaid: Restructures federal-state cost sharing; potential changes to coverage and eligibility verification.
  • Federal spending: Re-prioritizes discretionary funding across multiple agencies.

Each provision affects different people differently. The Council vetting standard is to describe what’s actually in the bill, not whether it’s good or bad.

Who votes when?

  • House: Already passed (per reconciliation rules, simple majority required)
  • Senate: Active consideration; reconciliation process ongoing
  • President: Signature expected if it reaches the desk

How does this affect cooperative-class families?

The cooperative compact is structurally independent of any specific federal tax or benefit policy. The platform’s $5/year membership and 83.3% creator-keep are constants regardless of what H.R.1 does.

But for families relying on SNAP or Medicaid: this bill matters directly. The cooperative is building Initiative #1 (Let’s Make Dinner — Freezer Host network + Homeless Help Solution) and Initiative #7 (Member Service Account / Medical Savings Accounts) precisely so the cooperative-class doesn’t depend on federal-program churn for food and basic healthcare access.
