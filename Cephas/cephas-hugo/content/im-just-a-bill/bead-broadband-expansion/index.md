# 👀 I'm Just a Bill — BEAD Broadband Expansion
## A Liana Banyan "I'm Just a Bill" Bounty Poster

## What is this bill?
Modifications to the Broadband Equity, Access, and Deployment (BEAD) program in the 119th Congress, addressing implementation challenges in the original 2021 Infrastructure Investment and Jobs Act broadband-deployment provisions.

## What does it actually do?
- **Modifies** state allocation formula for the $42.45B BEAD program.
- **Permitting:** Streamlines federal-permit timelines for broadband infrastructure.
- **Deployment standards:** Updates definition of "underserved" and "unserved" areas.
- **Affordability:** Modifications to the Affordable Connectivity Program successor.
- **Technology neutrality:** Provisions for fiber + fixed-wireless + satellite eligibility balance.

## Who votes when?
- House Energy & Commerce + Senate Commerce active consideration.

## How does this affect cooperative-class families?
Rural broadband is the structural floor for cooperative-class participation in nearly every Initiative. Members in BEAD-served areas can access **#9 Rally Group** organizing infrastructure, **#12 + #14** cooperative education, **#15 "I'm Just a Bill"** civic information, and **#3 Lets Go Shopping** marketplace participation that they cannot reach without broadband.
