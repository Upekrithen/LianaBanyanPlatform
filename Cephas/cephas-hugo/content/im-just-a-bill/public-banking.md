### 3. PUBLIC BANKING

#### Legislative Status 119th Congress
- **State-Level Activity**: California (AB 1177), New York, Washington active implementation or study
- **Federal Coordination Bills**: No comprehensive federal framework; piecemeal state support provisions
- **Treasury Guidance Gaps**: Federal Reserve master account access remains contested for public banks

#### Cooperative Finance Trio Application

**MSA (#7) Integration:**
Public banks operate as government-sponsored MSAs:
- **Jurisdictional Service Boundaries**: Municipal/state residents as defined membership base
- **Democratic Governance**: Electoral accountability creates MSA governance through civic participation
- **Public Benefit Mandate**: MSA services prioritize community need over profit extraction

**VSL (#10) Sequencing:**
Public banking value sequence:
```
Public Deposits (tax revenue, fees) → Public Bank Capitalization →
Local Infrastructure/Business Lending → Economic Multiplier Effects →
Increased Tax Revenue → Enhanced Public Services →
Deposit Growth (virtuous cycle)
```

VSL highlights **closed-loop value circulation** within jurisdiction versus **value leakage** to external commercial banks.

**LMB (#11) Bread-Making:**
- Commercial banking: **Selling bread ingredients** to external corporate bakeries, buying bread back at markup
- Public banking: **Community-owned bakery** processing local ingredients into bread for local consumption, retaining value at every stage

#### Blood Rule Compliance

**Critical Challenge:**
Public banks inherently operate in fiat (government-issued currency). Blood Rule compliance appears structurally impossible without radical redesign.

**Potential Accommodation:**
**Public banks as fiat-to-cooperative-value bridges:**
1. Public bank receives tax revenue (fiat)
2. Establishes **community development accounts** denominated in local currency units
3. Lends in **hybrid instruments**: partial fiat (for external purchases) + partial local currency (for local labor/goods)
4. Creates **substitution corridors** allowing local businesses to accept local currency, exchangeable for fiat only at public bank interface (preventing fiat proliferation)

**Blood Rule Interpretation:**
Public banks don't violate Blood Rule if they function as **controlled conversion boundaries** rather than fiat proliferation engines—similar to historical **currency boards** that issued local notes backed by (but distinct from) imperial currency.

#### Substitution-Only Routing

Public bank transaction architecture:
- **External Interface Layer**: Fiat transactions with federal/state governments, external vendors
- **Internal Clearing Layer**: Local currency transactions among municipal businesses and residents
- **Substitution Protocol**: Local currency instruments route through municipal clearing house; fiat backing held at Federal Reserve but not circulated internally

**Municipal Business Example:**
City contractor paid in **"Municipal Bonds"** (local currency) + limited fiat for external materials → Contractor pays employees in Municipal Bonds → Employees spend at local businesses accepting Municipal Bonds → Businesses pay local taxes in Municipal Bonds → City reinvests in municipal services

Fiat touches only the boundary transactions (external material purchases), substituted with local instruments for internal circulation.
