## II. LEGISLATIVE DOMAIN ANALYSIS

### A. SAVE Act (Safeguard American Voter Eligibility Act)

**Policy Substance:**
The SAVE Act mandates documentary proof of citizenship for federal election voter registration, requiring state and local election officials to verify citizenship status before accepting registrations. Proponents argue this prevents non-citizen voting; opponents contend existing safeguards are adequate and the measure creates registration barriers.

**Power-to-the-People Council Analysis:**
- **Empowerment Dimension:** Could reduce citizen confidence if perceived as addressing phantom problems rather than genuine voter input needs
- **Council Integration:** Local election boards (the frontline councils) report minimal non-citizen voting incidents; Act imposes federal mandates without incorporating ground-level empirical input
- **Participation Impact:** Creates documentation requirements that may disproportionately affect naturalized citizens, elderly voters without passports, and citizens born in unincorporated territories

**Federal-Congressional Body Cam Doctrine:**
- **Transparency Requirements:** Would necessitate public documentation of: (1) citizenship verification error rates, (2) rejected registration demographics, (3) administrative cost burdens, (4) provisional ballot usage increases
- **Accountability Mechanism:** Real-time dashboards showing verification timelines, appeal processes, and resolution rates per jurisdiction
- **Implementation Visibility:** Monthly reporting on voter registration processing delays attributable to new verification protocols

**Listen Before Talk Application:**
- **Consultation Deficit:** Minimal input from election administrators (NASS, NASED) who implement federal mandates
- **Stakeholder Engagement:** Limited engagement with naturalized citizen communities most affected by documentation requirements
- **Evidence-Based Dialogue:** Existing Heritage Foundation/Brennan Center data show non-citizen voting instances in 0.0001% range; policy response proportionality questionable

**Ambassador Position Assessment:**
- **Representative Legitimacy:** Raises questions about whether federal standardization respects state election administration sovereignty
- **Constituent Trust:** May signal distrust of election officials who currently verify eligibility through existing protocols
- **Democratic Functioning:** Creates potential disconnect between representatives and election infrastructure professionals

**By Their Fruits Evaluation:**
- **Measurable Outcomes:** Success metrics should include: eligible citizen registration rates, verification processing times, administrative costs, litigation expenses, voter confidence surveys
- **Unintended Consequences:** Potential registration decreases among eligible voters lacking documentary proof may outweigh prevented ineligible registrations
- **Comparative Performance:** States with strict documentation already provide natural experiments for efficacy assessment
