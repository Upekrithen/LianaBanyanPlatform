# 👀 I'm Just a Bill — Veterans Auto and Education Improvement Act (H.R.1289)
## A Liana Banyan "I'm Just a Bill" Bounty Poster

## What is this bill?
H.R.1289 in the 119th Congress would expand veteran education and transportation benefits, addressing gaps in the Post-9/11 GI Bill and VA-administered transportation assistance programs.

## What does it actually do?
- **Education benefits:** Extends Post-9/11 GI Bill eligibility windows and expands covered programs (apprenticeships, on-the-job training).
- **Transportation:** Improves VA transportation assistance for veterans accessing care, especially in rural areas.
- **Spousal benefits:** Extends spouse-and-dependent benefit transferability.
- **Workforce reentry:** Funds veteran-specific apprenticeship programs.

## Who votes when?
- **House Veterans' Affairs Committee:** Active markup
- **Senate Veterans' Affairs:** Companion under review
- **President:** Signature historically routine for veteran-benefit legislation

## How does this affect cooperative-class families?
Composes with the cooperative's veteran-Crown anchors on Initiative #15 (Mark Wahlberg, John Cena, Gary Sinise, Sam Elliott, Tom Hanks — all veterans-supporters) and with **Initiative #16 Brass Tacks** (apprenticeship-pipeline composition with Mike Rowe Council Crown).

The cooperative's veteran-class members benefit directly from federal education and transportation supports; the cooperative's Initiative architecture composes with these federal programs rather than duplicating them.
