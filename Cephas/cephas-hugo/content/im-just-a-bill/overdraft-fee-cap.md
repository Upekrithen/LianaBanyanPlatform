### 2. OVERDRAFT FEE CAP

#### Legislative Status 119th Congress
- **CFPB Proposed Rule**: $3-$14 cap range (January 2024 proposed, 119th Congress implementation)
- **Congressional Review Act (CRA) Threats**: Republican opposition positioning for potential nullification
- **Credit Union Exemption Debates**: Whether cooperative institutions face identical restrictions

#### Cooperative Finance Trio Application

**MSA (#7) Integration:**
Overdraft services constitute money service agreements between institutions and members:
- **Explicit Service Pricing**: Fee caps enforce transparent pricing, aligning with MSA principle of disclosed value exchange
- **Risk Mutualization**: Cooperatives can absorb overdraft costs across membership rather than punitive individual extraction
- **Service Unbundling**: MSA framework suggests separating overdraft from core deposit services with independent opt-in protocols

**VSL (#10) Sequencing:**
Traditional overdraft value flow:
```
Member Insufficient Funds → Bank Temporary Credit →
Bank Fee Extraction → Member Debt Spiral
```

Cooperative VSL alternative:
```
Member Insufficient Funds → Mutual Aid Pool Activation →
Peer Support Credit → Graduated Repayment →
Pool Replenishment → Community Resilience Increase
```

VSL documents **value creation** (community mutual aid) versus **value extraction** (predatory fees).

**LMB (#11) Bread-Making:**
- **Ingredient waste** (overdraft) in traditional banking means **discarding entire batches** (account holders) due to minor shortfalls
- Cooperative model: **Salvaging dough** through mutual resources, producing **imperfect but nutritious loaves** (financial inclusion despite temporary insufficiency)

#### Blood Rule & Substitution Routing

**Fee Cap as Blood Rule Enforcement:**
Overdraft fees represent **fiat extraction from those with least fiat**—a Blood Rule violation at conceptual level. Fee caps are **harm reduction**, but Blood Rule compliance requires:

**Structural Alternative:**
- **Overdraft as mutual credit** within closed cooperative loop
- Member overdrafts generate **time-debt or service-debt to community** rather than dollar-denominated interest
- Substitution routing: Overdraft request → Community credit allocation (non-fiat) → Service restoration → Member contribution to mutual pool (labor, expertise, goods)

**Implementation:**
Credit unions could operate **dual-ledger systems**:
- **Fiat ledger** for external transactions (no overdraft, hard limits)
- **Mutual credit ledger** for member-to-member obligations (flexible, non-extractive)
