# 👀 I'm Just a Bill — College Athlete Compensation Provisions
## A Liana Banyan "I'm Just a Bill" Bounty Poster

## What is this bill?
Federal Name-Image-Likeness (NIL) and college-athlete compensation legislation in the 119th Congress, addressing the post-2021 patchwork of state NIL laws and the related college-athletics labor questions.

## What does it actually do?
- **Federal NIL framework:** Sets uniform federal standards preempting conflicting state NIL laws.
- **Athlete employment status:** Addresses (or explicitly does not address) the question of whether college athletes are employees.
- **Revenue sharing:** Provisions for athletic-program revenue sharing with athletes.
- **Title IX integration:** Provisions ensuring NIL/compensation structures comply with Title IX.
- **Transfer portal:** May include provisions affecting transfer eligibility rules.

## Who votes when?
- House Energy and Commerce + Senate Commerce active consideration; bipartisan working groups.

## How does this affect cooperative-class families?
Composes with **#13 JukeBox** (cooperative IP licensing model — the cooperative-class structural pattern for athlete-NIL is similar to the cooperative-class structural pattern for music-NIL).

The cooperative does not endorse positions on college-athlete compensation policy. The Council exists to provide accurate plain-language explanation.
