# 👀 I'm Just a Bill — Bipartisan Background Checks Act
## A Liana Banyan "I'm Just a Bill" Bounty Poster

## What is this bill?
The Bipartisan Background Checks Act would close the "gun show loophole" by requiring background checks on virtually all firearm sales, including private sales between non-dealers.

## What does it actually do?
- **Universal background checks:** Extends NICS background check requirement to private firearm sales and transfers.
- **Limited exceptions:** Family transfers, temporary use at ranges, hunting situations.
- **State implementation:** Federal floor; states may add stricter measures.
- **No new registry:** Bill explicitly prohibits creation of a federal firearm registry.

## Who votes when?
- **House Judiciary + Senate Judiciary:** Active consideration
- **Cloture:** Major Senate hurdle (60-vote threshold)
- **President:** Signature dependent on chamber action

## How does this affect cooperative-class families?
The cooperative does not endorse positions on gun-safety legislation. The Political Expedition Council exists to provide accurate plain-language explanation of what bills do, leaving the values judgment to members.

Concrete impacts to consider:
- Estimated affected sales: ~20% of firearm transfers per available data
- State implementation experience: 14 states already have universal background check requirements
- Background-check infrastructure (NICS) operational since 1998
