# 👀 I'm Just a Bill — Section 230 Reform
## A Liana Banyan "I'm Just a Bill" Bounty Poster

## What is this bill?
Section 230 reform legislation in the 119th Congress. Section 230 of the Communications Decency Act provides liability protection for online platforms regarding user-generated content. Current proposals modify the scope of protection, with significant cross-aisle disagreement on direction.

## What does it actually do?
- **Algorithmic amplification carve-out** — some proposals remove Section 230 protection for content algorithmically amplified by the platform
- **Bad-actor carve-out** — strips protection for specific content classes (child sexual abuse material, terrorism content, deepfake non-consensual sexual content)
- **Good-Samaritan modifications** — adjusts the safe-harbor for platform content moderation
- **AI-generated content provisions** — addresses whether Section 230 protects AI-generated content (separate from user-generated)

## Who votes when?
- House Energy & Commerce + Judiciary + Senate Commerce + Judiciary active consideration

## How does this affect cooperative-class families?
The cooperative is structurally configured to avoid the algorithmic-amplification failure mode. The cooperative does not run engagement-maximizing recommender systems. Section 230 reform affects extraction-class platforms more than cooperative-class platforms.
