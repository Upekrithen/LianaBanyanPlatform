### G. Firefighter Cancer Presumption: Occupational Disease Recognition

**Legislative Summary:**
Proposals establish presumptive cancer coverage for firefighters under workers' compensation and federal disability benefits, recognizing carcinogen exposure correlation. Extends coverage to volunteer firefighters and shifts burden-of-proof to presumption of work-relatedness.

**Subject-Model Analysis:**
- Firefighters positioned as workers injured by employment conditions requiring compensation
- Regulatory presumption eases claims process but maintains employer-worker benefit structure
- Protects workers by improving benefit access for occupational disease
- Does not alter underlying employment relationship or risk-distribution structure

**Member-Model Analysis:**
- Firefighter cooperatives rare but conceptually viable (private fire services exist)
- Member-owners of fire service cooperatives would collectively manage risk and coverage
- Presumptive coverage more easily absorbed when workers govern risk-management policies
- Democratic governance allows direct voice in safety equipment and procedure decisions

**Council Crown Evaluation:**
- **Wahlberg Lens:** Public service workers deserve benefit-of-doubt on occupational injury; working-class solidarity with first responders
- **Cena Lens:** Firefighters embody service ethic; cancer presumption honors sacrifice
- **Stallone Lens:** Working-class heroes facing health consequences of dangerous work merit comprehensive support

**World's-Largest-Employer Implications:**
- *Limited Direct Application:* Most firefighting public sector or volunteer; cooperative model less applicable
- *Solidarity Principle:* Comprehensive support for firefighters models broader worker protection ethic
- *Hybrid Structures:* Municipal fire departments could incorporate worker governance mechanisms (policy voice, safety committees) short of full cooperative conversion

**Structural Recommendation:**
Support cancer presumption legislation while advocating for firefighter governance participation in municipal fire departments—
