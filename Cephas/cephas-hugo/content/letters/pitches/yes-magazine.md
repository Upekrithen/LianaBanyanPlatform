**The Hook:**

A cooperative platform has done something unusual: locked its economics in the operating agreement so they can't drift toward extraction. Creators keep 83.3%. The margin is fixed. And it took 15 years to build.

**Why Yes! Magazine:**

Your readers want solutions, not just problems. This is an attempt to solve platform extraction structurally—not with promises, but with architecture.

**The Platform:**

Liana Banyan is a cooperative commerce platform:

- **Cost + 20%** margin locked in operating agreement
- **83.3%** creator retention
- **$5/year** membership
- **14 initiatives** from meal sharing to microloans to cooperative manufacturing
- **Fractional ownership** for all members

**The Story:**

Jonathan Jones, 52, is an ARNG veteran, helicopter pilot, and father of eight. He's invested $5,000 annually from a $30,000 income for years. He started designing this platform in 2003 while on active duty.

The core insight came in 2010-2011: when businesses trade at cost instead of retail, both win. A pizza restaurant and ice cream shop exchanging product at cost—both pay pennies on the dollar, both get full value. No extraction from workers or customers.

That became Cost + 20%.

**The 14 Initiatives:**

The platform funds charitable work through commerce:

- **Let's Make Dinner** — Neighbors feeding neighbors
- **LifeLine Medications** — Prescription access
- **Defense Klaus** — Personal safety
- **Rally Group** — Crisis response
- **VSL** — $50 microloans
- **Let's Make Bread** — Cooperative manufacturing
- And 8 more

Three commercial portals permanently fund all fourteen. The structure means charitable initiatives can't be defunded—they're tied to transaction flow.
