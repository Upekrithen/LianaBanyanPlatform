**To:** MIT Media Lab
**Subject:** Platform Cooperative with Locked Economics — Research Collaboration Inquiry

---

**Summary:**

I've spent 15 years building a platform cooperative where the economics are architecturally locked—Cost + 20% margin, fixed in the operating agreement, structurally unchangeable. I'm seeking academic partners to rigorously examine whether the model does what I think it does.

**The Platform:**

Liana Banyan is a cooperative commerce platform:

- **83.3%** creator retention on every transaction
- **Cost + 20%** margin locked in operating agreement
- Three commercial portals fund fourteen charitable initiatives
- Fractional ownership via blockchain-tracked medallions
- **$5** annual membership

**Why MIT Media Lab:**

Your work spans digital economics, blockchain applications, and platform design. This platform touches all three:

1. **Economic architecture** — The margin lock prevents extraction by design, not policy
2. **Blockchain for verification** — Medallions track ownership without speculation mechanics
3. **Platform cooperativism** — An attempt to prove the model works at scale

**Research Questions:**

1. Does the operating agreement lock actually prevent platform drift toward extraction?
2. What game-theoretic vulnerabilities exist in the medallion ownership structure?
3. How does the 14-initiative structure affect resilience compared to single-purpose platforms?
4. Is Cost + 20% the right margin, or is there an optimal cooperative platform margin?

**What I Can Offer:**

- Full access to platform documentation (Cephas.LianaBanyan.org)
- The 2011 LCEC documents showing economic evolution
- Technical architecture deep-dive
- Founder interviews and context
- Real transaction data as the platform scales

**What I'm Asking:**

I'm a practitioner, not a scholar. I need rigorous academic examination of whether this architecture actually works. If there are flaws, I need to know. If it holds, the model should be documented for others to replicate.

Would anyone at the Media Lab be interested in examining this as a research project?
