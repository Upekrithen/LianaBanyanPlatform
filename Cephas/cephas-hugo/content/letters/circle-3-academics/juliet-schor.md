## WHAT I'VE BUILT

Liana Banyan is a cooperative platform built on one principle: **Cost + 20%**.

That's it. The platform margin is fixed in the operating agreement. Creators keep 83.3% of every transaction. The remaining 16.7% funds operations and fourteen charitable initiatives — food security, healthcare access, education, financial inclusion, crisis response.

No venture capital. No pressure to extract more. No algorithmic games to squeeze workers. The margin is locked. Permanently.

Your research identified four pathways out of gig work precarity:
1. Regulation
2. Collective action
3. Exit to traditional employment
4. Platform cooperativism

We're building option four. But not as a small-scale experiment — as a full ecosystem with manufacturing nodes, meal delivery, music licensing, microfinance, and eleven other interlocking initiatives.

---

## WHERE YOUR RESEARCH APPLIES

**On algorithmic management:** Our AI system (the Star Chamber) is designed for verification and anti-hallucination, not surveillance. Workers aren't rated by opaque algorithms. They're evaluated by peers with transparent criteria.

**On time sovereignty:** Our Position Funding system offers three options:
- 50% cash + 50% revenue share
- 100% deferred cash + 20% bonus
- 100% revenue share at 2x value

Workers choose based on their actual situation, not what the platform prefers.

**On extraction:** Cost + 20% is fixed. Not aspirational. Not "up to." Fixed in the legal structure. We can't enshittify because we're contractually prohibited from doing so.

**On ownership:** Every member earns ownership stakes through participation. Not equity grants from a benevolent founder — structural ownership as the default.
