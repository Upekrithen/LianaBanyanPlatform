## What We're Building

A **Community Innovation System** that allows platform members to:
1. Submit innovation suggestions (ideas for platform improvements)
2. Have those suggestions evaluated by other community members
3. Record qualified innovations in our IP Ledger (attribution database)
4. Optionally: Access a guided workflow for filing provisional patents

The goal is to give every member the same IP treatment as the Founder—same royalty rates, same attribution, same patent access.

## Specific Concerns Requiring Review

### 1. Unauthorized Practice of Law (UPL)

**Question:** Does our patent assistance system constitute practicing patent law?

**Our Current Structure:**

| Tier | Service | Our Assessment |
|------|---------|----------------|
| **Tier 1** | Educational content, blank USPTO forms, tutorials | No UPL risk |
| **Tier 2** | Guided questionnaire, template generator, prior art search | Low risk with disclaimers |
| **Tier 3** | Professional review, filing service | Requires licensed agent/attorney |

**What We Need:**
- Confirmation that Tier 1-2 are permissible with disclaimers
- Guidance on required disclaimer language
- Structure for Tier 3 (independent contractors? referral network?)

### 2. SEC Securities Classification

**Question:** Do our royalty payments to member innovators constitute securities?

**Howey Test Analysis:**

| Prong | Our System | Risk |
|-------|------------|------|
| Investment of money | USPTO fee ($320) | Yes |
| Common enterprise | Multiple contributors | Yes |
| Expectation of profit | Royalties from usage | Yes |
| Efforts of others | Platform implements | Yes |

**Our Proposed Mitigation:**

Frame royalties as "compensation for completed creative work" rather than "investment returns":
- Payment for PAST work (creating the innovation)
- Usage-based (only paid if innovation is actually used)
- Non-transferable (cannot sell royalty rights)
- Capped annually (limits accumulation)
- Granular tracking (per-innovation, not pooled)

**What We Need:**
- Opinion on whether this framing adequately avoids securities classification
- Recommended language for member agreements
- Any structural changes required
