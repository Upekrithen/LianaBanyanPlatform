**The Gentrification Problem**

You've talked about "self-gentrification" — the idea that neighborhoods should be able to develop themselves without being displaced by the development. The alternative to abandonment shouldn't be displacement.

Most platforms accelerate gentrification. When Airbnb enters a neighborhood, rents rise. When Amazon opens a warehouse, housing costs spike. The platforms extract value while communities bear the costs.

Liana Banyan is structured to keep value local.

**The Architecture**

The platform operates on **Cost + 20%**.

Creators keep **83.3%** of every transaction. The margin is locked in the operating agreement — not a policy but a structure. When a neighbor cooks for another neighbor through Let's Make Dinner, 83.3% stays in the neighborhood. When a local manufacturer uses Let's Make Bread, the ownership stays local.

**The Self-Gentrification Model**

You've argued that low-status communities deserve the same quality of life as everyone else — without being displaced by the improvement.

What if the platform that brought new services to a neighborhood was owned by the neighborhood? What if 83.3% of every transaction stayed in the community? What if the workers providing services were building ownership stakes, not just earning wages?

That's not community development from outside. That's communities developing themselves.
