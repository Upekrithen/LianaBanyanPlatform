**The Most Important Thing About Platforms**

You've written that the most important thing in investing isn't any single factor — it's understanding second-order effects and thinking about what everyone else isn't thinking about.

Here's what most people aren't thinking about with platforms:

**The take rate is a choice, not a law.**

Every platform that starts "creator-friendly" eventually becomes extractive. Not because the founders were evil, but because the incentives demanded it. VC money requires returns. Returns require margin expansion. Margin expansion requires take rate increases.

The cycle is predictable because the structure makes it inevitable.

**A Different Structure**

Liana Banyan operates on **Cost + 20%** — locked in the operating agreement.

Creators keep **83.3%** of every transaction. This cannot change through:
- Acquisition (operating agreement survives change of control)
- Leadership change (structure persists regardless of who runs it)
- Competitive pressure (we can't lower the margin below cost either)
- Time (there's no sunset clause)

I didn't take VC money. I mortgaged my house instead. The margin is designed for perpetuity, not exit.

**The Long-Term Bet**

You've argued that short-term thinking creates opportunities for long-term thinkers. That most investors are playing a game measured in quarters, while the best opportunities are measured in decades.

Liana Banyan is the logical extension: a platform measured in perpetuity.
