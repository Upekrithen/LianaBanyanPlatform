# LETTER: SETH GODIN
## Author & Marketing Legend
## Tribes Builder

**Date:** January 23, 2026

---

Dear Seth,

You've spent decades teaching people how to build tribes — communities of the faithful who believe in something and want to connect with others who believe the same thing.

I've built the infrastructure for a tribe that could number millions.

I need help leading it.

---

## WHO I AM

I'm a 53-year-old ARNG veteran — Infantry and Aviation, helicopter pilot with FAA Commercial Rotary Wing IFR rating. Father of eight. 21 years in IT development. Not a marketer. Not a writer. A builder who needs to become both.

I've been thinking about cooperative economics for four decades. Building this specific platform for nine. I have 18 handwritten journals, 1,200+ technical diagrams, and 150+ innovations with 928 patent claims filed.

I've read *Tribes*, *Purple Cow*, *This Is Marketing*, and probably a dozen more of your books. I understand the concepts. I struggle with the execution.

I can build systems. I can write code. I can solve engineering problems. What I can't do — yet — is tell a story that makes people care.

---

## WHAT I'VE BUILT

Liana Banyan is a cooperative platform built on one principle: **Cost + 20%**.

Creators keep 83.3% of every transaction. The margin is locked in the operating agreement — I cannot raise it even if I wanted to.

Three commercial divisions fund fourteen charitable initiatives: food security, healthcare access, education, financial inclusion, crisis response.

The platform launches in January 2026. I've built the infrastructure. Now I need to build the tribe.

---

## THE TRIBE I'M TRYING TO BUILD

**The believers:**
- People who think workers should keep what they earn
- People who believe communities can provide for themselves
- People who are tired of platforms that extract 30% for doing nothing
- People who want to own their work, not rent their opportunity

**The heretics:**
- Against the orthodoxy of venture-backed extraction
- Against the assumption that platforms must enshittify
- Against the idea that cooperative economics can't scale

**The change they want:**
- Economic dignity without gig economy precarity
- Community support without charity dependence
- Worker ownership without abandoning commerce

You wrote that tribes are about shared beliefs, not demographics. This tribe shares a belief: that the economy could work differently if someone built different infrastructure.

I built the infrastructure. Now I need to find the tribe.

---

## WHY I NEED YOUR HELP

You've said that marketing is about telling a true story that resonates with the people who want to hear it.

My story is true: I've spent nine years and my mortgage building cooperative infrastructure because I believe communities can provide for themselves.

But I don't know how to tell it in a way that resonates.

I can explain the economics. I can show the legal structure. I can demonstrate the technology. What I can't do is make people feel why it matters.

That's what you teach. That's what I need to learn.

---

## THE PERMISSION MARKETING ANGLE

You invented permission marketing — the idea that marketing works best when people invite you in rather than when you interrupt them.

Liana Banyan's entire structure is built on permission:
- Members choose to join ($5)
- Workers choose their compensation model
- Councils elect their own representatives
- Nothing is mandatory except the margin that protects everyone

But I don't know how to get the first permission — the invitation to tell the story.

How do you earn attention from people who don't know you exist? How do you tell a story that competes with platforms that spend millions on marketing?

I have $0 marketing budget. I have a true story. I have infrastructure that actually works.

How do I find the people who want to hear about it?

---

## WHAT I'M ASKING

I'd like your advice on how to build this tribe.

Specifically:
- How do you tell a story about economic infrastructure in a way that makes people care?
- How do you build a movement with no marketing budget?
- How do you compete with platforms that have billions when you have authenticity?

If you're interested, I'd welcome:
- A conversation about marketing cooperative platforms
- Advice on storytelling for economic movements
- An introduction to others who've built tribes around unconventional ideas

If this isn't your thing — if cooperative economics doesn't resonate — I understand. But if the idea of building tribe infrastructure for economic dignity interests you at all, I'd love to learn from you.

---

## THE STORY I'M TRYING TO TELL

*There's a platform where workers keep 83.3% of what they earn.*

*Where the margin is locked in legal structure, so it can never enshittify.*

*Where participation creates ownership — not as a gift, but as a right.*

*Where fourteen initiatives interlock: meals, music, microloans, manufacturing, medicine, and more.*

*Where communities provide for themselves instead of waiting to be rescued.*

*It's called Liana Banyan. And it needs you.*

Is that the right story? I don't know. I know how to build the platform. I don't know how to build the narrative.

I've built something worth believing in. I think. I hope.

Now I need to find the believers.

Help each other help ourselves.
