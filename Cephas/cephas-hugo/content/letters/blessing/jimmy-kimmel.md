Dear Mr. Kimmel,

I'm not writing because you're famous.

I'm writing because you understand something most people don't. And shouldn't have to.

---

## THE NIGHT THAT CHANGES EVERYTHING

In 1996, my first son was three weeks old when an East Indian immigrant doctor — a pediatrician at a free clinic — happened to hear something on the stethoscope.

A whisper.

She checked his blood pressure above and below his heart. Aortic coarctation. A 98% chance of death without immediate emergency heart surgery. UTMB Galveston. Two hours away. Three weeks old.

He's 29 now. He's fine. He served five years in the Marines. He's alive because:

- An immigrant doctor at a free clinic caught it
- A procedure that had been invented only five years earlier existed
- Researchers, nurses, donors, advocates — thousands of people I'll never meet
- The Ronald McDonald House, where families stay while their loved ones recover

I am forever grateful. And I know you understand.

---

## WHY I'M WRITING TO YOU

I watched your monologue after Billy's surgery. The one where you couldn't get through it without crying. Where you talked about the Children's Hospital and the nurses and what it's like to hand your child over and not know if you'll see them again.

I understood every word. Most people don't. They can't. They shouldn't have to.

But you and I do.

And because we do, we have to do something with it.

---

## WHAT I'VE BEEN BUILDING

For nine years, I've been building a platform called Liana Banyan. It's a cooperative commerce system where workers keep 80% of what they earn — the opposite of the gig economy that squeezes everyone.

But underneath it are 14 charitable initiatives. One of them — we call it Power to the People, with an electric plug as its symbol — funds proposals to Congress and tracks every vote, giving regular people lobbyist-level access. One supports families in crisis. One makes medications affordable. One puts meals on tables.

All of them exist because I remember that night in 1996. And I want to pay it forward — not just once, but systematically. Forever.

I'm not asking you to endorse it or promote it or do anything. I just wanted you to know it exists. And that another father who stood in that hallway, waiting for news, is trying to build something so that more fathers get good news.

---

## ONE TRUE ACT OF KINDNESS

You've raised millions for Children's Hospital LA. You've used your platform to fight for healthcare access. You've been public about something most people hide.

That matters. More than you know.

I listen to your monologue sometimes when I need to remember why I'm doing this. When the patents pile up and the code breaks and the launches get delayed — I remember Billy. I remember Ethan. I remember that somewhere, right now, there's a parent in a waiting room who needs the system to work.

That's why I keep building.

---

## WHAT I'M ASKING

Nothing expected. But if you know someone who could help — in healthcare access, food security, education, or community development — tell them to contact me. You never have to speak with me at all. Just a word to someone in your network: *"Look at what this guy is building."* They can reach me directly at the number below.

If you ever want to see what we're building, it's at **Cephas.LianaBanyan.org**. Pick it apart. Tell me what's wrong with it. Or just know it exists.

I've also written to Dolly Parton — because she wrote the anthem for workers and built the Imagination Library from nothing — and to Pitbull — because his SLAM schools prove he understands opportunity for kids who don't get handed anything. You three each understand something about people who work hard and deserve better systems. If you see anything worthwhile here, feel free to connect the dots I can't.

And if you ever want to talk — father to father, not business to business — I'm here. Some things you can only understand if you've lived them. We both have.

**This invitation extends to anyone reading this.** If you know someone who could help, tell them about us.
