# LETTER: SIMON SINEK
## Author, Start With Why
## Leadership & Purpose Expert

**Date:** January 23, 2026

---

Dear Simon,

You've spent your career asking one question: Why?

Why do some organizations inspire loyalty while others merely demand compliance? Why do some movements grow while others stall? Why do people follow some leaders to the ends of the earth while ignoring others with better credentials?

I've spent nine years building something, and I think I finally have a clear answer to Why.

---

## THE WHY

**Help each other help ourselves.**

That's it. Six words. Every initiative, every feature, every decision flows from that purpose.

Not "help the poor" — because that implies a permanent underclass needing rescue. Not "disrupt the industry" — because that's about destruction, not creation. Not "maximize shareholder value" — because that puts the wrong people at the center.

Help each other help ourselves.

Communities providing for themselves. Workers owning what they build. Neighbors feeding neighbors. The infrastructure for mutual aid, sustained by commerce, governed by the people using it.

---

I'm a 53-year-old ARNG veteran — Infantry and Aviation, helicopter pilot with FAA Commercial Rotary Wing IFR rating. Father of eight. 21 years in IT development. Chess player (2118 rating, top 0.4% globally).

I've been thinking about cooperative economics for four decades. Building this specific platform for nine. I have 18 handwritten journals, 1,200+ technical diagrams, and 150+ innovations with 928 patent claims filed.

I watched your TED talk when it came out. I've read *Start With Why* multiple times. And I've spent years trying to articulate the Why that drove me to mortgage my house for this project.

Help each other help ourselves. That's the Why.

## WHAT I'VE BUILT

Liana Banyan is a cooperative platform built on one principle: **Cost + 20%**.

Creators keep 83.3% of every transaction. The platform margin is fixed in the operating agreement — not by choice, but by legal structure.

Three commercial divisions fund fourteen charitable initiatives: food security, healthcare access, education, financial inclusion, crisis response, and more. The commerce funds the charity. The charity serves the community. The community uses the commerce. It's a flywheel.

## WHY THIS MATTERS TO YOU

You've written about organizations that lose their Why — that start with purpose and drift toward pure profit optimization. The pattern is so common it seems inevitable.

I've built legal structures specifically to prevent that drift.

The operating agreement locks the margin. Workers elect representatives to the Board. The cooperative structure distributes power rather than concentrating it. I can't enshittify even if I wanted to — the legal architecture won't allow it.

Is it possible to build an organization that structurally cannot lose its Why? That's what I've tried to create.

---

## THE LEADERSHIP QUESTION

You've said that people don't buy what you do, they buy why you do it.

I'm asking people to join a platform because they believe communities can provide for themselves. Because they believe workers should own what they build. Because they believe Cost + 20% is enough margin for everyone to thrive.

That's a big ask. It requires trust — in the mission, in the structure, in each other.

You've studied how leaders build that kind of trust. I've built a structure. Now I need to learn how to lead the movement.
