## WHAT I'VE BUILT

Liana Banyan is a cooperative platform built on one principle: **Cost + 20%**.

That's it. The platform margin is fixed in the operating agreement. Creators keep 83.3% of every transaction. The remaining 16.7% funds operations and fourteen charitable initiatives — food security, healthcare access, education, financial inclusion, crisis response. Not through donations. Through commerce. The structure generates the funding automatically, forever.

I know what you're thinking: *Everyone says they're different.*

So let me tell you what makes this actually different:

---

## WHY THIS ISN'T THE USUAL BULLSHIT

**On extraction:**
- DoorDash takes 30%+. We take 16.7%.
- But more importantly: their margin can increase. Ours can't. It's in the operating agreement.
- We can't enshittify because we're contractually prohibited from doing so.

**On labor:**
- Workers choose their compensation model (cash, revenue share, hybrid)
- Workers elect their own representative to the corporate Board
- Every initiative includes a Harper — a care coordinator paid by the platform, not the business

**On ownership:**
- Participation creates ownership stakes (Platform Joules)
- Not equity grants from a generous founder — structural ownership by design
- The more you contribute, the more you own

**On AI:**
- Our AI system (Star Chamber) is designed for verification, not surveillance
- It prevents hallucination and ensures quality
- Workers aren't rated by opaque algorithms — they're evaluated by peers

**On the exit:**
- There is no exit strategy
- No acquisition plans
- No IPO
- The platform is designed to outlive its founder, not enrich him

## YOUR QUESTIONS, ANTICIPATED

*"Who funds this?"*
Me. My mortgage. Some small donations. No venture capital. No growth-at-any-cost investors.

*"What's your business model?"*
Cost + 20% on transactions. Membership fees ($5/year). That's it.

*"What happens when you need to scale?"*
We've built manufacturing nodes — distributed production facilities where operators keep 60% of their time for scheduled work and 40% for their own projects. Scaling doesn't require extraction; it requires more nodes.

*"What about regulation?"*
Members are cooperative members, not misclassified contractors. Platform Credits are reward points, not securities. The structure is designed to work within regulation, not around it.

*"Why should I believe you?"*
You shouldn't. Yet. That's why I'm inviting you to examine it.
