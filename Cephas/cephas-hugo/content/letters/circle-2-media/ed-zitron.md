# LETTER: ED ZITRON
## Author, Better Offline
## Technology & Business Critic

**Date:** January 23, 2026

---

Dear Ed,

You've made a career of calling bullshit on tech's worst impulses. The growth-at-any-cost thinking. The user-hostile pivots. The executive class that mistakes extraction for innovation.

I've built the thing that structurally can't do any of that.

I read *Better Offline* because you say what other tech writers won't: that most of what passes for innovation is just new ways to squeeze money out of people who can't fight back. That the venture capital playbook is fundamentally user-hostile. That enshittification isn't a bug — it's the business model.

I'm writing because I built a platform where enshittification is literally illegal. And I want you to try to prove me wrong.

---

## WHO I AM

I'm a 53-year-old ARNG veteran — Infantry and Aviation, helicopter pilot with FAA Commercial Rotary Wing IFR rating. Father of eight. 21 years in IT development. Not a Stanford dropout with a manifesto. Not an ex-FAANG engineer "disrupting" something.

I've been thinking about cooperative economics for four decades. Building this for nine. I have 18 handwritten journals, 1,200+ technical diagrams, and 150+ innovations with 928 patent claims filed.

I've mortgaged my house. No venture capital. No board of investors who'll demand we "unlock value" by screwing our users.

I'm a guy who got tired of watching good ideas get captured and corrupted, so I built something that can't be captured.

---

## WHAT I'VE BUILT

Liana Banyan is a cooperative platform built on one principle: **Cost + 20%**.

The platform margin is fixed in the operating agreement. Not a target. Not "up to." Fixed. Creators keep 83.3% of every transaction.

We cannot raise it. Ever. To change the margin would require violating our own charter.

I built this because I watched every platform I ever liked turn to shit. Instagram. Twitter. Uber. Airbnb. The pattern is always the same: hook users, achieve dominance, extract.

So I made extraction structurally impossible.

---

## HOW WE PREVENT ENSHITTIFICATION

Your thesis: platforms start user-friendly, become extraction engines once they achieve market dominance. The incentives guarantee it.

My response: change the incentives by locking them in legal structure.

| Enshittification Pattern | Liana Banyan Prevention |
|-------------------------|------------------------|
| Raise take rates | Margin locked in operating agreement |
| Degrade service quality | Workers elect Board representatives |
| Algorithmic manipulation | AI is for verification, not surveillance |
| Sell user data | No data sales clause in charter |
| Lock-in and exploit | Portable reputation, open APIs |
| Squeeze workers | Workers choose compensation model |
| IPO and extract | No IPO planned — cooperative structure |

I know everyone claims they're different. The difference is I built it into the legal structure, not the mission statement.

---

## THE SPECIFICS YOU'LL WANT

**Revenue model:** Cost + 20% on transactions. $5 annual membership. That's it.

**Ownership:** Participation creates ownership stakes (Platform Joules). Not stock options that vest if you're lucky. Structural ownership from day one.

**Governance:** Each initiative has a council. Workers elect their own representative to the Board. The founder doesn't control the platform — the members do.

**AI:** Our AI system (Star Chamber) verifies quality and prevents hallucination. It doesn't rate workers with opaque algorithms. It doesn't optimize for engagement. It helps humans do better work.

**Exit strategy:** There isn't one. No acquisition. No IPO. The platform is designed to outlive me and continue serving its members.

---

Because you're mean.

I mean that as a compliment. You don't accept "we're making the world a better place" at face value. You ask what the business model actually does, not what the founder says it does.

I need that. I've been building this for nine years, mostly alone. I've probably developed blind spots. I need someone who will look at the structure and say "here's where you're going to fuck your users" — if that's true.

And if it's not true — if the structure actually prevents the usual bullshit — I need someone who can explain that to people who've learned to distrust tech promises.
