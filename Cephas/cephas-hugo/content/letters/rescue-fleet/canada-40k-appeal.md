# Canada 40K: A Rescue Fleet for Stranded Entrepreneurs

## 40,000 Dreams. One Platform. A Different Path Forward.

*Op-Ed — January 2026*

————————————————————————————————————————

Canada just canceled 40,000 startup visas.

Forty thousand entrepreneurs — people who uprooted their lives, invested their savings, bet on a dream — suddenly stranded.

Some came from India. Some from Pakistan. Some from Nigeria. They came because Canada promised a path: build a company, create jobs, become Canadian.

Then Canada changed its mind.

————————————————————————————————————————

## What Happens Now?

Most of these founders are stuck in limbo. Their visas are expiring. Their startups are in jeopardy. The investors they relied on are pulling back.

The conventional wisdom says: go home, or find another country willing to take a chance on you.

But what if there were a third option?

————————————————————————————————————————

## Why This Matters

I know what it's like to be stranded. Christmas Eve 1992, I opened a letter that told me I'd been expelled from college. Lost my dorm room, lost my R.A. job, lost everything at once — while home in Montana for the holidays. I slept in my Jeep for three days, then rented a one-bedroom apartment with a missing wall for $175 a month — about half my income. The landlord covered the gap with taped tarp.

Forty thousand entrepreneurs just opened their version of that letter.

The Canada 40K crisis exposes something broken in how we think about entrepreneurship.

The current model assumes you need venture capital. You need a visa that depends on investors who may or may not keep their commitments. You need permission from systems designed for a different era.

What if entrepreneurs could build real businesses without being dependent on these gatekeepers?

What if ownership was distributed from the start — so success didn't require concentrating power in a few hands?

What if the platform that supported your work was something you actually owned a piece of?

## The Three-Gear Currency

One of our most important innovations: a currency system that works for entrepreneurs from any economy.

**The Problem:** A $10 platform fee represents hours of labor in some economies, minutes in others. Traditional platforms either exclude people from weaker economies or create complex pricing tiers that don't really work.

**Our Solution:** Three interconnected currencies (Credits, Marks, Joules) that automatically adjust for economic differences. Everyone has equal purchasing power inside the platform, regardless of where they're located.

A Nigerian entrepreneur and a Swiss banker both receive the same internal value for their equivalent local payment. The system handles the adjustment automatically.
