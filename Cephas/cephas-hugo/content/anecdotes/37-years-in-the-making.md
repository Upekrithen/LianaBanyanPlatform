1989. Jonathan Jones is sixteen years old in a rural American household, watching his family navigate the gap between what work pays and what life costs. He begins sketching something — not in code, because there is no code to sketch in yet, but in numbers and diagrams — a different kind of economic architecture. One where the margin doesn't go to the intermediary. One where the people doing the work keep what they earn.

The year is different. The technology stack is different. Everything about the delivery mechanism has changed multiple times. The diagrams, however, are remarkably consistent with the platform you can visit today at lianabanyan.com.

Thirty-seven years of unbroken focus — not obsession in the pathological sense, but the kind of sustained attention that a carpenter brings to a house he's been building his whole life. Every tool upgraded. Every technique refined. The original blueprint still visible in the structure.

This is not a startup story. It is a craftsman's story. And 37 years in, the house is almost ready to open.
