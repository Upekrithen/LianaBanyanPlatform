# About Pnyx

## Why "Pnyx"?

The Pnyx (PNIKS) was a hill in ancient Athens — the world's first purpose-built democratic assembly space. Citizens gathered there not to watch leaders perform, but to speak, debate, and vote. The hill was the platform; the people were the power.

We chose this name deliberately. The Liana Banyan Platform is not a marketplace with a suggestion box. It is a cooperative where Members hold real governance authority. The Pnyx is where that authority is exercised.

## Cooperative-Class Governance

Pnyx operates on cooperative-class principles:

- **One Member, One Voice** — no weighted votes based on spending or tenure
- **Canonical receipts** — every deliberation and decision is logged to the substrate
- **Transparent tallies** — all vote counts are public and auditable
- **Standing for all** — any current Member may participate

## What Pnyx Is Not

Pnyx is not a forum. It is not a Discord server. It is not a customer feedback portal.

Pnyx is the formal deliberative body of the cooperative. Decisions made here have weight. They are recorded. They inform platform development, policy, and resource allocation.

## Relationship to the Platform

The Pnyx interfaces with:

- **Rally Group** — community organizing and mobilization
- **Brass Tacks** — foundational labor and economic infrastructure
- **Power to the People** — civic participation and democratic tools
