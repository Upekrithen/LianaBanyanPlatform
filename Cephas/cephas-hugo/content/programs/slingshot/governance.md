# Slingshot Governance

## The Alumni Council

Slingshot is governed by its Alumni Council — Members who have completed at least one Slingshot cohort. The Alumni Council:

- Reviews and accepts applications for each cohort
- Sets cohort themes and priorities (aligned with platform Pnyx decisions)
- Mediates disputes within cohorts
- Maintains the CANON contribution archive

Alumni Council membership is automatic upon cohort completion. Council decisions are made by simple majority and logged to substrate.

## Cohort Governance

Within each cohort, projects govern themselves. The cohort as a whole:

- Meets weekly (log to substrate required)
- Reviews each other's progress (peer accountability, not judgment)
- Votes on cohort-wide resource decisions (Marks allocation, shared tools)

## Slingshot and the Platform Pnyx

Slingshot reports to the platform Pnyx on:
- Each cohort's launch (cohort charter logged as canonical record)
- Each project's 90-day receipt (logged to substrate)
- CANON contributions made by graduating projects

The Pnyx may issue priorities to Slingshot (e.g., "this cohort should focus on health-access projects") but may not reject individual projects that pass the cooperative-class filter.

## Resource Governance

Marks allocated to Slingshot projects are governed by:
- **VSL pool rules** — cooperative credit, not grants; recovery expected within 24 months
- **Alumni Council oversight** — no single project receives more than 20% of cohort Marks allocation
- **CANON tie** — projects that contribute more to CANON may earn additional Marks

## Intellectual Property

All innovations developed in Slingshot:
- Are owned by the project's cooperative (not Slingshot, not Liana Banyan Corporation)
- Carry a CANON contribution commitment (innovations documented and shared)
- May be used freely by other platform cooperatives under cooperative-class license

No project forfeits IP to participate in Slingshot. The CANON commitment is a documentation obligation, not an ownership transfer.
