### What does Library of Congress registration mean?

It means your idea has a timestamp that nobody can dispute. Registered the moment you saved it. No lawyers needed.

When Mnemosyne writes an Eblet — your idea, your Pudding, your vote — it cryptographically signs it and timestamps it. That timestamp is your proof of existence. The Library of Congress Grand Project extends this to the entire cooperative corpus: every contribution we make together is permanently, publicly timestamped in a way that no one can retroactively alter. The cooperative's work becomes undeniable. So does yours.

### What is the Library of Congress Grand Project?

We're using thousands of regular people's computers — running Mnemosyne quietly in the background — to help make the Library of Congress easier for everyone to read, search, and learn from. **It's the first big thing we're building together as a cooperative.** Our goal is 10,000 people running the network. Help us get there.

### Why the Library of Congress?

It's one of the largest collections of human knowledge ever assembled — about 178 million items, with around 20 million already digitized as text. Most of it is hard to search · hard to navigate · hard to use unless you're a researcher who already knows what you're looking for. **We're going to fix that for everybody.**

When we're done · any kid in any town can ask Mnemosyne a question and get answers grounded in primary sources from the Library of Congress · for free · in plain English · on their own laptop.

### What does my Mnemosyne actually do?

When your laptop is sitting idle and you've opted in, Mnemosyne quietly reads a small piece of the Library of Congress · turns it into what we call an **Eblet** (a small, structured, fact-checked summary that's cryptographically signed so nobody can tamper with it later) · and adds it to the shared network. **One Eblet at a time. Thousands of laptops at once. It adds up fast.**

You don't have to do anything. You don't have to read it. You don't have to verify it. Mnemosyne handles it. You'll see a counter tick up in the corner showing your contribution.

### Will it cost me money?

**No.** Mnemosyne is free to download · free to run · free forever.

If you're contributing computing time and electricity, you can choose to use **AI Burst mode** (which costs a tiny amount per query using Anthropic's cloud) · OR you can use **Normal mode** with Ollama running locally on your machine (zero marginal cost · just your electricity) · OR you can use **Fallback mode** which uses our cached substrate (also zero marginal cost · works offline). Most members run Normal or Fallback. The Grand Project work happens fine in any of the three modes.

### Will I get paid?

**Yes — in Banyan Marks.** Every Eblet your Mnemosyne contributes earns you Marks. Marks are our cooperative's internal currency. You can spend them on:

- Premium features inside Mnemosyne (priority queries · expanded Substrate access · etc.)
- Code Breaker guild access (the cooperative-class research-and-development tier)
- Federation Caithedral upgrades (when those launch)
- Member-only Bounties and exchanges

Marks are **NOT money.** They're not securities. You can't trade them for dollars on an exchange. They're a contribution credit inside the cooperative. They're explicitly worth-something *to members of our cooperative for cooperative things*.

If you'd rather convert your contribution to actual money: we periodically license the aggregated (anonymous · k-anonymity gated · privacy-budgeted) research data to academic institutions and policy bodies. That revenue goes into a patronage pool that pays out quarterly to opted-in contributors — proportional to your Marks balance. **You don't have to participate in research to earn Marks · but if you do opt in, you get the patronage dividend too.**

### Do I have to pay $5/year?

**No.** $5/year is to become a full cooperative member (with voting rights · profit-share access · Stewards Guild eligibility · etc.). You can run Mnemosyne · contribute to the Grand Project · earn Marks · use the substrate · for free · forever · without ever paying anything.

Joining the cooperative is optional · for people who want to be part-owners and have a vote in how things go. It's NOT a paywall on the software. It's a membership in the organization that owns the software.

### What data goes out?

**By default · nothing.** Mnemosyne runs locally on your machine. The Substrate stays on your disk. The Library-of-Congress Eblets your machine produces are written locally and uploaded to the cooperative network · BUT they contain ONLY the Library of Congress source material (which is already public domain) · NOT anything about you.

If you opt in to research aggregation (which earns you the patronage dividend above), Mnemosyne also emits anonymized observation tabs about how you use the substrate — never PII · never your queries · never your content · just **action-class metadata** (e.g., "user clicked a Pudding link" not "user searched for X") · gated by k-anonymity (no result returned unless at least 50 members contributed to the slice) · differential-privacy noised.

You can opt out any time. We honor revocation by cryptographically retracting your prior contributions from future research pools — proof not promise.

### Is this safe?

The Library-of-Congress source material is **public domain** — we're not copying anything that isn't already free to copy. The Eblets we produce are licensed under **AGPL v3** (anyone can use them · anyone can audit them) and the cooperative-class **SSPL** (so big platforms can't repackage them extractively without sharing back).

Our patent strategy is **defensive only** (Cooperative Defensive Patent Pledge #2260) — we hold patents to prevent others from blocking the cooperative · not to sue creators. Per Saladin's Pattern™: we use IP for protection of the commons, not conquest of the commons.
