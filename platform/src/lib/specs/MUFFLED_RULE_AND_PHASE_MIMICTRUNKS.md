## 1. THE MUFFLED RULE

### Core Principle
**You must listen if you want people to listen to you.**

Your microphone only works (broadcasts) for as long as you have listened to other people speak. This applies to both voice AND text. You earn "Coverage Minutes" by consuming content; you spend them by producing content.

### How It Works

#### Voice (Round Tables)
- Only **one microphone works at a time** per round table
- There is **one table per topic** — structured discourse by design
- No shouting, no interrupting — physically impossible by architecture
- When your Coverage Minutes are spent, your mic mutes

#### Text (Written Discourse)
- You get **broadcast coverage** (published visibility) in equal proportion to what you've read
- Reading is tracked via the **Golden Key percentage-read mechanic** (same system as Cephas Articles)
- Writing consumes Coverage Minutes; reading earns them

### Coverage Minutes Accumulation

#### Earning
- **Listening** to others speak at round tables earns Coverage Minutes 1:1
- **Reading** earns Coverage Minutes proportionally:
  - Cephas Articles (LB's own publications)
  - Other members' published content
  - External publications that members subscribe to (newsletters, newspapers — see Section 5)
  - Fast readers earn MORE pages per minute (the metric is reading completion, not clock time — if you read fast, you cover more content and earn proportionally more Coverage)

#### Accumulation Levels
- Coverage Minutes accumulate in **3-minute increments**
- There are levels of accumulation based on sustained participation
- **Maximum single-session broadcast: 3 hours** (if you have the Minutes to spare)
- This maximum is **imposed regardless of donations received** — you cannot buy your way past the cap
- Purpose: Prevents "DingDongDash" behavior — fly-by-night speakers who blast and leave
- You **earn respect** through sustained listening before you can hold the floor at length

#### Spending
- Speaking at a round table: 1 Coverage Minute per minute of speech
- Publishing text: Coverage Minutes proportional to word count / reading time of your piece
- Once spent, must be re-earned through more listening/reading

### Coverage Minutes Donation

- **Members can donate Coverage Minutes to other members**
- **Membership required** (stamps are required — must be a paying member)
- Donations are **transparently recorded**:
  - WHO donated to WHOM is logged
  - **Interested parties can pay a fee** to find out who donated Coverage Minutes to a speaker
  - The act of **reading the donation record is ALSO recorded** — full transparency chain
  - This creates accountability: amplification is traceable, and tracing is traceable
- Donations do NOT bypass the 3-hour maximum — they add to your pool but the per-session cap remains

### Subscriber Exception (Under Analysis)
- **Possible exception:** People who subscribe to you might grant implicit Coverage Minutes?
- **Analysis needed:** This could create a "celebrity amplification" loop that undermines the earn-to-speak principle
- **Recommendation:** If implemented, subscriber-granted minutes should be capped at a small percentage (e.g., 10% of earned minutes) and clearly distinguished from earned minutes in the transparency records
- **Decision:** Founder to decide after seeing the system in practice

## 2. READING = COVERAGE (Expanded)

### What Counts as Reading

Reading ANY of the following earns Coverage Minutes:

1. **Cephas Articles** — LB's own knowledge base / encyclopedia
2. **Other members' published content** — anything published within LB
3. **External newsletters** that members subscribe to (see Section 5)
4. **External newspapers** integrated via co-op partnerships (see Section 5)
5. **Emails and publications** within the Library system
6. **Real-world sites** that are integrated via the Library system

### Reading Verification (Golden Key Mechanic)

Uses the **same programming system** as the Golden Key percentage-of-Cephas-Articles-read tracker:
- Progress tracked per article/piece
- Percentage completion recorded
- Cannot skim — the system measures engagement (scroll depth, time on content, interaction)
- Golden Keys are awarded at completion milestones

### Real World Puzzles

Members can create their own **Real World Puzzles** using the same Golden Key system:
- **ALLOWED IN:** The Library, real-world publications, emails, and external integrated sites
- **NOT ALLOWED IN:** LB islands (the game world) — UNLESS:
  - LB charges for it (paid feature)
  - The **Shirley Temple Policy** is applied (content standards enforcement)
- Each Golden Key exists on its **own separate plane** — there is NEVER more than one Golden Key per plane/location
- The plane you enter to search for a Golden Key is its own contained space

## 3. PHASE MIMICTRUNKS

### What Is a Phase MimicTrunk?

A Phase MimicTrunk is essentially a **personal or organizational server/instance** of the LB world, accessed through **Special Deck Cards**. It is a self-contained phase (dimension) that operates under all LB rules and policies.

Think of it as: "You can, in essence, have your own server."

### Golden Keys as Phase Entry Points

- Each Golden Key search takes you to a **different plane**
- These planes become the **basis for new MimicTrunks**
- People can enter into their own Phase via their Special Deck Cards
- The Phase operates with the same rules and policies as all of LB

### Types of Phase MimicTrunks

#### Personal Phase MimicTrunks
- Owned by individual members
- Accessed via Special Deck Cards
- Personal creative space within LB's rule framework
- Can host custom content, puzzles, experiences

#### Guild Phase MimicTrunks (see Section 6)
- One per Guild, by default
- Guilds PAY for their Phase MimicTrunk (sustaining revenue model)
- Can host massive multiplayer experiences (MMORPGs, Warhammer 40K-scale games)
- Contains Tribes as sub-organizations

#### Tribe Phase MimicTrunks (see Section 6)
- A Tribe within a Guild can have its OWN Phase MimicTrunk
- Nested governance: Guild → Tribe → Tribe's Phase
- Additional cost, additional autonomy

## 4. SOURCE CODE DISTRIBUTION (Tamper-Proof)

### The Promise
People can **download the entire LB source code.**

### The Protection
The downloaded code **cannot be tampered with** — not because people can't change it (they can modify their local copy), but because:

1. **Modified code won't connect to the LB system**
2. LB **constantly checks ALL setups, rules, and interactions**
3. Everything is **locked in place** by the Phase MimicTrunk's own copy of the...

### Immutable Test-Net-By-Design Ledger

Each Phase MimicTrunk download includes its own copy of the **Immutable Ledger** — specifically:
- The iteration of the ledger at the time of download
- This ledger is a **test-net by design** — it validates the code's integrity
- It creates a **DNA chain** of immeasurable strength and vitality
- It ties together:
  - The source code
  - The rules engine
  - The interaction policies
  - The transaction history
  - The governance constraints
- Any modification to ANY of these breaks the chain → connection refused

### How This Works Technically

```
Download Package:
  ├── Source Code (full LB platform)
  ├── Phase MimicTrunk Configuration
  ├── Immutable Ledger Snapshot (test-net)
  ├── Validation Checksums (DNA chain)
  └── Connection Handshake Protocol

On Connection Attempt:
  1. Local ledger snapshot compared to canonical ledger
  2. Source code checksums validated against expected hashes
  3. Rules engine integrity verified
  4. Interaction policies confirmed unchanged
  5. If ANY check fails → connection refused
  6. If ALL checks pass → Phase connects to LB network
```

## 5. NEWSLETTERS, NEWSPAPERS & SUBSCRIPTION PEDESTALS

### External Publication Integration

LB integrates with external newsletters and newspapers via co-op partnerships:
- Members can subscribe to external publications THROUGH LB
- Reading external content earns Coverage Minutes (same as reading Cephas Articles)
- This creates a bridge between LB's discourse system and the broader publishing world

### Subscription Model (Two Modes)

#### Private Portfolio Subscription
- Member subscribes to publications in their **private portfolio**
- Reading is tracked for Coverage Minutes
- Subscription details are private to the member

#### Public Pedestal Sponsorship
- Member can donate **part of their subscription** to **sponsor a Publicly Available Deck Card display feed on a Pedestal**
- The Pedestal is a public display of curated content from the subscribed publications
- Anyone can view a Public Pedestal's feed

### Pedestal Funding Rules

| Rule | Detail |
|------|--------|
| **Max per person** | No more than **5,000 Credits** from any single person to any single Pedestal |
| **Public threshold** | A Pedestal requires **20,000 Credits** total funding to become Public |
| **Transaction recording** | ALL Pedestal transactions are recorded in the Immutable Ledger (in the Pedestal section) |
| **Transparency** | Funding sources are auditable (same transparency principle as Coverage Minutes donations) |

### Why These Limits?

- **5K cap per person:** Prevents any single individual from controlling a Pedestal's editorial direction through funding dominance
- **20K to go Public:** Ensures a Pedestal has broad community support (minimum 4 funders at max contribution) before it gets public visibility
- **Ledger recording:** Full accountability for who funds what public information feeds

## 6. GUILD & TRIBE PHASE MIMICTRUNKS

### Guild Phase MimicTrunks

Every Guild gets a Phase MimicTrunk **by default** — but they **pay for it**.

#### Why Guilds Pay
- Creates sustainable revenue
- Ensures only serious Guilds maintain Phase MimicTrunks
- The payment model scales with usage/size

#### What Guilds Can Do With Their Phase
- **Host MMORPGs** — massive multiplayer online games at the scale of Warhammer 40K, World of Warcraft, etc.
- **Custom game worlds** within LB's rule framework
- **Persistent universes** for their community
- **Tournament hosting** with LB's transaction and governance systems built in

### Tribes Within Guilds

A Guild's Phase MimicTrunk contains **Tribes** (sub-organizations):

```
Guild Phase MimicTrunk
  ├── Tribe A (chapter / sub-guild)
  │     ├── Keeps (game lobbies)
  │     └── Members
  ├── Tribe B (chapter / sub-guild)
  │     ├── Keeps
  │     └── Members
  └── Tribe C
        ├── Keeps
        └── Members
```

- Each Tribe functions as a **chapter** or **sub-guild** of the larger Guild
- For governance purposes, Tribes are distinct entities within the Guild's structure
- Tribes can have their own Keeps (game lobbies) within the Guild's Phase

### Tribe-Level Phase MimicTrunks

A Tribe can have its **OWN Phase MimicTrunk** — nested within (or alongside) the Guild's Phase:

```
Guild Phase MimicTrunk
  └── Tribe A
        └── Tribe A's Own Phase MimicTrunk
              ├── Custom game world
              ├── Tribe-specific rules (within LB framework)
              └── Sub-tribe organizations (further nesting possible)
```

#### Benefits of Tribe-Level Phases
- **Autonomy:** Tribe controls its own space while still governed by Guild and LB rules
- **Scalability:** A popular Tribe can grow into something as large as the parent Guild
- **Revenue:** LB earns from each Phase MimicTrunk at every level
- **Governance:** Clear hierarchy: LB → Guild → Tribe → Sub-tribe (each level's rules must comply with the level above)

### Additional Research Findings Integrated

**R-002: Immutable Ledger Implementation** — Custom Merkle Hash Chain backed by PostgreSQL.
- Evaluated: public blockchain, private blockchain (Hyperledger), append-only DB (QLDB), Merkle hash chain
- Winner: Merkle hash chain — only approach that satisfies ALL requirements (snapshot bundling, offline validation, high speed, low cost)
- Like Git: Phase downloads receive database snapshot + Merkle tree, can independently verify by re-hashing
- Integrated into `ledgerSections.ts`: MerkleTreeNode, MerkleProof, MerkleSnapshot, FullLedgerSnapshot, buildMerkleTree(), verifyMerkleProof(), createFullLedgerSnapshot(), validateFullSnapshot()

**R-005: Anti-Abuse Mechanics for Coverage Minutes**
- Precedents: Stack Overflow reputation, Slashdot karma, Discord leveling bots
- Mitigations integrated into `coverageMinutes.ts`:
  - **90-day decay** on unused Coverage Minutes (prevents lurker hoarding)
  - **Donation friction tax** (donating 10 costs sender 12 — prevents circular passing)
  - **Diminishing returns** for same-author reading (forces diverse consumption)
  - **Bot detection** (reading speed > 1,000 WPM flagged, minimum 10-second reading time)
  - **Golden Key mechanic** already in spec provides interaction requirement
