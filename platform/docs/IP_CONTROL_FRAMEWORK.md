## Executive Summary

LianaBanyan's Three-Tier IP Control Model balances creator autonomy with organizational scalability, addressing a fundamental tension in collaborative innovation ecosystems: **How do you maximize IP utilization while respecting creator values?**

This framework provides a spectrum of control options that align creator compensation with their desired level of oversight, creating a market-driven solution to IP governance.

## Research Foundation & Rationale

### The Core Problem

Traditional IP management models present binary choices:
1. **Full Control** → Limited commercialization reach (small pie, all yours)
2. **Full Assignment** → Maximum reach but zero creator input (big pie, no control)

Both extremes create suboptimal outcomes:
- **Underutilized IP**: Creators who won't license broadly enough
- **Misaligned IP**: Organizations using IP in ways creators ethically oppose
- **Market Inefficiency**: Valuable IP locked behind incompatible governance structures

### Academic Precedents

#### 1. **Open Source License Spectrum** (GPL → MIT → Public Domain)
   - Different licenses serve different creator philosophies
   - Market has proven multiple models can coexist successfully
   - Creators self-select based on values vs. reach tradeoff

#### 2. **University Tech Transfer Models**
   - Stanford: Faculty retain significant control but share revenue
   - MIT: More permissive, faster commercialization
   - Both work because they match institutional culture

#### 3. **Creative Commons Framework**
   - Tiered permissions (BY, SA, NC, ND combinations)
   - Creators choose restrictions that match their intent
   - **Key Insight**: Offering choice increases total participation vs. forcing one model

#### 4. **Music Publishing Rights Evolution**
   - Mechanical rights vs. performance rights vs. sync rights
   - Artists can retain some while licensing others
   - **Lesson**: Granular control increases market liquidity

### Research-Backed Principles Applied

#### **Principle 1: Self-Selection Reduces Friction**
- Forcing one IP model creates "lost deals" (creators who won't participate)
- Offering tiers captures different risk/control preferences
- **Citation**: "Matching Markets and Market Design" (Alvin Roth, Nobel Prize 2012)

#### **Principle 2: Price Discrimination via Control**
- Creators willing to surrender control should receive premium compensation
- Creators demanding control accept lower absolute returns
- **Result**: Efficient allocation based on revealed preferences

#### **Principle 3: Ethical Alignment Reduces Legal Risk**
- Creators who feel violated by IP use are more likely to litigate
- Built-in ethical guardrails reduce reputational damage
- **Citation**: Behavioral research on contract compliance and satisfaction

## The Three-Tier Model

### **Tier A: Ethical Guardrails Only** (Recommended Default)

**Equity Split**: 49% Creator / 51% LianaBanyan (LB)

**Creator Veto Rights**:
- Human rights violations (forced labor, weapons for authoritarian regimes)
- Illegal activity (fraud, money laundering, sanctioned entities)
- Direct contradiction of LB charter values

**Why This Works**:
- Maximizes IP utilization (LB can move fast)
- Protects creator from extreme reputational harm
- Prevents "frivolous blocking" (must cite specific violation)
- **Economic Outcome**: Smaller equity % but MUCH larger absolute value

**Ideal For**:
- Creators who prioritize impact/scale over control
- General-purpose technologies with broad applications
- Creators confident in LB's ethical standards

**Anti-Shelving Protections**:
- **Veto Power**: Creator can block "strategic shelving" (buying IP to suppress competition)
- **Reversion Clause**: If LB doesn't commercialize within 18 months, rights revert to 60/40 creator favor
- **Max Dormancy**: 365 days max without active development or external licensing
- **Creator Voting Weight**: Creator gets 2x voting weight on decisions affecting their IP
- **External Licensing Approval**: Creator must approve any license to non-LB entities
- **Commercialization Timeline Transparency**: Creator receives quarterly reports on IP usage plans

**Tier C Downgrade Penalty**:
- If arbitrator rules creator is frivolously blocking (discrimination, anti-competitive behavior), IP reverts to Tier A for 1 year
- Repeat offenders: Permanent Tier A status

---

### **Tier B: Category Restrictions** (Recommended for Most Creators)

**Equity Split**: 60% Creator / 40% LB

**Creator Control**:
- Pre-select up to **5 prohibited use categories**
  - Political campaigns (specific parties or all politics)
  - Adult/pornographic applications
  - Military/defense applications
  - Religious organization exclusive use
  - Specific industry verticals (e.g., tobacco, fossil fuels)
  - Cultural/social movements the creator opposes
- **Auto-Approval Timeout**: If LB proposes a use and creator doesn't respond within 30 days, it's approved
- **One-Time Category Lock**: Categories set at IP submission, can only be modified once per year

**Why This Works**:
- Predictable restrictions (LB knows constraints upfront)
- Reduces case-by-case negotiation overhead
- Still allows 80%+ of potential applications
- **Economic Outcome**: Moderate equity but still significant scale

**Ideal For**:
- Creators with specific value-based concerns
- IP with obvious problematic use cases (e.g., facial recognition tech)
- Stakeholder's 29 patents (chosen tier)

**Frivolous Block Protection**:
- If creator blocks a use OUTSIDE their 5 categories, arbitration can override
- Mediation process available for borderline cases
- **Penalty**: If >3 blocks are overruled in 1 year, creator pays arbitration costs + forfeits 5% equity

**Example Implementation** (Stakeholder):
- **Pre-Declined Categories**: None (creator trusts LB charter + isn't concerned about specific uses)
- **Rationale**: Since LB is chartered to be apolitical and ethical, the Tier A protections + LB's governance are sufficient

---

### **Tier C: Case-by-Case Approval** (Invitation-Only)

**Equity Split**: 75% Creator / 25% LB

**Creator Control**:
- Must approve **every significant commercialization use**
- LB proposes application → Creator has 14 days to approve/deny
- If denied, creator must provide written justification
- If justification is deemed frivolous by mediation panel, creator pays arbitration costs

**Why This Tier Exists (Despite Being Inefficient)**:

LB offers Tier C **only to select creators** where the IP's strategic value outweighs operational friction:

#### **Scenario 1: Exceptionally Rare IP**
- **Example**: Nobel Prize winner wants to contribute quantum computing IP
- **Calculation**: 25% of transformative IP > 51% of nothing
- **Justification**: Some IP is so valuable that even heavy restrictions are worth it

#### **Scenario 2: Strategic Ecosystem Completeness**
- **Example**: LB needs specific patent to enable full product line
- **Calculation**: Unlocking 10 other products justifies one restrictive license
- **Justification**: Portfolio synergies create outsized value

#### **Scenario 3: Network Effects from Creator's Reputation**
- **Example**: Celebrity designer/inventor brings 50,000 followers to LB
- **Calculation**: New member acquisition cost = $500/member → $25M value
- **Justification**: Creator's brand attracts other creators + customers

#### **Scenario 4: Competitive Lockout**
- **Example**: Prevent competitors from accessing critical IP
- **Calculation**: Defensive patent portfolio value > direct commercialization
- **Justification**: Denying rivals access is the primary benefit

#### **Scenario 5: First-Mover Advantage**
- **Example**: Get exclusive access to emerging technology before it becomes mainstream
- **Calculation**: Option value of being first > immediate friction costs
- **Justification**: Strategic positioning for future market

**Operational Reality**:
- Tier C should represent <5% of total IP portfolio
- Used strategically, not as default offering
- **Invitation-only**: LB extends Tier C offers, creators don't request it
- If a creator insists on Tier C and doesn't meet above criteria → LB declines the IP

**Ideal For**:
- High-profile creators with massive reputational value
- Truly irreplaceable IP (no substitutes in market)
- IP so valuable that 25% equity still = huge absolute dollars

**Frivolous Block Protection** (Same as Tier B):
- Mediation for disputed blocks
- Penalty structure for repeated frivolous objections
- Arbitration costs paid by creator if ruled against

## Economic Modeling

### Value Maximization Calculation

Assuming base IP value of **$1M potential revenue**:

| Tier | Creator % | LB % | Utilization Rate | Creator Absolute $ | LB Absolute $ |
|------|-----------|------|------------------|-------------------|---------------|
| **Tier A** | 49% | 51% | 95% | **$465,500** | $484,500 |
| **Tier B** | 60% | 40% | 75% | **$450,000** | $300,000 |
| **Tier C** | 75% | 25% | 40% | **$300,000** | $100,000 |

**Key Insight**: Tier A creator earns MORE absolute dollars despite lower % because utilization is higher.

**Inflection Point**:
- Tier B makes sense if creator values control at **$15,500+ premium**
- Tier C makes sense if creator values control at **$165,500+ premium**

Most creators choose **Tier A or B** when presented with this analysis.

## Implementation in LianaBanyan Database Schema

### New Tables Required

#### 1. `ip_assets`
```sql
CREATE TABLE public.ip_assets (
  id UUID PRIMARY KEY DEFAULT gen_random_uuid(),
  creator_id UUID NOT NULL REFERENCES auth.users(id),
  asset_name TEXT NOT NULL,
  asset_description TEXT,
  asset_type TEXT, -- 'patent', 'copyright', 'trademark', 'trade_secret'
  control_tier TEXT NOT NULL CHECK (control_tier IN ('tier_a', 'tier_b', 'tier_c')),
  equity_split_creator NUMERIC NOT NULL, -- 49, 60, or 75
  equity_split_lb NUMERIC NOT NULL, -- 51, 40, or 25
  prohibited_categories TEXT[], -- For Tier B
  created_at TIMESTAMPTZ DEFAULT NOW(),
  updated_at TIMESTAMPTZ DEFAULT NOW()
);
```

#### 2. `ip_creator_controls` (For Tier A Anti-Shelving)
```sql
CREATE TABLE public.ip_creator_controls (
  id UUID PRIMARY KEY DEFAULT gen_random_uuid(),
  ip_asset_id UUID NOT NULL REFERENCES public.ip_assets(id),
  has_veto_power BOOLEAN DEFAULT true,
  requires_creator_approval_for TEXT[], -- ['shelving', 'external_licensing', 'strategic_pivot']
  min_commercialization_timeline INTEGER DEFAULT 18, -- months
  max_dormancy_days INTEGER DEFAULT 365,
  reversion_clause BOOLEAN DEFAULT true,
  reversion_conditions JSONB, -- {'dormancy_days': 365, 'equity_shift': '60/40'}
  voting_weight_in_lb NUMERIC DEFAULT 2.0,
  can_propose_uses BOOLEAN DEFAULT true,
  created_at TIMESTAMPTZ DEFAULT NOW()
);
```

#### 3. `patent_pool_usage_rights` (For Tier B Sandbox)
```sql
CREATE TABLE public.patent_pool_usage_rights (
  id UUID PRIMARY KEY DEFAULT gen_random_uuid(),
  ip_asset_id UUID NOT NULL REFERENCES public.ip_assets(id),
  
  -- Unlimited Internal Use
  unlimited_internal_use BOOLEAN DEFAULT true,
  can_derive BOOLEAN DEFAULT true,
  can_modify BOOLEAN DEFAULT true,
  can_combine_with_other_pool_ip BOOLEAN DEFAULT true,
  
  -- Authorization Model
  authorization_model TEXT CHECK (authorization_model IN ('dibs_with_premium', 'protest_premium', 'first_come')),
  dibs_holder_id UUID REFERENCES auth.users(id),
  dibs_expiration TIMESTAMPTZ,
  dibs_profit_share NUMERIC, -- Extra % for dibs holder
  unauthorized_use_premium NUMERIC, -- Higher % if used without approval
  protest_offset_penalty NUMERIC, -- Penalty to discourage frivolous protests
  
  -- Revenue Sharing (Tier B: Only applies to LB-connected products)
  applies_to_lb_products_only BOOLEAN DEFAULT true,
  base_revenue_share NUMERIC, -- Standard %
  with_authorization_share NUMERIC, -- If creator approves
  without_authorization_share NUMERIC, -- If used over objection (after arbitration)
  external_license_revenue_share NUMERIC, -- % of revenue from licensing to non-LB entities
  
  created_at TIMESTAMPTZ DEFAULT NOW(),
  updated_at TIMESTAMPTZ DEFAULT NOW()
);
```

#### 4. `ip_use_proposals` (For Tier B & C Approval Workflow)
```sql
CREATE TABLE public.ip_use_proposals (
```

## Governance & Mediation Process

### Dispute Resolution Flow

#### **Step 1: Informal Mediation** (7 days)
- LB and Creator discuss proposed use
- Attempt to find compromise (modify use case, add restrictions, etc.)
- 80% of disputes resolve here

#### **Step 2: Formal Arbitration** (14 days)
- Assign neutral 3rd party arbitrator from LB's ethics panel
- Both parties submit written arguments
- Arbitrator reviews against tier-specific criteria
- Binding decision issued

#### **Step 3: Penalties for Frivolous Actions**
- **If Creator loses**: Pay arbitration costs ($500-$2000) + 5% equity forfeit if >3 overruled blocks/year
- **If LB loses**: Pay arbitration costs + creator gets 10% bonus equity for that specific use case

### Tier C Downgrade Protection
- If creator is ruled against in **2+ arbitration cases within 1 year** for discriminatory/anti-competitive blocking
- IP automatically downgrades to **Tier A for 12 months**
- After 12 months of good behavior, can request upgrade back to Tier C
- **Permanent Tier A**: If 3+ arbitration losses in any 2-year period

## Why This Framework is Academically Sound

### 1. **Mechanism Design Theory** (Hurwicz, Nobel Prize 2007)
- Incentive compatibility: Tiers align payoffs with behaviors
- Self-selection: Creators reveal preferences through tier choice
- Truth-telling: No incentive to misrepresent control needs

### 2. **Transaction Cost Economics** (Coase, Nobel Prize 1991)
- Tier A minimizes transaction costs (LB has operational freedom)
- Tier C acknowledges some IP is worth high transaction costs
- Market efficiency via granular choice

### 3. **Behavioral Contract Theory**
- Perceived fairness increases compliance
- "Option value" of choosing your tier reduces litigation
- Ethical alignment reduces moral hazard

### 4. **Platform Economics**
- Two-sided market: Attract both control-seeking and scale-seeking creators
- Liquidity benefits: More total IP in pool = more cross-pollination
- Network effects: Diverse IP types strengthen ecosystem
