# Marketplace Catalog Strategy (3D Printed Miniatures, Cosplay, Functional Products)

Last updated: 2025-10-14
Owner: Product Team

## Goals
- Enable discovery-first browsing on .com with clear pathways to invest, pre-order, or request service.
- Align categories to gamer/collector mental models while staying manufacturable for B2B/B2C.

## Tier 1 Categories
1) Tabletop Gaming
- Fantasy Miniatures
- Sci‑Fi Miniatures
- Historical Miniatures
- Terrain & Scenery
- Game-Compatible Collections (e.g., Grimdark/OPR-compatible)

2) Cosplay & Wearables
- Full Armor Sets
- Helmets & Headpieces
- Props & Weapons
- Accessories & Details
- Functional Wearables (lightweight, durable)

3) Collectibles & Display
- Figurines & Statues
- Character Busts
- Diorama Bases

4) Functional Products
- Gaming Accessories (dice towers, trays, token holders)
- Home/Office (organizers, mounts)
- Tools & Fixtures

## Cross-Filters (Tier 2)
- Scale: 28mm, 32mm, 75mm, Full‑size
- Print Complexity: Beginner, Intermediate, Advanced
- Production Stage: Germination → Harvest
- License Type: Original IP, Compatible, Licensed

## Sourcing & Manufacturing Notes
- STL provenance tracked in backend; enforce license metadata.
- Pilot runs sized by "production level" voting; tie to unit price/volume discounts.
- Cosplay armor: prioritize modular segmentation, adjustable sizing, and finishing guides.

## Next Steps
- Seed categories into DB, connect BrowseMarketplace to dynamic categories.
- Define per-category landing pages (SEO: title, meta, H1).
- Add "Apply to Manufacture" CTA for promising Etsy‑style designs.
