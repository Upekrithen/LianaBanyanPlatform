# Crown Invitation — Tatiana Schlossburg Health Accords

Dear Ms. Schlossburg,

**We are inviting you to wear the Crown Health Accord Steward of Liana Banyan Initiative #6 — the Tatiana Schlossburg Health Accords.** This is not a ceremonial role. It is a working seat at the head of the cooperative healthcare-access architecture we have built into the platform substrate, and we have named it in your honor because the work you have already done in the environmental-health and climate-health space is the operating proof that the Initiative needs as its foundation.

There is a principle we have built this Initiative around:

> *"The health of a cooperative community is its most fundamental form of shared wealth."*

That principle is not aspirational. It is structural — the reason a cooperative platform cannot function at full capacity if the members who make it work are rationing insulin, skipping preventive care, or managing chronic conditions without reliable access to the healthcare system. Health is not a benefit layer on top of the cooperative economy. It is the substrate on which the cooperative economy runs.

Your work on the environmental and climate dimensions of health — the intersection of community conditions, systemic policy, and health outcomes — is precisely the lens the Health Accords needs at its head. The Initiative is not about building a health insurance alternative. It is about building the cooperative-infrastructure conditions under which community health improves structurally, not just at the individual-benefit level.

## What Liana Banyan is, structurally

Liana Banyan is a member-sovereign cooperative substrate. The terms are canonical:

- **Membership is $5/year.** That is the entire price of admission.
- **Creators and workers may earn 83.3%** of what they generate on the platform.
- **Margin on goods and services routed through the substrate is held to Cost+20%** — a hard ceiling, not a target.
- **No advertising layer. No data resale. No engagement-extraction machinery.** Members own their relationships and their work.

The Corporation (Wyoming, filed 2025-11-21) holds 19 USPTO provisional patent applications protecting the substrate primitives. The substrate is built to serve 16 Initiatives — coordinated cooperative responses to the categories of need that markets have systematically failed. Each Initiative is led by a Crown: a person whose body of work has already proved the cooperative thesis in their own field.

## The Tatiana Schlossburg Health Accords Initiative

The Health Accords Initiative (#6) is the cooperative platform's healthcare-policy and healthcare-access architecture. It does three things:

**First: it negotiates.** The Accord-class agreement framework enables the cooperative — representing the aggregated healthcare decisions and spending of its member base — to negotiate with healthcare providers, pharmaceutical supply chains, and community-health infrastructure at cooperative-purchasing scale. Not as individual patients, but as a cooperative constituency.

**Second: it coordinates.** The Health Accords connects the cooperative's healthcare-access primitives across the platform's other health-adjacent Initiatives — composing with Initiative #7 MSA (Medical Savings Accounts, Crown: Cathie Mahon) for cooperative healthcare-savings infrastructure, and with the Rally Group (#9) for crisis-response and mental-health routing.

**Third: it holds to account.** The Health Accords is the platform's mechanism for health-outcomes accountability — tracking, at the cooperative-community level, whether the platform's structure actually improves the health conditions of its members, and surfacing that data to the community in the cooperative's transparent-accounting discipline.

## The Crown Health Accord Steward seat

The Crown is the working voice for the Initiative's direction. Practically:

- **Setting the policy framework** for the cooperative's healthcare-access negotiations — what the Accord-class agreement standard requires from healthcare partners.
- **Shaping** the health-outcomes accountability methodology — how the cooperative measures and reports on community health as a cooperative metric.
- **Composing** with Cathie Mahon (Crown MSA · cooperative healthcare-savings) on the financial-access side of the health architecture.
- **Connecting** the cooperative's health-policy infrastructure to the broader environmental and systemic conditions that determine community health — the lens you have spent your career developing.
- **Mentoring** the community health infrastructure that the Accord class trains and credentiates.
