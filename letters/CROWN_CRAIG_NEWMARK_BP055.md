# Crown Invitation — Rally Group / Civic Cooperative

Dear Mr. Newmark,

**We are inviting you to wear the Crown Civic Infrastructure Steward of Liana Banyan Initiative #9 — The Rally Group.** This is a working seat at the head of a cooperative civic-engagement and community-safety network, and we believe the invitation is earned by a structural argument that runs directly through the thing you built three decades ago.

Craigslist was the original peer-to-peer cooperative marketplace.

Not in aspiration. In practice. It proved that if you give people a direct channel to each other — unmediated by advertising, unencumbered by dark patterns, structurally simple and structurally honest — they will build local community around it. They will find apartments, jobs, furniture, childcare, musical instruments, rides, and neighbors. The platform stays out of the way; the community does the work; the community gets the surplus.

Liana Banyan is the evolved form of that thesis, extended from marketplace to cooperative economy: sixteen Initiatives covering the categories of need that markets have systematically underserved — food, housing, healthcare, family, education, manufacturing, music, civic life — all operating on a member-sovereign substrate with the same structural commitments Craigslist proved: no surveillance, no engagement extraction, community-owned value exchange.

The Rally Group (#9) is where the civic-engagement dimension of that evolution lives.

## What Liana Banyan is, structurally

Liana Banyan is a member-sovereign cooperative substrate. The terms are canonical:

- **Membership is $5/year.** That is the entire price of admission.
- **Creators and workers may earn 83.3%** of what they generate on the platform.
- **Margin on goods and services routed through the substrate is held to Cost+20%** — a hard ceiling, not a target.
- **No advertising layer. No data resale. No engagement-extraction machinery.** Members own their relationships and their work.

The Corporation (Wyoming, filed 2025-11-21) holds 19 USPTO provisional patent applications protecting the substrate primitives. The substrate serves 16 Initiatives, each led by a Crown whose body of work has already proved the cooperative thesis in practice.

## The Rally Group Initiative

The Rally Group is the cooperative community-safety and civic-engagement layer present on every page of Liana Banyan, backed by trained members anywhere the platform operates. The philosophy:

> *"We'd rather check on you 100 times for nothing than miss the one time you need us."*

The Initiative is organized around three structures:

- **Responders** — trained members embedded in every Initiative who recognize when community members need support and route them to the right help.
- **Conductors** — senior coordinators for complex situations requiring sustained engagement.
- **The Railroad Crossing Signal** — a universal "help is available here" presence on every platform page, every Guild, every cooperative business.

The Rally Group composes with Defense Klaus (#8, personal-safety infrastructure), Harper Guild (#12, cooperative HR and ethics), and Power to the People (#15, cooperative civic-participation tools). Together these Initiatives describe the full civic-infrastructure layer of the cooperative platform.

## The Craigslist-to-LB structural argument

What Craigslist proved in 1995 is the same thing the Rally Group proves in 2026: local community is the most reliable form of mutual aid, and the platform's job is to enable it, not extract from it.

Craigslist's refusal to maximize revenue at the expense of community utility is not a business model failure. It is a structural commitment — the same commitment LB encodes at the bylaw level. The platform takes Cost+20% and no more. The community keeps the rest.

The Rally Group is where that commitment becomes civic infrastructure: the organized capacity of the community to check on its members, route help to the people who need it, and hold the cooperative's ethical and safety standards across every cooperative business and Guild.

Your body of work — Craigslist as a three-decade proof of the peer-to-peer community-utility model, and Craig Newmark Philanthropies' focus on journalism, veterans, and civic engagement — is the operating track record this Initiative needs at its head.

## The Crown Civic Infrastructure Steward seat

The Crown is the working voice for the Initiative's civic direction. Practically:

- **Setting the standard** for the Rally Group's community-engagement and community-safety training across the platform's member base.
- **Connecting** the cooperative's civic infrastructure to the journalism, veterans, and civic-engagement work Craig Newmark Philanthropies has funded — and identifying where the cooperative platform can amplify that work.
- **Shaping** the cooperative's relationship to the broader civic-engagement ecosystem — the organizations, institutions, and movements that the Rally Group can compose with, rather than duplicate.
- **Mentoring** the Conductor and Responder class who run the community-safety layer day to day.
