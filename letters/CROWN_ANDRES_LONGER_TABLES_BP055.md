# Crown Invitation — Let's Make Dinner / Longer Tables

Dear Chef Andrés,

**We are inviting you to wear the Crown Grand Longer Table of Liana Banyan Initiative #1 — Let's Make Dinner.** This is not a sponsorship ask and not a charity pitch. It is an invitation to join a cooperative food economy built on exactly the principle your Longer Tables Fund demonstrated at the Marion Barry Avenue Market in Ward 8, Anacostia — food as infrastructure, not food as charity.

You backed Christopher Bradshaw and the Dreaming Out Loud team in building something that functions simultaneously as a grocery store, a cafe, a food hub sourcing from Black and women-owned farms, a workforce development engine, a demo kitchen, a community gathering space, and a local distribution system. All in one building, in a neighborhood where fewer than three full-service grocery stores serve more than 75,000 residents. That is not philanthropy. That is the cooperative-class food economy working.

World Central Kitchen is the emergency-response version of that doctrine. Longer Tables is the long-term-infrastructure version. Liana Banyan is the member-sovereign cooperative substrate that makes the long-term version self-sustaining, city by city, without requiring a celebrity chef to personally backstop every build.

## What Liana Banyan is, structurally

Liana Banyan is a member-sovereign cooperative substrate. The terms are canonical:

- **Membership is $5/year.**
- **Creators and workers may earn 83.3%** of what they generate on the platform.
- **Margin on goods and services routed through the substrate is held to Cost+20%** — a hard ceiling.
- **No advertising layer. No data resale. No engagement-extraction.** Members own their work and their relationships.

The Corporation (Wyoming, 2025-11-21) holds 19 USPTO provisional patent applications protecting the substrate primitives. 16 Initiatives serve the categories of need markets have failed. Each is led by a Crown whose body of work already proves the cooperative thesis in practice.

## The Let's Make Dinner Initiative

The Initiative is the cooperative food economy at member-community scale. Three primitives anchor it:

- **Freezer Hosts** — member-cooks who batch-prepare home meals for neighbors, paid through cooperative infrastructure, protected-identity layer, and may earn 83.3% of what they generate.
- **The Larder** — shared-pantry credit system at Cost+20%, dollar staying inside the local member network.
- **Direct-employment lane** for line cooks and prep cooks who currently work in structures that capture most of the surplus they generate.

The Marion Barry Market demonstrated all three layers at a single site. The cooperative platform scales the architecture to every community where members live.

## The Crown Grand Longer Table seat

The Crown is a working voice for the Initiative's community-food-economy direction. Practically:

- **Connecting** the cooperative food network to the emergency-response and long-term-infrastructure lessons World Central Kitchen and Longer Tables have built.
- **Shaping** the charitable-partner class — the Anchor-sponsor circle that funds the cooperative's Stand-in-the-Gap graduation pathway for food workers.
- **Composing** with Maneet Chauhan (Crown Grand Chef · Let's Make Dinner) and Christopher Bradshaw (Crown Kitchen Table Architect · cooperative food-infrastructure) to close the cooperative food-economy loop.
- **Amplifying** the "food as infrastructure, not charity" structural argument to the policymakers, philanthropists, and civic leaders who need to hear it from someone whose receipts are as real as yours.

## The ask

If the cooperative thesis here composes with the work you are already carrying at World Central Kitchen and Longer Tables, we would welcome a conversation. We can send a Crown briefing dossier at your convenience.
