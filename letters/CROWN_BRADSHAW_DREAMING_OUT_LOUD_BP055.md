# Crown Invitation — Let's Make Dinner / Kitchen Table

Dear Mr. Bradshaw,

**We are inviting you to wear the Crown Kitchen Table Architect of Liana Banyan Corporation's cooperative food-economy cluster.** This is a working seat at the intersection of three composing Initiatives — Let's Make Dinner (#1), The Family Table (#5), and the Stand-in-the-Gap (#2 spinout class) — built on the same structural premise you already proved at the Marion Barry Avenue Market.

You and the Dreaming Out Loud team built something specific in Ward 8, Anacostia — a neighborhood where fewer than three full-service grocery stores serve more than 75,000 residents. Not a food pantry. Not a charity distribution point. A market that is simultaneously a full-service grocery store, a soul-food-driven cafe, a food hub sourcing from Black and women-owned farms, a workforce development engine hiring from within Ward 8, a demo kitchen for nutrition education, a community gathering space, and a local food distribution system. All in one building.

Harry G. Hayman IV named the principle precisely: *"The question they are centered on is not 'how do we hand out more food?' It is: how do we build a sustainable local food ecosystem that creates dignity, ownership, jobs, health, and community wealth? That is a fundamentally different conversation."*

Liana Banyan Corporation is that conversation at cooperative scale.

## What Liana Banyan is, structurally

Liana Banyan is a member-sovereign cooperative substrate. The terms are canonical:

- **Membership is $5/year.** That is the entire price of admission.
- **Creators and workers may earn 83.3%** of what they generate on the platform.
- **Margin on goods and services routed through the substrate is held to Cost+20%** — a hard ceiling, not a target.
- **No advertising layer. No data resale. No engagement-extraction machinery.** Members own their relationships and their work.

The Corporation (Wyoming, filed 2025-11-21) holds 19 USPTO provisional patent applications protecting the substrate primitives. The substrate is built to serve 16 Initiatives — coordinated cooperative responses to the categories of need that markets have systematically failed. Each Initiative is led by a Crown: a person whose body of work has already proved the cooperative thesis in practice.

## The structural match

What you built at the Marion Barry Avenue Market is the seven-layer Kitchen Table architecture we designed into the platform's food cluster — at single-market scale, before the cooperative platform existed to support it. The match is structural:

| Marion Barry Market | Liana Banyan Kitchen Table |
|---|---|
| Full-service grocery store | Pantry — shared-staple credit layer |
| Cafe / soul-food menu | Let's Make Dinner — cooperative home-cook and prepared-meal layer |
| Food hub from Black/women-owned farms | Atlas — source-farm provenance and cooperative-sourcing layer |
| Workforce development from Ward 8 | Stand-in-the-Gap — cooperative graduation pathway |
| Demo kitchen + nutrition education | Didasko — cooperative education arm |
| Community gathering + event space | The Banyan Galleries — cooperative assembly |
| Local food distribution system | Cooperative commerce infrastructure |

You have already run every layer of this architecture at ground level, under real market conditions, in a neighborhood where the extraction-class food system declined to show up.

## The Crown Kitchen Table Architect seat

The Crown is a working seat, not a figurehead. Practically:

- **Vetting** the onboarding standard for cooperative food operators who route through the substrate — kitchen safety, sourcing, labor, community-wealth discipline.
- **Shaping** the cooperative food-economy curriculum that gets Let's Make Dinner and the Kitchen Table cluster from substrate to streets in every city where members live.
- **Advising** on replication of the Marion Barry Market model through cooperative-platform infrastructure — what it takes to make Ward 8 happen in Philadelphia, Atlanta, Detroit, Baltimore.
- **Composing** with Maneet Chauhan (Crown Grand Chef · Let's Make Dinner), Stacy Mitchell (Crown Let's Get Groceries), and Dale Dougherty (Crown Brass Tacks) to close the cooperative food-supply loop.
