## The Family Table — What It Is

The Family Table (#5, Sweet Sixteen) is a cooperative platform for intergenerational connection, shared resources, and cooperative family-support infrastructure. Practically, it enables:

- **Meal coordination:** Family members coordinate home-cooked meals across generations, compensating the cook cooperatively (see: Let's Make Dinner, Initiative #1)
- **Caregiving coordination:** Elder care, child care, and mutual support organized cooperatively — caregivers earn 83.3% of the transaction; no extraction
- **Resource sharing:** The family unit as a cooperative: shared tools, shared transportation, shared knowledge
- **Knowledge transfer:** Elders contribute skills and expertise as platform content; they accumulate Marks and cooperative standing

## The Economics

Liana Banyan is not a charitable platform. It is a cooperative technology company. The economics:

- **Members may earn 83.3%** of every transaction value on the platform (never 83%)
- **Platform margin:** cost + 20% — transparent, bounded, cooperative
- **Membership:** $5 per year — the lowest possible barrier to full participation
- **No equity extraction:** The cooperative structure protects the community from the extractive dynamics that destroyed the sharing economy

A grandmother teaching her granddaughter to make tamales on The Family Table does not do it for free. She does it in a cooperative economic context that acknowledges her contribution as real value.
