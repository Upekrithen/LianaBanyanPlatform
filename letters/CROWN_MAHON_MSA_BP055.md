## WHY YOU FOR MSA

In 1999, when the National Federation of Community Development Credit Unions was navigating a sector that had been declared too risky, too thin, and too dispersed to matter, a generation of credit-union leaders chose to build the federation rather than fold into the conventional banking structure. You chose to build it. In the years since, Inclusiv — under your leadership as President and CEO — has grown to represent more than 500 community development credit unions, serving over 20 million people in communities that the conventional banking system has consistently defined as unbankable.

That is not advocacy. That is evidence. It is the operating proof that cooperative-class financial infrastructure works for the populations who need it most — not as a charity supplement to real banking, but as the real banking for communities the extraction-class institutions decline to serve.

Medical savings is the next iteration of that work. The same cooperative-class savings discipline. The same trust architecture: member-owned, democratically governed, transparent accounting. The same population: the people who are told by conventional healthcare finance that their savings are too small, their credit too thin, their needs too dispersed to matter. The Member Service Account applies the credit-union model — which you have stewarded for decades — to healthcare-savings instruments at member-cooperative scale.

You are the person for MSA because you have already built the institutional proof that the model works. The platform provides the substrate; what it needs is the standard-setter who has held the cooperative-savings line under every kind of pressure the conventional banking establishment can generate.

## What MSA is

**MSA = Medical Savings Accounts** — cooperative-class healthcare-savings infrastructure built into the platform substrate.

The mechanics:
- Members save in cooperative-currency instruments (Marks, Joules, Credits)
- Savings route through Substitution at participating healthcare merchants — urgent care, pharmacies, telemedicine providers, cooperative health practitioners
- Never fiat conversion at any boundary (preserves the cooperative's non-bank, non-health-insurer regulatory status by design)
- Cooperative-rate-negotiation with healthcare-provider class at aggregated-member-savings volume

MSA composes with:
- **Initiative #6 Tatiana Schlossburg Health Accords** — cooperative healthcare-policy and healthcare-access architecture; MSA funds what #6 negotiates
- **Initiative #10 VSL (Jessica Jackley)** — peer-to-peer cooperative microfinance; complementary cooperative-finance Crown
- **Initiative #11 Let's Make Bread (Muhammad Yunus)** — macro-institutional cooperative microfinance; the third leg of the cooperative finance trio

The three cooperative-finance Crowns (#7 + #10 + #11) cover three model-classes at three scales: credit-union (you), peer-to-peer (Jackley), and macro-institutional (Yunus). They are not redundant. They are the full architecture of cooperative finance at every scale the platform serves.
