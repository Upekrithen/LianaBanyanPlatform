## §2 — Foundational Principles

Liana Banyan Corporation is a Wyoming-chartered cooperative with a 50-year dissipation clause. The cooperative's purpose is to advance and preserve cooperative AI memory architecture as a public good while sustaining the substrate's continued development and the founders' ability to continue working.

These principles inform every trademark decision:

1. **Patent layer is the actual IP moat.** Our 19+ USPTO provisional patents and Cooperative Defensive Patent Pledge #2260 protect the substantive inventions. Trademarks are about identity and origin, not substance.
2. **Ubiquity is desired.** We want our terminology to enter the broader vocabulary of cooperative AI memory architecture. Every generic use cites us as the canonical origin.
3. **Weaponization must be prevented.** We register marks defensively to prevent third parties from filing them against us. We do not register to suppress use.
4. **Cooperative-class users get maximum freedom.** Individuals, cooperatives, academic institutions, journalists, and downstream developers receive the broadest possible permitted use.
5. **Bad-faith actors face enforcement.** Competitors attempting to brand-confuse consumers about the canonical CAI implementation or its cooperative origin will face full enforcement.

## §3 — Permissive Use Policy (Architectural Marks)

Applies to: **CAI™ · Caithedral™ · Banyan Metric™ · Eblet™ · TriFork™ · NotCents™**

Liana Banyan Corporation explicitly permits and encourages the following uses of architectural marks **without prior permission, license fee, or attribution requirement** (though attribution is appreciated):

### §3.1 Expressly Permitted Uses

1. **Academic citation** — papers, theses, dissertations, course materials, conference presentations, technical reviews
2. **Journalistic use** — news articles, op-eds, podcasts, video reviews, blog posts, social media discussion
3. **Derivative implementation** — building your own software that implements CAI / Caithedral / Eblet primitives, TriFork ownership structure, NotCents currency abstraction, or Banyan Metric scoring
4. **Interoperability claims** — "compatible with CAI™" · "implements Eblet™ format" · "Caithedral™-compatible cache layer" · "scored using Banyan Metric™" · "TriFork™ ownership model"
5. **"Powered by" attribution** — "Powered by CAI™" · "Built on Caithedral™ retrieval" · "Eblet™ substrate inside"
6. **Common-language reference** — using these terms as generic terms for cooperative AI memory architecture in technical writing, slides, conversations
7. **Educational content** — tutorials, courses, books, videos teaching the architecture
8. **Open-source forks** — forking the public repositories under SSPL v1 license terms

### §3.2 Linux-Model Defensive Stance

We model our enforcement policy on the Linux Foundation's stewardship of the Linux® trademark. Linus Torvalds and the Linux Foundation register the mark defensively to prevent third-party squatters, while permitting and encouraging widespread generic and derivative use. We do the same.

If a Liana Banyan trademark becomes the generic term for the technology it names (the "Xerox/Aspirin/Escalator effect"), we accept that outcome. We are the canonical implementation. Every generic use cites us as origin. The cooperative substrate succeeds when the terminology is ubiquitous.

### §3.3 The Only Use We Reserve Enforcement For

Within architectural marks, enforcement is reserved exclusively for:

- **Competitor brand-confusion** — another company calling its core product "CAI" or "Caithedral" or "TriFork" in a way that would mislead consumers about the canonical origin
- **Counterfeit installer distribution** — third parties distributing modified installers under our marks without authorization
- **Misrepresentation of certification** — claiming "official CAI implementation" or "Liana Banyan certified" without authorization
- **Fraudulent endorsement** — claiming Liana Banyan Corporation or the cooperative endorses a product when no endorsement exists

All other uses of architectural marks are explicitly welcomed.

## §3A — TM Convention for Architectural Marks

The following marks identify structural components of the Liana Banyan platform and carry the conventions below across all documents, installers, and public-facing material:

- **Caithedral™** — the AI Cathedral substrate architecture
- **TriFork™** — the three-tine cooperative ownership structure
- **NotCents™** — the cooperative-currency abstraction (Đ symbol)
- **Banyan Metric™** — the cooperative performance measurement system

### §3A.1 First-Use Rule

Architectural marks carry **™** on first use in any:
- Section heading or document title
- Product name or feature label
- Formal description (installer copy, press release, filing)

Body prose and technical documentation **may omit ™ after the first use per-document**. The ™ is not required on every occurrence — only the first appearance establishes the mark within a given document.

### §3A.2 Fair-Use Carve-Out

™ is **NOT required** in:
- FAQ body text and user-facing prose descriptions
- Help text, tooltips, or error messages
- Inline code, command-line output, or configuration values
- Natural-language references in conversation (e.g., "run the Caithedral cache")
